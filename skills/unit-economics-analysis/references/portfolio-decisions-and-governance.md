# Portfolio Decisions And Governance

Unit economics informs a bounded decision. It does not authorize channel spend, pricing, customer treatment, product changes, staffing, forecasts, or publication.

## Build Local Views First

Calculate compatible economics by acquisition cohort, spend band, product, offer, price, channel, campaign, sales motion, segment, market, language, locale, and marketplace side before aggregating. For every view show:

- original acquired entities and absolute retained entities;
- direct, allocated, accounting, cash, attributed, incremental, and marginal costs;
- observed revenue, gross profit, contribution, cash, and compatible forecast remainder;
- CAC variants, accounting payback, cash payback, and LTV compatibility;
- maturity, censoring, source coverage, uncertainty, calibration, and residuals;
- capacity, saturation, concentration, overlap, cannibalization, dependency, customer value, and risk.

Show aggregate weights and within-group results. A portfolio average can improve because mix shifts toward a high-value segment while every segment weakens, or decline while each local segment improves. Preserve segment reversals.

## Shared Systems And Overlap

Define customer overlap, cross-device and cross-product identity, organic and paid overlap, view-through and click-through overlap, cross-promotion, partner influence, sales assistance, shared creative, tooling, data, brand, product, support, and infrastructure. Allocate only with a decision-relevant driver and show an unallocated residual.

Keep fixed capacity, step costs, committed contracts, sunk investments, avoidable costs, transfer pricing, legal entities, taxes, and cash obligations visible. An allocation can support a portfolio view without making the cost incremental or avoidable.

## Freemium And Product-Led Paths

Model:

```text
eligible reach
-> signup or install
-> qualified activation
-> retained free value
-> qualified conversion opportunity
-> paying user or account
-> retained payer contribution and cash
```

Report cost per signup, activated user, paying entity, and retained payer separately. Define user-to-account identity, conversion and maturation windows, self-serve and assisted paths, free-user delivery and support cost, abuse, referral or collaboration value, and customer overlap. Do not compare signup cost directly with payer LTV.

## Marketplace And Network Economics

Build side-specific contracts for buyers, sellers, creators, consumers, advertisers, suppliers, or other roles. Separate acquisition and incentive costs, participant retention, availability, liquidity, successful interaction, order, transaction, GMV, take rate, refunds, platform revenue, contribution, and cash.

Do not add sides as one customer denominator or call GMV revenue. Define which side pays, which side receives an incentive, whether one side's cost is the other's value, how shared transactions are reconciled, and how multi-homing, congestion, quality, fraud, and local density change marginal economics. Route network-effect strategy to `network-effects-strategy`.

## Decision Brief

For each decision state return:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Decision | Owner, deadline, reversibility, and authority boundary |
| Comparable evidence | Entity, cohort, basis, market, horizon, maturity, source, and coverage |
| Economics | Absolute cost, value, contribution, cash, CAC, payback, and compatible LTV |
| Marginal view | Observed spend band, incremental evidence, saturation, capacity, and uncertainty |
| Risk | Concentration, dependency, overlap, cannibalization, forecast error, and customer harm |
| Gate | Threshold, trigger, counterevidence, smallest next evidence, expiry, and owner |
| Action | Approval-ready specialist handoff, monitoring, rollback, and re-read date |

Use `not comparable`, `evidence required`, `investigate`, `hold`, `bounded test candidate`, `eligible for specialist allocation review`, `refresh`, or `retire model` unless the user supplies an approved vocabulary. These states are not operating approvals.

## Targets, Benchmarks, And Causality

Keep observed result, attributed output, incremental estimate, forecast, scenario, target, benchmark, and approval separate. Record a benchmark's publisher, original source, population, market, product topology, entity, cost and value definitions, period, date, distribution, and known selection limits.

Do not backsolve retention, margin, LTV, CAC, or payback to meet a target. A ratio changing after a campaign, product release, or price change does not identify cause. Preserve customer mix, seasonality, cost timing, attribution, product, price, capacity, market, and measurement alternatives.

## Privacy, Fairness, And Model Governance

Prefer aggregate, permissioned, redacted, pseudonymous, and small-cell-suppressed cohorts. Record purpose, authority, minimization, access, lineage, retention, deletion, correction, fairness review, audit, fallback, incident, rollback, and retirement.

Do not request direct identifiers, exact location, private messages, employee notes, complete payment histories, protected traits, or device graphs when aggregates suffice. Do not rank individuals, reduce service or opportunity, personalize price, auto-bid, or route customers from predicted economics without separate authorization and appropriate privacy, fairness, finance, deployment, and monitoring controls.

## Market Transfer

Revalidate customer and payer entities, product, offer, contract, price, discount, channel, platform, attribution, acquisition, payment, invoice, tax, refund, currency, FX, revenue, costs, contribution, cash, retention, identity, consent, storage, transfer, source coverage, model calibration, and owner for every market.

Translation and currency conversion do not validate a local model. For mainland China, use current direct sources and locally accountable review for time-sensitive platform, payment, app, data, advertising, and provider conditions. Do not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, or provider conclusions.
