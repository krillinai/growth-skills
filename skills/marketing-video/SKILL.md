# Marketing Video

Turn one bounded audience decision into truthful, comprehensible, accessible video and purposeful adaptations. Optimize qualified customer understanding and downstream value, not video volume, opening-hook metrics, watch time, engagement, attributed conversion, or production polish alone.

Read [video-contract.md](references/video-contract.md) before accepting product, claim, script, footage, person, voice, music, brand, channel, or rights evidence. Read [scripting-storyboard-and-production.md](references/scripting-storyboard-and-production.md) before scripting, storyboarding, recording, generating, editing, or compositing. Read [localization-rights-and-accessibility.md](references/localization-rights-and-accessibility.md) before translation, subtitling, dubbing, voice work, market adaptation, or accessibility review. Read [delivery-qa-and-measurement.md](references/delivery-qa-and-measurement.md) before rendering, delivery, QA, performance analysis, or retirement. ## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `brief` | Freeze the communication, evidence, production, rights, delivery, and learning contract |
| `script` | Create or revise the script, narrative beats, storyboard, shot list, source plan, and acceptance criteria |
| `create` | Produce or render local video files through separately available capabilities under the frozen contract |
| `adapt` | Translate, subtitle, dub, reframe, shorten, or reconstruct an existing video for a declared placement or market |
| `audit` | Review supplied video, project, script, files, rights, formats, accessibility, lineage, or performance evidence |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, audience, customer situation, video role, channel, placement, market, deadline, and external-action boundary. A requested output format is not a mode. If no suitable production capability is available, complete the production-ready handoff rather than pretending a video was created.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder or platform statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Missing private evidence is unavailable, not a penalty and not permission to invent it.

Never fabricate product behavior, UI, customer identity, quotation, testimonial, result, statistic, review, rating, certification, award, scarcity, price, endorsement, news coverage, documentary event, source ownership, release, platform rule, or performance. A conceptual or future-state product sequence must be visibly labeled and cannot imply current availability.

## Separate Concept, Narrative, Edit, And Format

Use this lineage:

```text
audience situation x problem or belief x value pillar x proof mechanism
-> concept and intended decision
-> narrative beats and production treatment
-> edit variants
-> format and localization adaptations
-> placement, destination, and qualified outcome
```

A concept changes the audience, problem, belief, value, objection, offer, proof, or explanatory mechanism. A narrative treatment changes how that concept unfolds through demonstration, story, presenter, interview, animation, or comparison. An edit variant changes hook, opening shot, pacing, sequence, duration, CTA treatment, music, caption treatment, or B-roll while preserving the hypothesis. A format adaptation changes ratio, crop, safe area, resolution, language, subtitle or dubbing treatment, thumbnail, or placement packaging. File count is not concept diversity.

## Script From Product Truth

Choose the video's job: demonstrate a real workflow, explain a mechanism, answer an objection, show attributable customer evidence, establish a use context, present an offer, educate, document, support recall, or another explicit role. When customers need to inspect the product, show an approved real product state rather than generic atmosphere or fabricated UI.

Map every spoken line, on-screen claim, demonstration, result, quote, statistic, comparison, and disclaimer to an attributable source and approval. Build narrative beats around the intended decision, not a universal hook formula. Do not invent a mandatory duration, opening-seconds rule, shot count, caption style, posting volume, or benchmark.

Create a script, storyboard, and shot list detailed enough to reveal missing proof, footage, rights, product states, graphics, audio, localization, and accessibility before production. Keep spoken words, on-screen text, visuals, sound, source, time, and acceptance criteria synchronized.

## Produce From Traceable Sources

Use supplied or properly evidenced footage, approved product capture, attributable data, licensed or commissioned media, and controlled generation. Record source, owner, rights scope, edits, model or tool, prompt or instruction, output ID, date, and lineage where available.

Do not prompt for a living artist's exact style, counterfeit brand media, fake customers or documentary evidence, deceptive body or product outcomes, non-consensual intimate or sensitive depiction, or misleading impersonation. Do not reproduce competitor videos, remove watermarks or disclosures, expose private information, or treat public availability as reuse permission.

Use approved recordings for exact product behavior, logos, UI, charts, customer testimony, and factual events. Clearly disclose synthetic or materially altered people, voices, footage, or scenes when required by the contract. Never clone or imitate a voice, face, identity, or creator delivery without attributable scoped permission.

## Create Or Adapt Locally When Available

In `create` or `adapt` mode, use separately available video, audio, image, transcription, translation, dubbing, or rendering capabilities only after resolving the essential product, source, rights, person, voice, music, claim, channel, and output constraints. Preserve originals, work on local copies, create a small purposeful set, inspect the rendered files, and record the manifest.

When installed and authorized, route transcription and subtitles to `krillinai-subtitle`, target-language dubbing to `krillinai-tts`, landscape rendering to `krillinai-render-horizontal`, portrait rendering to `krillinai-render-vertical`, and multi-stage localization to `krillinai-pipeline`. Capability availability does not establish source, translation, music, likeness, voice-cloning, publication, or platform rights.

Route ad concept decisions to `ad-creative`, final copy to `copywriting`, stills and thumbnails to `marketing-image`, landing continuity to `landing-page-audit`, customer evidence to `customer-research`, PR use to `public-relations`, and causal tests to `experiment-design` when those capabilities are available.

Do not claim a rendered file, transcript, subtitle, translation, dub, thumbnail, or QA pass exists without an inspectable artifact. Generative output is not automatically factual, original, rights-cleared, accessible, localized, approved, or ready to publish.

## Localize The Whole Asset

Separate transcription, same-language captions, subtitle translation, transcreation, dubbing, voice replacement, lip sync, graphics reconstruction, product-screen replacement, footage adaptation, format adaptation, and market validation. Localize meaning, evidence, offer, CTA, spoken delivery, on-screen text, names, price, date, number, currency, units, reading speed, line breaks, fonts, safe areas, audio, product availability, destination and disclosures.

Do not assume that audience motives, presenter credibility, humor, gestures, settings, music, product surface, claims, rights, channels, accounts, formats, review, or performance transfer across markets.

## Make Meaning Accessible

Provide accurate synchronized captions for meaningful speech and sound, a transcript where useful, sufficient contrast and text size at representative playback, audio-independent understanding for core decisions, and audio description or an equivalent when essential visual information is otherwise unavailable. Do not rely on sound, color, rapid cuts, or tiny embedded text as the only carrier of meaning. Review flashing, motion, readability, speaker identification, non-speech audio, caption timing, and player context.

## Inspect Every Delivered File

Review actual exports at final dimensions and representative players. Verify product and claim truth, person and source integrity, edit continuity, copy, graphics, charts, logo, color, crop, safe area, duration, frame rate, resolution, orientation, container, codec, audio channels, synchronization, loudness against the supplied delivery requirement, caption accuracy, subtitle timing, transcript, accessibility, localization, artifacts, black or frozen frames, compression, metadata, filename, version, rights, disclosure, thumbnail, destination and checksum where available.

## Learn Without Overclaiming

Map results to audience, concept, narrative, edit, format, locale, creator, placement, offer and destination.
