# Localization, Rights, And Accessibility

## Adaptation Layers

Treat these as separate operations with separate owners, evidence and QA:

1. transcription of the source speech and meaningful sound;
2. same-language captions;
3. subtitle translation;
4. transcreation for intended meaning and market context;
5. dubbing or new voice recording;
6. lip-sync or timing adaptation;
7. on-screen text and graphic reconstruction;
8. product-screen, offer, proof and destination replacement;
9. footage, presenter, music or setting adaptation;
10. aspect-ratio, duration and placement adaptation;
11. local product, rights, claim, audience and review validation.

Transcription errors propagate into translation, dubbing and captions. Validate names, product terms, numbers, dates, prices, units, acronyms, pronunciation, speaker changes and meaningful non-speech audio before downstream work.

## Translation And Dubbing

Translate intended meaning, not isolated words. Preserve the exact factual and commercial scope. Fit reading and speaking pace through supported rewriting, timing, pauses or visual reconstruction; do not delete material qualifications to make a dub fit.

For each voice, record performer or model, owner, consent, source, allowed text, purpose, language, market, media, edits, synthetic or cloning permission, training permission, term, revocation, disclosure, approval and evidence. Permission to record a person is not permission to clone, translate, alter, impersonate, advertise with or reuse their voice.

Do not imply that a translated synthetic voice is the person's original speech. Preserve speaker identity only when authorized and disclose material synthesis or alteration according to the contract and current applicable requirements.

## Market Adaptation

Keep market, language, locale, audience, platform, account, placement and product surface separate. Validate local product availability, workflow, price, currency, units, offer, claim, proof, presenter credibility, music, gesture, setting, product screen, CTA, destination, review path and rights.

For China and Simplified Chinese, do not infer Douyin, Xiaohongshu, WeChat Channels, Kuaishou, Bilibili, account access, formats, distribution, content acceptance, advertising conditions, music rights, portrait rights, voice rights, font rights or audience behavior. Use China rather than a broader or narrower geography label unless the evidence requires a different scoped market.

## Caption And Subtitle QA

Check text against the mastered audio and image:

- exact words, meaning, names, technical terms, numbers and punctuation;
- speaker changes and meaningful sound labels;
- synchronization, entry, exit and shot-change conflicts;
- line length, breaks, reading pace and simultaneous text load;
- contrast, background treatment, size, font, glyphs, placement and safe area;
- obstruction of faces, product UI, claims, disclaimers or essential action;
- translation accuracy, naturalness, tone, claim scope and locale conventions;
- export or sidecar format, language tag, filename, encoding and player behavior.

Do not claim captions or machine translations are accurate without reviewing the actual text against the mastered asset.

## Accessible Meaning

Provide captions for meaningful speech and sound. Provide a transcript when it improves access, search, review or reuse. Ensure the primary decision remains understandable without audio; use on-screen support, captions or an alternate asset rather than treating music or voice as the sole carrier.

When essential visual information is not described in dialogue, provide audio description, an integrated spoken description, a companion transcript, or an equivalent accessible version appropriate to the placement. Preserve chart conclusions, product state, demonstrated action, identity and on-screen text that matter to understanding.

Check contrast, text size, reading time, flashing, strobing, intense motion, rapid cuts, auto-play behavior, pause and player controls where observable. Do not use color alone or sound alone to communicate state. Accessibility depends on the delivered player context as well as the media file; record both.
