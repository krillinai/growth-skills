# Marketing Video Contract

## Intake

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, project and asset IDs, state, deadline, budget or capacity, and allowed action;
- product, edition, feature, workflow, offer, price, availability, market, language, locale, destination, and actual customer experience;
- audience, job, situation, lifecycle or buying state, problem, belief, objection, value pillar, angle, proof, CTA, and qualified outcome;
- video role, concept ID, narrative ID, edit ID, format ID, source asset, parent version, experiment, expiration, and retirement state;
- channel, platform, account, campaign, placement, player, device, duration, dimensions, ratio, safe areas, frame rate, container, codec, audio, caption, thumbnail, file size, quality, naming, and current requirement source;
- exact spoken line, on-screen copy, demonstration, claim, statistic, price, disclaimer, quote, source, proof, limitation, owner, approval, expiry, prohibited wording, and timecode;
- script, storyboard frame, shot, location, product state, performer, wardrobe, prop, graphic, animation, B-roll, continuity, source, and acceptance criterion;
- brand version, logo, colors, typography, motion, transitions, photography, illustration, icon, product UI, sonic identity, and prohibited elements;
- footage, image, recording, animation, music, sound effect, font, template, creator, owner, license, model or property release, trademark, copyright, media, derivative, generative use, market, term, attribution, and evidence artifact;
- depicted or heard person, identity, likeness, age, sensitive context, role, testimony, consent, voice, voice clone, impersonation, edit, synthetic state, disclosure, and approval;
- transcript, caption, subtitle, speaker label, non-speech audio, reading speed, audio description, text equivalent, contrast, motion, flashing, and accessibility review;
- translation, transcreation, dub, voice casting, pronunciation, lip sync, graphics reconstruction, product-screen replacement, market validation, and local reviewer;
- tool or model, prompt or edit instruction, seed or output ID when available, source hashes or locators, project file, render date, output manifest, and QA result;
- source artifact, owner, capture date, evidence state, signal status, counterevidence, limitation, and freshness.

Version when concept, narrative, script, product, claim, source, person, voice, music, rights, brand, market, placement, format, localization, approval, or destination changes materially.

## Evidence States

Assign every evidence-bearing row exactly one state:

- `verified`: the cited supplied or inspectable source directly supports the row within scope;
- `inferred`: a bounded interpretation, creative choice, or production implication that is not treated as factual proof;
- `unavailable`: applicable evidence is absent, inaccessible, stale, conflicting, or too weak;
- `not applicable`: the category does not apply to the declared asset or placement.

Use `reported signal` only for an attributable stakeholder or platform statement without inspectable support. It is a signal status, not an evidence state. Missing private account, performance, research, or production data remains unavailable and does not create a negative score.

## Product And Claim Truth

For every spoken, shown or implied claim, record exact wording, source, population or product scope, market, time, method, unit, conditions, uncertainty, counterevidence, approval, expiration and prohibited stronger version.

Use approved real product capture when the audience needs to inspect behavior. Preserve errors, steps, delays, warnings, permissions, limitations and representative data. Label prototypes, simulations and future-state sequences clearly. Never fabricate reviews, messages, dashboards, customer stories, footage, news, awards, certifications, results or before-and-after proof.

## Source And Rights Record

Public availability does not establish ownership, license, consent, endorsement, confidentiality clearance, model or property release, edit, derivative, generative-use, paid-media, global, perpetual, voice, likeness or training rights.

Record asset, locator, creator, owner, subject, agreement or license, media, platform, placement, market, language, term, paid and organic scope, edits, derivatives, synthetic use, identity or voice use, exclusivity, attribution, expiration, restrictions, evidence artifact and reviewer. Missing rights block the affected use; they are never assumed.

Keep source footage, project files, masters, translations, captions, audio stems, thumbnails and delivery exports distinct. Preserve originals and lineage.
