# Delivery, QA, And Measurement

## Delivery Specification

Use current supplied or attributable placement requirements. Record requirement source and retrieval date. Never invent or silently reuse dimensions, duration, ratio, safe areas, frame rate, codec, file size, audio, caption, thumbnail or review requirements across platforms.

For each output, specify channel, platform, account, placement, player, device, market, locale, dimensions, ratio, orientation, duration, frame rate, container, video codec, audio codec and channels, quality or bitrate when supplied, file-size limit, safe area, caption method, thumbnail, filename and acceptance criteria.

## Render And File QA

Inspect the actual final file, not only the editor timeline or script:

- file opens, duration, dimensions, display aspect, orientation, frame rate, container, codecs, audio channels, size and metadata;
- no unintended black, frozen, blank, repeated or corrupted frames;
- no dropped essential audio, clipping, distortion, noise, silence, drift or lip-sync error;
- loudness and peak behavior match the supplied delivery requirement rather than an invented universal target;
- exact spoken and on-screen copy, spelling, numbers, prices, units, labels, disclaimers and CTA;
- product workflow, UI, states, timing, representative data, limitations and destination continuity;
- source integrity, person identity, testimony, synthetic state, disclosures, licenses, releases and expiry;
- logo geometry, color, typography, graphics, charts, animation, transitions and brand consistency;
- crop, focal point, safe areas, faces, product UI, captions, subtitles and disclaimer visibility;
- caption words, speakers, sounds, timing, line breaks, font, glyphs, contrast, placement and sidecar behavior;
- translation, dub, pronunciation, meaning, voice, sync, on-screen graphics, product, offer and local destination;
- contrast, text size, reading time, audio-independent meaning, transcript, audio description and motion or flashing review;
- thumbnail truth, crop, copy, product and source rights;
- filename, version, lineage, project or master reference, checksum, approval and manifest.

Test representative player contexts and placements when available. A file-level pass does not prove that autoplay, controls, captions, crop, overlays, safe areas, tracking or accessibility work in the destination player.

## Delivery Manifest

Record filename, project and asset IDs, concept, narrative, edit, format, source master, locale, channel, placement, dimensions, duration, frame rate, container, codecs, file size, caption and audio assets, thumbnail, product version, brand version, script and claim versions, footage, people, voice, music, rights and expiration, tool or model, prompt or edit ID, render date, QA result, approval, destination and checksum when available.

Never label an output approved, published, delivered or live from a local render alone.

## Measurement Contract

Map each output to eligible audience, assignment or delivery rule, actual exposure, concept, narrative, edit, format, locale, presenter, placement, offer, destination, campaign and measurement window.

Keep these layers separate:

- eligible, assigned, rendered, viewable-start and valid exposure counts;
- watch depth, watch time, completion and replay with explicit denominators;
- sound-on, captions-on and accessibility behavior when legitimately observable and appropriate;
- qualified interaction, CTA response, destination arrival and product first value;
- revenue, retention, refunds, contribution and support;
- hides, skips, complaints, trust, rights, safety and accessibility incidents;
- platform attribution, modeled attribution and causal incrementality.

A two-second view, completed play, click or platform conversion is not automatically attention, comprehension, permission, customer value, retention, contribution or incrementality. Do not choose a universal hook, completion, click, conversion, fatigue or scaling threshold.

## Learning And Governance

Compare only compatible audiences, delivery, placements, offers, destinations, maturity and measurement definitions. Preserve auction, budget, creator, seasonality, frequency, competing campaigns and adaptive-delivery explanations. Determine whether a result informs the concept, narrative, edit, format, localization, presenter, offer or destination before generalizing.

Define owner, review date, source and rights expiry, product or claim change trigger, localization refresh, performance maturity, stop rule, rollback, archive, deletion and successor lineage. Retire stale, unsupported, inaccessible, off-brand, expired-rights or misleading assets even when historical engagement is high.
