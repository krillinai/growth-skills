# Scripting, Storyboard, And Production

## Dependency Order

```text
decision and audience
-> video role and concept
-> truthful product, claim, proof and source
-> narrative treatment and beats
-> script, storyboard and shot list
-> rights, people, voice, music and production method
-> placement, format, localization and accessibility
-> edit and format variants
-> local render, inspection and delivery
```

## Script Table

Use one row per beat or time range:

| Time or beat | Visual and action | Spoken audio | On-screen text | Music and sound | Product, claim and source | Accessibility | Acceptance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

The timing is a planning estimate until validated by a table read, rough cut or recorded delivery. Keep spoken and on-screen text concise enough for the declared pace and placement. Do not compress disclaimers, captions or essential proof until unreadable.

## Narrative Treatments

Choose a treatment because it fits the decision and evidence:

- real product demonstration;
- problem-to-workflow explanation;
- mechanism or process animation;
- attributable customer story or interview;
- expert or presenter explanation;
- objection and response;
- comparison from supportable criteria;
- event, PR or documentary coverage;
- educational walkthrough;
- offer with explicit terms.

A popular style is not evidence of fit. Do not imitate a creator, customer, competitor or living artist. A presenter does not create expertise, endorsement or product proof by appearance alone.

## Storyboard And Shot List

For every frame or shot, specify beat, purpose, framing, camera or screen capture, subject, action, product state, location or background, composition, graphics, on-screen text, transition, audio, source, rights, continuity, accessibility and acceptance criteria.

Distinguish:

- approved real capture;
- attributable archival or supplied footage;
- commissioned footage;
- licensed stock;
- deterministic graphic or animation;
- clearly labeled conceptual mockup;
- generated or materially altered media.

Do not disguise one source class as another. If a needed source is unavailable, change the treatment, create a production request, or leave the shot blocked.

## Recording And Generation Instructions

For recording, specify device or capture method, resolution and frame rate from the delivery contract, orientation, camera movement, lens or screen area, light, background, audio path, room tone, performance, wardrobe, props, product data, takes, slate, releases and backups.

For generation, specify subject, action, setting, camera, motion, duration, continuity, composition, lighting, material, brand cues, copy-free regions, factual constraints, negative constraints, ratio and resolution. Keep exact product UI, logos, charts, small text, testimony and documentary facts deterministic or sourced rather than relying on generation.

For editing, specify source and in/out points, preserve list, sequence, transition, crop, speed, stabilization, color, audio, graphics, captions, mask or composite, disclosure, target format and acceptance criteria. Never remove a watermark, signature, attribution, disclosure, safety notice, warning or material limitation.

## Variant Lineage

Keep the concept and declared hypothesis stable unless intentionally testing a new one. Useful controlled edit dimensions include opening shot, beat order, pacing, duration, presenter take, CTA treatment, B-roll, music, caption presentation and thumbnail. Useful format adaptations include aspect ratio, crop, safe area, resolution, locale, subtitles, dub, product screen and placement packaging.

Change one interpretable dimension or label a bundled edit. Do not create dozens of near-identical exports without a placement, accessibility, localization or learning purpose.
