# Treatment, Acceptance, And Governance

## Select A Treatment Deliberately

| Treatment | Boundary |
| --- | --- |
| Avoid | Do not start or discontinue the exposure while preserving exit obligations |
| Reduce | Change likelihood, consequence, duration, detectability, concentration, or recovery through a defined mechanism |
| Transfer | Shift specified financial or operational consequences under an enforceable arrangement; accountability and nontransferable duties remain |
| Share | Allocate bounded responsibilities or consequences across accountable parties |
| Accept | An authorized owner knowingly retains a bounded residual exposure for an effective period |
| Monitor | Collect decision-relevant evidence while exposure and action state remain explicit; not mitigation by itself |
| Investigate | Resolve a material evidence gap before another treatment decision |
| Pause or stop | Hold or end new exposure under defined authority, triggers, and residual obligations |
| Exit | End a product, market, vendor, channel, system, or program with customer and decommissioning protections |
| Remediate | Correct a current issue, control gap, or harm under an accountable plan and verification |

For each candidate record risk and version, mechanism, affected population, evidence, expected exposure effect, assumptions, dependencies, capacity, cost and cash, opportunity cost, timing, customer and employee burden, privacy and security, accessibility and fairness, concentration, residual exposure, obligations, owner, approvals, verification, guardrails, stop, rollback, correction, review, and expiry.

Do not prescribe the same treatment to every risk. A task, policy, monitor, insurance policy, contract clause, vendor promise, or recommendation does not prove transfer, mitigation, remediation, or closure.

## Govern Transfer And Third Parties

Record counterparty, exact transferred consequence, retained accountability, contract and version, coverage, exclusions, caps, deductibles, dependencies, financial condition, concentration, subprocessor or fourth-party exposure, evidence access, notification, recovery, dispute, renewal, termination, data return or deletion, transition, and residual obligations.

Customer, legal, privacy, security, employment, fiduciary, and regulatory duties may be nontransferable. Do not issue legal or insurance conclusions; require current direct sources and accountable specialists.

## Bound Risk Acceptance

Acceptance requires:

1. one exact residual risk and version;
2. defined objective, population, product, market, resource, period, and authority;
3. inherent exposure, controls, operating evidence, residual exposure, uncertainty, and counterevidence;
4. credible alternatives and why they are rejected, deferred, or unavailable;
5. customer value and harm, economics, cash, capacity, concentration, reversibility, and residual obligations;
6. specialist reviews, dissent, conflicts, conditions, compensation or protection where applicable;
7. accountable decision owner and appropriate acceptance authority;
8. monitoring, indicators, limits, escalation, stop, rollback, correction, review, expiry, and reopen rules.

An analyst, risk owner, control owner, project owner, vendor, meeting, vote, silence, or senior statement does not automatically possess acceptance authority. Recommendation is not decision; decision is not approval; approval is not authorization; authorization is not acceptance; acceptance is not implementation or success.

Create an approval-ready acceptance request when authority or evidence is missing. Do not claim acceptance occurred.

## Escalate By Consequence And Authority

Define levels using affected customers, hard obligations, materiality, urgency, velocity, reversibility, spread, control failure, concentration, response time, and decision authority. Record trigger, initial owner, escalation recipient, response window, fallback, containment proposal, stop authority, communication boundary, independent verification, correction, and audit.

Do not escalate every alert to executives or suppress material signals to reduce noise. Preserve local ownership where authority and consequence permit; escalate shared, irreversible, regulated, concentrated, or cross-boundary exposure through the supplied governance path.

## Transfer Across Markets Carefully

Respond in the user's requested language. For Simplified Chinese, use native Simplified Chinese rather than mechanically translating labels; preserve exact source names, IDs, versions, formulas, quotes, and required record states where precision matters.

Treat market, language, locale, currency, customer, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, tax, data, content, support, operations, vendor, insurance, regulation, metrics, appetite, controls, decision rights, and authority as separate dimensions. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transfer.

Revalidate local objectives, customers, exposures, baselines, metric versions, thresholds, economics, cash, capacity, concentration, dependencies, scenarios, controls, owners, approvals, authorization, monitoring, response, effective period, review, expiry, exit, and residual obligations. Use current direct sources and locally accountable expertise. Avoid legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, provider, insurance, approval, authorization, and performance conclusions.

## Minimize Sensitive Data And Consequential Use

Use minimum-necessary aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, access-controlled, and small-cell-suppressed evidence. Use accountable roles rather than names unless identity is necessary, proportionate, and authorized.

Do not include credentials, payment details, private messages, health or precise financial information, protected traits, precise location, compensation, employee reviews, raw ratings, or full histories by default. Never expose secrets.

Do not rank named customers or employees for pricing, service denial, credit, insurance, health, employment, bonuses, promotion, discipline, or termination. Require accountable specialist review and route consequential decisions without claiming approval. Review accessibility, fairness, proxy discrimination, disparate effect, retaliation, manipulation, exclusion, feedback loops, and misuse.

Record purpose, authority or supplied basis, notice or consent, fields, population, granularity, access, sharing, residency, retention, deletion, correction, incident, and audit. Unknown permission remains unavailable.

## Maintain Specialist Handoffs

| Handoff | Required record |
| --- | --- |
| Question | Exact specialist decision or evidence needed and why it changes the portfolio |
| Boundary | Product, customer, market, system, population, period, cutoff, and prohibited inference |
| Ownership | Request owner, accountable specialist, decision owner, acceptance authority, and date |
| Artifact | Expected analysis, source, metric, control, decision, or verification version |
| Return | Evidence state, finding, limitation, contradiction, conflict, residual exposure, and expiry |
| Status | `unrequested`, `requested`, `acknowledged`, `blocked`, `returned`, `rejected`, `superseded`, or `expired` |

Do not replace specialist owners or claim routed work occurred.

## Specify Approval-Ready External Actions

For each proposed action record exact system and mutation, object and population, market, amount or resource, environment, decision and risk versions, evidence, unresolved exposure, owner, reviewers, approvers, authorizer, executor, segregation of duties, access, dependencies, privacy and security, dry run, staged exposure, indicators, limits, monitoring, stop, rollback, correction, incident path, independent verification, audit, and expiry.

Create local records and requests only. Do not access authenticated systems; accept risk; change appetite, limits, controls, permissions, users, campaigns, products, prices, data, models, vendors, contracts, systems, or records; notify; file; publish; sign; spend; procure; hire; fire; block; pause; roll back; deploy; or close incidents without separate task-level authorization and controls.

## Close Without Erasing Obligations

Close a risk only when an accountable owner records why it no longer governs new decisions, supporting evidence, final exposure state, treatment and control verification, affected consumers, unresolved uncertainty, customer and employee protections, contractual and financial duties, data and access handling, system and vendor decommissioning, retention, audit, reopen triggers, and residual-obligation owner.

`Task created`, `in progress`, `meeting held`, `control documented`, `alert closed`, or `no recent incident` is not closure proof. Preserve the immutable history and superseding record.
