# Growth Risk Management

Turn fragmented concerns into a bounded, versioned portfolio risk decision system. Preserve uncertain events, evidence, affected objectives and populations, interactions, current controls, residual exposure, authority, monitoring, and obligations without converting a heatmap, policy, lack of alerts, or favorable outcome into assurance.

Read [risk-portfolio-contract.md](references/risk-portfolio-contract.md) before accepting scope, taxonomy, states, appetite, owners, evidence, metrics, or versions. Read [aggregation-scenarios-and-controls.md](references/aggregation-scenarios-and-controls.md) before aggregating risks, rating interactions or concentration, designing scenarios, accepting controls, or defining monitoring. Read [treatment-acceptance-and-governance.md](references/treatment-acceptance-and-governance.md) before recommending treatment, accepting residual risk, transferring markets, handling personal data, escalating, closing, or proposing external actions. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) for the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `build` | Create a portfolio contract, risk register, appetite map, interaction view, control register, treatment plan, and monitoring design from supplied evidence |
| `audit` | Inspect an existing register or operating system for ambiguous risks, unsupported ratings, missing exposure, weak controls, concentration, stale acceptance, or false closure |
| `refresh` | Version a prior portfolio after evidence, assumptions, risks, appetite, controls, incidents, treatments, markets, dependencies, outcomes, or authority change |

Name one mode and state `draft`, `evidence-pending`, `review-ready`, `decision-pending`, `accepted-record`, `monitoring-pending`, `review-required`, `paused-record`, `closed-record`, `superseded`, or `expired`. State does not prove specialist review, risk acceptance, control operation, mitigation, incident response, closure, compliance, or improved outcomes.

Route privacy, security, legal, finance, accounting, employment, fraud, safety, model, platform, vendor, experiment, data-quality, market, lifecycle, customer, and operational conclusions to accountable specialists and owning Skills. Route one canonical decision to `growth-decision-record`, one initiative's work to `growth-initiative-planning`, recurring business review to `growth-operating-review`, an unexpected metric change to `growth-anomaly-investigation`, infrastructure choices to `growth-infrastructure-assessment`, and retrospective learning to `growth-postmortem`. This Skill owns the portfolio contract, aggregation, common controls, acceptance map, and monitoring system, not specialist analysis or execution.

## Freeze The Portfolio Contract

Record portfolio ID and version, mode and state, decisions and intended uses, accountable portfolio owner, risk owners, control owners, decision owners, specialist reviewers, acceptance authorities, verification and stop owners, scope and exclusions, products and business models, customers and participant roles, markets and locales, objectives and metrics, initiatives and systems, resources, risk classes, periods, cutoff, maturity, source and metric versions, evidence access, privacy boundary, review and expiry, prior portfolio, and external-action boundary.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing claims. Keep reported concern, uncertainty, assumption, dependency, vulnerability, risk, event, issue, incident, loss, and outcome separate. Keep inherent exposure, current control, control design, operation, residual exposure, treatment, acceptance, execution, verification, and result separate. Do not invent risks, owners, likelihood, impact, velocity, confidence, thresholds, monetary loss, scores, appetite, controls, or outcomes.

## Gate Portfolio Commitments

Populate assessed risk, rating, appetite, limit, owner, deadline, control, treatment plan, expected effect, acceptance, monitoring threshold, escalation, closure, downstream acknowledgement, or propagation only from attributable evidence for the exact scope, population, period, mechanism, authority, capacity, conditions, and version. A locally authored portfolio is not an organization-approved system of record.

When readiness is missing, keep the portfolio `draft` or `evidence-pending` and leave unsupported governing fields `unavailable`. Preserve requested scores, percentages, ranks, thresholds, limits, owners, relative dates, durations, 30/60/90 phases, controls, treatments, acceptance, closure, and actions only as exact reported requests with unsupported status. Do not convert them into calendar dates, proposed assignments, phased commitments, current controls, approved plans, or acceptance records. You may identify evidence requirements and candidate control or treatment classes, but do not populate exact designs, expected effects, owners, deadlines, or commitments without the required basis.

## Formulate Decision-Relevant Risks

State the objective or value at risk, uncertain event, cause or mechanism, consequence, affected customers and operations, scope, horizon, evidence, assumptions, dependencies, current controls, exposure, owner, decision, and review. Do not use a topic label such as `privacy`, `competition`, or `churn` as a complete risk statement.

Classify customer and trust, strategy and market, product and lifecycle, economic and cash, acquisition and channel, incentive and fraud, data and measurement, experiment and causal, model and automation, privacy and security, accessibility and fairness, platform and vendor, legal and policy, operations and reliability, people and capacity, and reputation risks as applicable. Taxonomy supports discovery; it does not prove existence or severity.

## Establish Appetite And Boundaries

Separate risk capacity, appetite, tolerance, limit, guardrail, trigger, stop rule, and target. For each record objective, risk class, population, product, market, metric definition and version, unit, baseline, current exposure, boundary, source, owner, authority, rationale, period, breach response, exception, review, and expiry.

Do not backsolve limits to make the portfolio pass. Preserve hard constraints, noncompensable harm, specialist requirements, and unavailable appetite separately. A slogan, target, benchmark, historical maximum, or lack of incidents is not an authorized boundary.

## Assess Without False Precision

Record evidence and ranges for plausible frequency or likelihood, consequence, duration, velocity, detectability, reversibility, concentration, recovery, and uncertainty only when supported. Show formulas, units, sources, assumptions, ranges, rounding, sensitivity, and limitations for calculations. Do not multiply ordinal labels as calibrated quantities or average severe harm and verified blockers into upside.

Keep customer, strategic, economic, cash, operational, legal, privacy, security, accessibility, trust, people, and authority dimensions separate when they are incompatible. Use accountable judgment under uncertainty without relabeling it as verified evidence.

## Aggregate Interactions And Concentration

Map shared customers, channels, platforms, identities, data, models, metrics, experiments, vendors, contracts, markets, cash, capacity, people, and controls. Identify common-cause, correlated, cascading, reinforcing, offsetting, competing, and duplicate exposure. Define concentration basis and denominator before reporting it.

Preserve item and portfolio views. Do not add incompatible scores, assume independence, count one cause or loss repeatedly, or claim diversification from many initiatives. Use bounded scenarios and stress conditions to expose capacity, propagation, control, recovery, and decision thresholds without assigning invented probabilities.

## Evaluate Controls And Treatments

Separate control objective, design, approval, implementation, operation, evidence, test, effectiveness, exception, failure, override, drift, and outcome. Classify preventive, detective, corrective, recovery, directive, and compensating controls. A policy, task, dashboard, review meeting, or tool purchase does not prove operation or effectiveness.

Compare avoid, reduce, transfer, share, accept, monitor, investigate, pause, stop, exit, and remediate treatments. Record mechanism, evidence, expected effect, affected population, owner, dependencies, capacity, cost, timing, tradeoffs, residual exposure, obligations, verification, stop, rollback, review, and expiry. Monitoring is not universal mitigation; transfer does not remove accountability.

## Govern Acceptance, Monitoring, And Closure

Require accountable acceptance authority for the exact residual exposure, scope, population, market, resource, conditions, effective period, and expiry. Preserve alternatives, dissent, unavailable evidence, protections, and residual obligations. Recommendation, decision, approval, authorization, acceptance, execution, and result remain separate.

Monitor leading signals, exposure, control state, breaches, near misses, incidents, losses, and lagging outcomes using versioned metric contracts and maturity. Preserve blind spots, missing coverage, false positives, false negatives, alert fatigue, suppression, gaming, and no-alert uncertainty. Define owners, frequency, response windows, materiality, tiered escalation, containment, verification, review, expiry, and reopen rules.

In `refresh`, preserve the ex-ante register, appetite, controls, assumptions, decisions, and evidence. Add new events, incidents, outcomes, corrections, control findings, changed exposure, and decisions by version. Do not rewrite old ratings with hindsight, rank people by outcomes, hide failed controls, or mark resolution from a meeting or task state. Close only with accountable evidence and retained customer, contractual, financial, data, system, vendor, and decommissioning obligations.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access authenticated finance, procurement, HR, analytics, CRM, billing, product, experiment, cloud, support, security, legal, contract, insurance, or communication systems; export or join personal data; accept risk; change appetite, limits, controls, permissions, users, campaigns, products, data, models, systems, vendors, contracts, or records; notify; file; sign; spend; procure; hire; fire; block; pause; roll back; deploy; or claim mitigation, containment, recovery, closure, certification, compliance, or reduced risk without separate task-level authorization and controls.
