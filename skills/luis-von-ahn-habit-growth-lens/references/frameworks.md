# Framework Reference

## Mission-Aligned Freemium

Map valuable free access, paid differentiation, advertising or other subsidy, serving cost, willingness to pay, conversion, retention, and mission reach. Preserve a useful free experience while giving paying users a credible advantage. Test whether the economics finance broader access.

## Ethical Motivation Audit

For streaks, leagues, rewards, progress, social comparison, and playful feedback, identify the behavior reinforced and its relationship to the intended outcome. Measure meaningful practice, outcome quality, retained motivation, autonomy, anxiety, gaming, exclusion, and fatigue.

## Progress-Loss-Recovery System

Make accumulated effort and next progress legible. Use loss carefully to prompt return, then provide bounded recovery so one interruption does not destroy a long-term habit. Segment by user maturity and natural cadence.

## Value-Linked Reminders

Trigger reminders around a credible next value opportunity. Control relevance, timing, frequency, channel, quiet periods, user settings, and recovery after inactivity. Measure return to meaningful practice and retention, not opens alone.

## Product-Personality Brand

Derive voice, characters, humor, and social behavior from an existing product personality. Define audience permission, creative autonomy, escalation boundaries, review, incident response, and consistency. Brand attention must reconnect to product value.

## Repeatable Learning-System Expansion

Test whether a new category shares broad demand, practice cadence, measurable progress, instructional structure, motivation design, content economics, and product capabilities. Validate learning value before transferring familiar mechanics.

## Data-and-Judgment Governance

Use experiments to compare behavior and outcomes while leadership defines which outcomes, risks, and brand boundaries matter. Record where evidence is causal, correlational, qualitative, or judgment-based.

## AI Learning-Outcome System

Map AI use to adaptation, practice quality, interaction, coverage, accessibility, and cost. Add factual accuracy, pedagogical validity, difficulty calibration, bias, safety, provenance, review, trust, and measured learning outcomes.

## Culture as Product Infrastructure

Evaluate whether hiring, collaboration, dissent, ownership, and harmful behavior affect experiment speed, creative risk, information flow, and product quality. Use fair processes and multiple signals; do not turn founder anecdotes into universal hiring rules.
