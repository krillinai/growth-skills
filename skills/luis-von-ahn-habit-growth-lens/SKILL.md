# Luis von Ahn Habit Growth Lens

Apply Luis von Ahn's public methods to mission-driven habit products. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Value System

Collect mission, core user outcome, natural practice cadence, free and paid value, serving cost, monetization, habit event, progress signal, streak and recovery rules, notification policy, learning or outcome evidence, brand permission, expansion category, AI quality controls, and decision. Mark unknowns; never invent metrics or von Ahn's views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| Reach and financial sustainability conflict | Mission-aligned freemium |
| Engagement rises without better user outcomes | Ethical motivation audit |
| Users abandon after breaking a streak | Progress-loss-recovery system |
| Notifications increase opens but create fatigue | Value-linked reminders |
| Social content feels disconnected from the product | Product-personality brand |
| A new category shares the interface but not behavior | Repeatable learning-system expansion |
| Experiments optimize activity while outcomes are unclear | Data-and-judgment governance |
| AI increases content volume but quality is uncertain | AI learning-outcome system |
| Creative speed is blocked by team behavior | Culture as product infrastructure |

## Diagnose

1. Define the meaningful user outcome and mission constraint.
2. Map free value, motivation, repeated practice, outcomes, monetization, and reinvestment.
3. Label evidence `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
4. Separate engagement proxies from learning or customer outcomes.
5. Select one primary constraint and preserve alternatives.
6. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
7. Define an experiment with population, intervention, outcome metric, engagement metric, harm guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
8. State blind spots and an alternative lens.

Do not treat daily activity as learning, loss aversion as automatically ethical, a mascot as a brand strategy, free access as a viable business model without economics, or AI production speed as outcome quality.

## Deliver

Use: **mission and user outcome**, **lens applicability**, **habit and business map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **outcome and harm guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Keep Duolingo examples category- and scale-bound.

Never write as von Ahn, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Optimizing streak length without measuring meaningful practice.
- Making the free product ineffective to force conversion.
- Using recovery mechanics that remove all commitment or create anxiety.
- Copying provocative brand behavior without audience permission and review.
- Expanding categories without shared pedagogy and motivation.
- Treating company anecdotes as controlled evidence.
