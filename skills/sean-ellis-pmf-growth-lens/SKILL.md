# Sean Ellis PMF Growth Lens

Apply Sean Ellis's public methods to validate value and build a growth learning system. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Evidence Base

Collect product, target segment, qualified-user definition, core value, survey timing and sample, response distribution, activation event, time to value, cohort retention, North Star, growth stage, experiment history, referral behavior, economics, and decision. Mark unknowns; never invent metrics or Ellis's views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| The team wants to scale before proving must-have value | PMF survey and segmentation |
| A 40% score is treated as a universal pass/fail gate | Heuristic quality audit |
| Survey enthusiasm does not match repeated use | Survey-to-retention evidence chain |
| Acquired users fail to reach the core benefit | Activation bridge |
| Teams disagree about what growth stage comes next | Three growth stages |
| The North Star tracks volume rather than value | Value-based North Star |
| Many ideas exist but learning is slow | Scientific growth loop |
| Experiment count is high but impact is low | High-tempo quality system |
| ICE scores override evidence and risk | ICE as discussion aid |
| Referral mechanics attract low-quality users | Value-led referral loop |

## Diagnose

1. Define the must-have customer value and decision scope.
2. Audit sample eligibility, timing, size, segment composition, and follow-up evidence.
3. Connect stated value to activation behavior and mature cohort retention.
4. Label claims `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
5. Identify one primary growth constraint and preserve alternatives.
6. Compare actions by value impact, reach, evidence, learning speed, effort, economics, and risk.
7. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
8. Define one decision-oriented experiment with hypothesis, population, intervention, metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
9. State blind spots and an alternative lens.

Do not treat 40% as a causal threshold, survey intent as retention, acquisition as delivered value, experiment count as learning, ICE as objective truth, or referrals as a repair for weak activation.

## Deliver

Use: **scope and value**, **lens applicability**, **PMF and growth-stage map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **metrics and guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Keep company examples time-bound and avoid single-person causal claims.

## Required Boundary

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Sean Ellis's public work. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Sean Ellis.

Never write as Ellis, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Surveying users before they have a fair chance to experience value.
- Combining materially different segments into one PMF score.
- Ignoring qualitative follow-up answers behind the headline percentage.
- Choosing a North Star that can rise without customer value.
- Running high-tempo tests on low-impact surfaces.
- Scaling channels or referrals before activation and retention work.
