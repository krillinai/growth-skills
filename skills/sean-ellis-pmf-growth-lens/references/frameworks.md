# Framework Reference

## PMF Survey and Segmentation

Ask qualified active users how they would feel if they could no longer use the product. Include “very disappointed,” “somewhat disappointed,” “not disappointed,” and “no longer use” options. Define eligibility and timing before collecting responses.

Segment results by use case, role, source, market, behavior, and product state. Ask very-disappointed users for the primary benefit, best-fit audience, importance, alternatives, and improvements. Use their language to refine product and positioning hypotheses.

## Heuristic Quality Audit

Treat roughly 40% “very disappointed” as a historical heuristic, not a law. Audit sample size, selection bias, timing, response stability, category cadence, network dynamics, switching costs, and segment concentration. Report uncertainty rather than issuing a binary PMF certificate.

## Survey-to-Retention Evidence Chain

```text
PMF survey -> why users say they care
activation -> whether they reach the benefit
retention cohorts -> whether the benefit persists
economics and trust -> whether growth is viable
```

Investigate disagreement between layers instead of averaging it away.

## Activation Bridge

Define the earliest behavior showing the core benefit, prerequisites, time to value, and major drop-offs. Optimize for downstream retention, not onboarding completion. Use interviews and behavioral evidence to remove obstacles and communicate the must-have value.

## Three Growth Stages

```text
Determine Product-Market Fit
-> Stack the Odds for Growth
-> Scale Growth
```

Before scaling, identify who loves the product, the benefit, the Aha Moment, the path to value, retained behavior, channel compatibility, and basic economics.

## Value-Based North Star

Choose a metric representing customers receiving core value. Add input metrics the team can influence and guardrails for retention, quality, revenue, trust, safety, and ecosystem health. Reject metrics that rise through empty traffic, registrations, or activity.

## Scientific Growth Loop

```text
analyze -> generate ideas -> prioritize -> test -> learn -> repeat
```

Maintain a shared backlog, explicit hypotheses, owners, experiment records, decision rules, and reusable learning. Failed tests are valuable only when interpretation changes a future decision.

## High-Tempo Quality System

Increase throughput by reducing queue time, unclear ownership, setup cost, and analysis delay. Keep tests focused on the largest constraint. Measure decision-changing learning and durable impact alongside test count.

## ICE as Discussion Aid

Score Impact, Confidence, and Ease to structure comparison. Record evidence behind each estimate. Use strategy, dependencies, downside, reversibility, and guardrails to override the ranking when appropriate.

## Value-Led Referral Loop

Map existing word of mouth before engineering referrals. Ensure the incentive reinforces product value. Measure invite participation, recipient conversion, activation, retained quality, cost, incrementality, and abuse. Do not amplify a leaking product.
