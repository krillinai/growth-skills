# Acquisition Market Entry And Risk

## Market Entry Contract

Treat every market as a distinct evidence unit. Record:

- customer problem, demand, intent, best-fit segment, local alternatives, category and trust context;
- market, language, locale, product surface, device, connectivity, app distribution, search and social behavior;
- channel access, advertising and content supply, creator and partner ecosystem, sales and support capacity;
- product readiness, identity, consent, data, attribution, payment, invoice, currency, tax, pricing, and offer;
- applicable platform, policy, privacy, commercial, accessibility, legal, regulatory, and operational review;
- shared infrastructure, local configuration, local decision rights, escalation, incident, and platform feedback;
- test population, budget and exposure cap, evidence horizons, guardrails, stop, exit, and recovery.

Translation proves only that text was translated. It does not establish demand, product fit, channel access, consent, payment, compliance, trust, economics, or operating capability. Do not infer any named platform, provider, legal rule, or customer behavior without attributable local evidence.

## Platform And Partner Risk

| Area | Contract |
| --- | --- |
| Access | current authorization, account and data owner, eligible surfaces, quotas, and prohibited uses |
| Economics | price or rev-share, variable cost, minimums, refunds, incentives, currency, tax, and renegotiation |
| Data | collection, purpose, identity, consent, transfer, retention, export, deletion, training, and audit |
| Rights | content, creative, audience, trademark, creator, derivative, placement, and territory rights |
| Operations | delivery, support, complaints, disputes, fraud, incidents, outages, and customer communication |
| Dependency | exclusivity, concentration, policy change, bargaining power, switching, migration, and continuity |
| Exit | termination, wind-down, customer state, data return or deletion, attribution close, and recovery |

Visible distribution does not prove durable access or favorable private terms. Treat borrowed distribution as both an asset and a dependency.

## Trust And Guardrails

Track audience relevance, misleading claims, frequency, complaints, opt-outs, accessibility, fraud, low-quality leads, partner conduct, creator disclosure, content rights, brand harm, destination continuity, customer support, policy incidents, and downstream product harm. A low CAC that shifts harm to customers, teams, partners, or future retention is not efficient acquisition.

For product-led and partner mechanisms, define recipient value, role, permission, destination state, invalid or expired states, first value, abuse, support, attribution, economics, and termination. A send, share, invite, code, or public artifact is distribution activity, not a qualified acquired customer.

## Operating And Privacy Boundary

Strategy may specify account access, audience, tracking, campaign, partner, market, privacy, legal, finance, experiment, approval, monitoring, rollback, and incident requirements. It does not grant those permissions.

Use aggregate, redacted, or pseudonymous evidence. Do not access accounts; export customer data; enrich or upload audiences; use credentials; contact prospects, creators, partners, customers, or vendors; change spend, bids, targeting, pages, prices, campaigns, permissions, tracking, contracts, or product state; launch markets or tests; publish; send; spend; procure; or claim deployment without separate authorization and controls.
