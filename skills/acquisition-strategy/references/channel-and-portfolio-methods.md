# Acquisition Channel And Portfolio Methods

## Channel Hypothesis

For each candidate, state:

| Field | Required decision evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Customer access | named customer, problem, intent, trigger, eligible demand, exclusions, and credible reach |
| Mechanism | why exposure can occur, who controls it, and what customer action distributes or captures demand |
| Promise and path | message, proof, offer, destination, entry state, qualified action, and first value |
| Model fit | retained contribution, full acquisition and service cost, payback, cash, capacity, and scale ceiling |
| Evidence timing | start time, proxy horizon, first-value horizon, retention and contribution maturity |
| Control | targeting, exposure, pause, audit, rollback, support, and policy rights |
| Scale and decay | remaining demand, frequency, saturation, competition, fatigue, and marginal performance |
| Dependency | platform, partner, data, policy, rights, bargaining, switching, concentration, and recovery |
| Capability | owner, creative or content supply, sales, partner, local execution, maintenance, and support |
| Decision | test, keep manual, scale band, cap, redesign, reallocate, or stop with evidence gate |

Do not use a generic channel ranking. When several candidates lack evidence, choose the smallest set that can produce conclusive source-to-retained-value learning within the declared capacity.

## Channel Families And Transfer Boundaries

| Family | Mechanism | Main evidence boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organic discovery | existing intent finds useful indexed or listed supply | rankings and traffic do not prove incremental retained demand |
| Earned | independent people or institutions choose to distribute | mentions and reach depend on trust, novelty, and audience fit |
| Owned | a permissioned audience or product surface can be reached | list size does not prove consent, attention, value, or incrementality |
| Paid | purchased exposure or response | platform attribution, auction dynamics, marginal cost, and fatigue require reconciliation |
| Product-led | a valuable product action exposes a recipient or public surface | notification is not recipient value; abuse and product fit constrain scale |
| Partner | another organization contributes audience, product, sales, or distribution | contracts, data, incentives, support, exclusivity, and termination are dependencies |
| Sales-led | people identify, qualify, explain, and close demand | ACV must fund sales, implementation, support, cycle time, and cash requirements |
| Portfolio | products or markets cross-distribute | relevance, cannibalization, identity, governance, and customer trust limit reuse |

## Kindle-To-Fire Sequence

1. Define one best-fit and intent hypothesis.
2. Reach customers manually or through a narrow controllable source.
3. Observe promise comprehension, qualified arrival, first and repeated value, economics, and objections.
4. Preserve failures and poor-fit evidence.
5. Standardize only the mechanism that repeatedly produces retained value.
6. Add capacity, tooling, automation, markets, or spend in evidence-gated bands.
7. Monitor marginal quality, saturation, dependency, maintenance, and customer harm.

Do not use broad launch, publicity, automation, or spend to manufacture Product-Market Fit. Define the audience and proof threshold for each expansion layer.

## Portfolio Record

| Channel or band | Mechanism and dependency | Eligible remaining demand | Marginal incremental customers | Retained contribution | Full marginal cost | Capacity | Maturity and evidence | Risk and concentration | Option value | Gate and stop rule |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Compare compatible horizons and units. Historical averages describe the past; marginal evidence governs the next band. Return low, base, and high scenarios when key inputs are uncertain. Do not disguise assumptions in one weighted score.

## Diversification And Dependency

Map platform, auction, identity, data, content format, creator supply, partner, measurement, policy, and operating owners. Multiple placements or vendors that share one failure path are one concentrated mechanism for risk purposes.

For each external dependency, document authorization, data access, contractual rights, exclusivity, policy exposure, bargaining power, switching cost, customer continuity, export or deletion, support, termination, and recovery. A useful second mechanism must reach qualified demand, fit the model, and fail differently; adding low-quality channels only adds cost.
