# Governance, Market Transfer, And Actions

## Version The Decision Record

Record case ID, version, mode, owner, contributors, approvers, decision, scope, strategy version, baseline, options, source and evidence cutoff, model version, recommendation, conditions, decision date, expiry, next review, and superseded versions.

For `refresh`, preserve old inputs, assumptions, formulas, scenarios, risks, recommendation, and rationale. Add new evidence and calculate only affected compatible views. Explain which customer, strategic, market, economic, capacity, risk, or timing change caused each revision. Never rewrite old assumptions as if an unexpected outcome had been predicted.

## Assign Decision Rights

Separate:

- analyst recommendation;
- accountable decision owner;
- financial, security, privacy, legal or regulatory, procurement, customer, product, data, and operational reviewers;
- budget, contract, system, employment, product, price, publication, or deployment approvers;
- execution owners and completion proof.

The Skill can prepare a recommendation and approval-ready request. It cannot claim a decision was approved, authorized, funded, contracted, implemented, or successful.

## Define Refresh Triggers

Refresh when material evidence changes for customer value, Product-Market Fit, market or segment, strategy, product or offer, price, conversion, retention, channel, competitor, platform, partner, regulation, data use, costs, contribution, cash, capacity, timing, risk, vendor, capital, or another portfolio commitment. Also refresh when a pilot matures, a gate changes state, a recommendation expires, or metric definitions change.

Distinguish measurement error, immature data, temporary variance, execution miss, and structural change. Do not reopen a rejected option without new evidence or keep an approved recommendation after its assumptions expire.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat each market as a new case boundary. Re-establish:

- customer, user, beneficiary, buyer, approver, payer, problem, alternatives, and value;
- product, language, locale, device, integration, accessibility, security, support, and delivery;
- channel, platform, partner, app distribution, sales, procurement, and reachability;
- price, currency, purchasing power, payment, invoice, tax, refund, contract, costs, contribution, cash, funding, and capacity;
- identity, consent, data source, storage, transfer, privacy, content, advertising, sector, and regulatory review;
- local baseline, options, owners, sources, risk, gates, expiry, stop, rollback, and exit.

Translation and currency conversion do not validate value, economics, risk, or readiness. For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, tax, data, content, advertising, support, source access, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, provider-availability, approval, or performance conclusions.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence by default. Define purpose, authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, data lineage, and small-cell suppression. Do not request credentials, private messages, health or financial details, protected traits, precise locations, raw identities, complete histories, or employee reviews when aggregate evidence is sufficient.

Do not identify, rank, or target individuals for consequential acquisition, pricing, service, employment, credit, insurance, health, housing, or similar decisions. Do not infer sensitive traits, reidentify people, join data beyond the declared purpose, exploit vulnerability, or hide material customer and employee tradeoffs.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access analytics, warehouse, CRM, billing, payment, finance, advertising, product, cloud, support, sales, partner, HR, procurement, contract, experiment, or BI systems; export, join, or reidentify people; approve or transfer budgets; purchase; sign; change product, price, package, contract, routing, permissions, systems, or records; launch a pilot or experiment; contact anyone; publish; send; spend; hire; fire; reorganize; deploy; or claim implementation or business results without separate task-level authorization and required controls.

For each proposed external action record owner, system or counterparty, exact mutation or communication, affected population, prerequisite evidence, budget, approver, privacy, security and other review, dry run, customer and operating guardrails, monitoring, cap, stop, rollback, correction, audit, and completion proof.

## Specialist Handoffs

Preserve the input contract, decision, expected artifact, owner, due or maturity date, and permission boundary for every handoff. Common routes include:

- `growth-strategy` for company arenas, portfolio, and exclusions;
- `growth-diagnosis` for the primary current constraint;
- `market-sizing`, `market-entry-strategy`, `customer-research`, `icp-segmentation`, and `competitive-intelligence` for external and customer evidence;
- `growth-model-design`, `growth-forecasting`, `unit-economics-analysis`, `ltv-analysis`, `attribution-analysis`, and `experiment-design` for analytical inputs;
- `growth-infrastructure-assessment`, `growth-organization-design`, `go-to-market-strategy`, and lifecycle specialists for readiness and delivery.

A handoff does not imply access or execution.
