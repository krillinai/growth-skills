# Evidence, Options, And Valuation

## Build The Value Thesis

Trace one nonduplicative chain:

```text
bounded customer and problem
-> product, service, capability, or operating change
-> customer value and proof
-> behavior or process change
-> retained customer or operating outcome
-> incremental revenue, cost, contribution, cash, capacity, or risk effect
```

For each link record evidence, attribution or causal status, lag, maturity, alternatives, counterevidence, and failure signal. Customer activity is not automatically customer value. Attributed revenue is not incrementality. Cost avoidance is not cash received. A strategic asset, capability, or option may matter without a current cash flow, but label its decision use and uncertainty rather than inventing financial value.

## Keep Benefits Compatible

Separate:

- customer value, welfare, trust, and harm;
- qualified demand, first value, repeated value, retention, referral, and expansion;
- users, customers, accounts, seats, orders, transactions, and other operational units;
- GMV, bookings, contracted value, billings, gross revenue, net revenue, gross profit, contribution, cash, and valuation;
- productivity, capacity released, cost reduction, cost avoidance, loss reduction, risk reduction, tax or subsidy effects, salvage, and option value.

Define who receives each benefit, when, on what baseline and value basis, and whether it overlaps another row. Use a deduplication and reconciliation key across customer, cohort, event, product, market, period, and value type. Do not add gross and net measures of the same value or count one customer across lifecycle stages as separate benefits without a causal and economic bridge.

## Separate Attribution And Incrementality

Preserve organic demand, selection, targeting, channel overlap, existing customers, cannibalization, displacement, substitution, seasonality, concurrent changes, refunds, churn, maturity, and interference. Observed before-after movement or dashboard attribution can nominate a mechanism; it cannot prove the investment caused the full outcome.

Route touchpoint and source claims to `attribution-analysis`, causal design to `experiment-design`, funnel behavior to `funnel-analysis`, cohorts to `cohort-analysis`, growth-state reconciliation to `growth-accounting`, and mechanism topology to `growth-model-design`.

## Build Comparable Options

Always include the current-state and do-nothing baseline. Add credible alternatives such as:

- smaller scope or shorter horizon;
- staged research, prototype, or bounded pilot;
- build, buy, compose, partner, license, outsource, or share;
- use an existing capability, channel, product, or team;
- sequence after a prerequisite or another bet;
- preserve an option, defer, pause, stop, salvage, or exit.

Compare options on the same customer, market, product, horizon, currency, accounting and cash bases where possible. Where bases cannot be reconciled, show separate views. Record value, evidence, cost, cash, capacity, time to learning, strategic fit, dependencies, risk, reversibility, residual obligations, opportunity cost, and transfer limits.

Do not create weak alternatives merely to make the preferred option win. Preserve rejected alternatives and the evidence that could reopen them.

## Use Market Evidence Correctly

Market size is context, not company revenue. Keep total, serviceable, reachable, qualified, attainable, and company outcome layers separate. Do not apply a universal market share, list price, conversion, retention, or margin. Route market estimates to `market-sizing`, new-market readiness to `market-entry-strategy`, customers and demand to `customer-research` and `icp-segmentation`, and product-market evidence to `product-market-fit-assessment`.

## Select A Valuation View

Use only views supported by the decision and supplied evidence:

| View | Appropriate use | Minimum contract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Incremental contribution | Compare recurring unit or cohort economics | Revenue and variable or avoidable costs, cohort, maturity, and baseline |
| Cash flow | Assess funding, timing, runway, and liquidity | Time-phased inflows, outflows, working capital, tax, collection, and exit |
| Payback | Estimate time to recover a defined outlay | Cash or contribution basis, start, cumulative schedule, and horizon |
| NPV | Compare time-distributed incremental cash on a declared capital basis | Cash schedule, discount basis, horizon, terminal treatment, tax, and scenario |
| IRR | Supplement, never replace, NPV for conventional cash flows | Complete sign-changing cash sequence and reinvestment limitations |
| Cost-effectiveness | Compare outcomes when benefits are not responsibly monetized | Common outcome unit, full cost, quality, equity, and harm |
| Break-even | Identify the assumption boundary that changes a decision | Compatible fixed, variable, price, volume, time, and capacity inputs |
| Real option | Evaluate staged learning and preserved choice | Option cost, expiry, trigger, follow-on decision, downside, and irreversibility |

Do not invent cash flows, discount rates, useful life, terminal value, probabilities, tax treatment, coefficients, or precision. Show formulas, substituted values, units, source, range, rounding, and limitations when calculating. An unsupported valuation remains `unavailable`, not zero.
