# Growth Investment Case

Turn one proposed growth resource commitment into a traceable decision record. Connect customer value, strategic alignment, evidence, alternatives, incremental benefits and costs, cash, capacity, opportunity cost, risk, reversibility, scenarios, and decision gates without manufacturing precision, approval, authorization, or results.

Read [investment-case-contract.md](references/investment-case-contract.md) before accepting the decision, baseline, options, evidence, strategy link, or recommendation state. Read [evidence-options-and-valuation.md](references/evidence-options-and-valuation.md) before interpreting benefits, attribution, market evidence, alternatives, or valuation. Read [economics-risk-and-decision-gates.md](references/economics-risk-and-decision-gates.md) before modeling resources, costs, cash, capacity, timing, scenarios, sensitivity, risks, pilots, or gates. Read [governance-market-transfer-and-actions.md](references/governance-market-transfer-and-actions.md) before refreshing a case, assigning decision rights, transferring across markets, using personal data, or preparing external actions. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) for the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Inspect whether an existing case is decision-ready and expose unsupported, stale, incompatible, duplicated, or omitted inputs |
| `build` | Construct an evidence-bounded case for one defined future commitment and decision |
| `refresh` | Re-evaluate a versioned case after material customer, strategy, market, product, cost, cash, capacity, risk, timing, or outcome changes |

Name one primary mode. Require one bounded investment, decision owner, scope, horizon, baseline, and alternatives. Route company-level arenas and portfolio allocation to `growth-strategy`, a current constraint and 30-day plan to `growth-diagnosis`, product-market motion design to `go-to-market-strategy`, detailed mechanism equations to `growth-model-design`, organization structure to `growth-organization-design`, and operating forecasts to `growth-forecasting`.

## Freeze The Decision Contract

Record case ID and version, decision, accountable owner, contributors and required approvers, scope and exclusions, product and business model, customers and participant roles, market and locale, strategy version and portfolio class, start and decision dates, operating and benefit horizon, evidence cutoff, current-state and do-nothing baseline, preferred and credible alternatives, constrained resources, target customer and business outcomes, review date, expiry, and external-action boundary.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing claims. An attributable unsupported statement may remain a `reported signal`. Keep actual, estimate, forecast, scenario, target, plan, benchmark, attribution, causal estimate, approval, zero, missing, immature, censored, and suppressed separate.

Do not invent customers, demand, market share, behavior, conversion, retention, prices, costs, margins, cash flows, capacity, useful life, discount rates, probabilities, causal effects, risk scores, forecasts, valuations, confidence, approvals, or outcomes. Public artifacts can verify visible claims at a dated capture; they do not establish private business performance or readiness.

## Establish Baselines And Options

The current-state and do-nothing baselines include residual value, organic change, existing obligations, unavoidable and avoidable costs, risks, capacity, and concurrent initiatives. They are not automatically zero.

Separate sunk, committed, avoidable, recoverable, salvage, exit, and future costs. Do not use sunk cost to justify future spend. Compare the preferred option with credible smaller, staged, build, buy, compose, partner, existing-capability, sequence, preserve, defer, pause, stop, salvage, or exit options as applicable.

Use common customer, market, product, horizon, currency, accounting, cash, capacity, and maturity bases. When options remain incompatible, show separate views rather than an opaque score. Include opportunity cost, displacement, dependency, time to learning, reversibility, residual obligation, and the evidence that could reopen a rejected option.

## Trace Incremental Value

Map one nonduplicative chain from bounded customer and problem through the proposed change, customer value and proof, behavior or process change, retained outcome, and incremental company or ecosystem effect. Record evidence, lag, maturity, causal status, alternatives, contradiction, and failure signal for every link.

Keep customer value, lifecycle behavior, operational units, GMV, bookings, revenue, contribution, cash, productivity, capacity, cost reduction, cost avoidance, loss reduction, risk reduction, salvage, and option value distinct. Define beneficiary, baseline, timing, value basis, overlap, and reconciliation. Do not count one customer or one economic effect repeatedly across lifecycle stages.

Attributed outcomes are not automatically incremental or causal. Preserve organic demand, selection, overlap, cannibalization, displacement, substitution, refunds, churn, seasonality, interference, concurrent changes, and maturity. Route detailed attribution to `attribution-analysis` and causal evidence to `experiment-design`.

Market size is not company revenue. Keep total, serviceable, reachable, qualified, attainable, and company outcome layers separate. Route estimates to `market-sizing`, local readiness to `market-entry-strategy`, customers to `customer-research` and `icp-segmentation`, and fit to `product-market-fit-assessment`.

## Reconcile Economics, Cash, And Capacity

Build an incremental resource and cost ledger covering relevant people, product, data, delivery, marketing, sales, partner, support, security, privacy, finance, procurement, infrastructure, vendor, incentive, payment, implementation, migration, training, working-capital, decommissioning, exit, and opportunity costs.

Keep revenue, contribution, accounting profit, cash, and valuation distinct. Make ramp, delays, adoption, maturity, utilization, seasonality, renewal, useful life, replacement, and exit visible. Connect expected volume and complexity to product, engineering, data, sales, support, fulfillment, security, finance, partner, and leadership capacity.

Use contribution, cash flow, payback, NPV, IRR, cost-effectiveness, break-even, or real-option views only when the decision and supplied inputs support them. Show formulas, units, sources, substituted values, ranges, rounding, and limitations. Unsupported values remain unavailable, not zero.

Route unit economics to `unit-economics-analysis`, lifetime value to `ltv-analysis`, forecasts to `growth-forecasting`, infrastructure to `growth-infrastructure-assessment`, and staffing or decision rights to `growth-organization-design`.

## Test Scenarios, Sensitivity, And Risk

Build conditional scenarios only for assumptions that change the decision. Define coherent state, source, range, lag, maturity, economic and capacity effect, trigger, and response. Do not invent probabilities, average scenarios into an expected case, or call scenarios forecasts.

Use sensitivity to expose the threshold where the recommendation changes. Test only material inputs on compatible bases. Do not generate decorative precision.

Keep customer harm, fairness, privacy, security, safety, fraud, regulatory review, contract, product, reliability, operations, people, partner, platform, concentration, supply, reputation, financial, cash, model, measurement, execution, and opportunity risks distinct. Record exposure, evidence, uncertainty, prevention, mitigation, owner, monitoring, escalation, stop, rollback, and residual risk. Do not average noncompensable harms into financial upside.

## Apply Gates And Recommend

Assess contract, strategic fit, customer value, mechanism, feasibility, economics and cash, risk and reversibility, and governance gates independently. Use `verified ready`, `bounded pilot only`, `specified not verified`, `verified blocker`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. Do not average gates into a readiness score.

Return `approve`, `bounded pilot`, `preserve option`, `defer`, `reject`, or `unresolved` only when evidence distinguishes it. State rationale, first decision-limiting gate, conditions, approvers, expiry, and refresh triggers. A recommendation never means approved, funded, authorized, contracted, implemented, or successful.

A bounded pilot has one decision, capped customer and resource exposure, baseline or alternative, prerequisites, metrics, maturity, positive, negative, harmful, and inconclusive rules, owner, cap, stop, rollback, correction, expiry, and residual-obligation controls. A full rollout or irreversible commitment is not a pilot.

## Refresh Without Rewriting History

Preserve prior inputs, assumptions, formulas, scenarios, risks, recommendation, and rationale. Add new evidence, recalculate only affected compatible views, explain every change, and update options, gates, conditions, expiry, and next review. Do not keep a recommendation because leadership has seen it or rewrite unexpected outcomes as predicted.

## Handle Markets, People, And Actions

Treat each market as a new evidence boundary. Revalidate customers, value, product, channels, payments, invoices, tax, data, regulation, price, costs, contribution, cash, capacity, risks, sources, owners, and gates. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transfer.

For China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, tax, data, content, advertising, support, source access, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not make legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, provider, approval, or performance conclusions.

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary data by default. Do not request sensitive data or rank people for consequential decisions when aggregate evidence is sufficient.

## Deliver In Order

Follow [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md). Deliver the contract; value and strategy thesis; evidence; alternatives; benefits, costs, contribution, cash, capacity, and opportunity cost; scenarios, sensitivity, valuation limits, and risks; gates and recommendation; proof plan and governance; handoffs; pinned sources; external actions; and completion record.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access authenticated company systems; export or join personal data; approve or transfer budgets; purchase; sign; change product, price, contract, systems, or records; launch; contact; publish; spend; hire; fire; reorganize; deploy; or claim implementation or business results without separate task-level authorization and controls.

## Completion Gate

Confirm the output passes [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md), all material claims retain evidence and value bases, alternatives are credible, benefits are incremental and deduplicated, resources and cash are time-phased, risks and gates remain distinct, recommendation is not authorization, handoffs are not execution, and no external action occurred.
