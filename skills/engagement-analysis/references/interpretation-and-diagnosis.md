# Interpretation And Diagnosis

## Evidence Ladder

Keep these states distinct:

1. **Observed activity:** attributable qualifying events inside a frozen contract.
2. **Observed engagement distribution:** reconciled frequency, depth, breadth, quality, or concentration.
3. **Association:** engagement bands differ with a later or concurrent outcome.
4. **Prediction:** pre-outcome features estimate a future state with calibration and error.
5. **Candidate mechanism:** a plausible explanation with supporting and contradictory evidence.
6. **Causal effect:** a credible design estimates what an intervention changed.
7. **Decision and action:** an accountable owner authorizes a bounded response.
8. **Outcome:** mature customer and business evidence after implementation.

Do not promote a lower state because the relationship is intuitive or desired.

## Diagnose Without Causal Leaps

For an observed pattern, record:

- what changed, for which entity, segment, window, and product version;
- compatible expectation, comparator, or prior distribution;
- customer and business relevance;
- source and quality state;
- candidate mechanisms, supporting evidence, contradictory evidence, and alternatives;
- discriminating evidence, owner, decision date, and guardrails.

Alternatives can include natural cadence, unequal opportunities, customer mix, tenure, activation, fit, workflow design, reliability, latency, permissions, collaboration, price or contract state, mandatory use, seasonality, product change, identity, instrumentation, support burden, incentives, or concurrent actions.

Do not call a low-frequency band a drop between first and second use unless the aggregate bucket and event sequence establish that transition. Do not infer a cause from a Power User Curve.

## Retention, PMF, And Outcome Boundaries

Engagement is behavior inside a window. Retention asks whether a frozen cohort continues receiving recurring value across natural opportunities. A high-frequency band can be older, better fit, healthier, seller-selected, contractually required, or already more likely to renew.

To connect engagement with a later outcome, freeze prediction time, engagement window, outcome window, cohort eligibility, maturity, source versions, and leakage boundary. Report later retention, renewal, expansion, customer outcome, quality, cost, and harm by compatible engagement band. Preserve selection, survivorship, reverse causality, tenure, segment, plan, and customer-health alternatives. If prediction is requested, use only information available at prediction time, validate out of sample, report calibration and error by material group, preserve drift and abstention, and keep prediction separate from treatment effect, target, forecast, and plan.

An association can support segmentation or a hypothesis. It does not prove increasing activity will cause retention. Route recurring-value curves to `retention`, PMF claims to `product-market-fit-assessment`, forecasts to `growth-forecasting`, causal effects to `experiment-design`, and operating commitments to `growth-planning-cycle`.

## Product Topology

### Consumer And Habit Products

Use a value-bearing action at the natural cadence. Preserve occasional, regular, and intense users, quality, fatigue, trust, and legitimate low-frequency use. Streaks, reminders, or daily targets require an evidence-backed recurring-value mechanism.

### B2B And Collaborative Products

Separate user, role, seat, workspace, account, administrator, champion, buyer, contract, and revenue. Track purchased, provisioned, invited, accessed, attempted, successful, repeated, collaborative, retained, renewed, and expanded states separately. Mandatory usage is not satisfaction; contracted seats may be shelfware.

### Marketplaces And Networks

Separate each participant side, role, and local network unit. Use verified opportunity where supply or demand is uneven. Distinguish availability, search, listing, match, message, transaction, fulfillment, cancellation, quality, incentive, and retained participant value. Route liquidity and defensibility to `network-effects-strategy`.

### AI Products

Prompts, tokens, model calls, generated outputs, and session length are volume. Define eligible task, successful outcome, quality, verification, correction, retry, abandonment, latency, cost, reliability, trust, and repeated workflow completion. Separate person, workspace, task, model call, product, and commercial entities.

### Low-Frequency And Episodic Products

Use a verified job, task, case, trip, filing, renewal, season, project, purchase, or service opportunity. An entity with no opportunity is not a failed daily user. Compare opportunity completion, readiness, saved state, and later return where appropriate.

## Intervention And Experiment Boundary

An engagement analysis may specify the smallest next evidence or decision test. It does not choose arbitrary sample allocations, holdouts, durations, targets, or expected uplifts. Define the mechanism, eligible population, intervention, primary value metric, guardrails, maturity, owner, and decision rule only from supplied or separately designed evidence.

Do not launch reminders, messages, onboarding changes, feature prompts, incentives, prices, outreach, or product changes. Route copy and eligibility to `lifecycle-marketing`, product mechanisms to the relevant lifecycle Skill, and causal design to `experiment-design`. A route is not authorization or execution.

## Common Mistakes

- Omitting zero-use eligible entities.
- Calling any login, click, prompt, token, session, feature, seat, contract, or revenue engagement.
- Replacing the distribution with DAU/MAU, an average, or one score.
- Inventing daily-use or industry benchmarks.
- Comparing incomplete and mature windows.
- Mixing users, devices, workspaces, accounts, and revenue.
- Calling high activity healthy when it reflects retries, failure, mandatory work, fatigue, or burden.
- Treating association as mechanism, prediction as causal effect, or engagement as retention or PMF.
- Recommending or claiming an intervention without evidence and authorization.
