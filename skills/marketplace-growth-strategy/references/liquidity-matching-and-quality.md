# Liquidity, Matching, And Quality

## Liquidity Contract

Liquidity is the probability and quality of completing a valuable interaction for eligible participants within the declared atomic market and service window. It is not inventory count, user count, search volume, algorithmic candidate generation, or GMV.

Measure the chain separately:

1. eligible demand and relevant available supply;
2. discovery or request creation;
3. candidate coverage and relevance;
4. response or offer;
5. match or allocation;
6. acceptance and payment or commitment;
7. fulfillment and quality;
8. repeat value and side-specific retention;
9. participant and platform economics;
10. trust, safety, and capacity.

For every metric, freeze entity, side, atomic unit, eligible denominator, numerator, identity, deduplication, attribution, window, source, version, incentive state, cohort maturity, and owner.

## Core Measures

| Group | Measures | Failure hidden by averages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Supply | qualified providers, relevant inventory, available hours or units, coverage | registrations without usable availability |
| Demand | eligible demand, high-intent searches or requests, willingness to pay | visits without a real job |
| Discovery | result coverage, candidate count, relevance, position, no-result rate | supply exists but cannot be found |
| Response | response rate and time, offer quality, expiration | listings exist but do not respond |
| Match | accepted match, time to match, choice quality | suggestions counted as matches |
| Fulfillment | completion, cancellation, failure, refund, dispute, recovery | accepted work never delivers value |
| Quality | outcome quality, rating distribution, complaints, rework, safety | high completion with harmful service |
| Repeat | side-specific repeat at natural frequency, retention, resurrection | one-time subsidized activity |
| Economics | utilization, participant earnings or margin, contribution, cost | GMV hides unviable sides |

Report distributions and tails, not only averages. Include zero-result, zero-response, long-wait, low-quality, failed, refunded, harmed, and exited populations.

## Hard-Side Diagnosis

The hard side is the current binding constraint on a complete interaction, not a permanent participant label.

Test:

- availability at the relevant place, time, category, quality, and price;
- demand intent, frequency, urgency, relevance, and willingness to pay;
- onboarding, qualification, trust, response, matching, payment, and fulfillment friction;
- participant utilization, earnings or margin, idle time, support burden, and retention;
- concentration, seasonality, perishability, capacity, congestion, and substitution;
- whether more of one side improves or harms the other side.

Buy neither side before identifying the limiting transition. The constraint can move from supply acquisition to availability, demand quality, matching, fulfillment, trust, repeat value, or economics as the market develops.

## Ranking And Matching Contract

Define decision, eligible candidates, objective, participant sides, service promise, constraints, allowed features, source lineage, model or rule version, exploration, tie-breaking, and consequence.

Balance:

- relevance and intent fit;
- current availability and response probability;
- expected fulfillment and quality;
- participant value and long-term retention;
- price, contribution, and capacity;
- fairness, accessibility, trust, safety, and concentration;
- cold-start opportunity and marketplace learning.

Predicted GMV, fee, or conversion alone can suppress affordable supply, starve new participants, intensify concentration, or exploit vulnerable demand. Measure selection, exposure, position, acceptance, completion, quality, repeat, economics, and harm by decision-relevant cohort.

Require minimum-necessary permissioned features, validation, calibration, drift monitoring, reason codes, transparency, correction, appeal, human review, override, audit, stop, rollback, incident, and recovery. Model confidence is not approval or authority.

## Quality And Congestion

More participation can create duplicate or low-quality inventory, spam, slow response, price pressure, provider oversupply, buyer overload, fraud, unsafe service, congestion, or support burden.

Preserve a negative or mixed verdict when volume grows but relevance, time, completion, quality, participant economics, trust, or retention falls. Do not let high-growth units compensate for harmed sides or failing local markets.
