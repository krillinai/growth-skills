# Dependencies, Priorities, And Governance

## Dependency Order

Do not automate or scale a dependent capability before its decision-critical foundations are supported:

```text
customer and value entity
-> identity, eligibility, permission, and authoritative sources
-> metric semantics, quality, lineage, and reconciliation
-> experiment assignment, exposure, economics, and causal boundaries
-> decision rights, service ownership, and operating controls
-> reliable workflow, adoption, and downstream feedback
-> bounded assistance or automation
```

Map prerequisite, consumer, failure propagation, control gate, evidence gate, and recovery path. A polished interface, model, or agent can amplify upstream error faster.

## Noncompensable Conditions

Never average away privacy, security, consent, accessibility, reliability, data quality, incident response, financial control, or customer protection. For each hard constraint, record:

- inherent exposure and affected people;
- control design, accountable owner, and required evidence;
- operating coverage, exceptions, incidents, and recovery;
- tolerance, residual exposure, acceptance authority, and expiry;
- verification, escalation, stop condition, and residual obligations.

Return `blocked`, `conditional`, or `not assessable` by decision when a hard constraint fails. Preserve capability strengths without allowing them to compensate for harm.

## Priority Method

Do not prioritize all gaps. A finding becomes a candidate only if it changes the declared recurring decision, current constraint, customer value, or hard constraint.

Compare candidates using:

1. customer value and protection;
2. current constraint and decision frequency;
3. prerequisite and downstream dependencies;
4. evidence strength and uncertainty reduction;
5. failure consequence, reversibility, and residual risk;
6. capacity, maintenance, management load, and adoption burden;
7. direct, operating, migration, lock-in, and opportunity cost;
8. smallest sufficient state and explicit stop rule.

Preserve `maintain`, `improve`, `standardize`, `keep local`, `pilot`, `compose`, `consolidate`, `retire`, `defer`, `route`, and `not applicable`. Build, buy, hire, reorganize, budget, procure, and implement are separate decisions requiring their owning Skills and approvals.

## Central, Embedded, Or Federated

Do not treat either centralization or autonomy as maturity. Define the recurring decision, customer context, consumer, owner, decision rights, service, dependency, and failure consequence.

Evaluate central, embedded, federated, platform-plus-local, temporary, vendor, and composed options against shared standards, local judgment, reuse, queues, latency, handoffs, rework, workarounds, adoption, duplication, cost, maintenance, incidents, and recovery. Keep one accountable outcome and an explicit interface for every capability.

Centralize stable reusable computation, controls, and services when shared reliability exceeds coordination cost. Keep customer, product, and market judgment local when context materially changes the answer. Use evidence-based change triggers rather than organizational ideology.

## Automation And AI

Use `manual`, `assisted`, `bounded automation`, or `autonomous` only for a named decision and consequence. Require:

- input, source, prompt or policy, model, tool, output, version, and lineage;
- reviewer, authority, allowed action, reason code, abstention, and escalation;
- validation, calibration, drift, reproducibility, quality, cost, latency, and capacity;
- access, audit, override, stop, rollback, incident, recovery, and change control;
- feedback-loop, leakage, hallucination, unsafe-action, customer-harm, and residual-risk review.

Separate draft, recommendation, approval, authorization, execution, verification, and result. Model confidence is not evidence, approval, or authority. Self-modification, budget allocation, pricing, customer scoring, and other consequential actions require proportionate specialist governance and explicit authorization.

## Market Transfer

For every market, separate geography, language, locale, currency, timezone, legal entity, product surface, customers, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, consent, privacy, support, vendor, staffing, and decision rights.

Revalidate sources, operating evidence, calendars, ownership, capability states, and review locally. Do not infer legal, labor, tax, regulatory, provider, accounting, consent, approval, causal, or performance conclusions from translation, geography, or another market's assessment.

## People And Privacy

Assess the work system, not individual worth. Use aggregate workload, queue, decision, service, incident, maintenance, adoption, and outcome evidence. Apply purpose limitation, minimum necessity, access control, retention, deletion, correction, audit, small-cell suppression, fairness, accessibility, dissent, appeal, and independent review.

Do not rank named people or teams, infer protected traits, surveil employees, set workloads, deny leave, change compensation, or support employment decisions. Route consequential people questions to accountable HR, legal, privacy, accessibility, and management review.
