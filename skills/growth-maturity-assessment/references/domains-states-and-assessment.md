# Domains, States, And Assessment

## Domain Map

Assess domains independently and only when they support the scoped decisions.

| Domain | Capability questions | Owning specialist examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Strategy | Is customer value, constraint, choice, horizon, economic logic, and stop rule explicit? | `growth-diagnosis`, `growth-model-design` |
| Lifecycle | Can acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, referral, and expansion decisions follow retained value? | lifecycle analysis and strategy Skills |
| Measurement | Are entities, identities, sources, metric contracts, cohorts, quality, lineage, and guardrails trustworthy? | `growth-metrics-design`, `growth-data-quality-audit` |
| Experimentation | Are eligibility, assignment, exposure, inference, guardrails, maturity, decisions, and learning valid? | experiment Skills |
| Infrastructure | Do shared data, decision, delivery, creative, and governance services meet real consumer needs? | `growth-infrastructure-assessment` |
| Organization | Are outcome ownership, decision rights, interfaces, capacity, and maintenance accountable? | `growth-organization-design` |
| Operations | Are planning, prioritization, review, incidents, change, cost, and follow-through reliable? | planning, review, risk, and change Skills |
| Risk | Are privacy, security, consent, accessibility, reliability, customer protection, and specialist boundaries operating? | accountable specialist review |
| Learning | Are decisions, evidence, failures, corrections, transfer limits, and expiry reusable? | `growth-learning-system`, `growth-postmortem` |

Do not average domain findings. A strong acquisition capability cannot repair broken identity, consent, reliability, or retained value.

## Capability State Lattice

States are separate evidence questions, not a mandatory ladder or numerical scale.

```text
needed -> defined -> approved/provisioned -> implemented -> operated
                                                   |          |
                                                   v          v
                                                adopted -> reliable
                                                   |          |
                                                   +----> decision-integrated
                                                               |
                                                               v
                                                     outcome-verified -> learned
```

The arrows show common dependencies, not automatic promotion. A capability can be implemented but unused, adopted but unreliable, reliable but irrelevant to decisions, or associated with good outcomes without causing them.

For each state, record required state, observed state, evidence, contradiction, limiting condition, and next evidence gate. Do not compress the state lattice into a 1-5 label.

## Activity Is Not Capability

Keep these measures distinct:

1. access and provisioning;
2. activity and throughput;
3. output and completion;
4. eligible adoption and repeat use;
5. quality and reliability;
6. efficiency and operating burden;
7. attributable decision use;
8. customer outcome and harm;
9. business economics;
10. reusable learning.

Always include zero-use, abandoned, invalid, reversed, harmful, disputed, stale, duplicate, and failed work. Event count, dashboards, experiments, creatives, messages, meetings, automations, tasks, and autonomy rate are activity measures until downstream evidence says otherwise.

## Decision-Specific Findings

Use `sufficient`, `conditional`, `insufficient`, `blocked`, `not assessable`, or `not applicable` only for a named decision, consumer, scope, consequence, and horizon.

Ask:

- Is the capability repeatedly needed, or is a manual/local method sufficient?
- Are definition, implementation, operation, adoption, reliability, and decision use supported separately?
- Does the consumer change or improve a bounded decision?
- Are customer value, economics, trust, and guardrails preserved?
- Can the capability degrade, fail, recover, be overridden, and be retired safely?
- Does its management and maintenance cost displace higher-value work?

Higher sophistication is justified only by repeated demand, decision value, consequence, scale, or risk. A small company can be sufficient with manual or standardized capability; an advanced platform can be insufficient if definitions, adoption, or controls fail.

## Outcomes And Causality

Separate:

- observed result;
- external conditions and selection;
- attribution or model estimate;
- causal effect and uncertainty;
- capability operation;
- decision quality;
- customer value and harm;
- durability and transfer;
- business economics.

Revenue growth alone does not certify strategy, lifecycle, systems, organization, or learning. Check market growth, price, spend, mix, one-off virality, concentration, churn, support burden, incidents, cash, contribution, customer trust, and whether repeatable decisions preceded the result.

## Case And Benchmark Transfer

Record source, period, company context, artifact type, claim, limitation, and transfer boundary. Distinguish historical fact, reported claim, transferable principle, implementation detail, and unsupported inference.

Revalidate local customer, product, business model, stage, market, scale, decision, constraint, economics, capacity, regulation, and risk. Use a case only when it changes a local capability question or evidence plan. Meta, Booking.com, Ramp, or any vendor stage cannot establish a local requirement, benchmark, causal claim, or current best practice.
