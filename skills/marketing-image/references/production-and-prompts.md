# Image Production And Prompts

## Production Dependency Order

```text
decision and audience
-> asset role and concept
-> truthful product, claim, proof, and source
-> channel, placement, dimensions, and safe area
-> composition and hierarchy
-> source, generation, edit, or capture method
-> purposeful variants
-> local file inspection and QA
```


## Generation Instruction

Specify subject, action, environment, viewpoint, framing, spatial hierarchy, focal point, lighting, materials, color, texture, depth, copy-free region, product requirements, factual boundaries, brand cues, negative constraints, ratio, and resolution.

Use positive concrete constraints before long negative lists. Avoid asking a model to render final small text, exact logos, detailed UI, data charts, or legal disclaimers when deterministic composition is required. Generate the visual field, then place approved text and brand assets through an appropriate deterministic design workflow when available.

## Edit Instruction

Specify source, selection or mask, add, remove, replace, preserve, edge, shadow, reflection, perspective, grain, color, light, resolution, crop, background, metadata, and acceptance criteria. Preserve original files and record lineage.

Do not remove a watermark, signature, attribution, safety marker, disclosure, product warning, or material limitation. Do not alter a person's body, identity, context, or testimony deceptively.

## Variant Matrix

Keep concept stable unless intentionally testing a new one. Useful controlled dimensions include placement ratio, crop, framing, background, visual proof, copy length, hierarchy, CTA treatment, locale, and accessible text alternative. Change one interpretable dimension or explicitly label a bundled execution.

Do not create dozens of near-identical files without a distribution, placement, or learning purpose.
