# Public Relations

Turn attributable facts and public relevance into truthful, audience-specific communication and accountable response. Optimize understanding, trust, and qualified downstream behavior, not release volume, impressions, mentions, share of voice, sentiment, links, or attributed pipeline alone.

Read [pr-contract.md](references/pr-contract.md) before accepting a fact, quote, source, relationship, embargo, claim, approval, incident, or outcome. Read [narratives-and-assets.md](references/narratives-and-assets.md) before planning a narrative or drafting a release, pitch, brief, FAQ, press kit, or contributed piece. Read [response-and-governance.md](references/response-and-governance.md) before issue response, crisis materials, media handling, measurement, correction, or postmortem. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `strategy` | Define a public, objective, narrative, evidence, media role, asset, timing, measurement, and governance plan |
| `asset` | Draft or audit one bounded local PR artifact from approved facts and sources |
| `response` | Build a verified-fact ledger, stakeholder map, holding structure, questions, approvals, updates, and correction plan for an issue |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, public or stakeholder, subject, market, horizon, timing state, sensitivity, and external-action boundary.

## Freeze The PR Contract

Record organization and real spokesperson; public, stakeholder, customer, employee, partner, regulator, investor, or community audience; objective, decision, public interest, news event, product and market scope; exact facts, claims, dates, numbers, quotes, sources, counterevidence, limitations, confidentiality, privacy, rights, approvals, embargo, timing, outlet or media category, relationship source, disclosure, paid or sponsored status, distribution, owner, correction, archive, measurement, and requested external action.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Never invent a news event, journalist interest, relationship, exclusive, quote, customer, employee, spokesperson, endorsement, award, ranking, study, market size, product capability, date, impact, cause, remedy, legal status, response, coverage, sentiment, or result.

## Establish Newsworthiness Without Manufacturing It

Test whether the subject contains a timely verified change, consequence, evidence, human or customer relevance, public-interest question, useful data, accountable point of view, or access to a qualified source. Distinguish company importance from public relevance.

Choose one honest narrative:

```text
verified event or evidence
-> affected public and why it matters now
-> context and real alternatives
-> bounded claim, proof, and limitations
-> accountable source and next verifiable information
```

Do not exaggerate routine features into industry transformation, fabricate urgency, borrow a tragedy, attack critics, create fake grassroots support, seed anonymous rumors, impersonate reporters or customers, pay for undisclosed coverage, suppress material facts, or promise favorable reporting.

## Match The Asset To The Decision

A press release records a publishable verified event, context, approved quotes, facts, resources, and contact handoff. A media pitch proposes why one bounded story may matter to a specific media audience; it is not a bulk template or proof of interest. A briefing document prepares facts, difficult questions, limitations, bridges, and escalation. A contributed article teaches a defensible point of view and discloses relevant interests; it is not disguised advertising.

Draft only from supplied or attributable sources. Mark unresolved facts and approvals visibly. Do not release private names, contact details, customer data, confidential terms, security details, personal or sensitive information, or quotes without scoped permission. Route final marketing prose to `copywriting`, edits to `copy-editing`, launch coordination to `campaign-planning`, positioning to `positioning`, customer evidence to `customer-research`, and visual production to the relevant image or video capability.

## Handle Issues With A Separate Gate

In `response` mode, first build a timestamped fact ledger: what is verified, reported, inferred, unavailable, contradicted, affected, disclosed, contained, corrected, under investigation, and owned. Separate operational action from public communication.

For safety, security, privacy, legal, financial, employment, health, or regulatory issues, require accountable incident and specialist owners. Do not speculate about cause, affected population, blame, intent, containment, resolution, liability, or required notice. A holding statement acknowledges only approved facts, affected stakeholders, current action, next update, and contact or help path.

Do not use PR to conceal harm, intimidate sources, coordinate deception, delete evidence, evade required reporting, manipulate markets, or retaliate. Preserve correction, accessibility, translation, archive, and update parity across channels.

## Measure The Communication Chain

Keep distribution, delivery, pickup, coverage, audience quality, message accuracy, comprehension, trust, qualified response, product or stakeholder behavior, business outcome, attribution, and incrementality separate. Classify earned, owned, paid, sponsored, partner, analyst, community, and social exposure independently.

Coverage volume and sentiment are descriptive. Share of voice depends on a defined query, source set, entity, period, geography, language, inclusion rules, and duplicates. Backlinks, traffic, leads, pipeline, or revenue attributed after coverage do not establish causal impact. Preserve negative, corrective, inaccurate, syndicated, inaccessible, and unavailable coverage.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, audience, subject, market, timing, sensitivity, and boundary;
2. fact, claim, quote, source, rights, privacy, approval, embargo, and limitation ledger;
3. public-interest and newsworthiness assessment with rejected angles;
4. narrative and message architecture with counterevidence and difficult questions;
5. asset brief or bounded local draft with unresolved items visible;
6. media, stakeholder, timing, disclosure, accessibility, localization, correction, and update plan;
7. measurement, learning, incident, postmortem, and governance plan;
8. approvals, handoffs, Playbook sources, and external-action boundary.

For China work, keep China, language, locale, organization and spokesperson identity, audience, media or platform, account access, relationship, content type, advertising or sponsored status, product and market claim, personal information, consent, content review, publication, hosting, data, archiving, correction, and applicable rules separate. Do not infer WeChat, Weibo, Douyin, Xiaohongshu, news outlets, media access, publication permission, content acceptance, or legal conditions from China or Simplified Chinese.

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not scrape or build journalist lists; discover or enrich personal contact data; access media databases, email, social, publishing, analytics, CRM, legal, incident, employee, customer, investor, or partner systems; contact, pitch, brief, invite, pay, threaten, or monitor people; promise exclusives; set or break embargoes; submit contributed articles; issue corrections; use logos, quotes, or private assets; publish, schedule, boost, syndicate, delete, or alter content; buy coverage; respond from live accounts; or claim outreach, coverage, approval, containment, resolution, or results.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that audience, objective, public relevance, facts, claims, quotes, sources, counterevidence, rights, privacy, approvals, timing, embargo, disclosure, media type, relationship, difficult questions, incident ownership, corrections, measurement, archive, and action boundaries are explicit; advertising is not earned media; a release is not coverage; mentions are not trust; attribution is not causality; unavailable facts stay unavailable; and no external action occurred.
