# Public Relations Contract

## Intake

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, mode, objective, subject, sensitivity, horizon, timing, and allowed action;
- organization, product, edition, market, language, locale, spokesperson, title, role, and real contact handoff;
- public, customer, user, employee, partner, investor, regulator, community, analyst, creator, or media audience;
- verified event, date, change, consequence, public interest, customer relevance, context, alternative, and next information;
- exact fact, number, claim, quote, source, proof, condition, limitation, counterevidence, owner, approval, expiry, and prohibited wording;
- named person, customer, company, logo, image, video, document, study, dataset, trademark, copyright, confidentiality, privacy, consent, and usage rights;
- outlet or media category, audience, relationship source, prior interaction, exclusivity, embargo, timing, channel, accessibility, localization, disclosure, and paid status;
- issue or incident ID, timestamp, affected stakeholder, verified status, investigation, action owner, help path, next update, correction, and archive;
- distribution, pickup, coverage, message, audience, response, trust, product, stakeholder, business, attribution, and causal evidence;
- source title or artifact, direct URL or locator, publisher or owner, capture date, evidence state, access, and limitation.

Version the contract when facts, scope, timing, claim, spokesperson, quote, approval, incident state, disclosure, market, or distribution changes.

## Evidence Rules

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. A `reported signal` is an attributable statement without inspectable support and has no evidence state.

Public artifacts can verify visible statements, published facts, product documentation, prior coverage, public filings, and dated claims. They cannot verify private approvals, journalist interest, relationships, embargo acceptance, confidential events, customer permission, internal cause, remediation, audience reception, or impact.

## Quote And Named-Source Record

Record exact supplied wording, speaker, role, organization, context, date, edit permission, translation, channel, market, term, approval, confidentiality, and withdrawal or correction path. Never compose a quote and attribute it to a real person unless that person supplied and approved the exact text through an authorized workflow.

Paraphrases, translations, summaries, and draft quote options must be labeled as such and never presented as approved speech.

## Privacy Boundary

Prefer public, aggregate, redacted, or explicitly approved evidence. Exclude raw personal contacts, credentials, private messages, customer records, health, family, protected traits, financial details, exact location, security details, employment records, and unnecessary incident information.

Record purpose, permission, confidentiality, minimum disclosure, audience, access, retention, deletion, correction, and escalation. Public interest does not eliminate privacy, safety, or source-protection obligations.
