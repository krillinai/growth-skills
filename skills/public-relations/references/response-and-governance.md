# PR Response And Governance

## Response Gate

For an issue or incident, create a timestamped ledger:

- verified fact;
- reported signal and source;
- inferred mechanism;
- unavailable question;
- contradiction;
- affected stakeholder and support path;
- operational owner and action state;
- communication owner and approval state;
- next evidence and update time;
- correction, translation, accessibility, distribution, and archive state.

Do not fill silence with speculation. Do not state containment, resolution, cause, scope, notice, liability, or safety without approved evidence and responsible specialist ownership.

## Holding Structure

Where appropriate, use:

```text
what can be acknowledged now
who may be affected and how to get help
what accountable action is underway
what remains under investigation
when and where the next verified update will appear
```

Avoid defensive adjectives, blame, attacks on sources, premature apologies that invent facts, guarantees, and promises outside operating control.

## Media And Stakeholder Handling

Define authorized spokespersons, inbound triage, verification, priority, response ownership, timing, recordkeeping, privacy, source protection, difficult questions, escalation, correction, and channel parity. Keep employees, customers, partners, investors, regulators, and media needs distinct.

An inbound question does not authorize disclosure. An embargo is a documented relationship condition, not text in an unsolicited email.

## Measurement

Record media universe, query, entities, language, geography, source types, period, duplicates, syndication, corrections, accessibility, reach method, message criteria, sentiment method, links, traffic, qualified response, downstream outcome, attribution, costs, and limitations.

Do not compare share of voice across different queries or source sets. Machine sentiment can miss irony, language, context, and factual accuracy. Coverage quality includes accuracy, relevance, audience, correction, and stakeholder value, not positivity alone.

## Governance

Maintain fact and claim registry, approved descriptions and bios, quote and rights records, media-resource versions, relationship and embargo records, disclosures, asset permissions, issue logs, corrections, approvals, access, retention, and postmortems.

Define intake, fact review, sensitivity classification, narrative, specialist review, approval, translation, accessibility, distribution handoff, monitoring, correction, archive, and retirement. Every transition needs an owner, input, output, exception, service expectation, and completion proof.
