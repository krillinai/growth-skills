# PR Narratives And Assets

## Newsworthiness Test

Assess:

- what changed and when;
- who is affected and why it matters;
- what evidence makes the change credible;
- what is genuinely new versus routine promotion;
- what context, alternative, consequence, limitation, or disagreement is useful;
- who can answer difficult questions accountably;
- what information is unavailable or not approved;
- why this audience and timing are appropriate.

Choose `proceed`, `develop evidence`, `use owned communication`, `respond only`, or `do not pursue`. A weak earned-media story may still support accurate customer or owned communication.

## Narrative Record

```text
verified event or evidence
public and current relevance
context and real alternative
bounded thesis or angle
facts, proof, quote, and demonstration
conditions, limitations, counterevidence, and hard questions
disclosure, source access, resources, and next verifiable update
```

Do not start with a superlative, trend claim, executive opinion, or desired headline and backfill evidence.

## Press Release

Include only as applicable:

1. accurate working headline and dateline;
2. concise verified event and relevance;
3. material facts, scope, dates, availability, and conditions;
4. approved exact quotes;
5. context, proof, limitations, and resources;
6. organization description consistent with approved positioning;
7. disclosure, rights, accessibility, media-resource, and contact handoff;
8. approval, embargo, publication, correction, and archive state.

Avoid SEO padding, unsupported market claims, invented quotes, exaggerated adjectives, fake urgency, and vague calls to learn more when a precise resource exists.

## Media Pitch

Specify one media audience, relevance, timely angle, source and proof access, why the story differs from known context, limitations, disclosure, timing, and a low-friction response option. Personalize only from attributable public or supplied evidence. Do not pretend familiarity, a prior relationship, an exclusive, or interest.

Drafting does not authorize contact. Do not discover or include personal emails or phone numbers.

## Briefing And FAQ

Prepare verified facts, chronology, message hierarchy, approved quote boundaries, likely and difficult questions, concise direct answers, unavailable items, do-not-speculate areas, bridging only after answering, demonstrations or resources, disclosure, escalation, correction, and next-update rules.

Do not use message discipline to evade a factual question. Record when an answer requires product, security, privacy, finance, legal, HR, or incident ownership.

## Contributed Content And Press Kit

Contributed material must teach a defensible point of view, cite evidence, preserve counterevidence, disclose relevant interests, and fit the publication's stated rules. A press kit may include approved facts, bios, product or company descriptions, images, logos, data notes, demonstrations, and rights records. Remove stale or superseded assets.
