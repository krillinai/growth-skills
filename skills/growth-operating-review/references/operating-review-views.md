# Operating Review Views

## Select Material Views

Include a view only when it can change a decision, reveal customer or business health, test an assumption, expose a risk, or close a prior commitment. Preserve stable items by reference to their version rather than recreating them.

## Customer And Lifecycle View

Trace qualified acquisition through first, repeated, monetized, referred, expanded, restored, or protected value where applicable. Use compatible eligible cohorts, identity, windows, and maturity. Report absolute populations, rates, time to value, downstream quality, segment mix, customer friction, harm, and guardrails.

Do not count one customer or outcome independently at activation, retention, and expansion. Route detailed analysis to `activation`, `retention`, `customer-expansion-strategy`, `funnel-analysis`, or `cohort-analysis`.

## Acquisition View

Connect source and spend to qualified demand, first value, retained value, contribution, cash, marginal performance, saturation, and concentration. Separate reported, attributed, incremental, and causal outcomes. Include new-customer mix, refunds, audience or channel overlap, and platform dependency where evidence exists.

Route account or channel work to the appropriate audit Skill and portfolio decisions to `acquisition-strategy`. A volume increase does not authorize more spend.

## Growth Accounting View

Reconcile opening customer or recurring-value stock through retained, new, resurrected, expanded, contracted, churned, adjusted, and closing states. Keep entity counts and ARR, revenue, contribution, or cash values separate. Preserve identity breaks, mix, currency, accounting, maturity, and adjustment limits. Route construction to `growth-accounting`.

## Economics And Cash View

Keep orders, GMV, bookings, recognized revenue, gross profit, contribution, invoices, cash, and valuation separate. Include discounts, acquisition, delivery, incentives, payment, refunds, fraud, support, success, sales, partners, infrastructure, compliance, working capital, and opportunity costs where relevant.

Keep average and marginal economics distinct. Match CAC, payback, and LTV to compatible customer cohorts, value bases, and maturity. Route detailed work to `unit-economics-analysis`, `ltv-analysis`, `monetization`, or `pricing-and-packaging-strategy`.

## Portfolio And Capacity View

For each material initiative or bet record strategy version, portfolio class, customer and business outcome, mechanism, evidence state, owner and rights, capacity, opportunity cost, dependencies, economics, risks, guardrails, commitment level, expiry, and graduation, scale, cap, stop, fallback, or rollback rule.

Use `retain`, `revise`, `accelerate`, `cap`, `pause`, `graduate`, or `retire`. Do not mark every initiative on track or keep it funded because work started. Route portfolio redesign to `growth-strategy` and one resource-approval case to `growth-investment-case`.

## Systems And Risk View

Review only capabilities and risks that affect current decisions: data freshness and quality, identity, metrics, experiments, delivery, creative, billing, reliability, security, privacy, trust, support, capacity, regulatory review, partners, concentration, and incident state. Record exposure, evidence, owner, prevention, monitoring, threshold, escalation, stop, rollback, and residual risk.

Do not average noncompensable customer, employee, fairness, privacy, security, safety, or regulatory harms into financial upside. Route capability assessment to `growth-infrastructure-assessment`.
