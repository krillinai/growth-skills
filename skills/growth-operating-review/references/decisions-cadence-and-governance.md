# Decisions, Cadence, And Governance

## Match Work To Cadence

| Cadence | Primary focus | Usually route elsewhere |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weekly | fresh operational signals, incidents, near-term gates, commitments, capacity, and exceptions | mature retention, LTV, market size, strategy, organization, or annual model rebuilds |
| Monthly | mature lifecycle, growth accounting, economics, forecast reconciliation, experiments, portfolio, and recurring risks | fundamental strategic choices without the required evidence or authority |
| Quarterly | strategy assumptions, Four Fits, portfolio allocation, capabilities, scenarios, concentration, and structural change | detailed specialist execution and project status |

Metric maturity can move a decision to a later review. Do not force a long-window conclusion into a weekly cadence.

## Record Decisions

For each decision include context, options, evidence, contradiction, customer and economic effects, risk, owner, required approver, state, rationale, conditions, expiry, and review trigger. Use:

- `decide`: evidence supports the bounded decision;
- `continue`: current commitment remains valid under named conditions;
- `investigate`: a discriminating evidence task is required;
- `escalate`: authority, risk, scope, or conflict exceeds the forum;
- `defer`: maturity, evidence, dependency, or timing blocks a decision;
- `stop`: a named condition invalidates continuation;
- `route`: a specialist workflow owns the next artifact.

Analytical decision states are not approvals, budget releases, authorizations, execution, or results.

## Govern Commitments

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Owner | One accountable person, not a team name alone |
| Artifact or outcome | Observable delivery or decision-changing evidence |
| Date | Due date or decision cutoff |
| Dependency | Upstream input, owner, and failure path |
| Completion proof | Attributable artifact or mature outcome, not `in progress` |
| Permission boundary | Systems, data, spend, contact, publication, and deployment allowed or excluded |
| State | `open`, `blocked`, `complete`, `revised`, `cancelled`, or `expired` |

Review every open commitment. Do not carry it indefinitely. Preserve prior decisions and explain revisions without hindsight rewriting.

## Control Attendance And Escalation

Invite people because they own evidence, a decision, approval, risk, or commitment. Separate read-ahead from meeting decisions. Escalate unresolved conflicts, noncompensable harms, material incidents, cross-portfolio capacity, or decisions outside authority with an owner, evidence packet, requested decision, and deadline.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat each market as a separate evidence boundary. Revalidate customers, definitions, baselines, cohorts, targets, forecasts, channels, platforms, prices, payments, invoices, economics, capacity, risks, data access, source freshness, owners, and cadence. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transfer.

For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Avoid legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, provider-availability, or performance conclusions.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence. Define purpose, authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, and small-cell suppression. Do not expose sensitive customer or employee data or rank individuals for consequential decisions when aggregate evidence suffices.

## Govern External Actions

A review record is not authorization. Create and read local artifacts only unless the user separately authorizes a bounded action. For every proposed external action record owner, system, exact mutation, affected population, evidence, approval, privacy and security review, budget, guardrails, dry run, monitoring, stop, rollback, audit, and completion proof. Never claim execution or impact from a decision record.
