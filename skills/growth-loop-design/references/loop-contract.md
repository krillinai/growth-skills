# Loop Contract

Freeze the mechanism before calculating or designing it. A loop is a repeated value and distribution system, not a circular diagram.

## Core Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Eligible source | Entity allowed and able to begin the declared cycle |
| Participant | Eligible source that performs the value-producing action |
| Value-producing action | Action that serves the source or receiving entity, not merely the company's distribution goal |
| Output | Artifact, invitation, content, supply, data, contribution, capacity, or resource produced by the action |
| Qualified next input | Output that becomes an eligible, useful input to a later cycle under an observable rule |
| Response | Receiving entity or system action after the qualified input arrives |
| Realized value | Observable customer or system benefit, not exposure or registration alone |
| Retained quality | Continued or matured value at the declared natural interval |
| Re-entry | Rule connecting retained or matured output to a subsequent value-producing action |
| Cycle time | Time from an eligible source entering one cycle to a qualified next input entering a later cycle |

The source and receiving entity can be the same or different. A shared document may recruit a collaborator; useful content may become future discovery inventory; marketplace supply may attract demand that attracts more supply; matured contribution may fund qualified acquisition. Each mechanism still needs a specific value exchange and observable re-entry.

## Loop, Funnel, Flywheel, And Network Effect

- A funnel measures movement and loss through ordered states. It can exist inside a loop, but its terminal output is not automatically a next input.
- A loop explains how one cycle produces a later qualified input. It can contribute growth without being self-sustaining.
- A flywheel is a strategic representation of mutually reinforcing forces. It needs operational loop definitions before it can be measured.
- A network effect means product value changes as network participation changes. Invitations, virality, or marketplace growth do not establish a network effect by themselves.
- A channel delivers demand. It becomes part of a loop only when a qualified output replenishes a later input under defensible economics and value.

## Required Decision Context

Record:

- decision, owner, decision date, and decision window;
- product, customer, business model, stage, market, language, and locale;
- final qualified customer and business outcome;
- loop family as a hypothesis, not a verdict;
- source entity, receiving entity or system, and level: person, account, team, workspace, content unit, listing, transaction, market, cohort, or another declared entity;
- eligibility and exclusion rules for every side;
- current activation, retained-value, and downstream-quality definitions;
- known constraints, external dependencies, and requested action.

Do not use `everyone`, `all users`, `viral`, `organic`, `network`, or `word of mouth` as an operating entity or eligibility rule.

## Mechanism Contract

For every node and transition, record:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Entity and level | Who or what moves, produces, receives, or re-enters |
| Eligibility | Observable inclusion, exclusion, and opportunity rule |
| Value | Benefit to source, recipient, and system where relevant |
| Entry and success events | Named, versioned, attributable events or state rules |
| Numerator and denominator | Counts using the same eligible population and window |
| Identity and deduplication | How repeated people, accounts, artifacts, or events are reconciled |
| Cohort and maturity | Entry rule, observation window, late arrival, censoring, and natural interval |
| Output and qualification | What is produced and what makes it useful as a next input |
| Re-entry | Which later cycle receives the input and when |
| Quality and economics | Activation, retention, contribution, payback, trust, abuse, or other declared guardrails |
| Source | Artifact, owner, system, date range, extraction or capture date, and limitations |

When customers can skip, repeat, reverse, branch, or participate in overlapping loops, use a state or graph model rather than forcing one linear sequence.

## Evidence Rules

Use exactly one evidence state for each material evidence-bearing claim:

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `verified` | Directly supported by a supplied attributable source with compatible scope and date |
| `reported signal` | Stated by a person or source but not independently supported by inspectable evidence |
| `inferred` | Reasoned interpretation with assumptions and competing explanations visible |
| `unavailable` | Needed evidence was not supplied, was declined, cannot be accessed, or is incompatible |
| `not applicable` | The question does not apply to the declared mechanism or decision |

Public artifacts can verify visible product mechanics, public content, offers, policies, destinations, and dated external surfaces. They cannot establish private eligibility, participation, conversion, retention, economics, incrementality, abuse, capacity, or system access.

Private evidence is optional. Missing or declined private data is not a defect. Ask for the smallest privacy-safe aggregate that changes the verdict or decision.

## Compatibility Gate

Calculate or compare only when entity, level, eligibility, event definitions, identity, exclusions, timezone, market, cohort, window, maturity, and source are compatible. Preserve zeroes, missingness, late arrival, duplicate identities, right censoring, re-entry, cross-device loss, and platform reporting changes.

Do not:

- multiply invitation rate from one cohort by acceptance from another and retention from a third;
- compare immature recipients with mature source cohorts;
- combine people, accounts, teams, content units, transactions, or markets without an explicit mapping;
- use attributed conversions as incremental outcomes;
- use average economics to govern marginal reinvestment;
- exclude failed recipients, low-quality supply, refunds, fraud, or churn merely because they weaken the loop;
- silently treat missing data as zero.

If compatibility fails, return the required reconciliation or instrumentation specification instead of a number.

## Verdict Rules

Assign one verdict:

### `verified closed`

Use only when compatible attributable evidence observes the declared output becoming a qualified later input, realized value, retained quality or matured economics, and subsequent participation. State the scope and cycle window. This is not proof of incrementality, a network effect, unlimited compounding, or future persistence.

### `partially observed`

Use when output-to-input behavior is observed but a required downstream transition, maturity, quality, economics, or later participation is unavailable or incompatible. Name the missing transition.

### `hypothesis`

Use when a proposed or reported mechanism has plausible value and re-entry logic but compatible closure evidence is unavailable. Do not emit a coefficient or forecast.

### `not a loop`

Use when the mechanism terminates, no qualified output returns, a campaign only produces attributed response, or a diagram merely connects its final state to its first label. Preserve any useful funnel or campaign diagnosis and route it appropriately.

## Attribution And Incrementality

Attribution assigns credit under a declared rule. It can operate a loop measurement system but does not establish that the mechanism caused the outcome. Incrementality requires a credible counterfactual design. Keep organic, referral, direct, paid, content, marketplace, and product-assigned outcomes visible under their source definitions; do not add overlapping platform credit.

## Privacy, Trust, And Market Boundary

Do not request or expose raw contacts, names, emails, phone numbers, customer IDs, message histories, custom audiences, payment records, credentials, tokens, or private graph edges. Use privacy-safe aggregates and suppress small cells where applicable.

Keep market, language, locale, product surface, identity, channel, platform, account, consent, incentives, payments, data access, abuse rules, content rules, and applicable review separate. Do not infer WeChat, WeCom, Mini Program, WhatsApp, SMS, email, contact, app-store, marketplace, ad, analytics, payment, or publishing availability from a market or language.

Avoid mechanics that pressure users to share, conceal commercial intent, preselect consent, impersonate customers, auto-message contacts, degrade a nonparticipant's experience, or trade recipient trust for gross activity.

## External-Action Boundary

The contract authorizes local analysis and design only. It does not authorize system access, contact or audience upload, invitations, messages, rewards, referral codes, content publication, product changes, ranking changes, instrumentation, pricing, spend, experiments, or deployment. Record separate permissions, owners, privacy and abuse review, approvals, monitoring, rollback, and audit logs before handing work to an operational system.
