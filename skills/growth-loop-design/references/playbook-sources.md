# Playbook Sources

## Pinned Handbook Basis

Use these Growth Playbook pages at commit [`b2ae914`](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/commit/b2ae914). Pinning keeps the conceptual basis reviewable as the Playbook evolves.

- [Referral & Expansion](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/handbook/referral-and-loops/README.md): participant value, referral, product loops, virality, network effects, collaboration, UGC, expansion, incentives, retained quality, incrementality, trust, abuse, and governance.
- [Growth Model](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/handbook/growth-model/README.md): relationship among lifecycle, funnels, loops, value, retention, monetization, constraints, and reinvestment.
- [Retention](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/handbook/retention/README.md): natural intervals, mature cohorts, recurring value, and retention-adjusted propagation.
- [Metrics](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/handbook/metrics/README.md): metric contracts, cohort compatibility, metric trees, guardrails, K-factor, virality, and decision use.
- [Funnel & Constraint](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/handbook/funnel-and-constraint/README.md): distinction among lifecycle maps, transition diagnostics, loops, growth models, and evidence-backed constraints.

These chapters define transferable questions and methods. They do not verify a private mechanism, threshold, causal effect, network effect, or result.

## Pinned Source Lenses

- [Brian Balfour](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/brian-balfour/README.md): loops as operating models, fit dependencies, content and launch loops, metric systems, and reinvestment. Use to test whether the complete system returns an output, not to copy a famous loop.
- [Andrew Chen](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/andrew-chen/README.md): retention-adjusted virality, cold start, marketplaces, network growth, channel saturation, and value-aligned sharing. Use to connect propagation to repeated use and recipient value.
- [Alex Schultz](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/alex-schultz/README.md): payload, frequency, conversion, retention, Product-Market Fit, and distinct viral mechanisms. Use to decompose exposure without treating frequency as unconditionally beneficial.
- [Casey Winters](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/casey-winters/README.md): business-specific growth models, marketplace supply, repeatable network units, liquidity, entry surfaces, and network decay. Use to avoid aggregate marketplace conclusions.
- [Sean Ellis](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/sean-ellis/README.md): value before referral, recipient quality, abuse, PMF, activation, retention, and scientific learning. Use to keep loop design downstream of delivered value.
- [Elena Verna](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/elena-verna/README.md): product-led acquisition, collaboration, word of mouth, content, brand, partnerships, and data. Use to compare loop families without assuming one PLG pattern transfers.
- [James Currier](https://github.com/krillinai/growth-playbook/blob/b2ae914/case-library/people/james-currier/README.md): network-effect families, white-hot centers, hard sides, market networks, local density, multi-homing, and AI-era networks. Use as a taxonomy and diagnostic lens, not a badge checklist.

People and company cases provide mechanisms, vocabulary, examples, and counterexamples. They do not establish a user's customer value, loop closure, benchmark, incrementality, defensibility, permission, or implementation result. Cite the local evidence that supports every user-specific claim.
