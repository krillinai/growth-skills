# Growth Loop Design

Determine whether a qualified output from one cycle creates a qualified input for a later cycle, where that mechanism weakens, and what evidence or design should come next. Do not call a funnel a loop because it is drawn as a circle, or treat gross sharing, revenue, content, or invitations as proof of retained growth.

Read [loop-contract.md](references/loop-contract.md) before mapping entities, value, evidence, identity, windows, economics, permissions, or a verdict. Read [analysis-methods.md](references/analysis-methods.md) before calculating transitions, yield, cycle time, retained quality, saturation, or economics. Read [diagnosis-and-design.md](references/diagnosis-and-design.md) before naming a constraint or proposing a mechanism. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Determine whether an existing mechanism closes and identify its most decision-relevant weak transition |
| `design` | Define a new loop hypothesis after customer or system value, retained value, and the next useful input are explicit |
| `measurement` | Specify entities, events, cohorts, identity, windows, quality, economics, and instrumentation needed to evaluate a loop |

Name one primary mode. When evidence supports only part of a mechanism, complete that useful work and mark the rest `unavailable` rather than inventing closure.

## Freeze The Loop Contract

Record the decision and owner; product, customer, business model, stage, market, locale, and decision window; source and receiving entities; eligible source population; current value and retained-value evidence; value-producing action; transferable output or reinvestable resource; next qualified input; recipient or system response; realized value; activation, retention, matured economics, and re-entry; events, identity, deduplication, attribution, cohorts, maturity and cycle windows; incentives, capacity, saturation, abuse, privacy, fairness, and platform dependencies; sources, counterevidence, limitations, and requested external action.

Do not choose mechanics before the value exchange and next input are explicit. Do not repeatedly request private evidence the user declines; return a measurement contract using the smallest privacy-safe evidence that could change the decision.

## Assign One Verdict

| Verdict | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| `verified closed` | Compatible attributable evidence observes output, next input, realized value, retained quality or matured economics, and subsequent participation across the declared cycle |
| `partially observed` | At least one output-to-next-input transition is observed, but closure, retained quality, economics, or another required transition is unavailable or incompatible |
| `hypothesis` | A plausible mechanism is proposed or reported, but compatible closure evidence is unavailable |
| `not a loop` | No qualified output returns as a qualified later input, or the request only describes a terminal funnel, campaign, activity, or diagram |

`verified closed` describes observed operational closure within the declared scope. It does not establish incrementality, causal lift, unlimited compounding, network effects, or durable economics without separate evidence.

## Map And Measure The Mechanism

Map the eligible source, participant, value-producing action, output, next input, response, realized value, retained quality or matured economics, and next-cycle participation. Preserve branches, repeated actions, overlapping loops, decay, re-entry delay, and external dependencies instead of forcing every mechanism into a referral sequence.

Calculate only from compatible attributable evidence with visible entities, numerators, denominators, cohorts, windows, maturity, identity, and sources. Measure participation, outputs per participant, qualified inputs per output, response, activation, retained quality, time to next input, cycle time, next-cycle participant yield, loop-sourced qualified outcomes, and relevant marginal economics. A zero denominator is `unavailable`.

Do not multiply averages from incompatible cohorts, require a K-factor above one, or invent a universal participation, conversion, cycle-time, saturation, payback, or compounding target. A loop can contribute useful growth without being self-sustaining; a coefficient above one does not prove customer value, retention, incrementality, or profit.

## Diagnose Before Designing

Separate an observed weak transition from candidate causes across value, eligibility, discoverability, motivation, effort, output quality, deliverability, response, recipient value, activation, retention, incentives, economics, capacity, abuse, measurement, and platform conditions. Name a primary constraint only when evidence distinguishes it and it limits the qualified next-cycle outcome. Otherwise rank alternatives and request the smallest discriminating evidence.

For new designs, begin with the customer or system value and intended next input. Then define the mechanism hypothesis, entities, action, output, value exchange, re-entry, prerequisites, quality, economics, failure and abuse risks, and a 30-day evidence or experiment plan with owner, maturity, decision rule, stop rule, and completion proof.

Route causal tests to `experiment-design`, transition analysis to `funnel-analysis`, first value to `activation`, recurring value to `retention`, economics and offers to `monetization`, mechanism research to `customer-research`, campaign requirements to `campaign-planning`, and broader constraint selection to `growth-diagnosis`.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
| --- | --- |
| Drawing a terminal funnel as a circle | Require a qualified output, later input, realized value, retention or matured economics, and re-entry |
| Maximizing invitations, content, supply, revenue, or data volume | Optimize qualified next-cycle value and preserve trust, quality, economics, and saturation |
| Multiplying the best rates from different cohorts | Use one compatible mature cohort or directly observed next-cycle yield |
| Treating attributed growth as incremental | Preserve attribution as an operating view and require a credible counterfactual for causality |
| Copying a famous feature or loop | Begin with this product's value, entities, next input, constraints, and evidence |

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat every market as a new loop evidence boundary. Revalidate participants, jobs, value exchange, eligible source population, action, output, next input, response, realized and retained value, re-entry, identity, channels, permissions, incentives, payments, abuse, capacity, saturation, economics, sources, and owners. Translation, currency conversion, or a familiar mechanism does not validate transfer.

For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, account, consent, incentive, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. When Simplified Chinese is requested, respond in native Simplified Chinese. Use current direct sources and local expertise; do not infer provider availability or make legal, tax, regulatory, causal, or performance conclusions. Keep the verdict `hypothesis` or `partially observed` until compatible local closure evidence supports another state.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, and loop boundary;
2. one loop verdict and the evidence required to change it;
3. entity, value, action, output, next-input, realized-value, and re-entry map;
4. evidence, definition, compatibility, data-quality, and limitation ledger;
5. transition, quality, cycle-time, economics, saturation, and risk measures;
6. observed weak transitions, candidate mechanisms, counterevidence, and unresolved alternatives;
7. the primary constraint or discriminating evidence plan;
8. a proposed design or 30-day validation plan with owners and decision rules;
9. handoffs, Playbook references, and the external-action boundary.

## External-Action Boundary

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not access product, analytics, CRM, warehouse, billing, payment, ad, publishing, messaging, or referral systems; upload contacts or audiences; send invitations or messages; issue rewards or referral codes; change product flows, ranking, pricing, tracking, or budgets; publish content; buy media; launch experiments; or claim those actions occurred.

If execution is requested, preserve the useful audit or design and specify the separate authorization, permissions, consent, privacy, fraud, approval, monitoring, rollback, and audit-log controls required for an operational workflow.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that the decision, entities, eligibility, value, action, output, next input, realized value, re-entry, events, cohorts, windows, maturity, identity, evidence, economics, quality, risks, owner, and guardrails are explicit; the verdict matches observed closure; funnels are not relabeled; attribution is separate from incrementality; incompatible cohorts are not multiplied; no benchmark, network effect, causality, or result was invented; and no external action occurred.
