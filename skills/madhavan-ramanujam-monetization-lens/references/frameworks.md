# Framework Reference

## Price-Before-Product Audit

Test monetization while defining product scope. Map the customer problem, current alternative, desired outcome, differentiated capability, willingness to pay, segment, package, metric, cost, and buying process. Use the evidence to add, remove, split, combine, or stop product concepts before sunk cost dominates.

## Willingness-to-Pay Evidence

Use multiple signals:

- current spend, budgets, alternatives, and switching behavior;
- forced tradeoffs, ranking, package choice, and price sensitivity;
- purchase, pilot, deposit, contract, or expansion behavior;
- qualitative explanation of value and constraints.

Control for sample eligibility, framing, hypothetical bias, procurement, and time. Report ranges and uncertainty rather than false precision.

## Value Segmentation

Segment by needs, value, behavior, use case, constraints, buying process, willingness to pay, and cost to serve. Test whether segments require different products, packages, metrics, sales motions, or service levels.

## Packaging Architecture

Define capabilities, usage allowances, service, support, controls, and proof for each offer. Ensure each package serves a coherent segment, exposes a meaningful upgrade path, and avoids artificial complexity. Good-better-best is optional.

## Price-Metric Design

Compare candidate units by:

- correlation with customer value;
- measurability and auditability;
- bill predictability and customer control;
- expansion potential and natural limits;
- cost and margin alignment;
- implementation, gaming, and dispute risk;
- incentives for beneficial or harmful behavior.

## Revenue-Model Selection

Evaluate subscription, usage, transaction, outcome, advertising, license, service, and hybrid models against value cadence, attribution, cost structure, buyer risk, capital needs, sales motion, accounting, and trust. Category convention is evidence, not a decision rule.

## Four Failure Modes

| Mode | Pattern | Evidence to seek |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Feature shock | Excess capability and complexity exceed willingness to pay | Low use, long implementation, discounting, confused choice |
| Minivation | Valuable opportunity is under-scoped, underpriced, or aimed too narrowly | Strong value, high willingness, constrained package or market |
| Hidden gem | High-value opportunity is not prioritized or commercialized | Repeated unmet demand, workaround spend, strong segment signal |
| Undead | Internal momentum sustains weak demand | Low urgency, weak purchase, poor retention, repeated subsidy |

## Freemium Guardrails

Define free user, free value, paid value, upgrade trigger, acquisition role, learning role, collaboration or ecosystem effect, serving cost, abuse, support, sales interaction, and stop rule. Measure incremental paid conversion and retained value, not free volume alone.

## Profitable-Growth Model

```text
retained contribution
= retained revenue
- acquisition
- serving and inference
- implementation and support
- incentives, refunds, fraud, and risk costs
```

Read conversion, expansion, contraction, churn, discounting, margin, payback, and trust by segment and cohort.

## Autonomy-Attribution Audit for AI

Assess how independently the AI acts and how clearly its work can be connected to an accepted customer outcome. Outcome pricing requires measurable outcomes, credible attribution, customer and vendor control, suitable timing, dispute rules, predictable cost, and buyer acceptance. When those conditions are weak, use subscription, usage, workflow units, or hybrids.

## Market-Share and Wallet-Share Map

Map new-customer acquisition, segment expansion, product adoption, cross-sell, usage expansion, price realization, and retained value. Identify whether the next growth constraint is market access or depth of value within current accounts. Protect retention and trust while expanding wallet share.
