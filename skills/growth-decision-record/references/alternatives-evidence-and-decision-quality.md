# Alternatives, Evidence, And Decision Quality

## Establish The Current-State Baseline

Record current behavior, customer and business state, resources, commitments, obligations, risks, organic or external change, and what happens if no new decision occurs. Do not define the baseline as zero or inevitable failure.

## Build Credible Alternatives

Include the preferred option, current state, and credible alternatives such as smaller scope, stage, reverse, preserve, defer, sequence, partner, build, buy, compose, manual path, pilot, pause, stop, salvage, or exit as applicable.

| Alternative field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Scope | Customer, product, market, channel, system, resource, period, and exclusions |
| Value | Customer problem, mechanism, expected outcome, retained value, and protections |
| Evidence | Supporting, contradictory, unavailable, maturity, causal status, and transfer limit |
| Economics | Full incremental cost, contribution, cash, working capital, capacity, and opportunity cost |
| Feasibility | Dependencies, capabilities, timing, approvals, operations, maintenance, and exit |
| Risk | Harm, trust, accessibility, privacy, security, reliability, partner, platform, financial, and residual risk |
| Decision | Select, preserve, defer, reject, reopen evidence, expiry, and rationale |

Do not create strawmen. Use compatible bases or keep options in separate views.

## Keep Evidence Layers Separate

Separate actual, reported signal, observation, estimate, benchmark, target, opportunity, forecast, scenario, plan, budget, attribution, association, causal estimate, recommendation, decision, approval, and result.

| Input | Minimum distinct record |
| --- | --- |
| Metric and actual | Entity, definition and version, population, numerator, denominator, unit, period, maturity, source, owner, observed value, and revision state |
| Baseline | Compatible metric, population, comparison period, maturity, source, owner, and external or organic change |
| Target | Metric version, baseline, horizon, target value or range, basis, owner, tradeoffs, guardrails, review, and expiry |
| Forecast | Method and vintage, as-of time, horizon, actuals cutoff, drivers, assumptions, range, calibration, owner, and revision |
| Scenario | Deliberately varied assumptions, purpose, range, sensitivity, and decision threshold; do not label it expected |
| Opportunity | Eligible population, plausible change, reach, retained value, overlap, economics, capacity, uncertainty, and evidence gate |
| Budget | Request, approved amount, available amount, period, funding source, restrictions, approver, authorization, and actual spend |
| Plan | Selected decision version, outcome, workstreams, dependencies, capacity, gates, owner, dates, and change rules |
| Recommendation and decision | Recommender and basis separately from accountable owner, selected alternative, rationale, conditions, date, and authority |

Never backsolve an actual from a target, a target from a forecast, a budget from an opportunity, a plan from a scenario, or a decision from a recommendation.

Leadership judgment is permitted when the accountable owner decides under uncertainty. Record evidence, assumptions, counterevidence, downside, authority, and why judgment is required. Do not transform authority or conviction into verified evidence.

## Bound Causal Claims

For a causal claim record intervention, population, baseline or counterfactual, assignment or evidence design, exposure, outcome, timing, maturity, supporting evidence, contradictory evidence, confounding, concurrent changes, interference, tested scope, and causal status.

Temporal order, rollback recovery, correlation, dashboard attribution, customer quote, popularity, repetition, analogy, or senior belief does not prove cause. Use causal language only from the owning analysis. Otherwise use descriptive, associated, contradicted, invalid, or unresolved language as supported.

## Use Calculations Without False Objectivity

Show formulas, units, sources, substituted values, ranges, rounding, assumptions, sensitivity, and limitations. Do not invent reach, impact, confidence, effort, probability, risk weights, discount rates, or decimal precision.

Keep customer value and harm, evidence, economics, cash, capacity, timing, dependency, reversibility, opportunity cost, privacy, security, accessibility, reliability, and authority separate. A score may organize supplied preferences but cannot average away a verified blocker or noncompensable harm.

## Record Risk And Tradeoffs

For each material tradeoff record affected population, benefit, harm or cost, evidence, uncertainty, duration, reversibility, owner, prevention, mitigation, monitoring, approval or risk-acceptance authority, stop, rollback, residual risk, review, and expiry.

Preserve dissent and unresolved objections. Record which tradeoff the decision owner accepts, under what authority and condition, rather than saying all tradeoffs are resolved.

## Evaluate Ex-Ante Decision Quality

Assess the decision against what was knowable at its cutoff:

1. bounded question and owner;
2. credible alternatives and current-state baseline;
3. attributable evidence, counterevidence, assumptions, and uncertainty;
4. customer value, economics, cash, capacity, risk, and opportunity cost;
5. authority, reversibility, conditions, stop, rollback, and expiry;
6. explicit rationale and what would change the decision.

Keep decision, evidence, execution, outcome, coordination, protection, and learning quality separate. A good result can follow a weak decision; a bad result can follow a sound bounded decision. Do not rank individuals from outcomes.
