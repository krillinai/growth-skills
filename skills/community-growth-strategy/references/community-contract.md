# Community Contract

Complete this contract before auditing, designing, comparing, migrating, monetizing, or measuring a community.

## Decision Header

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Mode | `audit`, `design`, or `portfolio` |
| Decision | The bounded community decision and choices this Skill cannot make |
| Owner | Accountable role, reviewers, decision date, and review cadence |
| Community unit | Named community, space, program, platform, segment, and boundary |
| Shared purpose | Participant job, promised value, product or customer connection, and exclusions |
| Participants | Person, account, team, organization, member, customer, host, moderator, partner, and payer units |
| Market | Country or region, language, locale, platform, channel, and relevant conditions |
| Time and capacity | Evidence cutoff, cohort and maturity windows, pilot horizon, staffing, response, and moderation limits |
| Action | Local analysis and separately authorized external actions |

## Evidence States

Use only `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. Keep a `reported signal` separate when a stakeholder, member, platform, sponsor, or vendor statement lacks inspectable support.

For every claim record source, community and platform, participant unit, role, program, market, version, event and processing time, cutoff, sample or denominator, limitation, contradiction, owner, privacy, and requested action. Missing private evidence does not make a community unhealthy.

## Community Test

A bounded community requires:

1. a shared purpose meaningful to participants;
2. a defined participant population and voluntary entry or legitimate membership basis;
3. a repeatable way to receive or create value;
4. interaction, contribution, or relationship rights beyond passive reach where appropriate;
5. norms, ownership, trust, and governance;
6. an operating model capable of keeping the promise.

An audience, subscriber list, event registration, support inbox, broadcast channel, product user base, or chat group may satisfy only part of this test. Keep each state distinct.

## Entity And Role Model

Map person, pseudonym, account, team, organization, workspace, customer, buyer, payer, beneficiary, and platform identity. Define joins, consent, confidence, merge and split, deletion, and re-entry.

Roles may include visitor, applicant, invitee, observer, member, participant, contributor, expert, host, moderator, volunteer, partner, sponsor, employee, customer, suspended, banned, deleted, and returned. For each role define eligibility, evidence, permissions, responsibilities, access, authority, conflicts, support, expiry, transition, and exit. One person may have several roles.

## Lifecycle Contract

Record eligible population, discovery, invitation, application, entry, consent, profile or context, orientation, matching, first community value, repeat value, contribution, recognition, product path, reduced activity, pause, exit, removal, suppression, deletion, re-entry, and return. Define natural frequency and opportunity; silence is not automatically churn.

## Value And Program Contract

For every participant role state job, promised value, contribution expected, value received, peer or host interaction, first-value event, repeat-value state, product or customer path, burden, risk, and healthy exit. For every program state eligibility, host, format, interaction, output, reuse, rights, moderation, capacity, cost, metric, review, and retirement.

## Platform And Data Contract

Record platform, account owner, terms and access, identity, history, permissions, notification, search, moderation, accessibility, integrations, data rights, export, retention, backup, fees, algorithms, regional access, policy risk, shutdown, migration, fallback, and member consent. Do not infer ownership or portability from access.

## Privacy Boundary

Prefer aggregate, redacted, or pseudonymous membership, interaction, value, and safety evidence. Record purpose, authority, consent, access, retention, correction, deletion, minimum cells, suppression, and reidentification risk. Exclude credentials, tax IDs, exact location, protected traits, health or financial disclosures, private messages, relationship graphs, and named value or risk profiles unless separately necessary, authorized, and governed.
