# Trust, Operations, And Governance

Community growth is constrained by trust, safety, labor, capacity, platform access, and accountable governance. These are part of the value system, not cleanup after scale.

## Rules And Moderation

Define visible rules, scope, examples, participant rights, reporting paths, evidence, triage, severity, response targets, emergency escalation, correction, and review. Separate content quality, spam, misinformation, harassment, threats, fraud, identity abuse, privacy, intellectual property, self-promotion, commercial conflict, and product-support issues.

Possible moderation states include reported, queued, assessed, no violation, warning, corrected, limited, removed, suspended, banned, appealed, restored, and archived. Match response to evidence, severity, recurrence, reversibility, and safety. Preserve appeal and correction where appropriate; protect targets, reporters, moderators, and bystanders.

Do not hide incidents to improve metrics, use activity as a safety proxy, give high-status members unchecked authority, or permanently punish people through opaque automation. Define where automated assistance may triage but cannot make final consequential decisions.

## Roles, Labor, And Capacity

For moderators, organizers, experts, ambassadors, translators, support contributors, and volunteers define:

- voluntary eligibility, recruitment, consent, scope, authority, and term;
- training, tools, documentation, supervision, escalation, and backup;
- expected time, scheduling, coverage, workload, exposure to harm, and wellbeing;
- recognition, compensation, expenses, tax or employment review owner, and conflicts;
- performance evidence, feedback, correction, appeal, revocation, handoff, and exit.

Do not use exposure as universal compensation, promise future benefit without evidence, hide labor from costs, or transfer company obligations to volunteers. Name accountable company owners for safety, privacy, customer support, payments, and incidents.

## Platform Dependency

Record platform owner, account ownership, terms, permissions, membership and identity model, content and data rights, APIs, bots, integrations, export, analytics, moderation, discovery, search, notifications, accessibility, localization, fees, policies, outage, ban, algorithm, rate, and deprecation risk.

Define backup, portability, consented owned-contact path, content archive, incident communication, continuity, migration, rollback, and exit. Do not assume access, data, identities, history, reach, or features are permanent or owned. Provider claims and documentation remain dated evidence, not guarantees.

## Privacy And Data

Use the minimum data needed for the decision. Prefer aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, consented, and small-cell-suppressed evidence. Define purpose, authority, consent, access, sharing, retention, deletion, correction, export, security owner, and incident response.

Do not request payment credentials, tax IDs, health or financial details, protected traits, exact locations, private messages, deleted content, minors' data, device graphs, personal dossiers, or moderator notes when bounded aggregates are enough. Do not reidentify participants or build personal influence, value, churn, or risk scores without a separately justified and governed purpose. Safety evidence cannot be repurposed automatically for growth, sales, sponsorship, or advertising.

## Commercial And Sponsor Boundaries

Keep participant, customer, buyer, payer, contributor, partner, and sponsor roles separate. Define what free and paid participants receive, what remains a safety or product obligation, sponsor visibility, editorial and moderation independence, data boundaries, disclosure, conflicts, and exit.

Do not sell member identities, prioritize safety by payment, convert every support interaction into an upsell, or hide sponsor influence. Measure trust, contribution supply, access, retention, service burden, and participant value alongside revenue. Route pricing, packaging, sponsorship offers, and economics to `monetization`.

## Market Transfer And China

Revalidate market, language, locale, platform, account ownership, product and app access, identity, membership, roles, content, moderation, consent, data access, storage, transfer, payment, sponsorship, events, notifications, integrations, exports, analytics, and operating ownership.

For mainland China, use current direct platform sources and locally accountable review for time-sensitive provider, data, advertising, payment, event, and regulatory conditions. WeChat, WeCom, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Bilibili, and other platforms have distinct products, permissions, content systems, data access, and operating models. Do not infer equivalence from translation or market label, and do not provide legal, tax, accounting, employment, or compliance conclusions.

## External Actions

Prepare local program specifications, rule drafts, role contracts, metric definitions, evidence plans, incident procedures, migration plans, and approval-ready handoffs. Do not access systems, export data, contact or enroll people, appoint roles, moderate, pay, publish, create programs, change configuration, launch, spend, deploy, or claim results without separate task-level authorization and required controls.
