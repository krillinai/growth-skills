# Measurement And Learning

Measure whether defined participants receive and contribute repeated value without unacceptable harm, burden, dependency, or economic loss.

## Metric Constellation

Use counts beside rates. Preserve entity, eligibility, event, denominator, identity, source, cohort, window, maturity, platform, market, and exclusions.

| Layer | Example measures | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reach and entry | Qualified arrivals, invitations, applications, registrations, accepted members | Audience and accounts are not unique people or value |
| First value | Eligible members reaching role-specific first value, time to value | Welcome, login, post, and attendance need explicit value meaning |
| Interaction quality | Demand, response, accepted answer, resolution, matching, useful connection | Message and reaction volume do not establish usefulness |
| Contribution | Eligible, submitted, accepted, reused, corrected, removed, and repeated contributions | Supply volume can hide quality and burden |
| Retention | Cohort return to repeated value, role progression, lapse, resurrection, exit | Activity frequency must match the natural value interval |
| Trust and safety | Reports, confirmed incidents, severity, time to response, recurrence, appeal, restoration | Low reports can mean safety or weak reporting access |
| Capacity and cost | Moderator, expert, organizer, support, tool, platform, event, and participant labor | Volunteer work and unresolved demand are not free |
| Product path | Product discovery, eligible entry, first and repeated product value, support, retention | Community and product value remain separate |
| Business value | Qualified demand, retained customer value, contribution, learning, risk reduction | Attribution is not incrementality |

Do not use one community-health score by default. If a score is required for a declared decision, expose dimensions, weights, missingness, evidence coverage, blocker rules, sensitivity, and conditions under which the decision changes. Do not penalize unavailable evidence.

## Cohorts And Maturity

Cohort by accepted membership, first community value, first contribution, role start, program exposure, product state, market, source, or platform only when that boundary answers the decision. Preserve the original eligible denominator and late or incomplete outcomes.

Compare cohorts at compatible ages and natural value opportunities. An annual professional community, event series, daily peer group, customer forum, and incident-response program require different windows. Do not call immature cohorts weak or short-term activity durable.

## Diagnostic Views

Separate:

- mix shift from within-cohort change;
- demand from supply and accepted quality;
- seekers from contributors and moderators;
- new, active, retained, lapsed, restored, suspended, and exited states;
- organic, invited, paid, partner, customer, employee, and migrated sources;
- community value from content reach, support resolution, product use, and revenue;
- platform reporting from canonical definitions;
- intended participation from spam, automation, duplication, and moderation actions.

Show distribution and concentration. Averages can hide unanswered long-tail topics, a few overloaded experts, local-language gaps, unsafe segments, or one dominant contributor.

## Causal Boundary

Participants often differ from nonparticipants before exposure. Check prior product use, tenure, segment, motivation, role, source, support need, account health, geography, and survivorship. A retention, spend, or activation difference can nominate a mechanism but does not establish causal impact.

Define assignment or eligibility, exposure, spillovers, interference, contamination, maturity, outcome, guardrails, and decision rules. Route randomized, quasi-experimental, or alternative causal design to `experiment-design`. Never multiply observational differences into incremental revenue or allocate budget from them alone.

## Portfolio Decisions

For each surface or program report role, eligible participants, value mechanism, quality, retention, product path, trust, safety, capacity, cost, dependency, evidence, and maturity. Use:

- `continue`: compatible evidence supports the current bounded role;
- `validate`: a consequential mechanism or value path remains unresolved;
- `cap`: value exists but capacity, risk, or dependency limits scale;
- `repair`: a defined defect is addressable with an owner and evidence gate;
- `consolidate`: overlapping programs can preserve value with less burden;
- `migrate`: platform or operating dependency requires a protected transition;
- `retire`: evidence supports closure with participant, data, and content protections.

Do not average a safety blocker away or rank incompatible communities by size.

## Learning Record

For every decision preserve hypothesis, mechanism, participant, program version, evidence cutoff, leading and mature outcomes, counterevidence, unexpected effects, decision, owner, action boundary, review date, and what future evidence will reopen it. Track false alerts, missing data, broken definitions, and learning reused in later programs.
