# Community Growth Strategy

Design a governed system where relevant participants repeatedly create or receive value around a shared purpose. Optimize recurring participant and customer value, useful relationships, trustworthy knowledge, and sustainable operations, not followers, group size, posts, reactions, messages, event registrations, or time spent alone.

Read [community-contract.md](references/community-contract.md) before accepting a community, member, role, interaction, platform, or outcome definition. Read [member-value-and-programming.md](references/member-value-and-programming.md) before designing cold start, onboarding, matching, programs, contributions, recognition, or product paths. Read [measurement-and-learning.md](references/measurement-and-learning.md) before calculating participation, retention, quality, economics, attribution, or incrementality. ## Freeze The Community Contract

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder, member, platform, sponsor, or vendor statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Keep public reach, registered membership, eligible members, active participation, contribution, recurring member value, product value, customer outcome, attributed value, and incremental value distinct.

## Distinguish Community From Audience And Channel

A follower list, newsletter, webinar, support inbox, broadcast channel, product population, or chat group can support a community but is not automatically one. Require a bounded participant group, shared purpose, repeatable value exchange, interaction or contribution rights, and governance. Define what participants can do for themselves, with peers, with hosts, and with the product.

Separate person, account, team, organization, customer, and platform identity. Allow multiple roles and role changes. Membership entry is not activation; presence is not participation; posting is not contribution; contribution is not product value; product value is not community causality.

## Design The Member Value Path

Map:

```text
relevant person and voluntary entry
-> understandable purpose, role, and norms
-> useful orientation, context, or matching
-> first community value
-> repeated value-bearing interaction or contribution
-> optional product, customer, relationship, or knowledge value
-> retained participation, healthy exit, or later return
```

Define the first useful outcome for every important participant role. Preserve observer value; not every healthy member must post. Use matching, expert access, peer exchange, reusable knowledge, practice, feedback, events, collaboration, support, recognition, or product education only when they solve the participant's job.

## Cold-Start With Value And Capacity

Start with the smallest participant set and program that can reliably deliver the promised interaction. Seed relevant people, questions, expertise, hosts, examples, and response capacity before broad acquisition. Use invitations only with relevance and permission. Do not buy lists, scrape restricted groups, force joins, hide sponsorship, trap members, or manufacture activity.

Set caps on members, programs, invitations, notifications, events, spend, host workload, moderator workload, and response promises. Expand only when first and recurring value, quality, safety, and operating capacity remain controlled.

## Build A Program Portfolio

Give every program one primary member job and outcome: orientation, question resolution, peer learning, expert access, practice, connection, contribution, product enablement, feedback, advocacy, or another explicit value. Define audience, eligibility, cadence, format, host, contributor, interaction, output, reuse, product path, moderation, accessibility, cost, metric, review, and retirement.

A company content calendar is not a complete community strategy. Route durable content pillars, editorial assets, reuse, and distribution to `content-strategy`. Preserve community-originated knowledge, contributor consent, attribution, rights, context, updates, and removal.

## Protect Trust And Sustainable Contribution

Define rules, reporting, triage, severity, evidence, privacy, action, escalation, appeal, correction, restoration, incident, communication, audit, and policy review. Safety outranks engagement. Do not tolerate harassment, hate, doxxing, scams, exploitation, threats, or harmful illegal content to preserve activity. Escalate imminent harm and child-safety concerns through accountable procedures; do not provide legal or clinical conclusions.

Do not replace accountable company functions with unbounded unpaid member labor. Define contributor and volunteer scope, authority, access, confidentiality, credit, compensation or explicit noncompensation, workload, support, breaks, succession, and exit. Status does not replace consent, decision rights, support, or fair terms.

## Measure Value, Quality, And Burden

Freeze entity, eligibility, denominator, event, identity, role, program, platform, market, cohort, window, maturity, source, version, cost, and evidence coverage. Track qualified entry, first community value, repeat value, value-bearing interaction, resolved need, useful contribution, reuse, healthy relationship, product path, recurring participation, safety, burden, complaints, exits, and costs as applicable.

Messages, reactions, daily active members, attendance, and time spent are operating signals, not universal outcomes. Report unanswered needs, low-quality activity, spam, exclusion, concentration, moderator load, contributor burnout, product harm, and trust. Community members often self-select; an outcome difference from nonmembers is not causal lift.

## Keep Adjacent Mechanisms Separate

An invite is not automatically a referral program, a conversation is not automatically a closed growth loop, and a larger group is not automatically a network effect. Route referral value and recipient paths to `referral-program-design`; loop closure to `growth-loop-design`; cross-participant value and defensibility to `network-effects-strategy`; rewards to `incentive-system-design`; product first value to `activation`; recurring product value to `retention`; and causal tests to `experiment-design`.

This Skill owns community purpose, member and role contracts, onboarding to community value, program portfolio, contribution and recognition, trust and moderation, operating capacity, platform portfolio, measurement, and community governance. It does not produce all content, send invitations, operate moderation, prove network effects, set product strategy, or approve budgets.

## Govern Platform And Commercial Dependency

Compare platforms by participant job, identity, access, history, interaction, moderation, search, knowledge reuse, accessibility, integration, data rights, export, fees, algorithms, regional availability, account control, policy, shutdown risk, migration, and fallback. Audience size alone is not platform fit. Preserve portable knowledge, consent, member continuity, ownership, and exit where possible.

Separate participant value, customer value, revenue, and contribution. Do not sell member data, hide sponsorship, prioritize paid answers without disclosure, revoke promised access silently, or exploit vulnerable members. Define commercial eligibility, conflicts, disclosure, price, data use, complaints, refunds, and exit before monetization.
