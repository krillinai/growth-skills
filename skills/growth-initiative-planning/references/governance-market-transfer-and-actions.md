# Governance, Market Transfer, And Actions

## Separate Planning From Authority

Use this sequence:

```text
analysis -> recommendation -> owner decision -> required approval -> bounded authorization -> execution -> verification -> mature result
```

Record who owns each state and what evidence changes it. A plan may describe approval-ready actions but cannot approve its own budget, staffing, customer exposure, price, data use, communication, publication, procurement, or deployment.

## Route Specialist Work

| Need | Route | Required handoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Company direction, portfolio, or exclusions | `growth-strategy` | decision, scope, alternatives, evidence, and expected choice |
| Current constraint and bounded response | `growth-diagnosis` | symptom, baseline, candidate mechanisms, evidence, and decision |
| Target contract | `growth-target-setting` | metric, baseline, forecast, opportunity, owner, horizon, and guardrails |
| Opportunity range | `growth-opportunity-sizing` | unit, baseline, levers, reach, value, cost, overlap, and uncertainty |
| Resource commitment and approval case | `growth-investment-case` | initiative unit, alternatives, benefits, costs, cash, capacity, risk, and gate |
| Organization, staffing, or portfolio ownership | `growth-organization-design` | outcome, decisions, work, capabilities, capacity, and interfaces |
| Metrics, tracking, and causal tests | `growth-metrics-design`, `tracking-plan`, `experiment-design` | decision, unit, definitions, evidence, implementation boundary, and maturity |
| Customer, product, lifecycle, channel, launch, pricing, or systems work | Relevant specialist Skill | input contract, owner, dependency, expected artifact, gate, and permission boundary |
| Recurring initiative review | `growth-operating-review` | plan version, decision log, mature metrics, gates, commitments, risks, and requested decisions |

A handoff is not execution. Keep the routed item `proposed`, `blocked`, or otherwise accurate until attributable output returns.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat each market as an independent evidence and operating boundary. Revalidate:

- customer roles, problem, alternatives, value, product, offer, motion, channel, and platform;
- eligibility, identity, metrics, baseline, maturity, evidence, target, and forecast;
- price, currency, payment, invoice, tax review, contribution, cash, and budget;
- language, locale, content, creative, support, accessibility, holidays, staffing, calendars, and lead times;
- legal entity, data, consent, privacy, security, fraud, advertising, distribution, partner, and approval paths;
- local owners, capacity, customer protections, gates, stop, rollback, and review.

Translation, a local domain, or currency conversion does not validate any of these. Preserve unavailable or contradictory local evidence and use current direct sources and accountable local expertise.

For China, explicitly distinguish market from Simplified Chinese language. Do not infer platform, app-store, provider, payment, invoice, data, consent, content, advertising, regulation, support, staffing, approval, or performance conditions. Avoid legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, or provider-availability conclusions.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence. Define purpose, lawful or accountable authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, small-cell suppression, and incident handling.

Do not place credentials, payment details, private messages, health or financial information, protected traits, precise locations, raw identities, employee reviews, or complete histories in a plan when bounded evidence suffices. Use accountable roles rather than unnecessary names.

Do not rank individuals for consequential targeting, pricing, service denial, employment, credit, insurance, health, housing, education, or similar decisions. Review fairness, exclusion, accessibility, customer, employee, trust, and misuse risks. Route sensitive or consequential use to accountable privacy, security, legal, risk, or ethics review without claiming a conclusion.

## Prepare External Actions Without Executing

For each proposed action record:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Action | Exact system, mutation, message, purchase, exposure, or deployment |
| Scope | Affected population, market, data, budget, duration, and reversibility |
| Accountability | Action owner, decision owner, required approver, and operator |
| Preconditions | Evidence gate, capacity, access, privacy, security, legal, finance, and customer-protection review |
| Controls | Dry run, least privilege, segregation of duties, monitoring, cap, stop, rollback, correction, and audit |
| Completion | Attributable execution and verification evidence, separate from outcome and impact |

Without separate task-level authorization, create local artifacts only. Do not log in, export, assign, approve, hire, change, launch, contact, publish, send, spend, procure, or deploy.

## Preserve Residual Obligations

Pause, cancellation, expiry, or completion does not erase customer support, refunds, communication, data deletion, access removal, monitoring, rollback, vendor, contractual, security, maintenance, documentation, or decommissioning obligations. Assign an owner, due date, evidence, escalation, and verification for each applicable obligation.
