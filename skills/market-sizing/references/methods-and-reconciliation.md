# Methods And Reconciliation

Use several independent compatible views when they reduce decision uncertainty. More sources do not help when they repeat one lineage or measure different markets.

## Top-Down

Start from an attributable population, category, spend, production, revenue, or transaction total and apply evidence-supported filters. For every filter show definition, numerator, denominator, period, geography, overlap, source, and uncertainty.

Top-down filters must match the same unit. Do not apply organization percentages to people, search share to purchases, or software share to a category that embeds services and internal labor without a bridge.

## Bottom-Up

Use:

```text
eligible demand units
x opportunities per unit in the period
x quantity per opportunity
x compatible economic value per quantity
= bounded demand or value estimate
```

Define natural opportunity, use, purchase, payment, repeat, retention, and value separately. Use distributions or segments when frequency and value vary materially. Do not use the highest observed frequency or price as universal.

## Supply And Capacity

Estimate current supply through providers, locations, labor, inventory, production, throughput, availability, utilization, fulfillment, quality, and constraints. Supply can reveal a current served market or bottleneck; it does not establish unmet demand or willingness to pay.

## Transactions And Value Pools

For transaction evidence define buyer, seller, order, item, payment, refund, period, coverage, cash, GMV, take rate, revenue, cost, and contribution. Avoid double counting both sides or gross and net value.

Value-pool methods estimate costs, savings, risk, time, or outcomes available to participants. They do not show what a provider can capture. Route price and value-capture design to `monetization`.

## Observed Demand Proxies

Searches, traffic, requests, inquiries, waitlists, pipeline, downloads, reviews, community activity, alternative use, and purchases have different entities and selection mechanisms. Map:

```text
source population
-> eligible signal
-> unique demand unit
-> qualified opportunity
-> purchase or use
-> retained customer value
```

Do not force missing transitions. Platform reach, query volume, and pipeline are not customer counts.

## Overlap And Deduplication

Classify relationships as disjoint, nested, overlapping, substitutable, complementary, sequential, multi-product, multi-market, multi-homing, or unknown. Deduplicate only with a declared key, hierarchy, time, geography, and confidence. Preserve duplicates and unresolved overlap rather than choosing an arbitrary subtraction.

For categories and value chains, show whether provider revenue, customer spend, internal labor, service fees, transaction value, and downstream value are additive, embedded, or alternative views.

## Reconciliation

Create a bridge for each method:

| Method | Starting universe | Filters or drivers | Estimate | Coverage | Overlap | Uncertainty | Residual |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Investigate disagreement through unit, category, time, geography, coverage, price, frequency, source dependence, or method assumptions. Do not average until estimates represent the same construct and an averaging rationale exists. A reconciled range can be more honest than a point.

## Marketplaces And Networks

Model each side, role, atomic geography or category, availability, demand, matching, fulfillment, quality, frequency, multi-homing, congestion, incentives, refunds, and economics. Distinguish participant population, active supply, active demand, successful transactions, GMV, platform revenue, and contribution. Route network-effect claims to `network-effects-strategy`.
