# Uncertainty And Scenarios

Market estimates inherit uncertainty from definitions, sources, coverage, overlap, frequency, value, serviceability, reachability, and attainable-share assumptions. Preserve these components rather than hiding them in a confidence score.

## Uncertainty Ledger

For every consequential input record:

- point, range, distribution, or unavailable state;
- source, method, sample, population, coverage, and date;
- measurement and sampling error where available;
- definition, classification, overlap, and source-lineage uncertainty;
- behavioral frequency, value, adoption, and timing uncertainty;
- serviceability, reachability, capacity, competition, and execution uncertainty;
- owner, sensitivity, expiry, update trigger, and alternative input.

Do not assign a probability distribution merely because a range is needed. Use bounded cases when calibration is absent.

## Ranges And Sensitivity

Return method-specific ranges and show how they are produced. Use one-way sensitivity to expose dominant drivers; multi-way or coherent scenario sensitivity when inputs interact; and break-even analysis when the decision has a threshold.

Do not combine the lowest values from unrelated definitions into a downside or every favorable value into the base. State correlations, shared sources, and mutually incompatible assumptions.

## Attainable Scenarios

An attainable scenario requires:

- product and segment fit;
- customer trigger, alternative, switching, and readiness;
- current serviceability and authorized reach;
- price, economics, retention, and customer value;
- competitive response and multi-homing;
- channel, sales, partner, support, fulfillment, and staffing capacity;
- capital, cash, time, sequence, market and regulatory constraints;
- evidence, uncertainty, trigger, counterevidence, owner, and expiry.

Do not use a universal 1%, 5%, or 10% share. Company share is not independent of the market denominator, product scope, value basis, geography, time, and accounting rules.

## Estimate, Scenario, Target, Plan, And Forecast

Keep distinct:

- `current market estimate`: bounded demand or value for a defined period;
- `scenario`: conditional market or captured-value result under assumptions;
- `target`: desired market or company outcome;
- `plan`: approved actions and resources;
- `forecast`: expected future outcome from a versioned forecasting process;
- `actual`: observed result under a compatible definition.

Do not invent scenario probabilities, average scenarios into an expected value without a basis, or change the market definition until a target is achieved. Route time-series and company forecasts to `growth-forecasting`.

## Historical And Forecast Sources

Record base year, forecast year, publication date, original publisher, method, category, geography, currency, prices, and revisions. A 2018 forecast for 2025 is not a 2025 actual. A later article repeating it is derivative evidence.

Compare historical forecasts with observed evidence where possible. Define category, price, product, regulation, technology, and macro structural breaks before extrapolating a CAGR. Preserve forecast vintages and expired assumptions.

## Calibration And Refresh

When repeated sizing estimates and later actual evidence exist, compare direction, magnitude, interval coverage, source bias, method bias, and decision effect. Actualization may use category spend, transactions, product adoption, served demand, or other compatible evidence; company revenue alone cannot validate total market size.

Define review cadence by volatility and decision consequence. Trigger refresh on source revision, category change, product access, price or currency change, new alternatives, regulation, capacity, channel access, structural demand shift, or material estimate error.
