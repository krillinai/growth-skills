# Xu Hongliang Growth Infrastructure Lens

Apply Xu Hongliang's public operating methods to scaled growth systems. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Operating System

Collect products, markets, user value, acquisition scale, LTV horizon, ROI rules, attribution, delivery automation, creative workflow, review layers, incentives, fraud patterns, portfolio traffic, localization, owners, audit controls, and decision. Mark unknowns; never invent internal ByteDance facts, metrics, or Xu's views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| Every product rebuilds the same growth capabilities | Shared growth platform |
| Short payback rules reject valuable cohorts | Multi-horizon value system |
| Media-buying decisions cannot scale consistently | Human-governed automation |
| Creative volume rises faster than quality and review | Creative-risk production system |
| Incentives raise activity but lower user quality | Segmented incentive system |
| Referral or reward programs attract fraud | Anti-abuse architecture |
| Portfolio traffic creates cannibalization or bad matches | Cross-product allocation |
| Domestic benchmarks fail in a new country | Localized growth system |
| Attribution certainty exceeds data quality | Measurement and calibration audit |

## Diagnose

1. Define durable customer and business value over the relevant horizon.
2. Map data, prediction, decision, execution, review, feedback, and audit layers.
3. Label evidence `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
4. Separate reusable platform capability from product- and market-specific judgment.
5. Identify one primary constraint and preserve alternatives.
6. Compare actions by incremental value, calibration, scale, latency, control, reversibility, fraud, brand, policy, and ecosystem risk.
7. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
8. Define an experiment or calibration plan with population, treatment, horizon, metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
9. State blind spots and an alternative lens.

Do not treat predicted LTV as truth, automation as removal of accountability, asset volume as learning, incentives as product value, K-factor as retained growth, or translation as internationalization.

## Deliver

Use: **scope and value horizon**, **lens applicability**, **infrastructure map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence and calibration**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **risk and audit guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Treat interview claims as participant accounts and keep ByteDance practices time- and context-bound.

## Required Boundary

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Xu Hongliang's public interviews. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Xu Hongliang.

Never write as Xu, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Optimizing one ROI window across products with different value timing.
- Automating decisions without overrides, audit, and drift monitoring.
- Centralizing tools while ignoring local product ownership.
- Scaling creative without permissions, review, and incident feedback.
- Measuring incentive participation without post-incentive retention.
- Transferring domestic channels, creative, and risk thresholds unchanged.
