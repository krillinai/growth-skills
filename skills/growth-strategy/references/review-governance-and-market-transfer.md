# Review, Governance, And Market Transfer

## Use Strategic Metrics

Use the smallest metric set that reveals whether the thesis works and whether it creates harm. Include:

- customer value and repeated-value outcomes;
- leading behavior tied to the selected mechanism;
- retained customer or account growth;
- revenue, contribution, cash, and reinvestment where applicable;
- capability readiness, capacity, reliability, and quality;
- customer, employee, partner, fairness, privacy, trust, regulatory, and concentration guardrails.

Every metric needs an entity, event or state, numerator, denominator, eligibility, identity, segment, market, window, cohort, maturity, source, owner, version, quality rule, and decision use. Targets are commitments or aspirations, not evidence. A metric movement is not automatically causal. Route contract design to `growth-metrics-design`, instrumentation to `tracking-plan`, causal tests to `experiment-design`, and cohort behavior to `cohort-analysis`.

## Govern The Strategy

Record:

- one accountable strategy owner and explicit approval roles;
- owners and decision rights for each bet, assumption, capability, metric, and external action;
- evidence and decision cutoffs, review dates, and expiry;
- changes since the prior version and the reason for each change;
- dependencies, unresolved disagreements, exceptions, and escalation paths;
- completion proof for learning, graduation, scale, pause, and retirement;
- where approved decisions are reflected in budgets, roadmaps, operating goals, and incentives.

A review is a decision forum, not a status recital. Compare actual evidence with the thesis and alternatives. Decide to retain, revise, accelerate, cap, preserve, pause, graduate, or retire. Do not rewrite old assumptions to fit outcomes or hide abandoned bets.

## Define Review Triggers

Use both scheduled and event-driven review. Triggers may include:

- Product-Market Fit, retention, quality, trust, or customer-harm deterioration;
- a segment, use case, or market becoming materially stronger or weaker;
- marginal acquisition, delivery, support, contribution, cash, or capacity crossing a declared boundary;
- a platform, regulation, competitor, technology, supply, partner, or capital change invalidating an assumption;
- an exploratory bet reaching graduation, stop, or expiry evidence;
- a missing capability becoming ready or a critical system becoming unreliable;
- unresolved evidence becoming available or metric definitions changing.

Distinguish temporary variance, measurement error, execution miss, and structural change. Use `refresh` mode when a durable choice or critical assumption may need revision. Use `growth-diagnosis` when the question is the current primary constraint or immediate 30-day response.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat every market transfer as a new evidence boundary. Preserve what may transfer as hypotheses, not validated facts. Re-establish:

- customer, user, beneficiary, buyer, approver, payer, job, alternatives, and willingness to pay;
- category language, positioning, proof, trust, accessibility, and service expectations;
- product, language, locale, device, integration, identity, payment, invoice, tax, refund, app-store, and support readiness;
- channel, platform, partner, media, sales, procurement, and distribution availability;
- data source, consent, storage, transfer, security, privacy, advertising, sector, and regulatory conditions;
- pricing, currency, purchasing power, costs, margin, contribution, cash, and capital requirements;
- local ownership, review, escalation, source freshness, and exit conditions.

Translation, currency conversion, a local domain, or team presence does not validate a strategy transfer. For China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, customer support, and local accountable review. Use current direct sources and locally accountable expertise. Do not provide legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, provider-availability, or performance conclusions.

Route market selection, readiness, pilot, and exit design to `market-entry-strategy`; market estimates to `market-sizing`; segments to `icp-segmentation`; local customer evidence to `customer-research`; and positioning to `positioning`.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence by default. Define purpose, lawful authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, small-cell suppression, and accountable review. Do not request credentials, private messages, health or financial details, protected traits, precise locations, raw identity, or complete histories when aggregate evidence is sufficient.

Do not rank or target individuals for consequential acquisition, pricing, employment, credit, insurance, health, housing, or similar decisions. Do not infer sensitive traits, join datasets beyond the declared purpose, reidentify people, exploit vulnerability, or hide material customer tradeoffs.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access analytics, CRM, advertising, billing, finance, product, cloud, support, sales, experiment, partner, HR, or data systems; export or join personal data; change budgets, prices, products, infrastructure, permissions, routing, contracts, or records; launch experiments; contact people; publish; spend; reorganize; deploy; or claim implementation or business results without separate task-level authorization and required controls.

For every proposed external action record owner, system, exact mutation, affected population, prerequisite evidence, approval, privacy and security review, budget, customer and operational guardrails, dry run, monitoring, stop, rollback, audit, and completion proof. A strategy recommendation is not authorization.
