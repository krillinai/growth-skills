# Growth Strategy Output Contract

## Required Order

Return:

1. mode, strategic decision, owner, horizon, scope, stage, target outcome, evidence cutoff, version, review date, and external-action boundary;
2. strategy contract, source ledger, evidence states, contradictions, uncertainty, and missing evidence;
3. durable strategic constraint or unresolved candidates, target change, and growth thesis;
4. arena, value, mechanism, advantage, economics, capability, and exclusion choices plus alternatives;
5. Four Fits coherence and lifecycle and system implications;
6. core, adjacent, exploratory, and system portfolio with capacity and opportunity cost;
7. sequencing, dependencies, capability plan, economics, risks, and decision gates;
8. conditional scenarios, sensitivities, triggers, robust commitments, contingencies, hedges, and options;
9. strategic metrics, governance, decision rights, review rules, and versioned decision record;
10. specialist handoffs, relevant pinned Growth Playbook sources, approval-ready external actions, and completion record.

## Choice Register

| Choice layer | Selected choice | Evidence | Alternative | Exclusion or sacrifice | Assumption | Failure signal | Reconsideration trigger | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Keep the choice stack coherent. Do not use goals, initiatives, KPIs, or organizational changes as substitutes for choices.

## Thesis And Four Fits Register

| Thesis link or Fit | Supporting evidence | Contradiction | Uncertainty | Required capability | Failure signal | Decision implication | Revalidation trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Include the customer and situation, external or internal change, value and proof, mechanism, leading behavior, retained outcome, critical assumptions, alternatives, and counterevidence. Include Market-Product, Product-Channel, Channel-Model, and Model-Market Fit without context-free scores.

## Portfolio Register

| Bet | Class | Thesis and outcome | Evidence state | Capacity and opportunity cost | Dependencies | Economics and guardrails | Commitment | Graduation / stop / fallback | Owner and review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use `core`, `adjacent`, `exploratory`, or `system`. Do not invent universal allocation ratios. Reconcile shared capacity and concentration across bets.

## Sequence And Capability Register

| Phase | Decision or milestone | Evidence required | Capability and dependency | Capacity | Owner and rights | Decision date | Scale / cap / stop / rollback |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Put evidence and prerequisites before dependent commitments. Do not turn the strategy into a detailed project-management plan.

## Scenario Register

| Condition | Assumptions | Evidence and uncertainty | Choice and portfolio effect | Economic and capacity effect | Trigger | Response | Owner and review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Do not invent probabilities or expected values. Mark each commitment as robust, contingent, hedge, option, or irreversible where useful.

## Metrics And Review Register

| Metric or trigger | Contract and source | Strategic assumption | Current evidence | Guardrail | Owner | Review rule | Decision changed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use a focused system. Keep targets, actuals, forecasts, scenarios, benchmarks, and causal estimates distinct.

## Handoff Register

| Workstream | Why specialist work is needed | Input contract | Skill or owner | Decision or artifact expected | Permission boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Do not duplicate specialist audits or imply that a handoff has been executed.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that the decision, horizon, owner, scope, stage, target outcome, evidence cutoff, constraint, thesis, customers, participants, jobs, alternatives, value, proof, mechanisms, Four Fits, choices, exclusions, portfolio classes, capacity, opportunity cost, sequence, dependencies, capabilities, economics, cash, risks, scenarios, sensitivities, triggers, metrics, guardrails, decision rights, reviews, version changes, market transfer, privacy, handoffs, pinned sources, and external actions are explicit; goals did not become strategy; targets did not become evidence; initiatives did not replace choices; channel volume did not replace retained value; a model or forecast did not make the decision; incompatible customers, markets, entities, or economics were not blended; scenario probabilities, causal claims, approvals, implementation, and results were not invented; and no external action occurred without separate authorization.
