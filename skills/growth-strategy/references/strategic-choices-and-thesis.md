# Strategic Choices And Thesis

## Build A Choice Stack

Express the strategy as connected choices, not an initiative inventory.

| Choice | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Arena | Customer, participant roles, job, buying situation, product, market, geography, and exclusions |
| Value | Problem, alternative, differentiated value, proof, first value, repeated value, and payer value |
| Mechanism | Acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, referral, expansion, network, content, sales, product, or reinvestment mechanism |
| Advantage | Capability, data, distribution, brand, relationship, cost, speed, ecosystem, network, or operating advantage and its transfer limits |
| Economic logic | Value unit, revenue, contribution, cash, reinvestment, capacity, and downside assumptions |
| Capability commitment | What must exist, be strengthened, bought, composed, partnered, governed, or retired |
| Exclusion | Customer, market, use case, product, channel, business model, feature, or commitment explicitly not pursued now |

Do not choose an arena from headline market size alone. Compare problem intensity, repeated value, strategic fit, serviceability, reachability, competition and alternatives, economics, capability readiness, trust, risk, learning speed, and opportunity cost. Use compatible evidence; do not reduce unlike dimensions to an unsupported composite score.

## Write The Growth Thesis

Use a testable chain:

```text
For [bounded customer and situation],
because [attributable change or unmet need],
we will deliver [value and proof]
through [growth and value mechanism],
which should change [leading customer behavior]
and then [retained customer and business outcome],
provided [critical assumptions and capabilities].
We will prefer this over [real alternatives]
and will reconsider when [observable trigger or counterevidence].
```

The chain is a hypothesis, not a causal fact. Name the weakest link and the evidence needed to distinguish the thesis from alternatives. Avoid strategy language that cannot be falsified, such as “delight users,” “be data driven,” “use AI,” “go viral,” or “grow efficiently” without a bounded mechanism and decision.

## Reconcile The Four Fits

Use the Growth Playbook's Four Fits as coherence checks:

- **Market-Product Fit:** the product and value path fit a bounded problem, customer, buyer, and market;
- **Product-Channel Fit:** the product's value and usage shape are compatible with how qualified customers can be reached;
- **Channel-Model Fit:** acquisition and delivery economics are compatible with revenue, contribution, cash, and reinvestment;
- **Model-Market Fit:** the market and attainable demand can support the business model and strategic outcome.

Do not assign context-free Four Fits scores. Record supporting evidence, contradiction, uncertainty, failure signal, decision implication, and revalidation trigger for each fit. Route detailed mechanism equations to `growth-model-design` and bounded Product-Market Fit evidence to `product-market-fit-assessment`.

## Compare Real Options

Keep at least one credible alternative and the current-state option. Compare them on:

- customer value and harm;
- evidence strength and uncertainty;
- strategic fit and differentiation;
- retained-value and economic logic;
- required capability and capacity;
- time to decision-changing learning;
- reversibility and option value;
- dependency, concentration, regulatory, platform, trust, and execution risk;
- opportunity cost and explicit sacrifice.

For each option state `select`, `preserve`, `defer`, or `reject`, with rationale and evidence. A rejected option remains visible so later teams do not unknowingly reopen it without new evidence.

## Connect Lifecycle And Systems

Trace the selected thesis through qualified acquisition, first value, repeated value, monetization, referral or expansion, and reinvestment where applicable. A channel result is not a company strategy; a lifecycle improvement is not durable if another stage or capacity becomes the constraint.

Specify which supporting systems must work: metric contracts, experiments or alternative evidence, identity and data, infrastructure, incentives, creative and delivery capacity, decision rights, and governance. Route detailed work to the relevant Skills instead of reproducing specialist methods.

## Define Exclusions Precisely

An exclusion must name what is not being pursued, why, for how long, what evidence could reopen it, and who may change the decision. Avoid vague “not now” lists that remain funded informally. Check that incentives, budgets, roadmaps, and team goals do not contradict the exclusion.
