# Strategy Contract

## Freeze The Decision

Record this contract before interpreting a plan or recommending a choice.

| Field | Required definition |
| --- | --- |
| Mode | `audit`, `design`, or `refresh` |
| Decision | The resource commitment, change, or choice this strategy must govern |
| Horizon | Usually 6–18 months, with explicit start, end, and evidence cutoff |
| Owner | One accountable decision owner plus contributing and approving roles |
| Scope | Company, product, business unit, market, segment, or portfolio and explicit exclusions |
| Stage | Current maturity and the evidence used to establish it |
| Target outcome | Customer and business outcome, entity, definition, window, and maturity |
| Current state | Versioned actuals and qualitative evidence, not targets or aspirations |
| Constraint | The durable condition that most limits the strategic outcome, or `unresolved` |
| External boundary | Local artifacts allowed; systems, people, spend, publication, and deployment excluded unless separately authorized |

Do not silently change entity, segment, product, market, currency, period, accounting basis, or metric definition across the strategy. A broad company strategy may contain bounded business-specific views; it must not average incompatible models into one fictitious customer or outcome.

## Evidence States

Use exactly these states for evidence-bearing claims:

- `verified`: attributable and inspectable within the declared source and cutoff;
- `inferred`: reasoned from named evidence with the inference and alternatives visible;
- `unavailable`: required but not supplied, accessible, mature, or compatible;
- `not applicable`: excluded by the frozen contract with a reason.

Use `reported signal` for an attributable statement that is not independently verified. Keep actual, estimate, forecast, scenario, target, plan, benchmark, causal estimate, and approval separate. Record source, date, owner, population or sample, entity, definition, segment, market, period, value, version, limitations, and contradictions where applicable.

Public artifacts can verify what they visibly state at a dated capture. They cannot verify private demand, behavior, capacity, unit economics, causal effects, organization readiness, or results. User enthusiasm, executive conviction, competitor action, and consultant opinion are inputs, not proof.

## Separate Strategic Layers

| Layer | Question | Owner or handoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Vision | What future does the organization seek to create? | Leadership context |
| Strategy | Where and for whom will the company grow, through what value and mechanisms, with which commitments and exclusions? | `growth-strategy` |
| Diagnosis | What is the primary current constraint and the smallest 30-day decision plan? | `growth-diagnosis` |
| Growth model | How do value, stocks, flows, transitions, loops, economics, and capacity interact? | `growth-model-design` |
| Portfolio | Which core, adjacent, exploratory, and system bets receive constrained capacity and in what sequence? | `growth-strategy` |
| Go-to-market system | How does one defined product-market unit connect readiness, offer, motion, routes, buying path, delivery, retained economics, pilot, and scale gates? | `go-to-market-strategy` |
| Operating plan | Who delivers which milestone, budget, or initiative and when? | Accountable operating owners |
| Specialist design | How should a bounded market, channel, lifecycle stage, price, system, or organization work? | Relevant specialist Skill |

A strategy is not a target, slogan, roadmap, KPI list, campaign, channel, pricing change, product feature, reorganization, hiring plan, or experiment backlog. Those may implement or test a strategy only when linked to explicit choices and assumptions.

## Establish Stage And Constraint

Describe the current stage with evidence about Product-Market Fit, customer and payer value, repeated value, distribution, economics, operating capability, and market context. Do not impose a universal stage label or maturity score.

Use a named diagnosis when current evidence supports one. Otherwise record candidate constraints and route primary selection to `growth-diagnosis`. A strategy may address a durable strategic constraint, but it must not convert a recent symptom into a long-horizon conclusion without evidence.

## Evidence Minimum

Before recommending an irreversible or high-cost commitment, require evidence for:

- bounded customers, participants, jobs, alternatives, and exclusions;
- delivered value, repeated value, and customer harm or trust risk;
- demand, reach, channel quality, and source-to-retained-value behavior;
- revenue, contribution, cash, capacity, and downside on compatible bases;
- product, data, delivery, security, support, legal or regulatory review, and organization readiness;
- counterevidence, structural uncertainties, and the next decision-changing evidence.

Missing evidence does not always block strategy. It changes the type of commitment: learn, preserve an option, run a reversible pilot, defer, or stop rather than scale or make an irreversible bet.
