# Growth Strategy

Turn a bounded growth ambition into a coherent set of evidence-bounded choices, sacrifices, portfolio commitments, and review rules. Strategy decides where and for whom to grow, through which value and mechanisms, with what economics and capabilities, in what sequence, and what not to pursue. It does not manufacture certainty, authorize execution, or replace diagnosis, modeling, forecasting, or specialist design.

Read [strategy-contract.md](references/strategy-contract.md) before accepting scope, evidence, stage, targets, or constraints. Read [strategic-choices-and-thesis.md](references/strategic-choices-and-thesis.md) before defining arenas, value, mechanisms, advantage, economics, capabilities, Four Fits, alternatives, or exclusions. Read [portfolio-sequencing-and-scenarios.md](references/portfolio-sequencing-and-scenarios.md) before allocating capacity, sequencing bets, evaluating economics, or using scenarios. Read [review-governance-and-market-transfer.md](references/review-governance-and-market-transfer.md) before defining metrics, governance, refresh triggers, privacy, external actions, or multi-market transfer. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) for the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Inspect an existing strategy, assumptions, evidence, choices, portfolio, coherence, governance, and execution state |
| `design` | Create an evidence-bounded strategy for a declared owner, decision, arena, and horizon |
| `refresh` | Re-evaluate a strategy after a material market, product, channel, economic, capability, regulatory, platform, or organization change |

Name one primary mode. A request about a current symptom, primary constraint, and 30-day action plan belongs to `growth-diagnosis`. A request for stocks, flows, mechanism topology, equations, coefficients, or scenario math belongs to `growth-model-design`. A request for monthly projections belongs to `growth-forecasting`. Use strategy only when a medium-term choice, commitment, exclusion, portfolio, or assumption must be governed.

## Freeze The Strategy Contract

Declare the decision, accountable owner, horizon, scope and exclusions, product and business model, customers and participant roles, stage, market and locale, target customer and business outcomes, current state, evidence cutoff, version, review date, source access, and external-action boundary.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing claims. An attributable statement may remain a `reported signal`. Keep actual, estimate, forecast, scenario, target, plan, benchmark, causal estimate, and approval separate. Preserve source lineage, definitions, entities, segments, periods, currencies, accounting bases, limitations, contradictions, and unknowns.

Do not invent customers, demand, behavior, coefficients, benchmarks, causal effects, economics, capacity, readiness, forecasts, confidence scores, approvals, or results. Do not average incompatible customers, participants, markets, metrics, business models, or value bases.

## Establish Stage And Strategic Constraint

Assess bounded evidence for customer value, repeated value, Product-Market Fit, distribution, lifecycle behavior, monetization, economics, capability, market conditions, and trust. Use a named current diagnosis when available. Otherwise keep candidate constraints visible and route primary selection to `growth-diagnosis`.

Separate a recent symptom or execution miss from a durable strategic constraint. State the change the strategy must produce, why a medium-term choice is required, what remains uncertain, and what evidence would change the decision.

## Build The Choice Stack

Define connected choices for:

1. **Arena:** bounded customers, jobs, buying situations, participants, product, market, and exclusions.
2. **Value:** alternatives, differentiated value, proof, first and repeated value, payer value, and customer protections.
3. **Mechanism:** how qualified demand, first value, repeated value, monetization, referral, expansion, network, sales, content, product, or reinvestment produces the intended outcome.
4. **Advantage:** capabilities or assets that can sustain the mechanism and their transfer limits.
5. **Economic logic:** compatible revenue, contribution, cash, capacity, reinvestment, and downside assumptions.
6. **Capability commitment:** what must be strengthened, built, bought, composed, partnered, governed, consolidated, or retired.
7. **Exclusion:** what will not be pursued, for how long, why, and what evidence may reopen it.

Write one testable growth thesis linking bounded customers and a named change to value, mechanism, leading behavior, retained outcome, critical assumptions, alternatives, and counterevidence. Reconcile Market-Product, Product-Channel, Channel-Model, and Model-Market Fit without context-free scores.

Keep at least one credible alternative and the current-state option. Mark each `select`, `preserve`, `defer`, or `reject`. Show evidence, opportunity cost, reversibility, downside, and the sacrifice created by the selected choice. Goals, slogans, channels, features, experiments, roadmaps, hiring, and reorganizations are not strategies by themselves.

Once the company-level arena and portfolio are selected, route the repeatable system for one product, offer, customer, buying situation, market, motion, and route to `go-to-market-strategy`. Growth Strategy owns the cross-arena direction and investment choices; Go-To-Market Strategy owns readiness, motion, buying path, bounded pilot, and scale gates inside a defined product-market unit.

## Construct The Portfolio

Classify each commitment as:

- `core`: protect or deepen validated value and the current engine;
- `adjacent`: transfer validated value or capability to a bounded neighbor;
- `exploratory`: resolve a strategic uncertainty or preserve an option;
- `system`: build shared data, delivery, infrastructure, governance, or organization capability.

For every bet record thesis, evidence state, owner and decision rights, capacity, opportunity cost, dependencies, economics, guardrails, commitment level, earliest decision-changing evidence, maturity, graduation, scale, cap, stop, fallback, rollback, expiry, and review. Do not apply a universal portfolio ratio or call every initiative critical.

Reserve capacity for core obligations, maintenance, reliability, trust, compliance, and change. Expose shared concentration and conflicts across channels, platforms, customers, positioning, pricing, data, teams, partners, and operating systems.

## Sequence Commitments

Put compatible definitions, safety, trust, prerequisites, and discriminating evidence before dependent scale. Prove customer and repeated value before distribution or monetization scale where the model requires them. Prefer a bounded segment or market, reversible pilot, and staged commitment before irreversible expansion.

Connect every phase to an owner, decision date, evidence gate, required capability, capacity, guardrail, and scale, cap, stop, or rollback rule. Do not turn the strategy into a feature roadmap or detailed project plan.

## Test Economics, Capacity, And Scenarios

Keep customer, account, seat, transaction, revenue, GMV, gross profit, contribution, cash, and valuation distinct. Keep average and marginal economics distinct. Include direct, delivery, support, sales, partner, incentive, refund, risk, infrastructure, and opportunity costs where relevant. Never backsolve assumptions to meet a target.

Build conditional scenarios only for uncertainties that change a decision. Define coherent assumptions, supporting and contradictory evidence, sensitivity, strategic effect, observable trigger, response, owner, and review date. Do not invent probabilities, average scenarios into an expected strategy, or call a scenario a forecast.

Identify robust commitments, contingencies, hedges, options, and irreversible commitments. Require stronger evidence and approval as cost, irreversibility, customer exposure, and downside rise.

## Govern And Refresh

Use a focused metric system spanning customer value, the selected growth mechanism, retained growth, economics, capabilities, capacity, and guardrails. Define every metric contract and route detailed design to `growth-metrics-design`. Targets do not become evidence and movement does not become causality.

Set scheduled reviews and event-driven triggers. At each review compare evidence with the thesis and alternatives, then decide to retain, revise, accelerate, cap, preserve, pause, graduate, or retire. Version the prior assumptions and decisions; do not rewrite history to fit outcomes.

Use `refresh` when a durable assumption or choice may have changed. Preserve unchanged choices with evidence, revise only affected layers, and update downstream portfolio, metrics, owners, exclusions, and review rules.

| Need | Route |
| --- | --- |
| Current constraint and 30-day plan | `growth-diagnosis` |
| Stocks, flows, mechanism equations, and scenario math | `growth-model-design` |
| Repeatable product-market unit, motion, routes, pilot, and scale gates | `go-to-market-strategy` |
| Market estimates and new-market readiness | `market-sizing`, `market-entry-strategy` |
| Customer evidence, segments, and value framing | `customer-research`, `icp-segmentation`, `positioning` |
| Product-Market Fit | `product-market-fit-assessment` |
| Acquisition, lifecycle, loops, and networks | `acquisition-strategy`, `activation`, `retention`, `customer-expansion-strategy`, `growth-loop-design`, `network-effects-strategy` |
| Monetization, pricing, and economics | `monetization`, `pricing-and-packaging-strategy`, `unit-economics-analysis`, `ltv-analysis` |

| Infrastructure and organization | `growth-infrastructure-assessment`, `growth-organization-design` |

A handoff defines the input, owner, decision, expected artifact, dependency, and permission boundary. It does not imply execution.

## Handle Market Transfer

Treat every market as a new evidence boundary. Revalidate customers, alternatives, value, product, channels, lifecycle, pricing, payments, invoices, economics, capacity, trust, data, regulation, and local ownership. Translation, currency conversion, or a local domain does not validate transfer.

For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not make legal, tax, regulatory, provider-availability, accounting, or performance conclusions.

## Deliver In Order

Follow [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md). Deliver the complete strategy contract and evidence record; constraint and thesis; choices, alternatives, and exclusions; Four Fits; portfolio and sequence; capabilities, economics, capacity, and scenarios; metrics and governance; handoffs; pinned sources; external actions; and completion record.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access authenticated company systems; export or join personal data; change budgets, prices, product, infrastructure, permissions, contracts, or records; launch experiments; contact people; publish; spend; reorganize; deploy; or claim implementation or business results without separate task-level authorization and controls.

## Completion Gate

Confirm the output passes the completion gate in [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md), all material claims have evidence states, strategy remains distinct from goals and adjacent workflows, every commitment has a choice and owner, every exclusion and scenario has a review rule, specialist execution is routed rather than invented, and no external action occurred.
