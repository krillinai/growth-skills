## Attribution Rules

- Prefer Chen's book, writing, and full talks over short clips or third-party summaries.
- Treat historical benchmarks as time- and category-bound evidence, not current universal targets.
- Describe Uber material as a partial operating perspective within a company-wide outcome.
- Distinguish Chen's own work from guest and coauthored essays.
- Do not copy long passages. Summarize the method and link the source.
- Do not imply endorsement, affiliation, private access, or current company facts.
- When first-party product evidence conflicts with the framework, preserve the evidence and lower confidence in the framework-based inference.
