# Market Profiles

Use market profiles to adapt meaning and decision context without reducing people to cultural generalizations.

## Separate The Fields

Record these independently:

| Field | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Market | Where is the offer being made and the audience decision occurring? |
| Locale | Which regional language and formatting convention applies? |
| Language | What language should the asset use? |
| Channel | Where will the audience encounter and act on the copy? |

Never infer one field from another. Simplified Chinese can serve audiences outside China. English can serve audiences inside a non-US market. A declared market does not prove that a particular platform, store, payment method, or distribution route is used.

Select channel treatment from the declared market and channel, not from language alone.

## Native Localization

Localize the decision, not the sentence structure:

1. Extract the source meaning: audience situation, job, promise, proof, objection, desired action, and voice.
2. Remove source-language idioms, metaphors, wordplay, rhythm, and unsupported emotional intensity from the required meaning.
3. Rebuild the message in the target language's natural information order for the declared channel.
4. Check every factual claim against the same source ledger; translation cannot strengthen or broaden it.
5. Record unavoidable meaning changes, missing context, or alternatives in the market notes.

Do not preserve awkward source syntax for traceability. Use the claim ledger, not literal phrasing, to preserve truth.

## Simplified Chinese

Compose concise, natural Simplified Chinese from the intended meaning. Prefer concrete reader situations, direct product value, visible proof, and a clear action. Use Chinese punctuation and locally normal CTA phrasing. Retain necessary product names or technical terms only when that improves recognition or accuracy.

Avoid literal versions of English hero idioms, aggressive verbs, exaggerated identity labels, stacked adjectives, forced rhyme, and English-style abstract noun strings. Do not automatically introduce expressions such as `颠覆`, `引爆`, `秒杀`, `王者`, `神器`, `遥遥领先`, or `闭眼入`; use strong language only when the approved voice and evidence support its exact meaning.

Do not describe Chinese audiences as collectivist, obedient, status-driven, bargain-seeking, hard-working, risk-averse, patriotic, family-oriented, or any other generalized trait. Use supplied audience research or state the audience need without a cultural explanation.

## China

Treat China as a declared market context, not a synonym for Simplified Chinese.

- Use the user-declared channel. Do not switch to WeChat, Douyin, Xiaohongshu, Kuaishou, Baidu, an app store, or another platform merely because the copy is Chinese.
- Use current direct sources for platform availability, advertising requirements, sector restrictions, data handling, certifications, required disclosures, app distribution, payments, identity or login dependencies, and other time-sensitive rules.
- If a current source is absent, mark the exact point `unavailable`; do not rely on memory or infer availability, blocking, compliance, or effectiveness.
- Do not assume one Android store or distribution path. Request the actual store, landing path, or distribution owner when it affects the CTA.
- Preserve approved Chinese product names, entity names, prices, dates, units, and legal wording exactly. If none is supplied, flag the naming or term decision rather than inventing an approved form.
- Keep medical, financial, education, employment, safety, privacy, and other regulated or sensitive claims within the source and review boundaries in [copy-contract.md](copy-contract.md).

Public pages, help centers, product documentation, terms, and dated platform policies can support a useful first draft. Private CRM, ad, analytics, research, and account context remain optional unless the requested claim specifically depends on them.

## International Markets

Do not treat `international` as one audience. Record either the declared country or region, or the explicit multi-market boundary and exclusions. Keep locale, currency, date, units, tax treatment, availability, price, guarantee, and legal wording tied to approved market-specific sources.

For English copy, retain the declared locale when supplied, such as `en-US`, `en-GB`, `en-CA`, `en-AU`, `en-NZ`, or `en-ZA`. Adjust spelling and ordinary usage, but do not invent local proof, market leadership, availability, customer names, or legal suitability.

For a multi-market asset, use only claims and terms valid across the full declared boundary. Otherwise provide market variants or mark the incompatible term unavailable.

## Market Completion Check

Before delivery, confirm that the market and locale are explicit; channel came from the brief rather than language; source scope matches the market; time-sensitive claims use current direct sources; Chinese reads as native composition; no stereotype substitutes for audience research; prices and terms match the jurisdiction; distribution and platform assumptions remain visible; and market adaptation did not strengthen any claim.
