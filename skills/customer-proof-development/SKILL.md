# Customer Proof Development

Turn attributable, permissioned customer evidence into bounded proof that preserves who experienced what, under which conditions, over what period, and with what limitations. Optimize credibility, decision usefulness, and traceability, not dramatic narratives, logo count, largest metric, quote strength, asset volume, or conversion claims alone.

Read [proof-contract.md](references/proof-contract.md) before accepting a customer identity, relationship, source, quote, metric, outcome, permission, or usage right. Read [evidence-claims-and-metrics.md](references/evidence-claims-and-metrics.md) before verifying quotes, calculations, before-and-after evidence, prevalence, contribution, or causality. Read [story-formats-and-reuse.md](references/story-formats-and-reuse.md) before drafting narratives, anonymizing, adapting formats, or building a proof library. Read [consent-privacy-and-governance.md](references/consent-privacy-and-governance.md) before using private evidence, incentives, regulated claims, customer identity, approvals, or external actions. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Inspect proof accuracy, evidence, lineage, consent, claims, freshness, channel use, risks, and gaps |
| `develop` | Turn supplied and authorized evidence into a bounded case, testimonial, reference brief, proof unit, or derivative draft |
| `library` | Design or govern proof taxonomy, source lineage, permissions, versions, usage, refresh, withdrawal, archive, and retirement |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, customer and role, product and version, proof unit, audience, channel, market, language, locale, evidence cutoff, usage horizon, and external-action boundary. Public customer logos, review snippets, posts, partner pages, and testimonials can verify only what is visibly and attributably stated. They do not establish current customer status, implementation, adoption, outcomes, retention, revenue, ROI, endorsement scope, or permission for new uses.

## Freeze The Proof Contract

Record company, legal entity, brand, customer relationship, participant, speaker, role, title, account, user, buyer, payer, beneficiary, industry, market, language, locale, product, edition, plan, version, workflow, problem, prior alternative, selection, implementation, adoption, first and repeated value, outcome, metric, source, quote, paraphrase, translation, screenshot, logo, artifact, date, period, baseline, comparator, sample, calculation, currency, cost, limitation, concurrent change, counterevidence, incentive, consent, usage right, edit right, approval, channel, audience, market, duration, expiry, withdrawal, privacy, owner, and requested external action.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A customer, employee, seller, partner, stakeholder, or vendor statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Keep customer statement, source fact, observed association, bounded contribution, causal effect, prediction, guarantee, and derivative claim separate.

## Build Atomic Proof Units

Every proof unit contains one customer and role, one scoped claim or quote, one product and version, one situation and period, one source lineage, one evidence state, material conditions and limitations, and one permission boundary. Keep source evidence immutable; derivatives point back to it.

Do not infer use or results from a logo. Do not combine several customers into an undisclosed composite, invent a company or quote, convert one report into population prevalence, or promise that future customers will obtain the same outcome. A clearly labeled hypothetical demonstration is not customer proof.

## Preserve Quote And Meaning Integrity

Keep direct quotes verbatim. Record speaker, role, company, source, date, context, prompt, surrounding qualifiers, language, translation, incentive, and approval. A shortened quote must not change scope, certainty, subject, timing, condition, or sentiment. Mark paraphrases and translations explicitly.

Do not remove setup difficulty, continued manual work, mixed adoption, uncertainty, or negative outcomes to create a stronger quote. Never attribute agent-written wording to the customer. Provide proposed wording separately for customer review when authorized.

## Verify Metrics Before Publishing Them

For every quantitative claim define metric, decision use, customer entity, baseline, comparator, numerator, denominator, unit, period, sample, eligibility, source system, calculation, exclusions, product version, market, currency, cost, maturity, uncertainty, and customer and internal approvals. Preserve exact source values and derived calculations separately.

An ambiguous statement such as `we saved 70%` remains a reported signal until its basis is verified. Do not convert it into cost, speed, money, annual value, ROI, or another metric. Do not select the highest metric while hiding denominator, timing, cost, or later reversal.

## Keep Contribution And Causality Bounded

Before-and-after movement, customer belief, product adoption timing, or attributed outcomes do not by themselves prove causal lift. Preserve changes in customer mix, headcount, process, pricing, channels, market, seasonality, other tools, implementation, and measurement. Use `reported by the customer`, `associated with`, `used in`, `supported`, or another bounded construction only when source meaning supports it.

For causal claims require a credible counterfactual, assignment or identification, exposure, compatible outcome, maturity, assumptions, and guardrails, then route method and interpretation to `experiment-design`. Do not calculate incremental ROI from a simple before-and-after difference.

## Tell The Complete Decision-Relevant Story

Structure proof around the customer situation, prior alternative, decision criteria, product and implementation conditions, path to first and repeated value, evidence-supported outcomes, tradeoffs, costs, limitations, best-fit conditions, current status, and next step. Include material delays, failed workflows, uneven adoption, increased cost, reduced usage, or other counterevidence.

The story need not be negative, but it must not imply effortless, universal, permanent, or guaranteed success. Separate customer experience from company product claims and category conclusions.

## Acquire Rights Per Use

Research participation, private reference, quote use, logo use, screenshot use, metric use, case publication, sales use, ad use, press use, translation, localization, sublicensing, model training, and public reuse are different permissions. Define exact item, wording, edits, identity, channels, audiences, markets, languages, duration, effective date, expiry, withdrawal, correction, deletion, and approval owner.

Possession of CRM, calls, tickets, logs, contracts, invoices, emails, or product data is not publication permission. Use the minimum authorized evidence, protect confidential and security information, and preserve research, operations, customer reference, sales, marketing, press, and model purposes separately.

## Adapt Formats Through Lineage

Create atomic approved proof units before deriving a case page, short testimonial, sales slide, reference brief, ad, press quote, app listing, analyst response, social post, or localized asset. Every derivative records source units, exact edits, format, channel, audience, market, language, owner, version, approval, publication state, usage locations, expiry, withdrawal, and retirement.

Do not use one long interview unchanged across formats. Adapt length and structure without expanding the claim. Route final prose to `copywriting`, edits to `copy-editing`, content portfolio decisions to `content-strategy`, seller and buyer use to `sales-enablement`, and press use to `public-relations`.

## Govern The Proof Library

Version customer role and relationship, product, claim, quote, metric, logo, screenshot, price, contract, market, permission, and outcome. Track active, review due, restricted, expired, withdrawn, corrected, superseded, archived, and retired states. Prevent expired or withdrawn proof from new use while preserving the audit history and removal obligations.

Refresh proof when the product, customer relationship, role, result, metric, market, claim, permission, regulation, or channel changes. The highest number is not automatically the most relevant proof.

## External-Action Boundary

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not access CRM, sales, support, call, product, analytics, warehouse, billing, finance, contract, email, ad, website, CMS, design, reference, or customer systems; contact customers or employees; request or obtain signatures; create public identities; change claims, quotes, metrics, permissions, reference states, or source records; publish, send, upload, schedule, or deploy cases, testimonials, ads, press, sales, web, social, app, analyst, or localized assets; spend; or claim conversion, win-rate, revenue, or other results without separate task-level authorization and required controls.
