# Story, Formats, And Reuse

## Evidence-Led Story

Use this modular structure only where source proof supports it:

1. customer and role boundary;
2. situation, job, and prior alternative;
3. trigger and decision criteria;
4. selected product, version, and why;
5. implementation, support, effort, and timing;
6. first and repeated value path;
7. verified outcomes, metrics, and customer reports;
8. costs, tradeoffs, limitations, failed paths, and concurrent changes;
9. current status, best-fit and poor-fit conditions;
10. permissioned next step and proof metadata.

Do not force a transformation arc when evidence supports only a narrow quote or workflow example. Do not imply the customer endorses every company claim.

## Anonymization

Removing a name is not sufficient. Assess whether job title, city, company size, rare industry, contract date, workflow, revenue, quote, screenshot, event, or combined details can single out the customer. Remove, band, generalize, delay, crop, redact, aggregate, or suppress nonessential identifiers.

Preserve evidence meaning and obtain customer and privacy approval. Label the case `anonymized`, `pseudonymized`, or `identity withheld` only according to the actual treatment. Record residual reidentification risk, reviewers, audience, access, expiry, and withdrawal.

## Atomic Proof Formats

| Format | Core requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Exact testimonial | Verbatim approved wording, speaker, role, context, source, and usage scope |
| Metric proof | Reproducible contract, approved wording, period, denominator, conditions, and source |
| Workflow proof | Product and version, customer job, implementation state, observed use, and limitation |
| Reference brief | Approved private or public scope, contact boundary, audience, questions, owner, and expiry |
| Case study | Multi-unit evidence-led story with mixed outcomes and full approvals |
| Proof card | One bounded claim, evidence state, audience use, source lineage, and expiry |

## Derivative Adaptation

Before adaptation, freeze approved source units and prohibited expansions. For every derivative record format, audience, channel, market, language, product, claim, exact edit, visual crop, duration or space constraint, accessibility, disclosure, owner, approvers, source IDs, version, publication state, usage location, review, expiry, and withdrawal.

Shortening cannot make a claim broader. A homepage hero, six-second ad, press quote, sales slide, analyst response, app listing, and localized campaign have different context and risk. Produce a channel-ready draft only inside approved source and usage boundaries.

Route prose to `copywriting`, editing to `copy-editing`, content portfolio decisions to `content-strategy`, seller assets to `sales-enablement`, public-relations uses to `public-relations`, and images or video to the appropriate creation Skill.

## Proof Library

Use stable IDs for customer, source, proof unit, permission, derivative, and usage location. Track:

- customer, role, relationship, product, version, workflow, market, and period;
- quote, metric, outcome, artifact, evidence state, conditions, and counterevidence;
- permission scope, incentive, disclosure, approvers, effective date, expiry, and withdrawal;
- derivatives, edits, channels, markets, languages, owners, and publication states;
- review due, stale reason, correction, supersession, archive, removal, and retirement.

Library states: `draft`, `evidence review`, `customer review`, `internally approved`, `approved for named use`, `active`, `review due`, `restricted`, `expired`, `withdrawn`, `corrected`, `superseded`, `archived`, and `retired`. Do not use one status to hide permission or evidence differences.

## Refresh And Withdrawal

Refresh when customer relationship, speaker role, product, plan, workflow, metric, outcome, price, contract, market, channel, claim, consent, regulation, or source changes. A withdrawal plan maps every active derivative and usage location, blocks new use, removes or updates authorized surfaces, preserves required audit evidence, confirms completion, and records unresolved third-party copies.
