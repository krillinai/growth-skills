# Evidence, Claims, And Metrics

## Quote Integrity

For every direct quote preserve exact words, source, speaker, role, company, date, prompt, context, surrounding qualifiers, language, translation, incentive, and approval. Keep these states separate:

| State | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Exact quote | Verbatim text from an authorized source |
| Shortened quote | Exact words with omissions that do not change meaning; mark omissions when needed |
| Bounded paraphrase | Faithful restatement explicitly labeled as paraphrase |
| Translation | Source language, translator or method, material choices, and speaker approval |
| Proposed wording | Agent or company draft not attributable to the customer until approved |

Never improve certainty, scope, sentiment, timing, subject, outcome, or causality. Do not remove a material caveat. Preserve setup effort, manual work, limitations, and mixed sentiment.

## Metric Contract

For each quantitative claim record:

```text
metric and decision use
customer entity and eligible population
baseline and comparator
numerator, denominator, unit, and calculation
start, end, observation, maturity, and attribution windows
source system, query or artifact, cutoff, and owner
product, implementation, market, currency, price, and cost versions
exclusions, missingness, uncertainty, concurrent changes, and counterevidence
customer and internal approval wording
```

Keep source values and derived calculations separate. Reproduce arithmetic where inputs are supplied. If a customer supplies only a rounded or ambiguous statement, preserve it as such. Do not derive annual savings, ROI, percentage improvement, population impact, or money from an undefined percentage.

## Before-And-After Evidence

Record baseline and outcome periods, entities, measurement changes, seasonality, customer mix, headcount, process, product, implementation, price, channels, market, other tools, and concurrent initiatives. A before-and-after difference can describe movement. It does not by itself identify the product's causal effect.

Use bounded phrasing:

- `the customer reported...` when the source is customer testimony;
- `the supplied records show... during...` when inspectable records support the trend;
- `the product was used in...` for workflow participation;
- `the customer attributes part of...` only when that attribution is itself the verified statement;
- `caused`, `incremental`, or `would not have occurred` only with suitable counterfactual evidence.

Route causal design and readout to `experiment-design`.

## Prevalence And Prediction

One customer can establish one bounded experience, not a universal pattern. Multiple selected cases do not automatically estimate prevalence. Record recruitment, inclusion, exclusions, sample, denominator, nonresponse, negative cases, and selection or survivorship bias before using frequency language.

Do not claim that another customer will achieve the outcome. State best-fit and poor-fit conditions, implementation requirements, observed range, uncertainty, and evidence limits. Avoid `typical`, `average`, `most`, `guaranteed`, or `expected` without a compatible basis.

## Claim Review

For every derivative claim show source unit, proposed wording, evidence state, scope, qualifier, risk, customer approval, internal owner, channel, market, version, and expiry. A customer's statement does not independently substantiate medical, financial, legal, security, compliance, privacy, safety, or universal product claims.

Regulated or high-risk claims require accountable product, safety, legal, clinical, financial, security, privacy, and market review as applicable. This Skill does not provide those professional conclusions.

## Mixed Outcomes

Preserve outcome strength and material counterevidence together. Include implementation time, adoption variation, cost, failed workflows, support needs, reduced use, churn, reversals, complaints, accessibility, or trust issues where they change interpretation. A credible case can show tradeoffs without becoming an attack or a promise.
