# Consent, Privacy, And Governance

## Purpose And Permission

Separate research, service operations, support, product analytics, customer reference, sales, marketing, advertising, press, analyst, event, localization, archive, and model-training purposes. Evidence collected for one purpose does not automatically transfer to another.

For each proof item define subject, customer entity, exact content, identity treatment, edits, channels, audiences, markets, languages, duration, sublicensing, paid use, data use, effective date, expiry, renewal, correction, withdrawal, deletion, approvers, and record location.

Do not use silence, a past interview, a contract, a public logo, an employee's agreement, or company possession as blanket permission. Preserve customer and internal authority; a participant may not control the company logo, data, metric, or contract.

## Incentives And Editorial Involvement

Record payment, free service, credit, discount, renewal term, access, gift, travel, status, or another benefit; payer and recipient; amount or value; timing; conditions; customer relationship; and editorial involvement. Participation and approval must be voluntary and decline must not trigger hidden punishment.

Disclose material incentive, sponsorship, and company-drafted wording according to the channel, audience, market, and accountable review. Do not present paid or drafted proof as spontaneous and independent.

## Private Evidence

Before using calls, recordings, transcripts, CRM, sales, support, contracts, invoices, product logs, billing, finance, emails, security reviews, or customer data, define original purpose, authority, access, confidentiality, customer and employee privacy, minimization, redaction, metric verification, usage rights, retention, and approvals.

Protect trade secrets, vulnerabilities, credentials, contract terms, personal information, private pricing, employee notes, and customer identities. Use approved excerpts or aggregates when they preserve the proof meaning.

## Privacy And Anonymization Review

Assess direct identifiers, quasi-identifiers, unique combinations, public linkage, internal linkage, screenshots, quotes, rare events, and small cells. Define audience and access because reidentification risk changes by context. Record residual risk and do not promise anonymity when only pseudonymization or name removal occurred.

Use purpose limitation, minimum fields, access control, encryption or security ownership, retention, correction, deletion, suppression, incident response, and audit. Do not build opaque customer value, influence, risk, or advocacy profiles from proof evidence.

## High-Risk Claims

Escalate claims about health, treatment, diagnosis, financial return, credit, employment, housing, education, children, safety, security, fraud, privacy, law, compliance, regulation, taxes, or guarantees to accountable specialists. Customer testimony does not waive substantiation, product truth, safety, fairness, or disclosure requirements.

Do not publish or recommend such claims merely with softened wording when the underlying claim is unsupported or unsafe. Preserve the source as restricted evidence where authorized.

## Approval And Change Control

Define evidence owner, customer relationship owner, metric owner, product owner, claim owner, privacy, legal or policy, brand, accessibility, localization, channel, and publication approvers as applicable. Record exact version, changes since customer review, unresolved comments, approval scope, effective date, expiry, and withdrawal.

Any material change to customer identity, quote, metric, claim, context, product, visual, channel, market, language, disclosure, or incentive reopens the relevant approval. Do not treat approval of a long case as approval of every shortened derivative.

## External Actions

Customer Proof Development may produce local evidence inventories, permission requests, source-traced drafts, anonymization plans, review packages, library schemas, and withdrawal plans. It must not access systems, contact customers, obtain signatures, change source records, publish assets, update claims or permissions, spend, deploy, or claim results without explicit task-level authorization and accountable controls.
