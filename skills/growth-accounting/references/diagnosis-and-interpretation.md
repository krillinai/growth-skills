# Diagnosis And Interpretation

A reconciled bridge describes movement. Diagnose possible mechanisms only after the entity, period, value, and evidence contracts pass.

## Diagnostic Sequence

1. verify opening and closing sources and residuals;
2. inspect absolute retained, new, resurrected, churned, expansion, and contraction movements;
3. calculate rates on fixed denominators;
4. split product, price, plan, cohort, segment, market, channel, and version where compatible;
5. decompose base-size, mix, concentration, operational, and adjustment effects;
6. preserve supporting, contradictory, and unavailable evidence;
7. rank alternatives only when the evidence distinguishes them;
8. define the smallest next evidence or test that changes the decision.

## Mix And Cohorts

Aggregate movement can improve while every mature cohort worsens, or worsen while within-cohort behavior improves. Compare cohorts at compatible age and opportunity. Separate entry-volume mix, product or price mix, market mix, customer-size mix, and within-cohort rates.

Route detailed cohort construction, survival, and maturity matrices to `cohort-analysis`. Do not use the current portfolio composition as proof that a prior intervention improved retention.

## Concentration And Distribution

Show movement counts and values, medians or distributions where valid, top-account concentration, long-tail contribution, and small-cell limits. One enterprise expansion can raise net revenue retention while most customers contract. One high-volume low-quality channel can raise new logos while retained value falls.

Do not call concentration good or bad without the decision, dependency, customer value, economics, capacity, and downside context.

## Source, Attribution, And Causality

Movement source labels describe joined evidence, not causal credit. Preserve unknown, direct, multi-touch, offline, partner, and migrated source states. Compare source cohorts on compatible retained outcomes before using quality language.

Route attribution model design and reconciliation to `attribution-analysis`, allocation to `acquisition-strategy`, and causal effects to `experiment-design`. A bridge cannot prove that a release caused expansion, a campaign prevented churn, or a touchpoint created a customer.

## Adjustment Effects

Separate reported and constant currency; organic and acquired populations; customer movement and product migration; behavioral change and pricing; operational movement and accounting timing; real activity and data correction. Keep each adjustment attributable and versioned.

## Benchmarks And Decisions

Use a benchmark only with attributable source, date, entity, business model, stage, segment, market, period, value basis, maturity, and transfer limits. Targets, plans, forecasts, and benchmarks are not actual movement. Do not invent universal NRR, GRR, churn, resurrection, quick-ratio, or growth-quality thresholds.

Return findings as observed decomposition, bounded interpretation, alternative explanation, missing evidence, and decision implication. Never use one composite score to hide the underlying bridge.
