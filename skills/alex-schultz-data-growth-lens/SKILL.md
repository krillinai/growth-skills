# Alex Schultz Data Growth Lens

Apply Alex Schultz's public methods to data-driven growth diagnosis. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Evidence

Collect product, natural usage interval, cohort retention, new/churned/resurrected users, candidate value behavior, activation curve, North Star, instrumentation gaps, segments, messaging events, channels, markets, viral mechanism, and decision. Mark unknowns; never invent metrics or Schultz's views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| Acquisition rises but cohorts decay toward zero | Retention foundation |
| Net growth hides conflicting user movements | Growth accounting |
| The team cannot define first experienced value | Magic Moment discovery |
| An activation number is copied as a universal threshold | Behavior-curve validation |
| Teams optimize competing top-level outcomes | One company goal |
| Diagnosis stalls because critical events were not logged | Data-debt audit |
| Notifications work for power users but not occasional users | Marginal-user messaging |
| Creative optimization begins before channel fit | Acquisition sequence |
| A retained product cannot reach a market | Structural barrier removal |
| “Virality” is discussed without a mechanism | Payload-frequency-conversion map |

## Diagnose

1. Define retained user value and the decision scope.
2. Decompose the system using the selected framework.
3. Label evidence `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
4. Separate correlation from causation and operating thresholds from natural cliffs.
5. Select one primary constraint and preserve alternatives.
6. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
7. Define an experiment or evidence plan with segment, intervention, primary metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
8. State blind spots and an alternative lens.

Do not scale a leaking product, treat net growth as a diagnosis, copy “ten friends in 14 days,” optimize a North Star without guardrails, or call a sharing feature viral without measuring transmission and recipient conversion.

## Deliver

Use: **scope and retained value**, **lens applicability**, **accounting or behavior map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **metrics and guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Keep Facebook examples historical and distinguish illustrative thresholds from universal rules.

## Required Boundary

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Alex Schultz's public work. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Alex Schultz.

Never write as Schultz, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Using monthly activity for a product with a different natural cadence.
- Treating a flattened aggregate curve as proof every segment retains.
- Selecting a Magic Moment from correlation alone.
- Instrumenting only successful paths and omitting failure events.
- Designing communication around the team's own power-user behavior.
- Translating a product before validating retained value and local constraints.
