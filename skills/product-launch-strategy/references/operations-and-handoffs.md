# Launch Operations And Handoffs

## Operating Map

Assign accountable owners for product, engineering, design, data, security, privacy, accessibility, legal or policy, finance, billing, infrastructure, support, success, implementation, moderation, sales, partnerships, marketing, PR, community, documentation, localization, incident response, rollback, and the final audience-expansion decision where applicable.

For each handoff record input, output, source of truth, owner, receiver, service expectation, capacity, exception, escalation, approval, completion proof, and review cadence. Shared ownership without one accountable decision owner is unresolved.

## Capacity And Promise Alignment

Before expanding access, reconcile demand with infrastructure, model or vendor, onboarding, implementation, support, success, moderation, sales, partner, billing, refund, documentation, and localization capacity. Record queue, operating hours, latency, incident threshold, variable cost, fallback, and customer-help path.

Every external promise must match the product version, access state, limitations, availability, price, support, and market actually offered. A launch cannot repair a promise-value gap with more distribution.

## Distribution Handoffs

The launch contract may define required assets and sequencing, but specialist Skills own their work:

- `positioning`: audience context, alternatives, value, category, proof, and message architecture;
- `campaign-planning`: coordinated channel, journey, asset, budget, and operating plan;
- `public-relations`: public relevance, media materials, issue response, and earned-versus-paid boundaries;
- `copywriting`, `copy-editing`, `marketing-image`, and `ad-creative`: production and review of messages and assets;
- `sales-enablement` and `partnership-marketing`: seller, buyer, partner, and ecosystem paths;
- `lifecycle-marketing`: permission-aware customer communications.

A handoff does not authorize publishing, sending, buying, or account access.

## Incident, Pause, And Rollback

Define detection, severity, owner, affected release and audience, customer impact, access containment, product fallback, data and evidence preservation, support response, communication owner, specialist escalation, rollback, recovery test, restart criteria, postmortem, and retirement.

Do not announce containment, cause, scope, resolution, or safety without approved attributable evidence. Route public response to `public-relations` and system remediation to accountable operators.

## Market And Localization Boundary

Route market attractiveness, beachhead choice, entry mode, local product and commercial adaptation, legal entity, data, payment, tax, operations, and exit to `market-entry-strategy`. Once entry conditions are supported, maintain a local launch contract for audience, access, value, product version, readiness, distribution, measurement, capacity, and rollback.

Translation does not establish product fit, channel access, payment, contract, identity, support, content acceptance, or metric transfer.

## AI Release Controls

Version model, provider, prompt, tools, retrieval, policy, data, product surface, pricing, cost, latency, quality, safety, evaluation, fallback, and disclosure. Measure accepted task or workflow outcomes and repeated value, not prompt, token, generated-output, or demo volume.

Use staged access, observable guardrails, human override, fallback, rollback, drift review, and cost controls. Speed changes the cadence of decisions, not the need for coherent versions and customer evidence.

## Post-Launch Learning

Review value, quality, support, economics, risks, distribution, decision accuracy, rejected hypotheses, and transfer limits. Update the release contract, product backlog, readiness gates, audience ladder, claims, documentation, and future portfolio only from attributable evidence. Retire stale assets, flags, waitlists, cohorts, dashboards, and temporary processes with owners.
