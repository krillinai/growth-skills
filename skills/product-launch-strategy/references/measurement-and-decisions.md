## Outcome Layers

Keep separate:

- attention: impressions, mentions, views, visits, event attendance, or social response;
- demand signals: waitlist, request, signup, demo, reservation, or qualified intent;
- access: offered, accepted, assigned, enabled, expired, rejected, and unavailable;
- exposure: rendered, viewable, opened, entered, or completed prerequisites;
- customer value: first successful outcome, repeated value, retention, quality, and trust;
- operations: errors, latency, incidents, support, success, moderation, implementation, and partner load;
- business: price, revenue, refunds, contribution, cash, payback, capacity, and concentration;
- causal: outcome caused by the release or distribution intervention under a credible counterfactual.

Product-Market Fit requires a separately bounded and mature fit assessment. Route it to `product-market-fit-assessment`.

## Cohort And Version Rules

Freeze customer entity, eligibility, audience stage, assignment, access, exposure, first-value event, repeat interval, cutoff, maturity, product, model, prompt, tool, policy, package, price, contract, market, and source. Preserve declined, failed, unsupported, rolled-back, churned, refunded, and unavailable outcomes.

Fast-moving AI releases still need versioned evidence. Shorten feedback loops by narrowing the decision and improving observability, not by mixing versions or treating immature proxies as durable value.

## Causal Boundary

Launch participants often self-select and differ by intent, tenure, role, product fit, channel, support, novelty, and timing. Concurrent PR, campaigns, discounts, sales, partners, product changes, and seasonality confound before-after results.

Define the estimand, eligible population, assignment unit, exposure, control, product version, maturity, interference, and guardrails. Accounts, teams, networks, shared quotas, invitations, and collaborative workflows violate independent-user assumptions. Route randomization, power, SRM, statistical analysis, and readout to `experiment-design`.

## Decision Record

For every decision capture release and audience stage, evidence horizon, observed value, guardrails, alternatives, counterevidence, confidence, capacity, economics, owner, approver, advance, hold, repair, pause, rollback, retirement, next evidence, and completion proof.

Do not decide from a composite launch score by default. If scoring is explicitly required, score only verified applicable checks, publish weights and deductions, and show evidence coverage separately.

## Portfolio Method

Compare customer problem, audience, value path, evidence, strategic role, readiness, dependencies, capacity, time to first and repeated value, distribution, economics, risk, reversibility, opportunity cost, maintenance, and learning reuse. Keep product, feature, migration, market, commercial, and communication-only units separate.

Return scenarios and gates. Do not schedule, fund, staff, publish, or launch from the portfolio output.
