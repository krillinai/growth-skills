# Product Launch Contract

## Decision And Release Unit

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, review date, horizon, deadline, capacity, and allowed action;
- release type, product, feature, workflow, API, platform, model, prompt, tool, migration, version, dependency, fallback, rollback, and retirement;
- customer, account, workspace, user, role, administrator, buyer, payer, partner, employee, support, and affected nonparticipant entities;
- original job and value, new problem and job, alternatives, prerequisites, limitations, unsupported state, first value, repeated value, and natural interval;
- initial audience, eligibility, exclusions, source, intent, waitlist, permission, suppression, market, language, locale, device, and platform;
- access, assignment, enablement, render, viewability, exposure, acceptance, activation, first value, repeated value, retention, renewal, support, commercial, and harm states;
- product, experience, measurement, operations, distribution, risk, legal or policy, finance, support, success, sales, partner, and incident owners;
- source artifact, owner, date, evidence state, counterevidence, limitation, freshness, and requested external action.

Version the contract when product behavior, model, prompt, tool, access, audience, value, eligibility, event, migration, price, package, market, or readiness rule changes materially.

## Evidence States

Use:

- `verified`: directly supported by an attributable inspectable artifact;
- `inferred`: reasoned from named evidence with assumptions and alternatives visible;
- `unavailable`: required but absent, inaccessible, declined, stale, or incompatible;
- `not applicable`: outside the scoped release decision, with reason;
- `reported signal`: attributable statement without inspectable support; leave evidence state blank.

Public pages, release notes, announcements, store listings, social posts, help docs, and visible product behavior can verify dated external surfaces. They cannot verify private eligibility, assignment, feature flags, exposure, value, retention, support, revenue, readiness, or causal effects.

## State Registry

For every launch state define entity, eligibility, event or rule, source of truth, timestamp, identity, version, numerator, denominator, window, maturity, exclusions, reversal, owner, and reconciliation. Preserve no-access, rejected, expired, no-response, unsupported, ineligible, suppressed, failed, rolled-back, and unknown states.

Do not substitute:

- announcement or email for access;
- account enablement for user exposure;
- render for viewability or comprehension;
- signup, waitlist, demo, or purchase for product value;
- first value for repeated value or retention;
- attributed revenue for incremental contribution;
- launch attention for Product-Market Fit.

## Privacy Minimum

Prefer aggregate, redacted, or pseudonymous audience, eligibility, assignment, exposure, value, capacity, support, and risk evidence. Exclude raw contacts, private messages, payment credentials, tax IDs, exact locations, protected attributes, employee notes, support bodies, full account graphs, and unrelated histories unless separately authorized and essential.

Document purpose, authority or consent, minimization, joins, access, retention, deletion, aggregation, small-cell suppression, correction, and incident handling. This Skill does not determine legal compliance.
