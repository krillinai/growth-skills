# Product Launch Strategy

Turn a bounded product change into a staged customer-value and learning system. Optimize verified first and repeated value for the intended audience with safe operating expansion, not announcements, impressions, waitlists, signups, feature enablement, first-week revenue, or launch volume alone.

Read [launch-contract.md](references/launch-contract.md) before accepting release, audience, access, exposure, value, readiness, or outcome evidence. Read [readiness-and-audience-ladder.md](references/readiness-and-audience-ladder.md) before sequencing access or assigning readiness. Read [measurement-and-decisions.md](references/measurement-and-decisions.md) before calculating transitions, comparing cohorts, interpreting waitlists, experiments, economics, or Product-Market Fit claims. Read [operations-and-handoffs.md](references/operations-and-handoffs.md) before coordinating distribution, support, incidents, market adaptation, AI releases, or rollback. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Diagnose an existing or completed release, readiness claim, audience stage, launch result, or decision |
| `design` | Define one release unit, initial audience, access sequence, readiness gates, value path, operations, and measurement plan |
| `portfolio` | Compare release candidates, dependencies, capacity, risks, learning value, and sequencing scenarios |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, release unit and version, customer, initial audience, market, horizon, and external-action boundary. Public pages, announcements, listings, and posts can verify visible publication and claims; they cannot establish private eligibility, access, exposure, activation, retention, readiness, support, economics, incrementality, or Product-Market Fit.

## Freeze The Launch Contract

Record product, feature, workflow, API, platform, model, migration, pricing or packaging dependency, release and rollback versions; customer, account, workspace, user, administrator, buyer, payer, partner, employee, support, and nonparticipant entities; original and new customer job, problem, value, prerequisites, alternatives, limitations, unsupported states, first and repeated value; audience, eligibility, exclusions, waitlist, source, intent, permission, suppression, access, assignment, enablement, render, viewability, exposure, acceptance, activation, retention, commercial, support, risk, and decision states; market, language, locale, device, platform, channel, product, data, identity, time, cohort, maturity, cost, capacity, evidence, owners, approvals, and requested actions.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Do not penalize unavailable private evidence or reconstruct it from public attention.

## Separate Release Units

Do not combine changes that have different customers, value paths, access, dependencies, risk, migration, or rollback. Distinguish:

- a new product or standalone value system;
- a feature, workflow, module, integration, or API capability;
- an optional beta, early-access, limited-availability, or general-availability stage;
- a mandatory migration, deprecation, compatibility, or data transition;
- a product model, AI model, prompt, tool, policy, or cost-basis change;
- a commercial price, package, entitlement, or contract change;
- a market-entry or localization decision;
- an announcement, campaign, event, or media moment with no product-state change.

One program may coordinate several units, but every unit keeps its own eligibility, readiness, exposure, value, measurement, support, pause, rollback, and retirement contract. Route pricing to `monetization` and market selection to `market-entry-strategy`.

## Preserve The Full State Chain

```text
public attention or qualified demand signal
-> eligible audience and assigned access
-> enabled and actually exposed experience
-> accepted or entered path
-> first customer value
-> repeated value at the natural interval
-> retained customer and product outcome
-> mature contribution and next audience decision
```

An announcement is not access. A feature flag is not user exposure. A render is not comprehension. Signup, waitlist, open, click, demo, provision, or purchase is not product value. First value is not repeated value. Attributed activity is not causal lift. Revenue is not healthy economics. A launch spike is not Product-Market Fit.

## Assign One Readiness Decision

| Decision | Gate |
| --- | --- |
| `advance` | Current audience receives reliable value, critical guardrails and capacity operate, and the next audience adds a bounded learning decision |
| `hold` | Evidence is incomplete, immature, incompatible, or capacity is insufficient for the next irreversible step |
| `repair` | A verified product, experience, measurement, support, commercial, or operating dependency blocks expansion |
| `pause or roll back` | Verified customer, security, privacy, safety, reliability, financial, trust, or operating harm exceeds the declared boundary |
| `not assessable` | Release, audience, access, value, evidence, or decision scope is not coherent enough to judge |

Readiness applies to one release version, audience stage, market, and time. Marketing assets or executive approval alone do not make a release ready. Do not invent universal readiness scores or launch checklists.

## Expand Through An Audience Ladder

Start with the smallest audience for which the current product and value hypothesis were designed. Use internal, employee, design-partner, trusted-customer, waitlist, beta, early-access, segment, geography, existing-customer, partner, or broad stages only when their role is explicit and appropriate.

For every stage define customer and role, eligibility and exclusions, why this audience now, access mechanism, capacity, first and repeated value, qualitative evidence, guardrails, evidence horizon, advance, hold, repair, pause, rollback, expiry, communication, and owner. Removing a filter spends future audience attention and may contaminate learning; expand only when the next audience answers a declared question.

Existing customers require account, administrator, contract, permission, data, migration, parity, training, support, renewal, and rollback rules. A power user or account enablement does not establish company-wide authority or user exposure.

## Audit Readiness In Dependency Order

1. `customer and value`: problem, audience, prerequisites, original and new value, limitations, and exclusions;
2. `product`: quality, reliability, performance, accessibility, compatibility, data, security, privacy, safety, and failure states;
3. `experience`: access, identity, permissions, onboarding, documentation, migration, destination continuity, and recovery;
4. `measurement`: eligibility, assignment, exposure, first value, repeated value, cohorts, maturity, lineage, and guardrails;
5. `operations`: infrastructure, implementation, support, success, moderation, sales, partners, billing, finance, incidents, and rollback;
6. `distribution`: positioning, proof, content, demonstrations, campaign, PR, seller, partner, community, and channel readiness;
7. `decision`: owner, evidence horizon, advance, hold, repair, pause, rollback, retirement, and post-launch learning.

Trace a symptom to the first broken dependency. Do not solve product or operating readiness with more publicity, discounts, forced access, or a larger waitlist.

## Measure Compatible Cohorts

Keep communication, assignment, enablement, rendered entry, viewable exposure, interaction, first value, repeated value, retention, support, refund, contribution, and harm separate. Report counts beside rates and name every denominator. Freeze entity, eligibility, identity, version, market, source, cohort start, window, maturity, cutoff, and exclusions.

Launches often create novelty, selection, survivorship, spillovers, shared-account interference, concurrent campaigns, and changing product versions. Observed before-after movement can nominate a mechanism but does not establish causality. Route randomization, power, SRM, interference, analysis, and readout to `experiment-design`.

## Coordinate, Do Not Absorb, Specialist Work

Own the release contract, audience ladder, readiness gates, access and exposure states, value path, operating coordination, expansion decisions, and rollback. Route positioning to `positioning`; customer evidence to `customer-research` and `icp-segmentation`; campaigns to `campaign-planning`; public relations to `public-relations`; copy and images to `copywriting`, `copy-editing`, and `marketing-image`; first-value details to `activation`; recurring value to `retention`; pricing and packaging to `monetization`; expansion inside existing customers to `customer-expansion-strategy`; market entry to `market-entry-strategy`; seller assets to `sales-enablement`; partners to `partnership-marketing`; metrics and tracking to `growth-metrics-design` and `tracking-plan`; experiments to `experiment-design`; and system or organization gaps to `growth-infrastructure-assessment` and `growth-organization-design`.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, release unit and version, audience, market, horizon, and action boundary;
2. release, customer, value, access, exposure, evidence, limitation, and dependency contract;
3. readiness decision with the first blocking dependency;
4. audience and access ladder with evidence-gated transitions;
5. product, experience, measurement, operations, distribution, risk, and rollback plan;
6. state and cohort reconciliation from attention through repeated value and mature outcome;
7. advance, hold, repair, pause, rollback, retirement, and portfolio decisions with owners and proof;
8. specialist handoffs, Playbook sources, unresolved evidence, and post-launch learning cadence.

For China work, keep China, language, locale, legal entity, product version, audience, platform, account, identity, permission, data, hosting, transfer, payment, contract, invoice, tax, support, content review, advertising, app distribution, measurement, and applicable rules separate. Do not infer channel, platform, account, product, customer, commercial, data, or launch compatibility from China or Simplified Chinese.

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not access product, feature-flag, app-store, cloud, analytics, warehouse, CRM, billing, support, success, messaging, social, ad, PR, sales, partner, experiment, identity, or customer systems; export customer data; change access, product, model, prompt, tools, policies, pages, tracking, prices, packages, contracts, budgets, or customer state; contact anyone; publish; send; spend; launch; deploy; or claim implementation or results without separate task-level authorization and required controls.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that decision, release unit, version, customer, audience, eligibility, exclusions, access, assignment, exposure, first and repeated value, readiness, dependencies, capacity, product, experience, measurement, operations, distribution, risk, cohort, maturity, economics, owners, approvals, advance, hold, repair, pause, rollback, retirement, market context, evidence states, privacy, handoffs, and action boundaries are explicit; attention is not value; enabled is not exposed; first value is not retention; attribution is not causality; launch is not Product-Market Fit; unavailable is not failure; and no external action occurred.
