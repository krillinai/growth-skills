# Market Assessment And Sequencing

## Dependency Order

```text
customer problem and real alternative
-> product can deliver the core job locally
-> position, proof, trust, and offer make sense
-> a permitted route reaches qualified demand
-> first and recurring value can be delivered and supported
-> retained contribution and operating controls work
-> adjacency can be tested without breaking the beachhead
```

Do not use downstream execution to compensate for an upstream blocker.

## Seven-Gate Record

For each gate record question, eligible population, required evidence, observed evidence, control state, source, freshness, limitation, counterevidence, owner, dependency, next decision, and completion proof.

Use these states:

- `verified ready`: attributable current evidence supports the declared prerequisite;
- `bounded pilot only`: evidence supports a reversible limited test but not broader operation;
- `specified not verified`: a design or approval path exists without operating evidence;
- `verified blocker`: attributable evidence confirms a constraint incompatible with the declared plan;
- `unavailable`: decision-relevant evidence is missing or inaccessible;
- `not applicable`: the gate does not apply, with reason.

## Portfolio Comparison

Compare markets only under the same decision horizon and strategic objective. Useful dimensions include customer evidence, product transfer, distribution access, retained economics, operating capacity, time to evidence, strategic learning, dependency, downside, reversibility, and option value.

Expose weighting and sensitivity. Do not combine incompatible estimates into false precision. Preserve one strong blocker, a small high-fit segment, concentration, or a slow evidence horizon instead of averaging it away.

Choose a sequence that resolves reusable uncertainty. A smaller market may be first because it tests product localization, payments, partner delivery, or support with less downside. A larger TAM does not automatically have higher decision value.

## Beachhead Contract

Specify:

```text
customer, job, trigger, alternative, and exclusions
product scope and required local adaptation
position, proof, offer, price, and trust mechanism
route, channel or partner, and qualified entry path
first value, repeated value, support, and implementation
eligible exposure and operating capacity
events, cohorts, windows, economics, and guardrails
decision owner, pilot cap, stop, rollback, and next adjacency
```

## Pilot Gates

Define decisions before exposure:

| Horizon | Required question |
| --- | --- |
| Feasibility | Can the product, channel, payment, data, support, and operating path function safely? |
| Demand | Does the bounded customer recognize the problem, alternative, value, proof, and offer? |
| First value | Can qualified customers reach the promised local outcome? |
| Repeated value | Does value recur at the natural frequency without hidden service or trust cost? |
| Economics | Does mature marginal contribution justify the full local acquisition and operating cost? |
| Scale | Can capacity, dependency, saturation, quality, governance, and cash support the next unit? |

Positive early response does not waive later gates. Set harmful and inconclusive actions as clearly as success actions.

## Exit And Defer

Define customer communication, contract and refund handling, partner and vendor closure, data retention and deletion, account and asset disposition, support tail, finance reconciliation, owner, audit record, and learning capture. Do not abandon active customers or data because the strategy changes.
