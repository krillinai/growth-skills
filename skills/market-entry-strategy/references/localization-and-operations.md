# Localization And Market Operations

## Adaptation Layers

Keep these distinct:

| Layer | Scope |
| --- | --- |
| Translation | Accurate transfer of supplied meaning between languages |
| Transcreation | Native expression adapted to audience, channel, and purpose without exceeding claims |
| Product localization | Locale, content, format, input, output, accessibility, device, identity, integration, and support behavior |
| Market adaptation | Customer, alternatives, position, proof, offer, price, payment, route, channel, partner, and trust |
| Operating localization | Entity, contract, invoice, data, security, service, staffing, support, incident, finance, and governance |

Translating copy completes only the first layer.

## Product And Journey Review

Audit discovery, account creation, identity, eligibility, consent, onboarding, data import, core workflow, output, collaboration, transaction, payment, notification, support, cancellation, deletion, first value, and repeated value. Record every market-dependent dependency and invalid, unavailable, denied, failed, duplicate, refund, and recovery path.

Do not localize only the homepage while the product, help, transactional messages, payment, legal text, support, or cancellation remains unusable.

## Commercial Review

Separate list price, displayed price, package, discount, currency, exchange assumption, payment method, settlement, refund, tax and invoice review, contract, term, variable cost, support, partner share, acquisition cost, contribution, payback, cash timing, and forecast. A currency conversion is not local pricing evidence.

Route detailed pricing and economics to `monetization`. Use accountable finance, tax, and legal owners for current specialist conclusions.

## Distribution And Partner Review

For every channel or partner record audience, customer job, entry moment, account and permission, product and format fit, data access, attribution, cost, capacity, control, policy dependency, saturation, termination, customer ownership, support, and recovery.

Do not infer channel access from market popularity. A partner statement is not verified reach, commitment, delivery, economics, or customer value.

## Operating Handoff

Assign owners for product, localization, growth, sales or partner, customer success, support, data, security, finance, legal or policy, and incident response as applicable. Define source of truth, review cadence, service expectation, exception, approval, monitoring, rollback, and completion proof.

Shared systems may standardize taxonomy, evidence, measurement, access, and controls. Local teams need explicit authority where customer, channel, commercial, content, support, or operating context changes the answer.
