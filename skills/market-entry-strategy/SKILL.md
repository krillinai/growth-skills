# Market Entry Strategy

Turn a proposed market into a bounded evidence and operating decision. Optimize retained customer value and viable local economics, not country count, translated assets, theoretical TAM, launch publicity, channel reach, signups, or attributed revenue alone.

Read [market-entry-contract.md](references/market-entry-contract.md) before accepting demand, product, channel, commercial, operational, or market evidence. Read [assessment-and-sequencing.md](references/assessment-and-sequencing.md) before comparing markets, selecting a beachhead, or designing a pilot. Read [localization-and-operations.md](references/localization-and-operations.md) before adapting product, message, price, payment, support, data, or delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `assessment` | Determine whether one market is ready for validation, a bounded pilot, delay, or rejection |
| `plan` | Define a dependency-ordered beachhead, adaptation, route, pilot, measurement, and operating handoff |
| `portfolio` | Compare and sequence several markets under one explicit strategic and capacity boundary |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, market unit, product unit, customer unit, horizon, investment boundary, and allowed action.

## Freeze The Market Unit

Record country or region, language, locale, customer and buying unit, use case, product surface, device, distribution path, legal and contracting entity, payer and currency, route to market, channel, platform, partner, competitor and status quo, category, price and package, payment, tax and invoice assumptions, product availability, localization, identity, consent, data, hosting, transfer, trust, security, review, support, implementation, logistics, capacity, economics, evidence dates, owners, dependencies, and requested external action.

Split markets when customer problems, alternatives, product access, prices, channels, payments, rules, operating owners, or economics differ materially. A language is not a market; a country is not one customer segment; a platform audience is not automatically reachable demand.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Use current direct primary sources for time-sensitive platform, product, payment, data, or regulatory claims and record their exact supported scope. Do not make legal, tax, accounting, or compliance conclusions.

## Audit Seven Readiness Gates

Assess independently:

1. `customer demand`: problem, trigger, alternatives, urgency, reachable segment, willingness to change, and retained-value evidence;
2. `position and trust`: category, terminology, differentiated value, proof, objections, brand, reputation, and local trust mechanism;
3. `product readiness`: availability, core job, language and locale, content, accessibility, device, performance, integrations, identity, safety, and supportability;
4. `distribution`: discovery, acquisition, sales, partner, product-led, app or marketplace access, permissions, dependency, saturation, and measurement;
5. `commercial fit`: offer, packaging, price, currency, payment, refund, contract, tax and invoice review, contribution, payback, and cash timing;
6. `delivery and operations`: onboarding, implementation, service, support, moderation, fulfillment, capacity, ownership, incident, rollback, and exit;
7. `data and governance`: purpose, consent, access, storage, transfer, retention, security, attribution, experiment, audit, and accountable review.

Assign each gate one control state: `verified ready`, `bounded pilot only`, `specified not verified`, `verified blocker`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. Unavailable evidence is not a failed gate.

## Make One Market Decision

Return exactly one decision:

- `validate`: important customer, product, distribution, or economic uncertainty remains upstream of launch;
- `pilot`: prerequisites support a capped, reversible test with explicit owners and stop rules;
- `enter`: compatible pilot evidence supports a controlled operating rollout inside the declared boundary;
- `defer`: another dependency or market has higher decision value under current capacity;
- `do not enter`: verified constraints make the scoped opportunity unattractive or unacceptable;
- `exit`: operating evidence supports stopping or withdrawing with customer and data protections.

Do not average away a verified blocker. Do not rank markets by an opaque score. If scoring is requested, show dimensions, weights, missingness, sensitivity, and evidence coverage separately; do not penalize unavailable evidence.

## Design A Beachhead Before Scale

Choose the narrowest customer, job, product, route, and operating unit that can prove local value. Define the entry hypothesis, prerequisites, local adaptations, non-goals, channel or partner role, offer, product path, first and recurring value, economics, capacity, risks, and evidence plan.

Use phased gates:

```text
problem and alternative evidence
-> product and trust readiness
-> reachable demand and qualified entry
-> first and repeated customer value
-> retained contribution and operational stability
-> adjacent segment, channel, product, or region
```

Set exposure, spend, customer, partner, geography, support, and time caps. Define positive, negative, harmful, and inconclusive decisions before the pilot. Expansion requires local repeatability; success in one segment, city, platform, or partner does not validate the whole market.

## Localize The System

Preserve the underlying position only where local evidence supports it. Adapt terminology, claims, proof, offer, price display, payment, product path, content, service, support, channel, partner, and measurement as needed. Distinguish translation, transcreation, product localization, market adaptation, and operating localization.

Route positioning to `positioning`, segments to `icp-segmentation`, customer evidence to `customer-research`, channels to `acquisition-strategy`, campaigns to `campaign-planning`, pricing and economics to `monetization`, product value to `activation` and `retention`, experiments to `experiment-design`, and infrastructure or vendor decisions to `growth-infrastructure-assessment`.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, market unit, product and customer scope, horizon, and boundary;
2. evidence ledger with freshness, counterevidence, and unavailable inputs;
3. seven-gate readiness matrix and limiting dependencies;
4. local customer, alternative, position, product, distribution, commercial, operational, and governance map;
5. beachhead or portfolio sequence with explicit rejected options;
6. adaptation backlog and current-primary-source review plan;
7. bounded pilot with owners, capacity, budget, measurement, decision and stop rules, rollback, and completion proof;
8. 30-, 60-, and 90-day handoffs, Playbook sources, and unresolved evidence.

For China work, keep China, language, locale, customer unit, city or region, product surface, app distribution, website, ICP filing or another hosting dependency, identity, account, platform, channel, partner, payment, tax, invoice, contract, seal or signature, content review, advertising, consent, storage, transfer, security, support, and applicable rules separate. Do not infer any of them from China or Simplified Chinese.

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not open market, platform, bank, payment, app-store, ad, social, partner, vendor, CRM, product, analytics, support, finance, data, or government accounts; contact customers, partners, media, vendors, or authorities; form entities; make filings; accept terms; buy domains; procure vendors; translate or publish production assets; change product, price, payment, data, support, contracts, or budgets; launch pilots or campaigns; spend; deploy; or claim results.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that market, language, locale, customer, job, alternatives, product, route, channel, partner, offer, price, payment, data, trust, review, operations, support, economics, capacity, evidence, owners, gates, decision, pilot, stop, rollback, and exit are explicit; unavailable is not failure; translation is not market entry; TAM is not reachable demand; attribution is not incrementality; one local success is not national validation; and no external action occurred.
