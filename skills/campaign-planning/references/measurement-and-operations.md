# Campaign Measurement And Operations

## Metric System

Use a decision-linked hierarchy:

| Role | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Qualified objective | Qualified pipeline, activated customers, retained demand, incremental contribution, launch adoption |
| Immediate diagnostic | Qualified reach, comprehension, response, arrival, cost, fraud, delivery |
| First-value outcome | Activation, time to value, qualified next action, errors, support |
| Downstream outcome | Retention, revenue, contribution, payback, renewal, trust |
| Guardrail | Complaints, opt-out, brand harm, support load, fraud, margin, dependency, cannibalization |

Record eligibility, numerator, denominator, window, source taxonomy, cost scope, exclusions, attribution rule, maturation, owner, and limitation for each metric.

## Taxonomy And Reconciliation

Define stable campaign, channel, source, medium, creative, audience, market, locale, offer, landing, experiment, and version IDs before launch. Map platform, analytics, CRM, product, billing, and finance events without assuming they reconcile automatically.

Preserve the original campaign and source through activation, retention, and revenue cohorts. Document identity loss, cross-device behavior, offline conversion, delayed events, duplicates, returning users, and platform-versus-company discrepancies.

## Attribution And Incrementality

Attribution assigns observed credit; incrementality estimates what would not have happened without the campaign. Keep platform attribution, first-touch, last-touch, multi-touch, and company reporting as operational views with stated limits.

Use randomized holdout, geo or market tests, time-based tests, or another credible design when causal contribution changes budget allocation. Route causal design and readout to `experiment-design`. Do not sum overlapping platform-reported conversions and call the total company growth.

## Budget Scenarios

For every scenario state objective, eligible audience, channel, currency, cost inclusions, unit-cost assumptions, reach and frequency assumptions, response, activation, retention, contribution, capacity, saturation, downside, and evidence status.

Use low, base, and high scenarios only when their assumptions are named. A stated budget is a constraint, not a requirement to spend it. Release or reallocate budget only under approved evidence, economics, frequency, quality, and stop rules.

## Operations

Build a dependency-aware calendar with strategy freeze, source capture, asset brief, production, localization, review, tracking QA, landing QA, audience QA, account readiness, launch approval, ramp, monitoring, stop checks, maturation, readout, and postmortem.

Name one accountable campaign owner plus channel, content, design, web, data, sales, lifecycle, support, finance, legal, privacy, and market owners where relevant. Record approval status rather than assuming it.

## Review And Postmortem

Review objective, audience, promise, offer, channel role, asset hypothesis, entry path, spend, operational incidents, immediate response, first value, downstream quality, attribution, incrementality, economics, trust, and market differences.

Separate what was planned, changed, observed, inferred, unavailable, and decided. Preserve negative, flat, harmful, delayed, and contradictory results. Record which assets, claims, audiences, channels, taxonomies, or mechanisms can be reused and under what boundary.
