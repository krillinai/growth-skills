# Campaign Channels And Journey

## Campaign Journey

Build the path around customer value:

```text
customer trigger or intent
-> qualified exposure
-> promise and proof
-> qualified response
-> owned entry surface
-> first value
-> retained value
-> revenue or contribution where relevant
```

Define failure, friction, and trust risks at each transition. A strong click rate with weak activation can indicate promise or audience mismatch rather than a landing-page problem alone.

## Channel Selection

Evaluate each channel by:

- whether it reaches the intended customer at a relevant moment;
- whether its format can communicate the promise and proof;
- whether the product, offer, sales motion, margin, and maturation fit the channel;
- whether exposure can be controlled, stopped, segmented, and audited;
- evidence time, operational capacity, scale, saturation, and creative decay;
- account, platform, policy, partner, data, and bargaining dependency;
- consent, suppression, targeting, content, and market requirements.

Assign one primary role per channel and document secondary roles. Do not add channels merely for coverage. Test one or two mechanism hypotheses deeply enough to connect audience, message, entry path, activation, retention, economics, and trust.

## Campaign And Lifecycle Boundary

A campaign has bounded dates, objective, audience, offer, assets, and review. Lifecycle communication responds to customer state and may continue outside campaign dates. Campaign sends through lifecycle channels must obey the stricter applicable eligibility, permission, suppression, fatigue, collision, completion, and service-message rules.

Do not force acquisition audiences into lifecycle states without verified identity and eligibility. Do not automatically switch channels after a delivery failure.

## Positioning And Message Architecture

Define the buying or use situation, current alternative including inaction, problem, differentiated value, proof, boundary, offer, and next action. Keep the same product truth across channels while adapting emphasis, terminology, detail, format, and proof for audience and market.

Translation alone is not market adaptation. Validate local demand, alternatives, terminology, category understanding, trust, regulation, channels, product readiness, payments, devices, and ownership before changing the campaign plan.

## Asset Matrix

For each asset record campaign, audience, market, locale, channel, journey role, format, core claim, proof, CTA, landing destination, source, owner, dependencies, due date, variant, accessibility, brand, legal, platform, and final approval state.

Use a small hypothesis-led asset system. Tag problem, promise, proof, format, creator, audience, market, CTA, and version so learning can accumulate. More variants without a clear taxonomy scale noise.

## Entry Surface And Offer

Continue the campaign promise on the actual entry surface. Preserve audience relevance, comprehension, proof, offer terms, pricing, eligibility, trust, accessibility, and path to first value. Do not optimize signup or lead capture while hiding weak understanding or downstream quality.
