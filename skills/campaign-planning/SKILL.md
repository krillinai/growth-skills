# Campaign Planning

Turn a bounded marketing objective into a coherent audience, promise, offer, channel, content, journey, measurement, operating, and decision plan. Work from supplied data or attributable public context. Private ad, CRM, analytics, sales, finance, brand, or channel data is optional; mark missing or declined inputs `unavailable`, never as failure.

Read [campaign-contract.md](references/campaign-contract.md) for intake, evidence, claims, permissions, privacy, and required outputs. Read [channel-and-journey.md](references/channel-and-journey.md) before selecting channels, entry surfaces, offers, asset roles, lifecycle boundaries, or market adaptations. Read [measurement-and-operations.md](references/measurement-and-operations.md) before defining budgets, taxonomy, attribution, incrementality, launch controls, reporting, or postmortems. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `strategy` | Define the objective, audience, buying or use situation, positioning, promise, proof, offer, and channel hypotheses |
| `plan` | Build an execution-ready campaign brief, journey, channel plan, asset matrix, budget scenarios, calendar, measurement, owners, approvals, and decision rules |
| `review` | Audit a proposed, active, or completed campaign for evidence, coherence, downstream quality, economics, market fit, operations, and learning |

Name one primary mode. Keep verified inputs, assumptions, strategic choices, execution requirements, and measured outcomes visibly separate.

## Freeze The Campaign Contract

Record the decision and owner; campaign type and bounded dates; customer, segment, job, intent, buying or use situation, market, language, locale, and exclusions; product, value, alternatives, positioning, promise, proof, offer, price or incentive; qualified objective and baseline; channel hypotheses and permissions; entry surface and next value; lifecycle and collision rules; asset requirements, claims, brand and accessibility constraints; budget and cost scope; taxonomy, attribution, incrementality, maturation, and guardrails; approvals, dependencies, launch, stop, rollback, and postmortem dates.

Do not treat publishing volume, impressions, followers, clicks, leads, installs, signups, platform conversions, or spend as the campaign objective unless the declared decision and evidence make them the real qualified outcome. Connect immediate response to activation, retained value, revenue or contribution, and trust where relevant.

## Apply Evidence Before Planning

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder-only statement may use signal status `reported signal` with a blank evidence state.

Public artifacts can verify visible product, offer, claims, market language, current landing surfaces, and public channel activity at a dated capture. They cannot establish private audience size, permissions, performance, cost, attribution, conversion, retention, incrementality, budget, or channel access.

Never invent audience size, channel availability, benchmarks, CPM, CPC, conversion, CAC, budget, reach, frequency, attribution, incrementality, revenue, retention, causal effects, or campaign results. When inputs are missing, provide scenarios, formulas, ranges requiring user-supplied assumptions, or an evidence-acquisition plan rather than false precision.

## Build One Coherent Journey

Define how the intended audience moves from a relevant trigger through promise and proof to a qualified response, owned entry surface, first value, retained value, and economic outcome. Preserve message continuity and customer control across channels.

Assign each channel a role: discovery, education, demand capture, proof, conversion, event, partner access, sales follow-up, lifecycle support, or re-engagement. Select channels by customer access, format and product fit, economics, evidence time, control, scale and decay, dependency, permissions, and market conditions, not by a generic channel list.

A campaign is a bounded coordinated initiative. Lifecycle communication is state-triggered and ongoing. When a campaign uses lifecycle channels, apply consent, suppression, frequency, collision, quiet-hour, and completion rules before content or scheduling.

## Plan Assets Without Drafting Them

Create an asset matrix with audience, job, funnel role, channel, format, claim, proof, CTA, source, owner, due date, variant, locale, accessibility, approval, and reuse rule. Campaign Planning defines requirements and message architecture; it does not draft final copy, design creative, render video, publish, or send.

Route copy to `copywriting`, revisions to `copy-editing`, lifecycle messages to `lifecycle-marketing`, one-to-one B2B outreach to `b2b-outbound-messaging`, causal tests to `experiment-design`, first-value work to `activation`, recurring value to `retention`, commercial offers to `monetization`, and broad constraint selection to `growth-diagnosis`.

## Measure Decisions, Not Activity

Preserve attribution and incrementality as different views. Attribution operates channels; incrementality governs causal allocation. Define source taxonomy, identity, qualified events, cost scope, campaign and cohort windows, maturation, downstream outcomes, guardrails, and reconciliation before launch.

Budget only against an explicit objective, eligible audience, channel role, cost basis, conversion and maturation assumptions, operational capacity, retained economics, and downside. Use scenario envelopes when evidence is incomplete. Do not allocate spend merely to use a stated budget.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, campaign boundary, and objective;
2. evidence, audience, positioning, offer, claims, and permission ledger;
3. campaign brief and journey;
4. channel-role and market-adaptation plan;
5. asset and content-requirement matrix;
6. budget scenarios, taxonomy, measurement, and experiment plan;
7. calendar, owners, dependencies, approvals, launch, stop, and rollback controls;
8. reporting, postmortem, reuse, and handoffs;
9. Playbook references and external-action boundary.

For China work, keep market, language, locale, product surface, audience source, channel, platform, account, app distribution, identity, payment, consent, content rules, data access, and applicable review separate. Do not infer WeChat, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Kuaishou, Baidu, app-store, ad account, payment, publishing, messaging, compliance, or performance conditions from market or language.

Strategy, planning, and review authorize local artifacts only. Do not access ad, analytics, CRM, publishing, payment, or social accounts; upload audiences; buy media; change bids or budgets; create campaigns; schedule, publish, or send content; contact customers; issue incentives or discounts; alter landing pages; or deploy tracking without separate task-level authorization and capability review.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that the objective, audience, market, situation, promise, proof, offer, channel roles, entry path, lifecycle boundary, assets, claims, permissions, budget assumptions, taxonomy, attribution, incrementality, maturity, guardrails, owners, approvals, stop rules, and postmortem are explicit; activity metrics are not substituted for customer or business value; scenarios expose assumptions; downstream quality remains visible; Playbook sources are pinned; and no external action occurred.
