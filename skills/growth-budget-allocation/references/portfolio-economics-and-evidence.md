# Portfolio Economics And Evidence

## Define One Allocation Unit

An allocation unit can be a bounded channel band, lifecycle program, product or market commitment, experiment portfolio, system capability, protected obligation, or option. Do not mix units that require different decisions or owners.

| Field | Required evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Identity | Stable ID, version, portfolio class, owner, decision rights, product, market, period, and resource type |
| Value | Customer, problem, current alternative, mechanism, first and repeated value, retained outcome, and harm protections |
| Strategy | Selected choice, exclusion boundary, current constraint, dependency, and why this commitment belongs in the portfolio |
| Evidence | Sources, states, dates, maturity, supporting and contradictory evidence, causal status, missingness, and next discriminating evidence |
| Resources | Current, requested, minimum viable, staged, protected, contingent, and maximum band by period |
| Economics | Incremental customers or units, full and marginal cost, retained contribution, cash, payback, sensitivity, and unavailable inputs |
| Capacity | Build, operate, support, govern, maintain, exit, and leadership capacity plus contention and ramp |
| Portfolio | Overlap, interaction, cannibalization, displacement, concentration, shared dependency, option value, and opportunity cost |
| Decision | Preserve, fund, stage, cap, reduce, pause, stop, reserve, reject, or unresolved with gates, expiry, and review |

Classify commitments as `core`, `adjacent`, `exploratory`, `system`, or `protected`. The class describes purpose and evidence expectations; it does not prescribe a percentage.

## Trace Incremental Retained Value

Build a nonduplicative chain:

```text
resource band
-> implemented capability or exposure
-> eligible customer interaction
-> first value
-> repeated, retained, expanded, restored, or protected value
-> incremental contribution and cash, if supported
```

Record population, identity, timing, lag, maturity, source, evidence state, causal status, alternatives, contradiction, and failure signal at every link. Tasks, activity, impressions, clicks, traffic, leads, installs, spend, utilization, and shipping are not retained value by themselves.

Keep customer value, operational units, GMV, bookings, billings, revenue, gross profit, contribution, accounting profit, cash, cost avoidance, capacity release, risk reduction, option value, and valuation separate. Define beneficiary, baseline, counterfactual, horizon, cost basis, and overlap.

## Reconcile Attribution And Incrementality

Attributed ROAS, dashboard revenue, last touch, multi-touch credit, modeled conversion, or channel reporting does not establish incrementality. Define source taxonomy, identity, lookback, model, denominator, unknown state, refunds, churn, retained-value maturity, and reconciliation to company records.

Preserve organic demand, selection, timing, cross-device ambiguity, overlap, cannibalization, displacement, concurrent changes, interference, seasonality, and market mix. Use controlled experiments, valid natural experiments, calibrated models, or bounded triangulation only to the degree supported. Route detailed attribution to `attribution-analysis` and experiment validity to `experiment-design`.

Do not add attributed value, causal lift, forecast upside, opportunity headroom, halo, cost savings, and observed outcomes. Reconcile identities and effects once, report interaction and unknown residual, and separate accountable ownership from contributor activity.

## Compare Average And Marginal Evidence

Historical average CAC, ROI, conversion, retention, LTV, payback, contribution, and productivity describe the measured population and period. The next band requires marginal evidence for remaining eligible demand, auction or supply pressure, saturation, fatigue, creative or content supply, sales and service capacity, cohort quality, cash, market conditions, platform dependency, and lag.

For each band record:

| Band | Eligible remaining demand | Incremental retained units | Full marginal cost | Contribution and cash | Maturity and evidence | Capacity and dependency | Cap and stop rule |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- | --- | --- |

Do not extrapolate a linear return beyond observed or defensible bounds. When marginal evidence is unavailable, use a bounded learning band or leave the decision unresolved.

## Include Full Cost, Cash, And Obligations

Include relevant research, product, engineering, design, data, analytics, creative, sales, channel, partner, support, success, moderation, fulfillment, security, privacy, compliance, finance, procurement, implementation, migration, training, leadership, media, incentive, commission, payment, infrastructure, storage, model inference, vendor, inventory, refund, chargeback, fraud, tax, working-capital, maintenance, incident, replacement, decommissioning, exit, residual, and opportunity costs.

Time-phase cash outflow, billing, collection, refunds, inventory, incentives, adoption, maturity, renewal, replacement, and exit. Test funding gaps and downside liquidity. Keep financing availability unavailable unless verified. Route detailed unit economics to `unit-economics-analysis`, LTV to `ltv-analysis`, and outlooks to `growth-forecasting`.

## Protect Customer And Operating Capacity

Establish explicit floors for existing-customer value, support, contractual service, accessibility, safety, trust, reliability, maintenance, security, privacy, compliance, fraud prevention, measurement integrity, incident response, documentation, transition, and decommissioning. For each floor record owner, standard, evidence, capacity, period, exception authority, monitoring, escalation, and exit condition.

Do not offset noncompensable harm or mandatory obligations with upside. A portfolio that acquires more customers while degrading retained value, reliability, contribution, or trust has not demonstrated healthy growth.
