# Positioning Contract

## Minimum Context

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, product unit, customer unit, buying unit, route to market, horizon, version, and review date;
- user, buyer, payer, approver, beneficiary, job, trigger, urgency, decision criteria, and current alternative;
- market, language, locale, product surface, price and package context, access path, and exclusions;
- current position, category, promise, claims, proof, objections, qualification, and message variants;
- product capabilities available now, prerequisites, operational consequences, customer outcomes, and limitations;
- direct, adjacent, manual, service, general-tool, status-quo, inaction, and budget alternatives;
- public sources, supplied research, win/loss, churn, support, sales, behavioral, commercial, and retention evidence;
- source ID, capture date, population, unit, definition, evidence state, counterevidence, and privacy limit;
- decision options, validation method, owner, decision date, guardrails, and migration constraints.

## Evidence Ledger

Use one row per atomic proposition. Record exact claim, source, source type, date, product, segment, situation, market, evidence state, interpretation, counterevidence, limitation, owner, approval, and review date.

`verified` means the cited artifact supports the bounded observation. It does not mean a customer claim is objectively true, representative, differentiated, causal, or durable. Stakeholder consensus is not customer evidence.

## Positioning Version

Store the current thesis, scope, assumptions, approved claims, prohibited claims, proof, variants, owner, effective date, review trigger, and superseded version. Reopen the position after material product, market, customer, alternative, price, route, or retained-value changes.

## Privacy And Output Boundary

Prefer aggregate or redacted evidence. Do not reproduce direct identifiers, private deal notes, raw customer lists, credentials, or unrelated personal data. Missing private evidence should produce a bounded hypothesis and acquisition plan, not repeated requests or fabricated certainty.
