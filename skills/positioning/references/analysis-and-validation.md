# Analysis And Validation

## Alternative Map

For each buying situation, compare inaction, status quo, manual work, internal build, service, general tool, adjacent product, and direct competitor where applicable. Record why it is chosen, strengths, switching costs, where it breaks, customer evidence, counterevidence, and status. Do not build a feature grid before identifying the actual choice set.

## Capability-To-Value Chain

Test each link separately:

| Layer | Required question |
| --- | --- |
| Capability | Is it available now, and under which product or operating conditions? |
| Difference | Can relevant alternatives match it under the same conditions? |
| Consequence | What changes in workflow, behavior, access, quality, speed, cost, control, or risk? |
| Value | Which outcome matters to the scoped customer and buying situation? |
| Proof | Which demonstration, technical record, customer evidence, or outcome supports this exact claim? |
| Boundary | Where does the claim stop being true or useful? |

Treat a product benchmark, demo, testimonial, case, and observed customer outcome as different proof types. None automatically proves the full chain.

## Positioning Thesis

Write a decision artifact, not necessarily public copy:

```text
For [best-fit customer] in [buying situation], [product] is [useful market frame]
that delivers [differentiated value] because [available capabilities and proof],
unlike [actual alternatives], subject to [conditions and limits].
```

List prerequisites and explicit poor-fit cases. A position that cannot disqualify demand is usually too broad.

## Category Decision

Compare existing category, subcategory, reframe, and new category across comprehension, inherited criteria, competitive frame, differentiation, education effort, proof, sales cycle, channel fit, ecosystem support, and risk of attracting poor-fit demand.

## Translation Matrix

Translate the same position by audience, buying stage, use situation, and channel. For every variant retain the audience, task, message, claim, proof, CTA, prohibited inference, and source. Do not make translation responsible for an unsupported strategic change.

## Validation Portfolio

Use multiple layers:

- alternatives: win/loss, churn destinations, replacement behavior, search and sales language;
- comprehension: message interviews, five-second recall, category and alternative identification;
- value and proof: customer research, demonstrations, objection handling, willingness-to-pay evidence;
- qualification and buying: progression, no-decision, win/loss, cycle, discounting, implementation;
- delivery: promised-use-case activation, time to value, reliability, support burden, retained value;
- economics: price acceptance, service cost, contribution, renewal, expansion, and contraction.

Define the population, source, baseline, window, maturity, decision rule, contradiction signal, guardrails, owner, and date. Separate message response, attribution, downstream quality, and causal evidence.

## Repositioning Comparison

Compare current and proposed positions on scope, alternatives, value, proof, category, best-fit customer, exclusions, product changes, route, price, sales, service, migration, customer expectation, brand coherence, measurement, and reversibility. Do not announce a new position before the required product and proof exist.
