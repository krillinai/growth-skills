# Casey Winters Product Growth Lens

Apply Casey Winters' public methods to product and marketplace growth. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Diagnostic Unit

Collect only consequential context: product, segment, value event, natural frequency, cohort retention, entry surface, setup requirements, supply structure, market unit, channel mix, economics, team ownership, and current decision. Mark unknowns explicitly; never invent metrics or Winters' views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary lens and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| Acquisition grows but durable usage is unclear | Retention evidence for PMF |
| Registration improves but repeat use does not | Setup-Aha-Habit onboarding |
| The team has many tactics but no causal diagnosis | Product-specific growth model |
| Marketplace demand has weak choice or fulfillment | Supply as product |
| Geographic or category expansion fragments value | Repeatable marketplace unit |
| Search traffic is high but low quality | SEO product system |
| Conversion work focuses on the homepage | Actual entry-surface conversion |
| Manual acquisition works but scaling fails | Kindle-to-fire transition |
| The growth team lacks leverage or authority | Constraint-aligned ownership |
| AI-generated expertise creates trust risk | Consent and attribution system |

## Diagnose

1. Define retained customer value and the decision scope.
2. Map the selected framework with `verified`, `inferred`, and `missing` evidence.
3. Trace the path from entry through setup, value, habit, retention, economics, and reusable acquisition.
4. Identify one primary constraint; retain alternatives and contradictory evidence.
5. Compare interventions by reach, retained impact, confidence, effort, reversibility, economics, and trust risk.
6. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
7. Define an experiment or evidence plan with population, intervention, primary metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
8. State what this lens may underweight and name an alternative lens.

Do not optimize onboarding completion without downstream retention, treat total marketplace supply as liquidity, call indexed pages an SEO strategy without user value, or scale a manual channel before retained economics are understood.

## Deliver

Use: **scope and value**, **lens applicability**, **growth-system map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **metrics and guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Distinguish operating accounts, advisory observations, short clips, and company-wide outcomes.

## Required Boundary

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Casey Winters' public work. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Casey Winters.

Never write as Winters, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Using a universal retention benchmark instead of the product's natural cadence.
- Removing setup steps that are required to reach value.
- Copying another company's growth model or org chart.
- Assuming historical network scale guarantees defense.
- Optimizing search traffic or signup conversion without retained quality.
- Treating AI content volume as a substitute for consent, faithful representation, and control.
