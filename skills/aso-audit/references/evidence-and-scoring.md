# Evidence and Scoring

Use this protocol for a bounded ASO audit. Identify one primary mode (`listing`, `focused`, `performance`, or `out-of-scope`), then record any modifier or surface bound, platform, canonical URL or identifier, requested country and locale, and capture date before scoring. `competitive` is a modifier, not a primary mode: compare with the same platform, fixed card, market, locale, and date. A pure listing comparison is `listing + competitive`; keep `performance` primary for decline diagnosis even when adding competitive comparison or a focused surface bound. `focused` is primary when the decision concerns only named surfaces and may also bound another primary mode without replacing it. Start with supplied or directly captured public listing evidence; optional private evidence can refine a check but is never a prerequisite to begin.

**Evidence states.** `verified` is directly observable in a dated public capture. A private measure is verified only from an attributable supplied artifact or export that identifies the source/system, scope, metric definition, date or window, and value with enough detail to inspect. An owner's conversational claim is a reported signal, not verified or scored evidence. `inferred` is a reasoned explanation or expected effect that is not directly measured. `unavailable` is applicable evidence that was not supplied, could not be accessed, or failed to render; record the limit and the smallest useful request. `not applicable` is outside the selected platform or stated scope. Do not substitute a similar platform, country, locale, or historical capture. A partial load is evidence only of the rendered state, not the whole listing.

**Finding record.** Each adverse assessed result requires one finding with stable ID, scope, source, date, check, impact, action, completion, and effort. A finding ID is unique per audit, app, and finding: use `{app-slug}-{check-id}-{sequence}`, such as `trail-ledger-APL-CRT-03-01`; keep the check ID in its own field and retain the finding ID if later evidence changes state. In a competitive audit, the app slug prevents collisions across listings. Scope names the platform, market, locale, and bounded surface. Source is the capture, export, or primary policy URL. Date is the capture/retrieval date. Impact states the observed consequence first; any expected business effect is `inferred`. Action is an auditable change or validation step. Completion names the observable proof. Effort is `S`, `M`, or `L`.

**Unavailable register.** List unavailable applicable checks separately from findings. Include check ID, requested evidence, why it is unavailable, and next collection step. Missing console, attribution, keyword, experiment, ranking, conversion, or acquisition data is not a defect and does not become a finding. Never claim rank, installs, conversion, causality, or uplift without dated evidence that measures it.

**Assessment rule.** An assessed check receives exactly `pass = 100%`, `partial = 50%`, or `fail = 0%` of its fixed maximum. Directly adverse `inferred` evidence is `partial` until verified; a positive inference cannot pass and is `partial` at most. A verified observation may pass, partial, or fail only against the stated check. Every partial or fail deduction must map to a finding; no finding is needed for unavailable or not-applicable checks.

**Fixed-row properties.** Before assessing a fixed scoring row, enumerate every stated property. If the property set cannot be established or any required property is unavailable, the whole row is `unavailable`; keep its full maximum only in the all-applicable coverage denominator. When all applicable properties are assessed, the row is `pass` only if all pass, `fail` only if all fail, and `partial` for mixed pass/fail results. An inferred adverse property keeps the row `partial` until verified, and a positive inference cannot supply a passing property. Properties receive no subweights or new rows, and the auditor cannot choose a different aggregation. This property aggregation is within one row; collection aggregation below applies one stated property across multiple members.

**Collection-level checks.** A fixed check that evaluates a collection against one stated property keeps one check ID and its listed maximum. Define the collection membership from the dated listing/capture or supplied asset inventory, then record each member under the parent ID and assess it against the property. If membership is unknown, or any applicable member is unavailable or unrendered, the entire parent check is `unavailable`: retain its full maximum only in the all-applicable coverage denominator and award or deduct no points. Report observed member notes and preserve links to adverse-member findings, but do not score the parent from a partial collection. Only when every applicable member is assessed may the parent result be `pass` when all pass, `fail` when all fail, or `partial` when results are mixed. Do not create member rows, assign member subweights, or otherwise change the fixed inventory.

**Math.** For each applicable check, its fixed maximum is part of the platform's 100-point card. `earned` is the sum of assessed check awards. `assessed max` is the sum of applicable checks with a pass, partial, or fail. `all applicable max` is the sum of every applicable check, including unavailable checks. `score = assessed earned / assessed max * 100`. `coverage = assessed applicable max / all applicable max * 100`. Report both to two decimals with their numerators and denominators. If assessed max is zero, report score as `Not scored`; if all applicable max is zero, report coverage as `Not scored`. Unavailable earns and loses zero points: it remains in the coverage denominator but is excluded from score math. Not applicable is excluded from both denominators.

**Priority.** Order findings by (1) verified fail before verified partial before inferred adverse partial, (2) larger fixed-point deduction, (3) dependencies that unblock several checks or later validation, then (4) lower effort. A dependency is a prerequisite, not a reason to inflate impact. Keep public-surface fixes and evidence-collection tasks distinct.

**Policy and benchmarks.** A platform-rule statement is verified only with a current primary source, source URL, retrieval date, and an exposed publication or update date when available. Otherwise label the exact rule unavailable and provide the official verification path. Market benchmarks are context only: date, market, cohort, and method must be stated; they never create deductions or promised uplift. Brand fame, volume, or aesthetic preference is context, not proof of discovery, relevance, conversion, or quality.

**Report trace.** Present: primary mode, modifiers and surface bounds; scope and capture limits; evidence table; scored checks and point trace; unavailable register; findings; priority; and validation plan. A competitive modifier uses the same platform, market, locale, date, and card for every app; label a pure listing comparison `listing + competitive`, and retain `performance` as primary for decline diagnosis. Listing-page evidence cannot establish query rank. Performance reports treat a stated symptom as a reported signal unless dated data verifies it. Requests for application implementation, release submission, or unrelated engineering are out of scope and receive a concise handoff.
