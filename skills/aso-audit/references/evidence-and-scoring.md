# Evidence and Scoring

Use this protocol for a bounded ASO audit. Identify the mode (listing, focused, competitive, performance, or out-of-scope), platform, canonical URL or identifier, requested country and locale, and capture date before scoring. Start with supplied or directly captured public listing evidence; optional private evidence can refine a check but is never a prerequisite to begin.

**Evidence states.** `verified` is directly observable in a dated capture or a supplied, attributable export. `inferred` is a reasoned explanation or expected effect that is not directly measured. `unavailable` is applicable evidence that was not supplied, could not be accessed, or failed to render; record the limit and the smallest useful request. `not applicable` is outside the selected platform or stated scope. Do not substitute a similar platform, country, locale, or historical capture. A partial load is evidence only of the rendered state, not the whole listing.

**Finding record.** Each adverse assessed result requires one finding with stable ID, scope, source, date, check, impact, action, completion, and effort. Use IDs such as `APL-CRT-03`; keep the same ID if later evidence changes its state. Scope names the platform, market, locale, and bounded surface. Source is the capture, export, or primary policy URL. Date is the capture/retrieval date. Impact states the observed consequence first; any expected business effect is `inferred`. Action is an auditable change or validation step. Completion names the observable proof. Effort is `S`, `M`, or `L`.

**Unavailable register.** List unavailable applicable checks separately from findings. Include check ID, requested evidence, why it is unavailable, and next collection step. Missing console, attribution, keyword, experiment, ranking, conversion, or acquisition data is not a defect and does not become a finding. Never claim rank, installs, conversion, causality, or uplift without dated evidence that measures it.

**Assessment rule.** An assessed check receives exactly `pass = 100%`, `partial = 50%`, or `fail = 0%` of its fixed maximum. Directly adverse `inferred` evidence is `partial` until verified; a positive inference cannot pass and is `partial` at most. A verified observation may pass, partial, or fail only against the stated check. Every partial or fail deduction must map to a finding; no finding is needed for unavailable or not-applicable checks.

**Math.** For each applicable check, its fixed maximum is part of the platform's 100-point card. `earned` is the sum of assessed check awards. `assessed max` is the sum of applicable checks with a pass, partial, or fail. `all applicable max` is the sum of every applicable check, including unavailable checks. `score = assessed earned / assessed max * 100`. `coverage = assessed applicable max / all applicable max * 100`. Report both to two decimals with their numerators and denominators. If assessed max is zero, report score as `Not scored`; if all applicable max is zero, report coverage as `Not scored`. Unavailable earns and loses zero points: it remains in the coverage denominator but is excluded from score math. Not applicable is excluded from both denominators.

**Priority.** Order findings by (1) verified fail before verified partial before inferred adverse partial, (2) larger fixed-point deduction, (3) dependencies that unblock several checks or later validation, then (4) lower effort. A dependency is a prerequisite, not a reason to inflate impact. Keep public-surface fixes and evidence-collection tasks distinct.

**Policy and benchmarks.** A platform-rule statement is verified only with a current primary source, source URL, retrieval date, and an exposed publication or update date when available. Otherwise label the exact rule unavailable and provide the official verification path. Market benchmarks are context only: date, market, cohort, and method must be stated; they never create deductions or promised uplift. Brand fame, volume, or aesthetic preference is context, not proof of discovery, relevance, conversion, or quality.

**Report trace.** Present: scope and capture limits; evidence table; scored checks and point trace; unavailable register; findings; priority; and validation plan. Competitive reports use the same platform, market, and card for every app; listing-page evidence cannot establish query rank. Performance reports treat a stated symptom as a reported signal unless dated data verifies it. Requests for application implementation, release submission, or unrelated engineering are out of scope and receive a concise handoff.
