# Retention Diagnosis And Intervention Design

## Recurring Value Patterns

Classify the supported mechanism without forcing one:

| Pattern | Retention question |
| --- | --- |
| Habit | Does repeated individual action continue producing value? |
| Network | Do other participants make return more valuable? |
| Stored value | Does accumulated data, history, configuration, or learning deepen value? |
| Workflow | Does the product remain part of a repeated team or business process? |

Switching cost without continuing value may delay churn but is not healthy retention.

## Diagnostic Sequence

1. Verify measurement and cohort maturity.
2. Check acquisition and activation mix.
3. Locate the largest curve loss or state transition.
4. Compare recurring value, frequency, depth, reliability, collaboration, and commercial evidence.
5. Separate observed facts, reported signals, inferred mechanisms, and unavailable evidence.
6. Select one mechanism only when it changes the next decision.

Useful churn categories include activation failure, value loss, reliability failure, changed or episodic need, competitive displacement, commercial or packaging friction, involuntary payment failure, and account-level champion or workflow loss. Categories may coexist; do not assign one without evidence.

## Lifecycle And Resurrection

Define entry, exit, minimum duration, precedence, and evidence source for `new`, `activated`, `retained`, `at risk`, `lapsed`, `resurrected`, `expanded`, and `churned`. A resurrection event is a return after a valid inactive interval; measure its second retention curve rather than only the return.

## Intervention Record

Every intervention must state:

- supported mechanism and evidence;
- eligible entity, cohort, and lifecycle state;
- intervention and responsible owner;
- expected recurring value event, not only an open, click, login, or purchase proxy;
- natural interval, maturation window, and follow-up period;
- early diagnostic and primary retention outcome;
- quality, trust, fatigue, support, revenue, and customer-harm guardrails;
- randomization or alternative evidence design;
- decision rule and follow-up action;
- dependencies and external-action authorization still required.

Do not default to notifications, streaks, discounts, save offers, or sales outreach before testing the underlying value or reliability mechanism. When messaging is appropriate, define eligibility, suppression, fatigue, and outcome measurement here, then hand copy creation to `lifecycle-marketing`.

## Prioritization

Prioritize interventions by decision relevance, evidence strength, affected eligible population, mechanism control, learning value, time to mature, reversibility, customer risk, and economic exposure. These are judgment dimensions, not an invented numeric score.
