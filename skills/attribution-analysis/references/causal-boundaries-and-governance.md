# Causal Boundaries And Governance

## Attribution Is Not Incrementality

| Claim | Required support |
| --- | --- |
| Observed touchpoint | Inspectable event under a declared source and identity contract |
| Attributed outcome | Outcome plus explicit credit rule, window, eligible touches, and model version |
| Reconciled outcome | Compatible entities and states aligned across accountable systems |
| Predicted outcome | Frozen prediction time, features, label, validation, calibration, and uncertainty |
| Incremental effect | Credible counterfactual, assignment or identification, exposure, maturity, assumptions, and guardrails |

Attribution may help operate channels even when incrementality is unavailable. Label the limitation and do not convert credit into causal lift, incremental CAC, or budget authority.

Preserve organic demand, prior intent, other channels, returning customers, cross-device gaps, spillovers, brand effects, selection, seasonality, saturation, market changes, and simultaneous interventions. Route randomized holdouts, geo tests, switchbacks, shutdowns, phased rollouts, matched designs, interrupted time series, power, interference, and readout to `experiment-design`.

## Algorithmic Model Contract

Freeze decision, entity, eligible population, prediction time, eligible pre-outcome touchpoints, source period, features, label, label window, training and validation cutoffs, model version, output, allowed use, and prohibited use.

Exclude post-outcome revenue, renewal, churn, future campaigns, later support notes, final routing, protected attributes, raw private communications, and other leakage or unnecessary sensitive fields. Require lineage, reproducibility, reason codes, out-of-time and segment validation, calibration when applicable, stability, missingness, fairness, drift, abstention, override, fallback, audit, and retraining criteria.

A model may predict association in historical data. It does not show which touch caused an outcome, which channel is incremental, or which treatment would help a customer.

## Governance

| Control | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Ownership | Decision, data, identity, model, finance, privacy, and system owners are explicit |
| Versioning | Taxonomy, identity, windows, model, outcomes, currencies, and effective dates are reconstructable |
| Access | Read, export, change, approve, and deploy rights are separated |
| Audit | Inputs, rules, overrides, outputs, consumers, incidents, and corrections are logged |
| Override | Reason, owner, scope, duration, expiry, and later review are visible |
| Stop | Harmful or unreliable reporting and automation can be disabled or reverted |
| Privacy | Purpose, authority, consent, minimization, retention, correction, deletion, and suppression are enforced |
| Incident | Severity, owner, evidence preservation, communication, recovery, and retrospective are defined |

Do not auto-route budgets, bids, campaigns, leads, accounts, customers, or offers from attribution output. Attribution quality does not grant product, commercial, platform, or customer-contact authority.

## Market Transfer

For every market, freeze legal entity, product, customer, platform, channel, device, app distribution, identifier, consent, purpose, data access, storage, transfer, provider, ad account, CRM, payment, invoice, currency, timezone, customer journey, lookback, outcome, model, applicable review, and owner.

Shared taxonomy and metric principles may transfer. Provider capability, identifiers, consent, cross-device joins, source coverage, windows, commercial systems, and customer behavior require local evidence. Translation is not compatibility. Preserve unavailable conditions and do not provide legal advice.

## External Actions

Attribution analysis may produce local source maps, event and identity requirements, rule specifications, queries, model comparisons, reconciliation tables, QA checks, causal handoffs, and approval-ready change plans. It must not access systems, export journeys, change tracking or model configuration, publish dashboards, route decisions, spend, deploy, or claim results without explicit task-level authorization and required controls.
