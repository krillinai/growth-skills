# Incentive Economics And Incrementality

## Compatibility Gate

Before calculating or comparing, require compatible:

- entity, eligibility, identity, opportunity, exposure, and denominator;
- target action, quality, value event, and reward version;
- segment, participant side, market, product, channel, and source;
- cohort start, age, natural frequency, horizon, maturity, cutoff, and timezone;
- currency, contribution policy, cost boundary, cash timing, and discounting;
- attribution and counterfactual design;
- fraud, cannibalization, fairness, trust, and support definitions.

When the gate fails, preserve observations separately and return the exact measurement contract needed for calculation.

## Incremental Behavior

For equal-size treatment and holdout under a valid randomized design:

    action lift = treatment action rate - holdout action rate

    incremental actors = treated eligible population x action lift

For unequal allocation or non-randomized designs, calculate arm counts and uncertainty under the actual design. Do not use this shortcut when interference, noncompliance, identity failure, selection, or missing exposure invalidates it.

Attribution answers which program claimed an action. Incrementality asks what changed because of eligibility or exposure. Keep them in separate fields.

## Full Incremental Program Cost

Include decision-relevant incremental:

    reward value paid, redeemed, or granted
    + payment and fulfillment
    + media, sales, affiliate, and partner cost
    + engineering, platform, and operations
    + support, dispute, and recovery
    + fraud and abuse loss
    + margin displacement
    + cannibalized organic or portfolio value
    + risk, compliance, tax, and legal operation
    + opportunity and capacity cost
    = full incremental program cost

For credits, points, storage, quota, seats, access, or virtual currency, separate face value, expected redemption, accounting liability, serving cost, breakage, support, and opportunity cost. Non-cash does not mean free.

## Retained Incremental Contribution

Under one mature compatible horizon:

    retained incremental actors
    = incremental actors x retained value conversion

    retained incremental contribution
    = retained incremental actors x contribution per retained actor

    net incremental contribution
    = retained incremental contribution - full incremental program cost

If contribution per retained actor already includes a cost, do not subtract the same cost again. State price, revenue, variable cost, support, fulfillment, risk, discounting, and refund boundaries.

Report uncertainty, maturation, concentration, cash timing, payback, and sensitivity. Do not justify a weak observed cohort with an optimistic uncalibrated LTV forecast.

## Cannibalization And Pull-Forward

Measure at customer and portfolio level:

- organic behavior paid for by the program;
- purchase or action shifted earlier without more total value;
- full-price demand moved to a subsidized offer;
- demand moved between channels, markets, products, or participant sides;
- supply drawn away from healthier marketplace units;
- intrinsic motivation replaced by reward dependence;
- customers trained to wait for promotions.

Categories can overlap. Define mutually exclusive components or report ranges; do not mechanically subtract every observed movement twice. Use customer-level and portfolio-level holdouts where possible and reconcile later periods.

## Post-Incentive Durability

Compare rewarded, holdout, and compatible organic cohorts at the product's natural frequency after reward taper or removal. Report value attained during exposure, post-incentive repeat behavior, retention, quality, contribution, fraud, and trust.

Segment by reward version and amount, value attained, source, market, participant side, fraud risk, and maturity. Immediate response is not retained value. A program that collapses after removal may have purchased activity, selected reward seekers, or failed to deliver product value.

## Marginal Portfolio Allocation

For every additional segment, market, tier, or budget band, compare marginal retained incremental contribution with full marginal cost, payback, capacity, saturation, fraud, cannibalization, quality, fairness, trust, and strategic dependence.

Average historical ROI cannot justify the next dollar. Allocate only where compatible marginal evidence passes the declared contribution and guardrail rules. Preserve explore capacity for bounded uncertain mechanisms; taper or stop destructive tiers.
