# Incentive Mechanisms, Risk, And Governance

## Mechanism Selection

| Barrier | Candidate mechanism | Required challenge |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product value is undiscovered | temporary credit, trial benefit, or risk reversal | is explanation, onboarding, or product quality the real issue? |
| Action has real participant cost | cash, credit, access, capacity, or protection | is the underlying value exchange structurally viable without permanent subsidy? |
| Marketplace is temporarily uncoordinated | local side-specific guarantee or promotion | are frequency, density, fulfillment, and participant economics sufficient after taper? |
| Valuable repetition is fragile | progress visibility, grace, recovery, status, or recognition | does the mechanic support value or exploit loss aversion and proxy activity? |
| Referral has effort or social risk | recipient-aligned, sender, or double-sided benefit | do recipients activate and retain without spam or trust damage? |
| Provider and customer outcomes conflict | contract or business-model alignment | is customer outcome measured and risk genuinely shared? |

Choose the smallest reward aligned with the value and barrier. Test non-incentive alternatives and the mechanism, recipient, timing, settlement, and taper, not only reward amount.

## Marketplace And Referral

For marketplaces, define the atomic geography, time, category, and participant side. Target the constrained transition and measure availability, demand, match, completion, cancellation, utilization, earnings, contribution, quality, safety, cross-side retention, and durability after taper. Aggregate volume can hide local harm.

For referrals, separate eligible satisfied sender, invitation, delivery, qualified recipient, activation, retained recipient value, reward validation, fulfillment, reversal, repeat, spam, complaints, collusion, concentration, and social trust. Route detailed loop closure and propagation measurement to `growth-loop-design`.

## Progress, Status, And Gamification

Anchor every point, badge, streak, league, level, protection, recovery, or notification to a meaningful customer action and durable outcome. Monitor point farming, meaningless repetition, coercive loss aversion, notification fatigue, status exclusion, wellbeing, accessibility, trust, and behavior after protection disappears.

Do not monetize fear of losing progress, hide material terms, or call engagement successful when the rewarded action no longer creates customer value.

## Fraud Controls

Apply controls at four stages:

1. before eligibility: identity, account age, relationship, market, consent, and risk prerequisites;
2. during action: device, network, graph, velocity, transaction, quality, and anomaly signals;
3. before fulfillment: qualification, completion, settlement delay, duplicate and attribution reconciliation, review, and reserves;
4. after settlement: refund, reversal, recovery, graph expansion, repeat abuse, appeal, and model feedback.

Keep risk signal, blocked attempt, confirmed fraud, estimated prevented loss, false positive, recovered value, and customer harm distinct. Calibrate rules and models by relevant segment; preserve human review, appeal, remediation, and support capacity. Delayed rewards improve recovery but can weaken motivation; choose explicitly.

## Fairness, Trust, And Terms

Document eligible and excluded populations, legitimate reason for differential treatment, minimum data inputs, visibility, terms, expiration, withdrawal, reversal, accessibility, market constraints, complaint, appeal, and remediation. Review selection and error rates by relevant groups without using protected attributes or precise location by default.

Avoid deceptive countdowns, hidden differences, unclear withdrawal, dark patterns, status exclusion, social spam, or progress loss designed primarily to force payment. State tax, invoice, reporting, and dispute requirements as locally reviewed conditions, never universal assumptions.

## Experiment, Taper, And Stop

Before exposure, freeze decision, owner, target behavior, population, assignment or counterfactual, reward versions, primary incremental outcome, value and retention metrics, full cost, fraud, cannibalization, fairness, trust, budget, capacity, interference, maturation, taper, and decision rules.

Stop or redesign when marginal retained incremental contribution falls below full marginal cost; post-incentive behavior fails; fraud, operational loss, complaints, unfairness, policy, privacy, or compliance exceeds limits; organic or portfolio value is materially cannibalized; quality deteriorates; reward requirements escalate; or the coordination barrier no longer exists.

## Data And Model Feedback

Label reward eligibility, assignment, exposure, amount, version, timing, fraud review, taper, and post-incentive outcome before using behavior in recommendations, personalization, risk, LTV, or product learning. Preserve untreated or holdout data, validate calibration and drift, and define rollback.

Rewarded behavior can reflect incentive response rather than stable preference. Training without exposure labels can amplify distortion and make later organic value harder to identify.

## Operating Controls

Name accountable owners and approval boundaries for budget, eligibility, fulfillment, finance, risk, legal, privacy, tax, experiment, product, support, communication, portfolio, incidents, and stop decisions. Keep builders, operators, reviewers, and exception approvers able to challenge one another.

An incentive design is not authorization to create codes, issue value, change eligibility, block accounts, contact users, move spend, launch experiments, or operate systems.
