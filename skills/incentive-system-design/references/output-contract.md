# Incentive System Output Contract

## Required Order

Return:

1. primary mode, decision, owner, target value and behavior, population, horizon, budget, and action boundary;
2. observed barrier, mechanism hypothesis, counterfactual, and non-incentive alternatives;
3. eligibility, reward, exposure, action, fulfillment, reversal, taper, and stop contract;
4. evidence, cohort, quality, attribution, incrementality, and limitation ledger;
5. full and marginal cost, retained incremental contribution, payback, sensitivity, and reconciliation;
6. cannibalization, fraud, fairness, trust, privacy, compliance, support, and incident controls;
7. validation design or portfolio allocation plan;
8. operating cadence, adjacent-Skill handoffs, and pinned Growth Playbook sources.

Show formulas and reconcile counts and currency. Preserve `unavailable`, immature outcomes, overlapping cannibalization, excluded costs, and range assumptions. Do not output a gross-redemption dashboard as an incentive evaluation.

## Decision Record

Use one decision: `measure`, `pilot`, `continue`, `scale bounded segment`, `redesign`, `taper`, or `stop`.

Use a capped budget and exposure. Include taper and post-incentive observation before scale. Do not turn the output into pricing, referral-loop, experiment-statistics, event-implementation, product, messaging, or production execution; route specialist work.

## Privacy And External Action

Exclude credentials, direct identifiers, financial credentials, raw private payloads, exact locations, unnecessary protected attributes, and unrelated histories. Use decision-required aggregate or pseudonymous evidence under declared purpose, access, retention, and cell-size rules.

Local analysis does not authorize system access, data exports, code creation, eligibility or price changes, rewards, credits, cash, blocks, customer contact, spend, experiments, programs, publication, messaging, or deployment. Record required product, engineering, data, finance, risk, legal, privacy, tax, support, approval, fulfillment, validation, rollback, monitoring, and audit controls as handoffs.
