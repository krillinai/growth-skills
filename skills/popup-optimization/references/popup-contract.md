# Popup Contract

## Intake

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, objective, horizon, priority, review date, and allowed action;
- site, app, product, page, route, entry source, page or product job, content state, and underlying primary action;
- visitor, user, account, lifecycle, customer, market, language, locale, device, viewport, browser, and assistive context;
- eligibility, exclusions, identity, session, assignment, permission, consent, opt-out, do-not-contact, suppression, prior exposure, dismissal, and completion;
- popup type, version, message class, offer, value, price, claim, proof, urgency, CTA, alternative, destination, and expiry;
- trigger, delay, scroll, exit or behavior condition, frequency, cooldown, page count, session count, cross-device rule, priority, collision, and fallback;
- form field, required and optional status, validation, autofill, error, privacy notice, consent, submission, duplicate, and recovery;
- dialog role, accessible name and description, focus entry, trap, return, keyboard, Escape, close, backdrop, screen reader, contrast, zoom, reflow, motion, touch, safe area, and orientation;
- render, load, layout, responsiveness, script, dependency, failure, SEO or platform review, and performance evidence;
- events, properties, source, identity, cohort, windows, outcomes, guardrails, experiment, and maturity;
- source artifact, owner, capture date, evidence state, counterevidence, limitation, and freshness.

Version when audience, purpose, offer, claim, surface, trigger, form, permission, interaction, frequency, destination, or measurement changes.

## Evidence Rules

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. A `reported signal` is an attributable statement without inspectable support and has no evidence state.

Rendered public inspection can verify visible copy, layout, interaction, keyboard behavior, mobile view, network requests, and observed performance at a capture. It cannot verify audience logic, consent, identity, frequency across devices, data destinations, experiment assignment, customer quality, or impact.

## Purpose Boundary

Separate marketing and promotional experiences from service, transaction, safety, age, region, authentication, privacy, consent, and legal requirements. An optional marketing action must not masquerade as required access or a system alert.

Do not bundle unrelated optional marketing consent into a necessary service action. Record exact message classification, purpose, data, recipient, channel, permission owner, withdrawal, and suppression behavior.
