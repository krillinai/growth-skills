# Sizing Methods

## Use A Traceable Chain

A common multiplicative form is:

```text
gross headroom = eligible population x bounded transition gap
reachable outcomes = gross headroom x reachable share x adoption or delivery
retained outcomes = reachable outcomes x repeated-value or survival factor
gross value = retained outcomes x compatible value per outcome
net decision value = gross value - incremental cost - recurring cost - expected bounded loss
```

This is a schema, not a license to invent factors. Show every factor's definition, evidence state, source, range, unit, maturity, and sensitivity. Use a state-transition, cohort, queue, capacity, or cash-flow model when multiplication would hide timing or dependencies.

## Select The Opportunity Type

| Type | Starting evidence | Required bridges |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Acquisition | Qualified reachable demand and source-to-value baseline | incrementality, activation, retention, marginal economics, saturation |
| Activation | Eligible entrants and first-value transition | cause, reach, adoption, downstream retained quality |
| Retention | Mature cohorts and repeated-value states | preventability, reachable intervention, survival, contribution, capacity |
| Monetization | Payer value, offer, package, price, and payment evidence | response, churn, migration, discount, tax, cost, cash timing |
| Expansion | Active customer value, usage, seats, teams, products, or signals | eligibility, realized value, adoption, commercial path, retention |
| Referral or loop | Closed value-producing transitions | participant eligibility, cycle time, quality, incrementality, saturation |
| Market or segment | Serviceable and reachable demand | fit, product and channel readiness, adoption, retained economics, capacity |
| System or capability | Repeated decisions, losses, incidents, delays, or constrained workflows | adoption, reliability, capacity unlocked, dependent decisions, total cost |
| Risk reduction | Attributable incidents or exposure | probability source when valid, consequence range, control effect, residual risk |

Do not use total addressable market as company opportunity. Route demand construction to `market-sizing`, then require explicit serviceability, reachability, qualification, adoption, retention, and economic bridges.

## Bound The Change

Use the strongest compatible basis available:

1. mature randomized or valid causal evidence for the same mechanism and population;
2. attributable internal variation with known selection limits;
3. compatible historical cohorts or operational limits;
4. credible external evidence with explicit transfer assumptions;
5. a transparent assumption used only as a conditional scenario.

Benchmarks establish comparison context, not causal or attainable uplift. Customer quotes establish attributed experiences, not prevalence. Competitor claims establish what was stated, not company performance. Founder or team conviction can nominate a hypothesis, not verify a factor.

## Model Time And Maturity

Record time to evidence, implementation or readiness lead time, customer adoption ramp, time to first and repeated value, cohort maturity, benefit duration, decay, saturation, renewal or churn, and cash timing where material. Do not combine a short-window conversion with a long-window retention rate without a compatible cohort bridge.

An opportunity range is not a forecast. When calendar timing, seasonality, drivers, revisions, or probability calibration drive the decision, hand the frozen opportunity contract to `growth-forecasting`.

## Reconcile Economics

Keep orders, GMV, revenue, bookings, recognized revenue, gross profit, contribution, cash, and valuation distinct. Include acquisition, incentive, discount, delivery, implementation, payment, refund, fraud, support, success, sales, partner, infrastructure, model inference, compliance, maintenance, and opportunity costs where relevant.

Keep average and marginal economics separate. A scale opportunity must account for saturation, mix, capacity, and changing marginal cost. A price change must account for customer response, migration, contraction, churn, discounting, collection, and fairness. Route detailed contracts to `unit-economics-analysis`, `ltv-analysis`, `monetization`, or `pricing-and-packaging-strategy`.

## Use Conditional Ranges

For each low, central, or high case record the named condition, assumptions, supporting and contradictory evidence, range, sensitivity, failure signal, and evidence trigger. Do not invent probabilities or average scenarios into a guaranteed outcome. Report the factors that dominate the decision and the value of resolving them.
