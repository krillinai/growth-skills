# Validation And Governance

## Match Evidence To Commitment

Require stronger evidence as cost, irreversibility, customer exposure, operational complexity, and downside rise. A reversible research or instrumentation step can proceed with less evidence than a price migration, market entry, large budget shift, organization change, or production deployment.

For each material uncertainty record:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Decision | What changes if the uncertainty is resolved |
| Alternatives | Competing mechanisms or explanations |
| Evidence | Smallest valid observation or test that distinguishes them |
| Metric contract | Entity, eligibility, event, denominator, window, maturity, source, and guardrail |
| Owner and date | Accountable person and decision cutoff |
| Rule | Advance, revise, cap, preserve, defer, or stop condition |

Use `experiment-design` for randomized or alternative causal designs, `tracking-plan` for event and identity implementation, and `cohort-analysis` for maturity-aware group comparisons. An opportunity estimate does not make a causal claim valid.

## Audit And Refresh

For an audit, reconstruct the original decision, as-of date, sources, definitions, assumptions, ranges, contradictions, owner, and action. Evaluate whether the process was sound using only information available then. Report later outcomes separately. Do not rewrite old assumptions with hindsight or count sunk cost as future value.

For a refresh, version what changed and why. Preserve unchanged factors with evidence, recalculate affected chains, reconcile portfolio overlap again, and update owners, expiry, validation, and review rules. Never overwrite the prior estimate.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat each market as a new evidence boundary. Re-establish customer and payer roles, eligibility, baseline, alternatives, product, channel, lifecycle, price, payment, invoice, currency, cost, contribution, support, capacity, trust, data, and local review. Translation, a local domain, or currency conversion does not validate transfer.

For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not provide legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, provider-availability, or performance conclusions.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence by default. Define purpose, authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, and small-cell suppression. Avoid raw identity and complete histories when aggregate cohorts or state transitions answer the decision.

Do not infer sensitive traits, reidentify people, exploit vulnerability, or rank individuals for consequential acquisition, pricing, employment, credit, insurance, health, housing, or similar outcomes. Escalate sensitive or consequential uses to accountable privacy, security, legal, and domain review.

## Govern External Actions

Opportunity analysis is not authorization. Create and read local artifacts only unless the user separately authorizes a bounded action. Do not access authenticated systems, export or join personal data, change budgets or prices, mutate product or records, launch experiments, contact people, publish, spend, or deploy.

For a proposed external action record owner, system, exact mutation, affected population, prerequisite evidence, approval, privacy and security review, budget, customer and operational guardrails, dry run, monitoring, stop, rollback, audit, and completion proof. Never claim an action or growth result occurred from a plan.
