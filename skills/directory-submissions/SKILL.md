# Directory Submissions

Create accurate, maintainable listings only where a qualified customer, partner, buyer, or ecosystem participant can discover or verify the organization. Optimize qualified discovery and trust, not submission count, backlinks, domain metrics, category count, profile completeness, review volume, or attributed traffic alone.

Read [listing-contract.md](references/listing-contract.md) before accepting entity, location, category, claim, review, URL, asset, ownership, or platform evidence. Read [selection-and-submission-pack.md](references/selection-and-submission-pack.md) before prioritizing directories or preparing fields. Read [governance-and-measurement.md](references/governance-and-measurement.md) before handling duplicates, verification, updates, reviews, paid placement, tracking, or retirement. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Freeze The Listing Contract

Record legal and public organization name; brand, product, location, practitioner, branch, service area, or partner entity; address, phone, URL, domain, contact handoff, hours, category, description, products, price, availability, market, language, locale, claims, proof, certifications, awards, customer names, reviews, images, logos, trademarks, rights, directory, account, ownership, eligibility, fields, limits, current direct requirements, verification, paid or sponsored status, canonical and tracking URLs, duplicate and merge rules, update owner, source, evidence state, and requested external action.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder or platform statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Do not invent or normalize away organization names, addresses, locations, service areas, hours, categories, prices, credentials, certifications, customers, reviews, ratings, awards, rankings, product capabilities, availability, ownership, eligibility, or performance.

## Select For Customer Value

Classify the directory's primary job:

- local discovery or navigation;
- software, product, app, or vendor evaluation;
- industry, profession, association, or accreditation verification;
- partner, integration, reseller, or marketplace discovery;
- customer review or reputation evidence;
- startup, launch, community, or editorial discovery;
- another bounded ecosystem role.

Evaluate actual audience and intent, customer fit, eligibility, category fit, trust, profile usefulness, verification, moderation, review integrity, source visibility, traffic or demand evidence, downstream path, maintenance, data and rights, paid dependence, duplicate risk, platform control, exit, and full effort. A backlink or high domain metric alone is not a directory strategy.

Choose `prepare`, `update`, `claim after authorization`, `merge`, `remove after authorization`, `monitor`, `defer`, `reject`, or `needs evidence`. Do not submit to irrelevant, deceptive, scraped, abandoned, low-quality, pay-to-rank, or rights-incompatible directories merely to increase count.

## Build Platform-Specific Packs

Use current direct requirements for each directory. Produce only fields supported by attributable organization sources and directory rules. Preserve one canonical fact registry while adapting category, length, format, locale, CTA, URL, image, and proof to the directory's customer job.

Include entity and market scope, field map, source per claim, character limits, prohibited content, canonical and tracking URL plan, image and logo requirements, rights, verification requirements, account owner, paid disclosure, review and approval, unresolved fields, submission handoff, expected confirmation evidence, update cadence, and rollback or correction path.

Do not keyword-stuff names or categories, create virtual locations, hide sponsorship, use fake identities, reuse unlicensed assets, fabricate reviews, solicit only positive reviews, review competitors, or promise rankings. Route final descriptions to `copywriting`, image preparation to `marketing-image` when available, structured data to `structured-data-builder`, local page issues to `landing-page-audit`, and app-store work to `aso-audit`.

## Govern Identity And Duplicates

Keep organization, brand, product, location, branch, practitioner, department, service area, and partner entities separate. Define authoritative source, stable ID, hierarchy, merge and split rules, relocation, closure, rebrand, acquisition, domain change, and unknown handling.

Do not claim, merge, delete, or alter listings without verified ownership and separate authorization. Preserve existing reviews, platform history, public identifiers, redirects, and customer access where applicable. Treat conflicting listings as a reconciliation problem, not permission to overwrite whichever record is inconvenient.

## Measure Beyond Listings And Links

Keep eligibility, prepared, submitted, accepted, verified, published, accurate, visible, engaged, qualified arrival, first value, retained value, review, support, cost, attributed, and incremental states separate. Define directory, listing version, entity, query or category, market, URL, identity, window, maturity, source, costs, and downstream event.

Referral traffic and platform-reported actions are operating evidence. A link does not establish ranking benefit; a review count does not establish trust; attributed leads or revenue do not establish incrementality. Do not use a universal directory count, authority threshold, traffic target, review target, or update cadence.

Do not scrape directories, discover personal contacts, create accounts, accept terms, claim ownership, verify by phone, email, mail, payment, or identity, submit or edit listings, merge or delete records, upload assets, post or solicit reviews, answer reviews, buy placement, change URLs or tracking, access analytics or CRM, contact platforms or customers, publish, or claim acceptance, verification, ranking, traffic, leads, or results.
