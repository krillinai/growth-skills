# Directory Governance And Measurement

## Listing States

Keep distinct:

```text
candidate -> eligible -> prepared -> approved -> submitted
-> accepted -> verification pending -> verified -> published
-> updated, disputed, duplicate, suspended, rejected, removed, or retired
```

This Skill may support candidate through approved local pack. Later states require external evidence and authorization.

## Duplicate And Ownership Reconciliation

For every conflicting record capture directory ID, URL, entity, displayed facts, claimed owner, verification status, review history, paid status, source, date, conflict, customer impact, desired disposition, platform process, authorized owner, and completion evidence.

Do not overwrite, merge, move, close, or delete. Preserve unknowns and platform history. Escalate identity, legal, franchise, practitioner, relocation, acquisition, or malicious-listing disputes to accountable owners.

## Portfolio Governance

Maintain one registry for directories, listings, entity IDs, URLs, owners, access state, versions, sources, claims, assets, rights, verification, payment, review policy, duplicates, status, updates, incidents, performance, and disposition.

Review on source or entity change, platform notice, ownership change, relocation, rebrand, product or price change, rights expiry, broken destination, duplicate, suspension, customer complaint, or material performance change. Do not use one universal refresh cadence.

## Measurement

Define listing entity, directory, version, market, category, query where available, source, URL, eligible exposure, view, action, qualified arrival, product or contact entry, first value, retained value, cost, review, complaint, cohort, window, maturity, attribution, and counterfactual.

Report counts beside rates. Preserve zero, unknown, unavailable, duplicate, bot, employee, existing-customer, paid, and organic states. Platform visibility, profile completeness, reviews, domain metrics, backlinks, referral traffic, and attributed conversions are diagnostic or operating evidence, not retained incremental value.

## Incident And Retirement

Define owner, affected entity, platform, status, customer risk, evidence, correction path, public and support communication, paid state, access, recovery, review preservation, redirects, data, archive, and completion proof. A directory should be retired when customer value, accuracy, rights, ownership, trust, maintenance, cost, or platform conditions no longer justify it.
