# Directory Listing Contract

## Authoritative Entity Record

Capture:

- legal name, public organization name, brand, product, location, branch, practitioner, department, service area, and stable internal ID;
- parent, child, ownership, franchise, partner, relocation, closure, rebrand, acquisition, merge, split, and domain relationships;
- street or service-area status, address components, phone, hours, timezone, URL, domain, contact handoff, and accessibility information;
- market, language, locale, category, subcategory, audience, customer job, product, service, availability, price or price range, and CTA;
- short and long descriptions, claims, proof, credentials, certifications, awards, customer references, reviews, and limitations;
- logo, image, video, screenshot, trademark, copyright, model, property, customer, and derivative rights;
- source artifact, owner, effective date, evidence state, approval, review date, and prohibited use.

Do not force all entities into one name-address-phone record. A service-area business, software product, professional, branch, and marketplace integration have different identities.

## Directory Record

Capture directory name, direct official URL, type, owner, customer audience, eligibility, category taxonomy, required and optional fields, length and format limits, URL rules, asset requirements, verification, account and role, paid status, review rules, moderation, data use, update path, duplicate policy, termination, source dates, and limitations.

Use current direct directory documentation for changing requirements. A third-party article or old template can guide discovery but cannot verify current fields, eligibility, pricing, review policy, verification, or account access.

## Evidence Rules

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. Use `reported signal` only for an attributable statement without inspectable support and leave evidence state blank.

A visible listing verifies public content at capture time. It does not verify current ownership, authority, customer identity, accuracy, review authenticity, paid status, platform traffic, ranking, leads, or downstream value.

## Claim And Review Boundaries

Every claim requires exact wording, source, product and market scope, date, proof, limitation, owner, approval, expiry, and prohibited stronger wording.

Never create customer reviews or ratings, compensate without permitted disclosure, gate by sentiment, suppress negative customers, review the organization or a competitor through connected identities, or copy reviews between platforms. A review platform's public text does not grant reuse rights.
