# Partnership Measurement And Governance

## Measurement Layers

Keep separate:

- contribution delivered by each party;
- qualified audience or account eligibility;
- actual exposure, delivery, and customer permission;
- attributed response, lead, opportunity, order, or revenue;
- jointly reconciled customer first and recurring value;
- retention, contribution, support, complaints, and partner quality;
- incremental effect versus a credible counterfactual;
- strategic learning, dependency, concentration, and option value.

Define entity, identity, party, source, precedence, overlap, cohort, window, maturity, currency, cost, product, market, version, and owner. Never add partner, platform, marketing, sales, and finance attribution views.

## Full Economics

Include marginal production, event, media, affiliate, commission, channel, discount, incentive, revenue share, integration, infrastructure, implementation, training, sales, partner management, support, fraud, refunds, disputes, privacy, security, legal and policy operation, cannibalization, opportunity, termination, migration, and customer-protection costs.

Use retained incremental contribution when the decision requires causality. Average partnership ROI cannot justify the next partner, segment, market, or spend band.

## Portfolio Dispositions

Assign one: `validate`, `pilot`, `continue`, `scale bounded scope`, `renegotiate`, `pause`, `exit`, or `needs evidence`. Base it on shared-customer value, contribution delivery, quality, economics, trust, capacity, concentration, conflict, compliance review, and reversibility.

Do not keep a partnership because of executive sponsorship, sunk integration cost, announcement visibility, or fear of partner reaction.

## Governance Cadence

Define operational, customer, commercial, data and rights, performance, incident, renewal, and portfolio reviews. Each produces a decision, owner, evidence, due date, and follow-up.

Maintain agreement and term versions, contribution records, approvals, rights registry, data map, lead and account disputes, customer incidents, commercial reconciliation, source and metric definitions, exceptions, changes, and exit artifacts.

## Exit

Specify customer ownership and communication, active deal and service handoff, contracts, prepaid value, refunds, leads and suppression, data return and deletion, accounts, assets and trademarks, integration deprecation, support tail, billing and settlement, dispute, confidentiality, audit, and learning capture. A partnership ends operationally only when customer and data obligations are resolved.
