# Research And Evidence

Choose the smallest evidence program that can change the pricing decision. More responses do not repair the wrong population, role, offer, stimulus, method, or market.

## Research Question Contract

Freeze decision, owner, customer and payer roles, segment, job, alternative, product, package, price metric, price range, currency, market, source cutoff, current evidence, competing hypotheses, threshold, research method, privacy boundary, and action not authorized.

Separate questions about:

- problem urgency, customer outcome, alternatives, budgets, and switching;
- capability and package preference;
- price comprehension, fairness, predictability, and control;
- stated willingness, purchase likelihood, observed purchase, renewal, and retained use;
- price metric, billing, commitment, discount, procurement, payment, and risk;
- contribution, capacity, cash, and operational feasibility.

## Evidence Sources

| Source | Useful for | Cannot establish alone |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer or buyer interviews | Language, value mechanism, alternatives, budget process, objections, and risk | Market prevalence, exact willingness, elasticity, or causal conversion |
| Win/loss, sales, support, and success | Buying criteria, objections, discount and implementation patterns | Representative demand without coverage and selection review |
| Purchase, quote, invoice, and contract history | Realized terms and observed buying behavior | Counterfactual demand or unobserved lost willingness |
| Usage and outcome evidence | Value realization, frequency, scale, and service requirements | Payer willingness or price response without a bridge |
| Competitor pages | Visible dated alternatives, packages, metrics, and prices | Competitor success, realization, margins, conversion, or customer value |
| Offer and market tests | Behavioral response under a defined exposure | Long-term retention, economics, or general transfer before maturity |

Preserve non-users, prospects, current customers, payers, lost deals, churned customers, discount seekers, excluded customers, and unavailable populations where decision relevant. Record recruitment, relationship, incentive, nonresponse, survivorship, free-access, and social-desirability bias.

## Interview And Qualitative Work

Ask about actual jobs, alternatives, purchase decisions, budgets, trade-offs, implementation, outcomes, renewals, and rejected options before asking abstract price questions. Preserve verbatim evidence, source role, market, product and offer context, date, consent, contradictions, and selection limits.

Do not infer the whole market from a small sample, strengthen unsupported claims, or contact and recruit participants without authorization. Route interview operations and synthesis beyond supplied artifacts to `customer-research`.

## Gabor-Granger

Define respondent eligibility, product and package stimulus, price levels, currency, order or randomization, purchase context, question wording, response scale, sample and weighting, exclusions, field dates, and analysis before estimating a stated demand curve.

Do not extrapolate beyond tested prices without an explicit model and uncertainty. Stated purchase likelihood is not observed demand. Report response counts, distributions, intervals or scenario uncertainty, sensitivity to order and framing, and reconciliation with observed purchase and retention.

## Van Westendorp

Define the four questions, respondent and role, product and package, currency, market, price range and anchors, sample, exclusions, field dates, weighting, and analysis. Inspect monotonicity, inconsistent responses, sparse regions, segment mix, and confidence or resampling uncertainty where appropriate.

Intersections describe stated price perceptions under the instrument. They are not automatically an optimal price, demand curve, revenue maximum, or forecast. Use them to refine a range and follow-up evidence.

## Conjoint Or Discrete Choice

Define decision, respondent, choice context, alternatives, attributes, levels, price levels, prohibitions, task design, randomization, sample, weighting, quality checks, model, holdouts, uncertainty, segment exploration, and external validity. Avoid impossible products, dominant tasks, excessive cognitive load, and post-hoc segment stories.

Part-worth utilities and importance are model outputs, not direct willingness or observed market share. Translate them only through a declared model and tested range. Validate holdout choice, parameter stability, segment replication, and behavior where available.

## MaxDiff And Package Trade-Offs

Use MaxDiff to rank relative importance when eligible respondents can make repeated best-worst choices among well-defined items. Define item wording, task design, randomization, prohibitions, sample, quality checks, model, holdouts, uncertainty, and segment replication. It does not estimate an absolute price or willingness to pay unless combined with an identified price model and compatible design.

Avoid treating security, privacy, accessibility, portability, customer control, or another essential protection as an ordinary tradeable feature without ethical and customer-protection review.

## Competitors And Real Alternatives

Capture competitor and alternative product, package, metric, price, currency, billing, contract, market, availability, source, and date. Include manual workflows, internal build, doing nothing, and budget substitutes when customers actually consider them.

Visible prices cannot establish competitor conversion, realized discounts, retention, margins, costs, customer value, or success. Use dated alternative evidence to understand reference points and category expectations, not to copy architecture.

## Experiments And Causal Evidence

For a price, package, metric, or term experiment define eligible population, ethical and fairness review, assignment, exposure, identity, interference, sales overrides, product and offer versions, primary outcome, activation, retained value, churn, refund, support, contribution, cash, trust guardrails, maturity, power, analysis, decision, rollback, and correction.

Observational conversion, before-after change, sales exceptions, and platform attribution do not establish causal effect. Route counterfactual design and interpretation to `experiment-design`.

## Synthesis

Return supporting evidence, counterevidence, contradictions, unavailable populations, method limits, and the smallest next evidence. Keep stated, observed, attributed, incremental, forecast, target, and benchmark claims separate. Do not average unlike methods into a confidence score.
