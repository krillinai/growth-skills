# Output Contract

Return Markdown unless the user requests another local format. Keep public facts, supplied evidence, reported signals, stated preference, observed behavior, inferences, attributed outputs, incremental effects, forecasts, scenarios, targets, benchmarks, unavailable inputs, causal claims, and approvals separate.

## 1. Decision And Pricing Header

State mode, decision, owner, customer and payer entities, segment, job, outcome, product and version, current offer, market, currency, source cutoff, horizon, review date, non-goals, and external-action boundary.

## 2. Contract And Evidence Ledger

| ID | Claim or value | Participant and segment | Product, offer, market, date | Source and lineage | State | Coverage, uncertainty, limitation, next check |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Include participant roles, buying unit, alternatives, value and proof, commercial terms, contradictions, inaccessible evidence, privacy, and source freshness.

## 3. Research Specification Or Synthesis

Return decision question, competing hypotheses, method, population, roles, sample, recruitment, incentive, stimulus, package, price range, currency, market, questions or tasks, order and randomization, field dates, quality, analysis, uncertainty, privacy, limitations, behavior reconciliation, and smallest next evidence.

For supplied research, preserve raw counts and distributions where available; show sample and method limitations beside findings. Do not return an exact optimum or forecast unless the method and evidence support it.

| Package or path | Eligible customer and job | Core value | Entitlements and limits | Service and controls | Add-ons | Upgrade and downgrade | Exclusions and evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Show current and proposed versions, package count rationale, provisioning and support feasibility, essential trust constraints, and retirement dependencies.

## 5. Price-Metric Comparison

| Candidate | Customer and payer value | Measurement and audit | Predictability and control | Cost and capacity | Expansion and incentives | Fairness and trust | Operability | Evidence and counterevidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Do not collapse trade-offs into an unsupported score. State which candidates are excluded and why.

## 6. Commercial Architecture And Scenarios

For each candidate return price or bounded range, currency, billing, commitment, included use, overage, minimum, maximum, trial or free rules, discounts, credits, renewal, cancellation, refund, tax, invoice, payment, service, implementation, realized-price bridge, compatible unit economics, uncertainty, and strongest counterevidence.

## 7. Validation And Migration

Return eligible and excluded states, current-to-target mapping, contracts and grandfathering, notice and applicable review, entitlements and metering, billing and support readiness, cohort and exposure, activation and retained-value measures, economic and trust guardrails, owner, decision and stop rules, staged rollout, correction, rollback, refresh, and retirement.

## 8. Governance And Completion

List market-transfer gaps, privacy and fairness controls, specialist handoffs, source and design expiry, approval-ready external actions, and completion evidence. Confirm pinned Playbook sources, allowed evidence states, no fabricated willingness, price, precision, causality, legal conclusion, or result, and no unauthorized access, research contact, commercial change, experiment, publication, sending, spending, or deployment.
