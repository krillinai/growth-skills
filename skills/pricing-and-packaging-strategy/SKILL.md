# Pricing And Packaging Strategy

Connect a defined customer and buying situation to delivered value, participant roles, alternatives, an offer, coherent packages, an operable price metric, price and terms, retained economics, and a governed migration. Treat every price recommendation as a versioned hypothesis supported by attributable evidence, not a fact discovered from competitors, one survey, or a target model.

Read [pricing-contract.md](references/pricing-contract.md) before accepting segments, participants, offers, packages, prices, or research. Read [research-and-evidence.md](references/research-and-evidence.md) before interpreting interviews, sales evidence, purchase behavior, Van Westendorp, Gabor-Granger, conjoint, discrete-choice, MaxDiff, competitor prices, or market tests. Read [packages-and-price-metrics.md](references/packages-and-price-metrics.md) before designing offers, tiers, entitlements, limits, add-ons, free access, trials, metrics, prices, terms, discounts, or AI and marketplace pricing. Read [architecture-migration-and-governance.md](references/architecture-migration-and-governance.md) before setting price levels and scenarios, governing discounts, migrating customers, validating prices, handling fairness or privacy, transferring a strategy across markets, or preparing external actions. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) for the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Inspect an existing offer, package, entitlement, limit, metric, price, term, discount, research claim, migration, or outcome |
| `design` | Build an evidence-bounded package and pricing architecture for defined segments, participant roles, and value mechanisms |
| `migration` | Plan a governed commercial transition with eligibility, contracts, communication handoffs, measurement, stop, rollback, and retirement |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, customer job, segment and exclusions, user, beneficiary, champion, administrator, buyer, approver, and payer, market, product and offer version, current and proposed package or price state, research cutoff, rollout or analysis cohort, decision date, review date, and external-action boundary. Public pages can verify visible products, offers, prices, currencies, terms, and claims at a dated capture. They cannot establish private willingness to pay, elasticity, discounts, conversion, retention, margin, contribution, cash, causal effects, or commercial success.

## Freeze The Pricing Contract

Record customer job, segment, buying situation, alternatives, switching cost, budget and procurement context; participant roles, delivered and perceived value, evidence and proof; offer, package, entitlements, limits, add-ons, service level, rights and support; price metric, unit, included quantity, minimum, maximum, overage, price, currency, billing frequency, commitment, renewal, discount, credit, tax, invoice, payment, refund, cancellation, and exception rules; free, trial, promotional, self-serve, assisted, sales-led, partner, and marketplace paths; eligibility, exposure, identity, cohort, product, plan, price, contract, market, language, locale, channel, platform, and version; activation, paid conversion, realized price, retention, expansion, contraction, churn, refund, margin, contribution, cash, payback, and maturity; research question, sample, source, method, design, raw evidence, limitations, uncertainty, owner, privacy, fairness, migration, approvals, and requested actions.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. An attributable unsupported statement may be a `reported signal` without an evidence state. Keep stated preference, observed behavior, estimate, forecast, scenario, target, benchmark, causal estimate, approval, zero, missing, late, censored, suppressed, and not-applicable separate.

## Map Participants, Value, And Segments

Keep beneficiary, user, champion, administrator, buyer, approver, procurement, legal, security, finance, and payer distinct. Define who receives value, experiences risk, controls budget, signs, pays, implements, supports, expands, renews, and can veto. Willingness to pay is not ability, authority, budget, procurement readiness, or trust.

Use segments only when observable differences change value, alternatives, package needs, metric, terms, route, service, economics, or migration. Preserve exclusions and overlap. Do not average consumers, startups, enterprises, public-sector buyers, and marketplace sides into one persona or create a package for every anecdote.

Route segment discovery to `icp-segmentation`, primary customer evidence to `customer-research`, and category and alternative framing to `positioning`.

## Build An Evidence Program

Begin with the decision, not a preferred survey. Reconcile:

- current purchase, realized price, discount, renewal, expansion, contraction, churn, refund, support, and win-loss evidence;
- qualitative jobs, alternatives, budget, value, risk, procurement, and trade-off evidence;
- stated price perception or choice research with its sampling and design limitations;
- bounded market or causal tests with exposure, maturity, fairness, and retained-value guardrails;
- current competitor offers and prices as dated alternative evidence, never proof of their economics or fit.

Do not invent raw responses, utilities, elasticity, demand curves, or confidence. A small or biased survey cannot produce an exact optimal price or revenue forecast. Preserve sample frame, recruitment, incentives, participant role, segment, product and package context, price and currency levels, response quality, weighting, exclusions, uncertainty, counterevidence, and transfer limits.

## Design Package Architecture

Start with core value and the buying situation. Define a coherent base offer, segment-relevant differentiators, proof before upgrade, entitlements, limits, service levels, add-ons, upgrade and downgrade triggers, expansion path, portability, security, privacy, accessibility, support, and exit. Use the fewest packages that make customer and operating decisions clearer.

Do not tier by arbitrary feature count, hide essential trust or accessibility behind a premium tier without justification, use deceptive decoys, or maximize package count. Show why a capability belongs in the core, a tier, an add-on, usage, service, or a separate product and how billing, provisioning, sales, support, analytics, and governance can operate it.

For free and trial access, define the customer value path, eligibility, duration or usage boundary, activation, first and repeated value, qualified conversion opportunity, paywall, abuse, service cost, collaboration or referral value, assisted path, retained payer outcome, and migration. Do not optimize first-week conversion alone.

## Select A Price Metric And Structure

Evaluate seat, account, workspace, usage, transaction, GMV, take rate, outcome, license, subscription, service, advertising, and hybrid metrics against:

- correlation with customer and payer value;
- measurability, auditability, predictability, explainability, and dispute resolution;
- natural expansion, customer control, budgetability, caps, alerts, and safeguards;
- cost alignment, margin, capacity, saturation, and downside exposure;
- incentives, gaming, fairness, trust, privacy, and unintended behavior;
- metering, billing, sales, partner, tax, invoice, support, and operational feasibility.

Then define metric units, included amount, floors, ceilings, bands, overage, commitment, billing cadence, contract, renewal, discount, and exception policy. Price level must remain a hypothesis bounded by evidence, economics, alternatives, and decision risk.

For AI products, define successful task or workflow value and quality before using tokens, model calls, compute, or outcomes. Include inference, tool, retry, failure, human-review, latency, support, safety, privacy, vendor, and budget-predictability costs. For marketplaces, keep buyer, seller, participant, transaction, GMV, platform revenue, incentive, refund, contribution, liquidity, and multi-homing separate.

## Validate Retained Economics

Keep list price, contracted or booked value, billings, cash, realized revenue, gross profit, contribution, and LTV separate. Reconcile discounts, credits, free periods, services, commissions, channel shares, refunds, taxes, payment losses, delivery, support, success, implementation, and other costs.

Read exposure, activation, paid conversion, realized price, retained use, renewal, expansion, contraction, churn, refunds, support, trust, gross profit, contribution, cash, payback, and customer outcomes together. Short-term conversion does not validate a package or price. Route detailed unit economics to `unit-economics-analysis`, LTV to `ltv-analysis`, retained-value mechanisms to `retention`, and overall commercial-system diagnosis to `monetization`.

Do not backsolve price, conversion, retention, margin, or LTV to hit a target. Separate actual, estimate, forecast, scenario, target, plan, and benchmark. Record benchmark source, population, definition, date, and transfer limits rather than enforcing universal ratios or payback periods.

## Plan Migration And Experiments

For migrations, version current and target offers, packages, entitlements, metrics, prices, terms, discounts, contracts, cohorts, eligibility, grandfathering, notice, consent, renewal, partner, sales, support, billing, invoice, tax, payment, app-store, data, and communication states. Define staged exposure, customer protection, measurement, stop, fallback, rollback, correction, support, dispute, refresh, and retirement.

For validation, define decision, hypothesis, eligible unit, assignment or alternative evidence, exposure, identity, interference, sales overrides, sample, power or feasibility, metric contracts, maturity, retained-value and economic outcomes, guardrails, fairness, customer harm, decision rule, owner, and expiry. Do not secretly discriminate, target vulnerability, use sensitive traits, call observational changes causal, or auto-roll out a winning price.

Route causal design and readout to `experiment-design`, pricing-page copy to `copywriting`, seller and buyer assets to `sales-enablement`, lifecycle communication to `lifecycle-marketing`, and product launch sequencing to `product-launch-strategy`.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, segment, participant roles, market, current and proposed states, cutoff, versions, review date, and action boundary;
2. pricing contract, value and participant map, alternatives, sources, evidence ledger, contradictions, privacy, and unavailable evidence;
3. research question, method, sample, design, analysis limits, findings, uncertainty, counterevidence, and next evidence;
4. offer and package architecture with core value, entitlements, limits, add-ons, service, upgrades, downgrades, exclusions, and operating implications;
5. price-metric comparison, proposed structure, units, bands, included use, overage, terms, discounts, exceptions, and alternatives;
6. realized-price, conversion, retention, expansion, churn, refund, contribution, cash, payback, LTV handoff, and customer guardrails;
7. migration or validation plan with cohorts, exposure, approvals, communication handoffs, measurement, stop, rollback, correction, refresh, and retirement;
8. specialist handoffs, pinned Playbook sources, approval-ready external actions, and completion record.

For mainland China work, keep market, language, locale, legal entity, customer and payer, product, offer, package, price, currency, channel, platform, app distribution, contract, payment, invoice, tax, refund, procurement, identity, consent, data, storage, transfer, research, costs, source access, migration, review, and local owner separate. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transferred pricing. Use current direct sources and locally accountable review for time-sensitive conditions; do not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, or provider conclusions.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access analytics, CRM, billing, payment, finance, app-store, sales, support, product, experiment, BI, partner, or cloud systems; export, join, or reidentify people; change offers, packages, entitlements, limits, meters, prices, contracts, discounts, credits, taxes, invoices, payments, refunds, routing, product, or customer records; launch experiments; rank or price individuals; contact anyone; publish; send; spend; deploy; or claim pricing or business improvement without separate task-level authorization and required controls.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that decision, owner, customer job, segment, participant roles, alternatives, value, proof, offer, package, entitlements, limits, add-ons, service levels, metric, unit, price, currency, terms, discounts, exceptions, free and trial paths, eligibility, exposure, identity, cohort, market, research question, method, sample, raw evidence, uncertainty, activation, paid conversion, realized price, retention, expansion, contraction, churn, refunds, contribution, cash, payback, migration, experiment, fairness, privacy, market transfer, handoffs, pinned sources, and external actions are explicit; user enthusiasm did not become payer willingness; biased research did not become a forecast; competitor price did not become strategy; feature count did not become value; tokens or activity did not become outcomes; list price did not become realized value; short-term conversion did not become retained economics; targets did not become evidence; unsupported precision, causality, recommendations, approvals, and results were not invented; and no external action occurred.
