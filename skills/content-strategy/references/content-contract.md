# Content Strategy Contract

## Intake

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, strategy horizon, review date, and permitted action;
- product, business model, customer, segment, role, job, trigger, intent, lifecycle or buying state, and exclusions;
- market, language, locale, product surface, channel, platform, account access, and permission;
- positioning, category, alternatives, value, promise, offer, CTA, claims, proof, tradeoffs, and prohibited claims;
- first value, repeated value, sales or service path, customer and business outcomes, and guardrails;
- current asset inventory, content roles, formats, channels, performance evidence, reuse, freshness, and ownership;
- customer research, search, sales, support, community, product, competitive, and public question sources;
- source ID, exact language, date, population, evidence state, prevalence boundary, counterevidence, and privacy;
- production capacity, skills, budget assumptions, tools, rights, brand, accessibility, review, and approval;
- metric, entity, taxonomy, identity, attribution, experiment, window, maturity, cost, and decision rule.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing records. An unattributed or uninspectable stakeholder statement may use `reported signal` with no evidence state.

## Audience Question Record

Record stable ID, customer unit, role, situation, exact question or bounded paraphrase, source, source type, date, market, language, locale, intent, alternatives, decision stage, product relevance, content role, evidence state, observed frequency only when a denominator exists, counterevidence, privacy, and review owner.

Never invent quotes or turn several anecdotes into market prevalence. A search query, sales objection, support issue, social comment, and product behavior are different evidence types.

## Claim And Proof Record

For each proposed claim record exact wording, product and market scope, content role, source, date, proof type, conditions, limitation, counterevidence, owner, approval, expiration, and prohibited stronger wording. A customer story, demo, benchmark, product record, and causal outcome support different claims.

## Content Brief

Every brief includes decision, audience and state, question, content role, intended change, thesis, outline, claims and proof, objections, format, channel, native constraints, CTA, product path, sources, rights, brand, accessibility, market and locale, owner, reviewers, due date, distribution, reuse lineage, metric, maturity, guardrails, update trigger, and completion proof.
