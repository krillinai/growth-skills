# Portfolio And Operations

## Pillar Contract

A pillar needs a durable customer problem or decision, a defensible point of view, attributable questions, product relevance, proof, enough useful subtopics, multiple suitable roles or formats, a distribution path, an owner, and an update reason. Define what the pillar excludes to prevent thematic sprawl.

Do not use a vague topic such as innovation, growth, AI, or productivity as a pillar until the scoped audience, problem, alternatives, and editorial value are explicit.

## Portfolio Inventory

For each existing or proposed asset record ID, title, URL or local artifact, audience, question, role, stage, pillar, format, channel, claim, proof, CTA, product path, source date, publish date, last review, performance evidence, downstream evidence, rights, accessibility, owner, cost, status, and duplicates or lineage.

Assign one disposition:

- `keep`: still accurate, useful, distinct, accessible, and connected to a live path;
- `update`: useful intent remains but facts, proof, format, accessibility, or path need repair;
- `consolidate`: overlapping assets can become one stronger source without losing distinct intent;
- `repurpose`: the thesis and proof support a new native format or audience moment;
- `localize`: a declared market needs validated alternatives, terminology, proof, channel, and product adaptation;
- `retire`: value, accuracy, rights, product path, or maintenance no longer justifies the asset;
- `needs evidence`: a decision cannot be made from current artifacts.

## Portfolio Priority

Prioritize customer importance, role fit, evidence strength, product continuity, distribution access, downstream value, freshness risk, reuse leverage, effort, rights, and strategic gaps. Do not use one composite score unless components, weights, sensitivity, and missingness are visible. Prefer a short rationale and dependency order.

## Workflow

```text
question intake -> research -> brief -> source and claim review
-> draft or production -> brand, rights, accessibility, and market review
-> approval -> authorized publication and distribution handoff
-> outcome capture -> update, reuse, or retirement
```

Define owner, input, output, status, service expectation, approval, exception, and audit record for every transition. Fast-track work still needs source, claim, rights, accessibility, and publication ownership.

## Calendar And Capacity

Allocate capacity across new pillar work, proven formats, experiments, product or market changes, updates, localization, distribution, customer response, and retirement. Use dates to coordinate dependencies, not to manufacture output quotas.

## Repurposing Lineage

Record source asset, thesis, claims, proof, rights, audience, role, target format, channel, changes, omitted nuance, new context, reviewer, publication status, and outcome. A derivative cannot strengthen the source claim or reuse rights beyond their scope.
