# Content Strategy

Turn customer questions, positioning, product truth, and distribution evidence into a coherent content portfolio and operating system. Content must perform a declared role and lead to a useful customer or product outcome; publishing volume, followers, impressions, mentions, rankings, and asset count are not success by themselves.

Read [content-contract.md](references/content-contract.md) before selecting audiences, roles, topics, claims, formats, or channels. Read [portfolio-and-operations.md](references/portfolio-and-operations.md) before building pillars, briefs, calendars, reuse, review, refresh, or retirement. Read [measurement-and-governance.md](references/measurement-and-governance.md) before defining metrics, attribution, experiments, rights, privacy, or market adaptation. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `strategy` | Define audience questions, content roles, pillars, proof, formats, channels, journeys, and decision rules |
| `portfolio` | Audit current assets and decide keep, update, consolidate, repurpose, localize, retire, or needs evidence |
| `operations` | Build briefs, taxonomy, workflow, calendar, owners, reviews, distribution, refresh, measurement, and learning cadence |

Name one primary mode and one decision the content system must change. Keep research evidence, strategy choices, creative briefs, produced assets, distribution, observed results, and causal conclusions separate.

## Freeze The Content Contract

Record decision and owner; product and business model; audience, job, trigger, intent, lifecycle or buying state, market, language, locale, and exclusions; positioning, alternatives, value, offer, claims, proof, and prohibited claims; content role; channels and permissions; entry surface and next value; formats and constraints; current inventory; sources and rights; brand and accessibility; production capacity; review and approval; measurement; date, horizon, version, and refresh triggers.

Public artifacts can support visible product facts, current content inventory, public customer questions, search or social context, and channel observations. They cannot establish private audience size, permission, conversion, sales influence, retention, contribution, or incrementality. Mark missing private evidence `unavailable` rather than blocking useful strategy work.

## Assign A Content Role

Every content item has one primary role:

| Role | Decision supported |
| --- | --- |
| `category education` | Help the audience understand a problem, change, alternative, or evaluation frame |
| `demand capture` | Satisfy an existing specific intent and lead to a qualified next action |
| `product proof` | Demonstrate a capability, workflow, outcome, tradeoff, or trust condition |
| `customer enablement` | Help a customer reach first or repeated value |
| `sales enablement` | Help a buyer, champion, or reviewer evaluate and progress a decision |
| `public participation` | Contribute useful expertise to a community, conversation, or ecosystem |

Secondary roles may exist, but do not optimize one asset for every stage and channel. A campaign is bounded and coordinated around an objective; content strategy governs reusable knowledge and assets across time.

## Build From Questions And Evidence

Create atomic audience-question records with customer unit, situation, exact language, source, date, frequency or prevalence boundary, intent, alternatives, decision stage, evidence state, and counterevidence. Group into pillars only when a shared customer problem, product truth, and editorial point of view justify a durable system.

For each pillar define audience, problem, questions, point of view, value and proof, content roles, formats, channel jobs, product path, exclusions, claim boundaries, evidence gaps, and update triggers. Do not turn keyword lists, broad themes, product features, or executive preferences into pillars without customer and decision logic.

## Design Portfolio And Distribution

Map each planned asset from question to content role, audience and state, claim and proof, format, channel, native adaptation, source, CTA, entry surface, first value, reuse lineage, owner, due date, review, accessibility, rights, measurement, and refresh date.

Choose format by the audience's task and evidence: explanation, comparison, demonstration, template, case, tool, checklist, report, webinar, short video, long video, audio, social post, email, sales asset, or product education. Choose channel by customer access, native behavior, format fit, permissions, control, shelf life, dependency, economics, market conditions, and downstream path, not by a generic best-channel list.

Repurposing preserves the source thesis, claims, proof, rights, and lineage while adapting structure and emphasis to a new audience moment or native format. Cropping one asset into many sizes is distribution work, not a new content insight.

## Operate A Learning System

Define intake, research, brief, source verification, drafting, design or production, claim review, brand review, accessibility review, localization, rights, approval, publication handoff, distribution, moderation, measurement, update, and retirement. Keep owners and service expectations explicit.

Use an editorial calendar only after portfolio priorities and capacity are clear. Reserve capacity for updates, customer questions, product changes, distribution, experiments, and retirement. Do not fill dates with low-value assets merely to maintain cadence.

## Measure Downstream Value

Match measures to role. Track qualified reach and consumption, comprehension, return, assisted discovery, qualified response, product entry, first value, retained value, sales progression, support deflection, trust, contribution, and cost where relevant. Define entity, eligibility, source, identity, window, maturity, attribution, and guardrails.

Attribution can support content operations; causal incrementality requires a credible counterfactual. Do not add platform-reported influence, search attribution, sales self-report, and last click together as company growth. Use content cohorts and role-specific maturity rather than one universal ROI formula.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, horizon, audience, market, and external-action boundary;
2. evidence, customer-question, claim, proof, rights, and inventory ledger;
3. content roles, positioning continuity, pillars, and exclusions;
4. audience-question-topic-format-channel-journey map;
5. portfolio decisions or prioritized content briefs;
6. native distribution, reuse, localization, and lifecycle plan;
7. workflow, taxonomy, owners, capacity, calendar, approvals, refresh, and retirement;
8. role-specific measurement, attribution, experiment, guardrail, and learning plan;
9. handoffs and pinned Playbook sources.

Route research to `customer-research`, positioning to `positioning`, final prose to `copywriting`, revisions to `copy-editing`, images or video to the relevant creation capability, campaign-specific coordination to `campaign-planning`, search page systems to `programmatic-seo`, sales assets to `sales-enablement` when available, and causal tests to `experiment-design`.

For China work, keep market, language, locale, audience, content role, channel, platform, account, product surface, app distribution, payment, identity, consent, publishing rights, content review, data access, and applicable rules separate. Do not infer WeChat, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Kuaishou, Bilibili, Baidu, Zhihu, channel access, publishing permission, or audience behavior from market or language.

This Skill creates local strategy and planning artifacts only. Do not scrape restricted communities, access private accounts or analytics, contact customers or creators, commission work, acquire rights, publish, schedule, send, moderate, spend, change product or website content, or claim results without separate task-level authorization.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that the decision, audience, job, state, market, positioning, role, questions, pillars, claims, proof, formats, channels, native adaptation, product path, sources, rights, brand, accessibility, owners, capacity, workflow, approvals, measurement, maturity, attribution, guardrails, refresh, retirement, and external boundary are explicit; volume is not the objective; customer language is not fabricated; channel assumptions are not inferred from market; claims do not exceed evidence; and no external action occurred.
