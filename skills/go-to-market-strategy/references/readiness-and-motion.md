# Readiness And Motion

Readiness applies to one GTM unit, decision, period, and evidence cutoff. A strong downstream plan cannot repair an upstream customer, product, or delivery blocker.

## Six Readiness Gates

| Gate | Required evidence | Common false positive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer and problem | Bounded customer and buying unit, trigger, job, alternatives, urgency, value evidence, exclusions | TAM, traffic, waitlist, broad interest |
| Position and offer | Differentiated value, proof, role-specific narrative, package, price, terms, trust, objections | Category slogans, feature count, competitor copying |
| Product and value | Availability, prerequisites, reliability, onboarding, first and repeated value, retention, failure recovery | Feature shipped, account enabled, demo applause |
| Motion and routes | Buying complexity, intent, route fit, sales path, partner role, destination, overlap, saturation | Channel reach, cheap clicks, large pipeline |
| Delivery and operations | Implementation, support, success, billing, fulfillment, incidents, capacity, owners, rollback | Sales target, executive approval, temporary heroics |
| Measurement and economics | Entity, source, exposure, cohort, maturity, contribution, cash, CAC, payback, uncertainty, decisions | Attributed revenue, blended ratios, immature cohorts |

Assign `verified ready`, `bounded pilot only`, `specified not verified`, `verified blocker`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. Show evidence coverage beside the state. Do not average gates or penalize unavailable evidence as failure.

## Motion Fit

Compare motions through:

- customer intent, urgency, discovery behavior, and trust;
- product complexity, time to value, trialability, collaboration, integration, and migration;
- user, administrator, buyer, payer, procurement, security, legal, and finance roles;
- contract value, transaction frequency, sales cycle, implementation, support, and success;
- channel access, feedback speed, capacity, cost, contribution, cash, concentration, and risk.

| Motion | Appropriate when | Required controls |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Self-serve | Customer can discover, evaluate, buy, activate, and succeed with low assistance | Clear value path, transparent offer, billing, support, retained-value measurement |
| Product-led | Product experience creates qualified value and expansion signals | Access, first and repeated value, free/trial economics, account and buying handoffs |
| Sales-assisted | Product or marketing creates intent but buying or implementation needs bounded help | Qualification, ownership, promise handoff, capacity, sales conflict |
| Sales-led | Buying group, risk, contract, integration, or value requires active selling | Milestones, proof, procurement, forecasting, implementation, success, economics |
| Partner-led | Partner owns trusted access or complementary delivery value | Shared customer, contribution, rights, data, attribution, economics, operations, exit |
| Marketplace | Platform creates discovery, transaction, or distribution access | Eligibility, ranking, fees, multi-homing, data, participant value, dependency |
| Community-led | Participant value and relationships create qualified demand and learning | Membership, quality, moderation, product path, capacity, platform resilience |
| Developer-led | API, tool, docs, sandbox, and implementation produce technical value | Technical activation, usage quality, account conversion, support, security, costs |

Use hybrids only with explicit segment, entry, handoff, attribution, customer experience, incentive, ownership, and economic rules. Avoid parallel motions that compete for the same customer or create price and promise conflict.

## Motion Graduation

Define prerequisites, initial cohort, owner, capacity, value milestone, buying milestone, retained outcome, economics, quality, evidence horizon, threshold, stop, rollback, and expiry. A motion graduates when compatible evidence supports repeatability within its boundary, not when one seller, channel, partner, or customer succeeds.

## Readiness Decisions

Return one of `validate upstream`, `repair dependency`, `bounded pilot`, `hold`, `eligible for specialist execution review`, `pause`, or `retire GTM unit`. These are analysis states, not permissions to launch or spend.
