# GTM Contract

Freeze one product-market contract before readiness, motion, route, pilot, portfolio, or scale work. Split the unit whenever one row cannot describe every product, customer, value path, market, motion, owner, or outcome being combined.

## Decision Header

| Field | Required definition |
| --- | --- |
| Decision | Choice, owner, deadline, reversibility, threshold, and actions not authorized |
| Product-market unit | Product and offer version, customer and buying unit, job, segment, market, and horizon |
| Customer | Trigger, alternatives, value, proof, prerequisites, exclusions, and switching cost |
| Participants | User, beneficiary, champion, administrator, buyer, approver, payer, procurement, partner, and delivery roles |
| Commercial | Offer, package, price, currency, terms, contract, payment, invoice, tax, refund, and renewal |
| Motion and routes | Self-serve, product, sales, partner, marketplace, community, developer, channel, handoffs, and conflicts |
| Value and delivery | Access, first and repeated value, implementation, support, success, reliability, and capacity |
| Measurement and economics | Entity, identity, source, exposure, cohort, maturity, attribution, contribution, cash, and uncertainty |
| Action | Local artifacts allowed now and separately authorized operating work |

## Unit Boundary

Keep product, feature, API, workflow, service, migration, and commercial change distinct. Keep person, user, seat, workspace, account, legal customer, buyer, payer, contract, order, transaction, partner, and marketplace side distinct. Define who receives value, buys, pays, implements, supports, retains, renews, and expands.

Record segment and exclusion rules, market, language, locale, product and offer version, price, sales motion, channel, partner, delivery model, implementation, data, risk, economics, and owner. One brand can operate several GTM units; one GTM unit cannot hide materially different paths.

## Value And Buying Contract

Define job, trigger, urgency, existing alternatives, switching, capability-to-value chain, proof, objections, trust, time to value, repeated-value interval, participant roles, buying milestones, qualification, disqualification, procurement, security, legal, finance, implementation, promise handoff, adoption, renewal, and expansion.

## Evidence Ledger

| ID | Claim or value | Product, customer, market | Period and version | Source and lineage | State | Coverage, limitation, next check |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use only `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. Keep `reported signal` attributable but outside the evidence-state set. Preserve actual, attribution, causal estimate, forecast, scenario, target, plan, benchmark, approval, missing, immature, and suppressed states.

## Compatibility And Versioning

Before combining outputs align customer and payer entity, job, segment, market, product, offer, price, motion, route, version, period, cohort, maturity, source, value and cost basis, evidence state, uncertainty, and owner. Version definitions, sources, readiness, architecture, assumptions, pilots, decisions, outcomes, overrides, and revisions. Preserve prior versions and explain changes.
