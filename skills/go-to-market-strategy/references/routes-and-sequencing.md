# Routes And Sequencing

Build the smallest route that can prove customer value, buying progression, delivery, and retained economics. More simultaneous channels reduce interpretability and consume capacity.

## Route Contract

For every route record customer and intent, source and access, role in the journey, positioning and proof, offer, native format, destination, identity, permission, response, buying or product handoff, owner, capacity, cost, attribution, incrementality, maturity, saturation, dependency, risk, and stop rule.

Separate route families:

- product, invitation, collaboration, referral, integration, and marketplace distribution;
- search, content, GEO, social, community, PR, directory, and earned discovery;
- paid search, social, video, display, app, and retargeting;
- outbound, seller, partner, affiliate, channel, event, and field motions;
- lifecycle, customer success, advocacy, renewal, and expansion routes.

Each route has a distinct mechanism and specialist owner. Do not force one funnel or attribution rule across them.

## Channel And Motion Fit

Evaluate customer intent, reachable unit, message and offer fit, native format, destination continuity, product and sales path, expected value quality, attribution, incrementality, full and marginal cost, saturation, capacity, concentration, platform dependency, market compatibility, and learning speed.

Historical attributed CAC is not future marginal CAC. Reach is not qualified demand. A partner logo is not partner-sourced value. Content output is not distribution. Route design and budget allocation belong to `acquisition-strategy`.

## Buying And Delivery Handoffs

Define source, acceptance, qualification, disqualification, owner, service level, customer milestone, data and context transferred, promise, proof, next state, suppression, feedback, correction, and expiry for every product-marketing-sales-implementation-success-support handoff.

Preserve user, champion, buyer, payer, procurement, security, legal, finance, implementation, support, and success roles. A demo or verbal interest is not approval. Do not forecast all qualified records as won.

## Pilot Sequence

Sequence dependencies:

1. customer, problem, and alternative evidence;
2. positioning, proof, and offer comprehension;
3. product access, first value, and reliability;
4. repeated value, delivery, and support;
5. one bounded route and buying path;
6. retained contribution, cash, and operating stability;
7. adjacent segment, route, motion, offer, or market.

For each stage define owner, prerequisite, initial cohort, channel and product exposure, cap, time horizon, measurement, capacity, positive, negative, harmful, and inconclusive outcome, decision, stop, rollback, and expiry.

## Portfolio Sequencing

Compare initiatives by upstream dependency, customer decision value, evidence quality, reversibility, time to learn, capacity, retained economic upside, downside, concentration, interaction, and option value. Do not rank with one opaque score. Show assumptions, weights if explicitly requested, missingness, sensitivity, and alternatives.

Preserve conflicts: the same customer may see different prices or promises; one channel may cannibalize another; sales may override experiments; implementation capacity may bind; a partner may own the relationship; one market may need different product or data conditions.

## Expansion And Exit

Expansion requires repeatable customer value, buying progression, delivery, measurement, economics, and ownership in the current unit plus readiness for the next unit. Success in one account, city, channel, partner, or cohort does not validate a category or global market. Define pause, cap, repair, rollback, customer protection, data handling, partner exit, and retirement before scale.
