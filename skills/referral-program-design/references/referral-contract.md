# Referral Program Contract

## Decision And Scope

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, program status, version, dates, horizon, budget, review cadence, and allowed action;
- product, offer, business model, customer value, natural frequency, lifecycle role, market, language, locale, and product surface;
- sender, recipient, payer, administrator, partner, platform, support owner, and business entity;
- relationship, relevance, authority, permission, consent, contact source, exclusions, and recipient controls;
- sender value state, eligibility, trigger, motivation, effort, social risk, benefit, frequency, and suppression;
- referral action, payload, artifact, context, editable message, channel, link, code, attribution token, expiry, and delivery;
- destination, carried context, identity state, permissions, landing continuity, recipient job, first value, and retained value;
- organic word of mouth, prompted sharing, rewarded referral, product invitation, advocacy, employee, partner, affiliate, creator, and paid paths;
- reward payer, recipient, type, amount, currency or unit, qualification, timing, visibility, disclosure, cap, expiry, settlement, reversal, liability, tax, and dispute terms;
- eligibility, exposure, attempt, delivery, arrival, qualification, value, retention, reward, complaint, abuse, and re-entry events;
- identity, sender-recipient linkage, deduplication, attribution precedence, cohort, window, maturity, cutoff, timezone, and version;
- baseline, counterfactual, interference, cost boundary, contribution, payback, fraud, cannibalization, fairness, privacy, trust, accessibility, support, capacity, and uncertainty;
- source artifact, source owner, date, evidence state, counterevidence, limitation, and freshness.

Version the contract when participant definitions, eligibility, trigger, payload, destination, reward, attribution, risk controls, product value, market, or terms change materially. Do not merge incompatible versions into one result.

## Evidence Rules

Use:

- `verified`: directly supported by an attributable inspectable artifact;
- `inferred`: reasoned from named evidence, with the inference explicit;
- `unavailable`: decision-relevant but not supplied or observable;
- `not applicable`: outside the declared program and decision;
- `reported signal`: attributable stakeholder statement without inspectable support; leave evidence state blank.

Do not treat a public invitation, testimonial, program page, creator post, code, terms page, or referral landing page as evidence of participant permission, ownership, private exposure, delivery, unique identity, reward settlement, incremental acquisition, retention, economics, fraud, or satisfaction.

## Mechanism Classification

Classify each path before combining results:

| Path | Primary job | Required distinction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organic word of mouth | Voluntary discussion after experienced value | No identifiable product flow may exist |
| Product invitation | Enable collaboration, access, transaction, or another job | New-user acquisition can be secondary |
| Customer referral | Identifiable sender introduces a relevant recipient | Require recipient value and retained quality |
| Rewarded referral | Referral includes a contingent benefit | Preserve reward exposure and organic overlap |
| Advocacy | Public or private endorsement grounded in experience | Preserve consent, truth, rights, and sponsorship |
| Affiliate or partner | Compensated distribution under a commercial relationship | Preserve contract, disclosure, media, and attribution terms |
| Employee referral | Worker introduces a candidate, customer, or partner | Preserve role, conflict, privacy, and program boundary |

One participant can appear in multiple paths. Keep exposure, version, economics, permission, and attribution separate.

## Privacy Minimum

Prefer pseudonymous sender, recipient, relationship category, eligibility, exposure, event, outcome, cost, and risk records. Exclude raw contacts, message bodies, credentials, payment credentials, tax IDs, protected attributes, precise location, and complete social or device graphs unless an authorized decision cannot be supported without them.

Document purpose, permission, permissible use, joins, access, retention, deletion, withdrawal, minimum-cell reporting, review, appeal, and output aggregation. Never assume that access to a device contact book authorizes marketing messages.
