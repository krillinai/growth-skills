# Referral Mechanism And Experience

## Dependency Order

Use this sequence:

```text
delivered sender value and recipient relevance
-> eligible participants and allowed relationship
-> natural trigger and motivation
-> useful payload, channel, and delivery
-> destination continuity and recipient first value
-> retained recipient quality
-> reward only if it removes a supported barrier
-> later referral opportunity, measurement, and operations
```

If an upstream dependency is unavailable, specify the smallest evidence needed and do not compensate with stronger prompts or larger rewards.

## Participant Value Map

For each sender, recipient, payer, administrator, partner, platform, and business role, state:

- value received and cost or risk borne;
- action, permission, context, and control required;
- failure, complaint, recovery, and exit path;
- evidence, counterevidence, and unresolved assumptions.

Do not create value for the business by externalizing spam, disclosure, privacy, support, coordination, financial, or reputation costs to another participant.

## Trigger And Eligibility

A defensible trigger follows attributable value or a natural collaborative need. Define value event, required tenure or state, exclusions, cooldown, frequency, suppression, and expiry. Candidate triggers are hypotheses until their relevance is verified.

Avoid prompts before first value, after failure, during support incidents, on sensitive screens, or where declining blocks the current job. Do not infer that high activity means satisfaction or willingness to recommend.

## Payload And Channel

Specify:

- useful artifact, role, invitation, proof, context, offer, or benefit;
- sender identity and editable or sender-controlled message;
- recipient relationship and relevance rule;
- channel, classification, permission, frequency, delivery evidence, and recipient controls;
- honest reward, sponsorship, affiliate, or employee disclosure;
- accessibility, locale, link safety, expiration, and recovery.

Do not preselect contacts, upload address books, send on the sender's behalf without explicit authorization, hide commercial intent, fabricate personal language, or make declining difficult.

## Destination And First Value

Carry only the context needed for the recipient's job: sender or artifact identity where allowed, role, workspace or item, offer terms, permissions, referral state, locale, and next action. Define invalid, duplicate, expired, existing-user, incompatible-market, and permission-denied paths.

Measure first value, not landing, install, signup, seat creation, or code entry alone. Preserve recipient choice and do not make reward qualification depend on irrelevant data collection or forced communication.

## Reward Decision

Before adding a reward, state the observed barrier and non-incentive alternatives. Compare:

- no reward and stronger inherent value;
- sender-only, recipient-only, two-sided, community, charitable, status, access, credit, discount, or cash mechanisms;
- immediate, delayed, milestone, retained-value, capped, tiered, randomized, or tapered fulfillment.

Choose only after participant value, qualification, cost, fairness, abuse, disclosure, settlement, reversal, and support implications are explicit. Route detailed incentive mechanics to `incentive-system-design`.

## Experience Specification

Return one row per state:

| State | Required fields |
| --- | --- |
| Eligible | Entity, value prerequisite, relationship, exclusions, source, and version |
| Prompted | Trigger, placement, message, frequency, suppression, and decline behavior |
| Composing | Payload, editable fields, channel choice, disclosure, consent, and preview |
| Delivered | Delivery evidence, recipient control, privacy, expiry, and failure handling |
| Arrived | Destination, carried context, identity, permissions, locale, and continuity |
| First value | Recipient job, value event, window, quality, and owner |
| Qualified | Deduplication, existing-user, fraud, cancellation, and maturity rules |
| Rewarded | Payer, recipient, settlement, reversal, notification, support, and ledger |
| Retained | Natural-frequency value, cohort, contribution, trust, and later opportunity |

Include copy requirements rather than fabricated production copy when facts, terms, claims, or approvals are unavailable.
