# Customer Expansion Methods

## Retained-Core-Value Gate

Before expansion, verify:

1. the original scope has a declared recurring value event and mature cohort;
2. eligible customers repeatedly realize value at the natural opportunity;
3. critical reliability, implementation, support, trust, and renewal risks are visible;
4. the expansion does not substitute for unresolved activation or retention work;
5. product, success, support, commercial, engineering, and governance capacity exists;
6. the original scope has explicit health and cannibalization guardrails.

If these conditions are unsupported, narrow the work to measurement or core-value repair. A seller request, contract date, feature release, or revenue target is not an exception.

## Type-Specific Contracts

### Seat

Separate eligible roles, contracted, provisioned, invited, active, first-value, repeated-value, retained, billable, guest, service, suspended, and inactive seats. Define collaboration or role value, permissions, admin load, support, renewal, and shelfware. More seats help only when relevant participants realize value.

### Usage

Define valuable job, eligible opportunity, successful completion, quality, error, latency, variable cost, repeat behavior, and customer outcome. Separate organic, prompted, rewarded, mandatory, retried, duplicated, failed, and synthetic usage. Usage expansion is healthy only when retained value grows faster than cost and harm.

### Workflow

Define the adjacent job, users and roles, prerequisites, implementation, first and repeat value, reliability, support, data and permission boundaries, and original-workflow health. Avoid feature sprawl and workflow count as the outcome.

### Product Or Module

Define best-fit need, eligibility, exclusions, product readiness, value continuity, overlap, attach, provision, activation, retained use, renewal, total-customer value, support, and cannibalization. A forced bundle or signed add-on can hide no adoption.

### Account Or Team

Define the receiving unit, job, user and buying roles, authority, security, procurement, change management, implementation, local admin, first value, repeat value, governance, account breadth, renewal, and burden. A power user is not company authorization.

Use `measure`, `nurture`, `assist`, `validate`, `expand`, `hold`, `repair core`, or `stop` only with an owner, evidence horizon, rule, and completion proof. These are local recommendations, not CRM or customer actions.

## Portfolio Method

Compare:

- eligible customers and absolute retained-value opportunity;
- adjacent need, urgency, readiness, authority, and best fit;
- product, implementation, success, support, sales, engineering, security, and operations capacity;
- time to first and retained value, evidence maturity, and uncertainty;
- full cost, contribution, cash, cannibalization, concentration, trust, and reversibility;
- strategic learning, reuse, maintenance, and opportunity cost.

Do not rank from contract value, attach, opportunity count, product usage, seller activity, or propensity alone. Provide scenarios and explicit allocation gates rather than one false-precision score.

## Scope Boundaries

Customer expansion stays inside an existing relationship. Route new-country or new-market entry to `market-entry-strategy`; local marketplace or network replication to `network-effects-strategy`; product-market fit in a new segment to `product-market-fit-assessment`; and company-wide product portfolio positioning to `positioning`.
