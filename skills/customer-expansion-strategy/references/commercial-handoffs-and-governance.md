# Expansion Handoffs And Governance

## Product-To-Commercial Handoff

| Area | Contract |
| --- | --- |
| Signal | source, entity, original value, adjacent need, reason, evidence, confidence, version, expiry |
| Eligibility | best fit, exclusions, role, authority state, product and account prerequisites |
| Customer context | incident, implementation, support, success, renewal, prior contact, consent, and preference |
| Action | product path, success assistance, research, seller follow-up, admin review, or no action |
| Ownership | product, success, sales, operations, support, security, finance, and final decision owner |
| Capacity | queue, priority, SLA, fallback, reassignment, expiry, and no-owner behavior |
| Feedback | accepted, declined, reason, exposure, first value, repeat value, contract, renewal, harm |
| Governance | access, privacy, fairness, suppression, audit, override, appeal, incident, retirement |

Product-Led Sales is a coordinated value path, not an automatic lead-scoring shortcut. Product retains responsibility for product value; success supports realized outcomes; sales or commercial roles validate need, authority, buying process, and terms; operations preserves routing and reconciliation.

Route CRM entity, stage, owner, assignment, forecast, and finance reconciliation defects to `revops-audit`. Route claims, proof, discovery, objection, security, implementation, and buyer materials to `sales-enablement`.

## Customer Controls

Preserve clear entitlements, role and admin permissions, invitation choice, usage visibility, billing predictability, downgrade and removal paths, data boundaries, security review, support, accessibility, contract terms, notice, dispute, correction, and appeal. Do not use hidden overage, forced bundles, inactive-seat billing, coercive invitations, opaque scoring, or difficult downgrade as expansion mechanics.

Every new workflow, product, team, or entitlement needs an operating owner, support path, version, migration and rollback, incident response, and retirement condition.

## China And Local-Market Contract

Separate market, language, locale, legal entity, account hierarchy, role, product, data, identity, consent, security, procurement, CRM, contract, payment, invoice, tax, currency, support, channel, accessibility, applicable review, and local owner. Translation does not establish compatible buying roles, authority, product usage, commercial process, or expansion thresholds.

Shared teams may own product contracts, event and metric semantics, security standards, tooling, and portfolio visibility. Local owners retain customer, procurement, commercial, support, legal, channel, and operating judgment where conditions differ. Define configuration, override, escalation, and feedback.

## Operating Controls

Name owners and approval boundaries for product eligibility, entitlements, pricing, discounting, contract, finance, billing, tax, security, privacy, legal, experiment, sales, success, support, communication, incidents, stop, and customer remediation.

An expansion strategy can specify required actions and controls but cannot access systems, score or route accounts, contact customers, alter product or commercial state, launch offers or messages, change budgets, or declare results.
