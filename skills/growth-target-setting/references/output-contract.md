# Growth Target Setting Output Contract

## Required Order

Return:

1. mode, target set ID and version, decision, owner, approver, scope, horizon, market, strategy and metric versions, evidence cutoff, effective date, review, expiry, and external-action boundary;
2. source, evidence, metric-contract, baseline, forecast, opportunity, benchmark, capacity, contradiction, and unavailable-input ledger;
3. target architecture with outcome targets, input commitments, thresholds, aspirations, guardrails, and relationship labels;
4. target-basis, forecast-to-target gap, scenarios, sensitivities, economics, and tradeoffs;
5. hierarchy, overlap, shared ownership, residual, currency, accounting, and reconciliation views;
6. capacity, dependency, guardrail, incentive, gaming, fairness, and risk register;
7. proposal, approval, review, breach, reset, supersession, and version history;
8. specialist handoffs, relevant pinned Growth Playbook sources, approval-ready external actions, and completion record.

## Target Register

| Target | Type and metric role | Baseline / forecast / target | Basis and evidence | Owner and contributors | Capacity and dependency | Guardrail and decision rule | Period / maturity / review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use `outcome target`, `input commitment`, `threshold`, `aspiration`, or `guardrail`. Unsupported numerical values remain unavailable.

## Target-Basis Ledger

| Input | Definition and version | Value or range | As-of and maturity | Evidence state and source | Limitation or contradiction | Sensitivity and target implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Keep actual, baseline, benchmark, forecast, scenario, opportunity, target, plan, budget, quota, and result distinct.

## Hierarchy And Reconciliation Register

| Parent or shared outcome | Child or contributor | Relationship | Unit, period, currency, accounting | Overlap and residual | Reconciliation method | Owner and unresolved issue |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Add only mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive children. Do not duplicate value across markets, products, lifecycle stages, channels, or functions.

## Capacity And Guardrail Register

| Capacity, tradeoff, or guardrail | Current state and evidence | Target implication | Warning / breach | Owner | Escalation / cap / stop / rollback | Review / reset authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Do not average noncompensable harms into target upside.

## Approval And Change Register

| Target version | Proposed state | Owner acceptance | Required approver | Effective period | Reset trigger and evidence | Superseded version | Downstream action boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

A recommendation is not approval, adoption, communication, system mutation, execution, or result.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that target set, version, mode, decision, owner, contributors, approver, scope, exclusions, product, model, customer, market, locale, strategy, metric contract, type, role, entity, eligibility, formula, identity, source, segment, window, cohort, maturity, currency, accounting, baseline, benchmark, forecast, scenario, opportunity, target, plan, budget, quota, basis, hierarchy, overlap, residual, capacity, dependencies, economics, tradeoffs, guardrails, incentives, gaming, fairness, risks, effective date, review, expiry, reset, history, privacy, market transfer, handoffs, pinned sources, and external actions are explicit; targets did not become forecasts, evidence, or causes; forecasts were not forced to targets; benchmarks did not become commitments; activity did not become customer value; shared outcomes were not double counted; unavailable capacity did not become a commitment; guardrails were not bought off; targets and history were not rewritten; approvals, adoption, execution, and results were not invented; and no external action occurred.
