# Governance, Incentives, And Market Transfer

## Govern The Lifecycle

Record target proposal, review, approval, effective date, owner acceptance, monitoring, breach, reset, supersession, expiry, and retirement. At each review compare mature actuals with the frozen target, baseline, and original as-of forecast. Preserve errors, misses, positive surprises, revisions, and contradictions.

A target miss can reflect execution, an invalid assumption, a metric change, a forecast error, a structural shift, capacity, or unresolved evidence. Do not infer cause from variance. Route anomalies to `growth-anomaly-investigation` and recurring decisions to `growth-operating-review`.

## Reset Without Rewriting History

Use `refresh` only when an attributable trigger affects the target contract, such as:

- metric, identity, source, accounting, or baseline break;
- strategy, customer value, product, market, channel, or price change;
- forecast revision from newly available evidence;
- capacity, cash, reliability, trust, regulatory, partner, or platform change;
- target achieved early, made impossible, or made harmful under the original contract.

Preserve the prior target, period, basis, owner, approval, actual-to-date, and decision. Record changed fields, evidence, rationale, reconciliation, downstream plans and incentives affected, approver, effective date, expiry, and next review. Never lower or raise a target merely to hide or amplify performance.

## Audit Incentives And Gaming

For every target inspect incentives to:

- change eligibility, denominators, windows, attribution, identity, or accounting;
- select easier customers or exclude weak cohorts;
- buy low-quality volume or shift value between periods;
- suppress refunds, churn, incidents, complaints, or negative evidence;
- overload customers, employees, partners, systems, or support;
- optimize activity at the expense of retained value, contribution, trust, or fairness.

Add quality, maturity, audit, anti-gaming, and harmed-group checks. Separate organizational goals from individual employee evaluation. Route reward and behavioral mechanism design to `incentive-system-design`. Do not give employment, compensation, pricing, credit, or other consequential guidance from a single growth metric.

## Transfer Across Markets

Treat each market as a new evidence boundary. Revalidate customers, metrics, baselines, forecasts, opportunities, strategy, channels, prices, payments, invoices, costs, contribution, capacity, guardrails, source access, owners, and review rules. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transfer.

For China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, channel, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not provide legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, provider-availability, or performance conclusions.

## Protect People And Data

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence. Define purpose, authority, consent where required, access, retention, deletion, audit, and small-cell suppression. Do not expose sensitive customer or employee data, infer protected traits, set individual customer revenue targets, or rank people for consequential decisions.

## Govern External Actions

Target recommendations are not approval or adoption. Create and read local artifacts only unless the user separately authorizes a bounded action. For each proposed system or organizational change record owner, approver, exact mutation, affected population, effective date, prerequisite evidence, privacy and security review, incentives, guardrails, dry run, monitoring, breach, stop, rollback, audit, and completion proof. Never claim a target was entered, accepted, communicated, funded, or achieved from a plan.
