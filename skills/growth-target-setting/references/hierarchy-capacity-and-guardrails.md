# Hierarchy, Capacity, And Guardrails

## Map The Hierarchies

Keep these dimensions separate unless a declared model relates them:

- company, legal entity, business unit, portfolio, product, and plan;
- market, geography, language, locale, and currency;
- customer, account, user, buyer, payer, beneficiary, and participant role;
- segment, use case, lifecycle state, channel, motion, and partner;
- function, team, shared service, and accountable owner;
- customer count, usage, orders, GMV, bookings, revenue, contribution, and cash.

Add children only when they are mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive on compatible units and periods. Record hierarchy method, shared values, overlap, unknown, residual, rounding, currency bridge, and reconciliation status.

## Assign Ownership Without Duplicating Value

Use one accountable owner for an outcome. Contributors own input commitments, dependencies, or specialist evidence. Do not assign the same account, contract, revenue, retained value, or expansion to multiple functions as separate company growth.

Reconcile commercial states such as qualified demand, opportunity, pipeline, booking, contract, recognized revenue, invoice, cash, renewal, and expansion. Route detailed work to `revops-audit`.

## Test Capacity And Feasibility

For each target map relevant limits:

| Capacity | Questions |
| --- | --- |
| Demand and channel | reachable qualified demand, saturation, competition, attribution, and incrementality |
| Product and delivery | value readiness, reliability, implementation, fulfillment, inventory, and localization |
| Commercial | sales cycle, seller capacity, security and procurement review, discounting, and collections |
| Customer operations | onboarding, support, success, moderation, trust, and service quality |
| Data and systems | metric, identity, experiment, billing, automation, and incident readiness |
| Economics and cash | marginal acquisition, contribution, payback, working capital, and downside |
| People and partners | specialist time, hiring lead time, leadership attention, partner bandwidth, and change load |

Classify capacity as `verified available`, `committed`, `contingent`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`. Do not make contingent or unavailable capacity a committed outcome assumption.

## Surface Tradeoffs

Record what the target may displace or degrade: another segment, channel, product, lifecycle state, margin, cash, quality, reliability, trust, accessibility, privacy, employee health, partner value, or strategic option. Targets are a portfolio, not independent wishes.

Route opportunity comparison to `growth-opportunity-sizing`, medium-term portfolio choices to `growth-strategy`, and one bounded resource decision to `growth-investment-case`.

## Define Guardrails

Guardrails may cover customer value, retention, refunds, quality, contribution, cash, capacity, reliability, accessibility, privacy, security, safety, fairness, fraud, support, employee health, regulatory review, partner health, and concentration.

For each guardrail record metric contract, affected population, basis, current state, warning, breach, maturity, owner, monitor, escalation, cap, stop, rollback, correction, and reset authority. Keep noncompensable harms separate; outcome upside cannot buy them off.
