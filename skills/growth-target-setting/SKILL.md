# Growth Target Setting

Turn desired growth outcomes into explicit, versioned commitments and decision rules. A target says what an accountable owner chooses to pursue or protect; it does not prove likelihood, cause, capacity, approval, or future performance. Keep ambition visible without forcing forecasts or assumptions to match it.

Read [target-contract.md](references/target-contract.md) before accepting a metric, baseline, target, owner, hierarchy, period, or evidence. Read [target-basis-and-types.md](references/target-basis-and-types.md) before classifying or deriving outcome targets, input commitments, thresholds, aspirations, or guardrails. Read [hierarchy-capacity-and-guardrails.md](references/hierarchy-capacity-and-guardrails.md) before allocating targets across products, markets, segments, motions, or teams or testing feasibility and tradeoffs. Read [governance-incentives-and-market-transfer.md](references/governance-incentives-and-market-transfer.md) before approving, reviewing, resetting, incentivizing, transferring, or operationalizing targets. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) for the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `audit` | Inspect existing goals, targets, OKRs, quotas, cascades, evidence, ownership, incentives, and change history |
| `design` | Create an evidence-bounded target system for a declared decision, scope, and horizon |
| `refresh` | Version targets after a material metric, baseline, forecast, strategy, opportunity, capacity, market, or risk change |

Name one primary mode. Route metric roles and reproducible definitions to `growth-metrics-design`, time-indexed projections and calibration to `growth-forecasting`, opportunity ranges to `growth-opportunity-sizing`, strategic choices to `growth-strategy`, one resource-commitment case to `growth-investment-case`, organization and staffing design to `growth-organization-design`, and recurring review to `growth-operating-review`.

## Freeze The Target Contract

Record target set ID and version, decision, accountable owner and contributors, required approver, scope and exclusions, product and business model, customer and participant roles, market and locale, strategy and metric versions, target type, entity, eligibility, formula, identity, segment, period, window, cohort and maturity, currency and accounting basis, baseline and as-of date, forecast version and origin, opportunity basis, capacity and dependencies, evidence cutoff, effective and review dates, expiry, and external-action boundary.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing claims. Keep actual, baseline, benchmark, estimate, forecast, scenario, opportunity, target, plan, budget, quota, approval, action, completion, and result separate. Preserve source, date, owner, version, definition, segment, market, period, maturity, limitations, contradictions, revisions, and lineage.

Do not invent metric definitions, baselines, benchmarks, forecasts, opportunities, target values, driver relationships, capacity, economics, probabilities, confidence scores, approvals, adoption, or results. Do not blend incompatible entities, denominators, cohorts, windows, markets, currencies, accounting bases, or target types.

## Classify Every Target

Use one primary type:

| Type | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `outcome target` | Desired customer or sustainable business outcome at a declared maturity |
| `input commitment` | Owned controllable work or capability expected to support an outcome without claiming causality |
| `threshold` | Boundary that triggers a decision, escalation, or service state |
| `aspiration` | Directional ambition not supported as a commitment or forecast |
| `guardrail` | Noncompensable or bounded quality, trust, risk, customer, economic, or capacity condition |

Activity volume can be an input commitment, not proof of customer or business growth. A target can have one primary type in one decision context. Separate a desired outcome from the work, evidence, and controls needed to pursue it.

## Establish The Basis

Build a target-basis ledger from compatible evidence:

1. versioned baseline and natural maturity;
2. as-of forecast and uncertainty, kept independent of the target;
3. strategy and customer-value outcome;
4. bounded opportunity and dominant sensitivities;
5. historical variation, seasonality, and structural changes;
6. product, channel, sales, delivery, support, data, cash, and people capacity;
7. unit economics, marginal effects, and customer protections;
8. dependencies, risks, alternatives, and opportunity cost.

Show the forecast-to-target and baseline-to-target gaps. Describe required driver or capability changes as scenarios or input commitments, not facts. Never backsolve assumptions, suppress downside, or rewrite a forecast to make a target appear likely.

External benchmarks provide context only when definitions, populations, maturity, products, models, markets, and periods are compatible. A competitor claim, survey, or case does not establish an operating target.

## Build A Coherent Target System

Use the smallest set that connects customer value, sustainable business outcome, controllable inputs, business health, and guardrails. Label metric-tree relationships as arithmetic, hypothesized, associated, causal, or tradeoff. A controllable metric is not automatically a causal driver.

For every target state the current evidence, desired value or bounded state, owner, contributors, period, maturity, target basis, dependencies, capacity, downside, guardrails, decision rule, expiry, and review trigger. When evidence is insufficient, return `unavailable`, an `aspiration`, a `threshold`, or an evidence commitment rather than a false numerical commitment.

## Reconcile Hierarchies And Shared Ownership

Map company, portfolio, product, market, segment, customer, motion, channel, lifecycle, and team hierarchies. Add child targets only when they form a mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive partition on compatible units and periods. Otherwise show separate views, shared contributions, overlap, residual, and reconciliation method.

Assign one accountable owner per outcome. Contributors can own input commitments. Do not give marketing, sales, product, and customer success separate credit for the same customer, contract, revenue, or retained value. Keep pipeline, bookings, recognized revenue, cash, renewal, and expansion distinct.

## Test Capacity, Tradeoffs, And Guardrails

Connect the target to reachable demand, product readiness, acquisition saturation, sales cycle, implementation, support, fulfillment, security, finance, partner, infrastructure, cash, and leadership capacity where relevant. Show which capacity is verified, committed, contingent, unavailable, or displaced.

Keep customer value, retention, quality, contribution, cash, reliability, privacy, security, fairness, fraud, support, employee health, regulatory review, and concentration guardrails independent. Noncompensable harm cannot be averaged into target upside. Define warning, breach, escalation, cap, stop, rollback, and reset rules.

## Govern Incentives And Changes

Audit how targets may change eligibility, denominators, timing, attribution, customer selection, quality, refunds, accounting, reporting, or employee behavior. Add anti-gaming checks and harmed-group review. Do not rank individuals or make consequential employee or customer decisions from aggregate growth targets.

Preserve every target version, basis, approval, effective period, actual, miss, and decision. A miss does not automatically invalidate the target; a target does not move merely to hide a miss. Use `refresh` when a predeclared trigger or attributable structural change affects the contract. Record old and new values, reconciliation, rationale, approver, downstream effects, and next review.

## Handle Markets, People, And Data

Treat every market as a new evidence boundary. Revalidate customer value, metric contracts, baselines, forecasts, opportunities, channels, prices, costs, capacity, guardrails, source access, owners, and review rules. Translation and currency conversion do not validate transfer.

For mainland China, distinguish market, legal entity, language, locale, product, platform, app distribution, identity, payment, invoice, data, content, advertising, support, and locally accountable review. Use current direct sources and local expertise. Do not make legal, tax, regulatory, provider-availability, accounting, or performance conclusions.

Use aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence by default. Do not request credentials, private messages, payment histories, health data, protected traits, precise locations, raw identities, employee reviews, or full histories when aggregate evidence suffices. Do not set individual customer targets or rank people for consequential pricing, employment, credit, insurance, health, housing, or similar decisions.

## External-Action Boundary

Create and read local artifacts only. Do not access authenticated analytics, finance, CRM, billing, advertising, product, planning, HR, experiment, or data systems; export or join personal data; change metrics, targets, forecasts, plans, budgets, bonuses, quotas, dashboards, alerts, products, prices, permissions, contracts, or records; notify people; publish; send; spend; deploy; or claim adoption or business results without separate task-level authorization and controls.

## Completion Gate
