# TikTok Ads Evidence And Boundaries

## Evidence States

Use `verified`, `reported signal`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for each material claim. Verification means a supplied attributable source supports the statement within compatible scope and date; it does not establish causality, representativeness, ownership, permission, or customer value beyond that scope.

Missing private data is not poor performance. Request the smallest privacy-safe evidence that changes the decision.

## Claim Controls

- International TikTok Ads evidence does not describe Douyin or Ocean Engine.
- TikTok attribution does not establish incrementality, contribution, payback, retention, or customer quality.
- Organic post engagement does not establish the paid effect of Spark Ads.
- A creator, identity, post, or Spark label does not verify ownership, permission, endorsement, authenticity, or rights.
- A hook, view, completion, CTR, CPA, or conversion movement does not establish its cause.
- A platform performance label, recommendation, or optimization status is a recorded signal, not a business verdict.
- A screenshot verifies only the visible scope and capture time.
- External benchmarks require compatible market, format, placement, audience, objective, definition, attribution, maturity, and economics.

Preserve counterevidence and cite local files, row ranges, captures, and dates.

## Unsupported Precision

Never invent universal hook length, video duration, aspect ratio, posting or creative volume, concept or creator count, frequency, fatigue, CTR, CPM, CPA, ROAS, payback, budget, bid, audience size, scaling rule, stopping rule, attribution window, maturation window, or incremental effect.

When economics are incomplete, return the blocked decision, scenario formula, missing inputs, and evidence plan instead of a temporary platform target.

## Read-Only And Security Boundary

This Skill may read supplied local files, validate a local bundle, calculate descriptive measures, compare dated creative, Spark, destination, measurement, and business evidence, and write local findings or experiments.

It must not access TikTok, creator, shop, or ad accounts; ask for or use credentials or authorization codes; call a live API; create, edit, publish, boost, pause, or delete entities; change identity, placements, targeting, bids, budgets, optimization, audiences, events, tracking, or destinations; authorize or revoke Spark Ads; upload customer or event data; or claim an action occurred.

Do not process direct identifiers, device identifiers, IP addresses, audience lists, raw event payloads, payment records, credentials, tokens, secrets, or Spark authorization codes. Stop using the affected artifact without reproducing sensitive values and request a redacted or aggregated replacement.

An execution or Douyin request uses `out-of-scope`. Preserve supported work and list the separate platform-specific capability, authorization, policy, creator-rights, privacy, approval, monitoring, rollback, and audit-log controls required.
