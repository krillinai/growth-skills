# Timeline, Outcomes, And Evidence

## Build A Versioned Timeline

Record proposal, evidence cutoff, decision, approval, authorization, implementation, QA, eligibility, assignment, access, exposure, adoption, outcome maturity, concurrent change, incident, guardrail, stop, rollback, recovery, correction, and follow-up separately.

For each event include timestamp and timezone, event time versus processing or reporting time, source, version, owner, affected unit, evidence state, uncertainty, contradiction, and downstream decision. A release date is not universal exposure. A rollback date is not immediate recovery.

## Use An Evidence Hierarchy Without Erasing Context

Prefer original versioned artifacts and reproducible records for what they can establish. Use direct observations, customer evidence, participant accounts, and public artifacts with their declared boundaries. Preserve:

- source access, query or report version, definitions, freshness, corrections, and lineage;
- missing, late, immature, censored, suppressed, sampled, and unknown states;
- supporting, contradictory, negative, harmful, invalid, and inconclusive evidence;
- accounts from different roles without forcing consensus.

Do not cherry-pick one metric, segment, window, cohort, attribution model, experiment look, or quote. Mark post-hoc analysis exploratory and protect small populations.

## Reconcile Planned And Actual States

| Layer | Planned or expected | Observed and mature | Required boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Input | capacity, budget, data, asset, and dependency | used, unused, delayed, displaced, or residual | owner, period, approval, and opportunity cost |
| Activity | work intended | work performed, changed, stopped, or not started | completion evidence and reason |
| Output | artifact or capability | verified delivered and accepted state | version, eligible use, and maintenance |
| Exposure | eligible population and rollout | assigned, accessible, exposed, adopted, and excluded | identity, timing, coverage, and contamination |
| Customer outcome | first, repeated, retained, expanded, restored, or protected value | compatible mature outcome and harm | metric contract, denominator, cohort, and guardrails |
| Business outcome | revenue, contribution, cash, capacity, or option value | compatible mature value | currency, accounting, period, and uncertainty |

Tasks and outputs do not become customer or business impact without the supported chain.

## Reconcile Variance Without Calling It Cause

Compare mature actuals with the original compatible baseline, target, forecast, scenario, plan, and budget. Preserve each role and vintage. Show absolute and relative change, numerator, denominator, mix, maturity, revisions, and residual.

Build bridges for:

- actual versus target, without assuming the target was valid or causal;
- actual versus forecast as of the original cutoff, without rewriting the forecast;
- planned versus used capacity and budget;
- expected versus observed eligibility, exposure, adoption, and value;
- expected versus observed costs, guardrails, risks, and residual obligations.

## Prevent Duplicate Impact

Reconcile entities, identity and lifecycle states. Use mutually exclusive partitions or explicit overlap, interaction, cannibalization, residual, and reconciliation. Do not add attribution, experiment lift, forecast upside, opportunity headroom, local initiative outcomes, and portfolio effects.

Keep outcome owner and contributor work distinct. Keep observation, attribution, contribution, association, causal estimate, transferred effect, and realized result distinct.

## Include Full Economics And Capacity

Include incremental, recurring, residual, and opportunity costs across media, incentives, refunds, chargebacks, support, sales, implementation, infrastructure, vendors, fraud, risk, compliance, maintenance, incidents, migration, and decommissioning where applicable. Show time and capacity retained after launch.

Separate revenue, gross profit, contribution, cash, and valuation; average and marginal; budget and actual; requested and approved. Return economics unavailable rather than inventing missing cost or value.

## Bound Mechanisms And Causes

For each candidate record observation, affected population, expected onset, lag, duration, evidence, contradiction, alternatives, confounding, selection, interference, concurrent changes, maturity, causal status, and next discriminating evidence.

Use:

- `descriptive`: shows where or when an outcome appears;
- `associated`: compatible relationship without identified causal effect;
- `causally supported`: valid design supports an effect in the tested boundary;
- `contradicted`: material evidence opposes the mechanism;
- `invalid`: design or data cannot support the claimed inference;
- `unresolved`: current evidence cannot distinguish alternatives.

Do not force one root cause. A corrective action can be prudent under bounded risk without proving a complete causal model; state that distinction.
