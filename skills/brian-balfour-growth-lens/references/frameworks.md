# Framework Reference

## Growth Model and Constraint Map

Map how acquisition inputs become activated and retained value, monetization, loop outputs, and the next inputs. Identify the current limiting transition rather than generating a tactic list.

```text
Acquisition input
-> first value
-> repeated value
-> retained economics
-> reusable distribution output
-> next input
```

Check transition rate, quality, cycle time, cost, saturation, and dependency at every edge.

## Four Fits

| Fit | Core question | Useful evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Market-Product Fit | Does a defined market repeatedly receive important value? | Interviews, activation, mature cohort retention, alternatives |
| Product-Channel Fit | Does the product fit how the channel distributes and converts demand? | Audience, promise, time to value, sharing or search behavior, retained quality |
| Channel-Model Fit | Can retained revenue and margin support channel cost and friction? | ARPU or ACV, CAC, conversion delay, payback, gross margin, capital need |
| Model-Market Fit | Can price, customer count, and attainable share support intended scale? | Market definition, reachable customers, willingness to pay, realistic share |

Changing one Fit requires reviewing the others. A new market tier may require a different product, channel, price, packaging, or sales motion.

## Channel Selection Matrix

First choose the current objective: learning, volume, cost efficiency, or access to a segment. Compare candidate channels on:

- targeting precision;
- upfront and marginal cost;
- input time and evidence maturity time;
- control over exposure and segmentation;
- qualified scale and saturation;
- product and model compatibility.

Select one or two hypotheses. Define what evidence will prove viability, trigger iteration, or stop the test.

## Channel-Model Fit

Use retained annual contribution, not price alone.

```text
retained revenue
- variable service and support cost
= retained contribution
-> acceptable CAC and payback
-> viable channel set
```

Low-friction, low-ARPU products usually require low-CAC distribution. Higher-cost content, partnerships, inside sales, or enterprise sales require sufficient account value and margin. Evaluate each tier separately.

## Model-Market Fit

```text
annual revenue per customer
x target customers
x realistically attainable share
= potential annual revenue
```

Treat every variable as uncertain. Include retention, margin, competition, reachability, expansion sequence, and time to capture share. A narrow initial market may establish value without supporting the long-term scale target.

## Metric Constellation

Use a small set of:

- **output metrics** for durable customer or business results;
- **input metrics** for behaviors the team can change;
- **trade-off metrics** for quality, trust, economics, or ecosystem harm.

Do not combine opposing dimensions into one number. Replace input metrics that move without improving the output.

## Scoped Launch Loop

```text
Define initial audience
-> access audience
-> filter for fit
-> observe qualitative signal
-> validate retained value
-> expand one audience layer
```

Specify eligibility, success signals, core-product guardrails, attention cost, and expansion rule. Public attention does not establish product or feature fit.

## Retention and the PMF Treadmill

Choose a retention interval matching the product's natural value frequency. Segment cohorts by source, use case, market, activation path, and product state. Revalidate fit when product, market, channel, model, technology, or expectations change.

## Growth Loops

Identify input, participant motivation, action, output, recipient, recipient value, and the next input. Measure participation, payload quality, recipient conversion, retained quality, cycle time, incrementality, and cost.

For AI-assisted UGC loops, add originality, expert attribution, contributor consent, misinformation, moderation cost, search dependency, and content-decay guardrails.
