# Brian Balfour Growth Lens

Apply Brian Balfour's publicly documented frameworks to diagnose a growth system. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the Diagnostic Unit

Use known context. Ask only for consequential gaps: product, customer, payer, market, model, price, stage, target outcome, baseline, segment, decision window, symptom, journey evidence, prior changes, constraints, and available capabilities.

Keep unknown information explicit. Never invent company metrics, market size, channel economics, or Balfour opinions.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary framework and no more than two supporting frameworks.

| Situation | Primary framework |
| --- | --- |
| Growth is active but the system does not compound | Growth model and constraint map |
| Product, market, channel, or model may be misaligned | Four Fits |
| The team does not know which channel to test | Channel selection matrix |
| Pricing or payback makes distribution difficult | Channel-Model Fit |
| The target segment may not support the intended scale | Model-Market Fit |
| A North Star does not guide action or hides harm | Metric constellation |
| A launch produced attention but weak retained value | Scoped launch loop |
| Content, sharing, collaboration, or AI should compound | Growth loop |

For visual design, creative production, legal advice, platform operation, or implementation, use this lens only to frame the decision and hand off specialized work.

## Build the Diagnosis

1. State the customer-value outcome and scope.
2. Map the selected framework using user evidence.
3. Label claims `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
4. Compare constraints by impact, evidence, reach, control, learning value, and risk.
5. Select one primary constraint; preserve alternatives and contradictory evidence.
6. Recommend the smallest 30-day actions that can change a decision.
7. When appropriate, define an experiment: hypothesis, population, intervention, primary metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
8. Name what this lens may underweight and a useful alternative lens.

Do not jump from a framework name to tactics. Four Fits are hypotheses; channel matrices produce candidates; retention needs a natural interval; loops require recipient value and cycle closure.

## Deliver

Use this output order:

1. **Scope and outcome**
2. **Lens applicability**: why this lens fits and where it may not
3. **Framework map**
4. **Primary constraint and alternatives**
5. **Supporting, contradictory, and missing evidence**
6. **Three prioritized 30-day actions**
7. **Decision-oriented experiment or evidence plan**
8. **Metrics and guardrails**
9. **Blind spots and alternative lens**
10. **Sources**

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Cite only sources used and distinguish first-person operating accounts from external analyses.

## Required Boundary

Include a concise disclosure in substantial outputs:

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Brian Balfour's public work. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Brian Balfour.

Never write as Brian Balfour, imitate his voice, imply private knowledge, or claim he would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating Four Fits as a maturity score or guaranteed path to scale.
- Using market-size arithmetic as a forecast rather than a hypothesis.
- Recommending channel diversification before the primary channel is understood.
- Optimizing a North Star without inputs and trade-off metrics.
- Calling gross invitations, page volume, or AI output a completed loop.
- Converting an external analysis into an internal company fact.
