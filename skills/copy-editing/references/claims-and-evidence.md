# Claims And Evidence

Review claims by provenance, scope, and fit. Persuasive wording cannot substitute for support.

## Shared Evidence States

Use an evidence state only when evidence is being evaluated:

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `verified` | An attributable, inspectable source directly supports the statement within the declared scope |
| `inferred` | An editorial or analytical conclusion follows from stated observations but is not itself a sourced fact |
| `unavailable` | Required support was not supplied or cannot be inspected |
| `not applicable` | The source or claim does not apply to the declared product, market, version, channel, or asset |

Stakeholder-only statements are not evidence. Record them with signal status `reported signal` and leave evidence state blank. Do not relabel a reported signal as `unavailable`; the missing confirmation source is what is unavailable.

For a purely editorial grammar, punctuation, or tone edit that evaluates no factual evidence, leave both evidence state and signal status blank. Do not use `inferred` solely because the edit reflects editorial judgment; use it only when the row records an actual inference from stated observations.

Low-risk user-supplied product identity or ordinary capability statements may remain as explicitly labeled working facts in a bounded revision when that is useful. Repeat them under unresolved inputs and request the smallest inspectable confirmation. This allowance does not apply to prices, metrics, outcomes, comparisons, superlatives, testimonials, customer identities, guarantees, certifications, legal or regulatory status, safety, health, finance, employment, privacy, or other material-risk claims.

## Claim Decisions

Use the smallest supported wording. Preserve the source's population, period, geography, plan, version, method, permission, and limitations.

| Claim type | Minimum review check |
| --- | --- |
| Metric or outcome | Definition, measured value, population, period, method, limitations |
| Comparison or superlative | Compared set, method, date, market, direct substantiation |
| Testimonial or customer result | Exact wording, identity, context, authenticity, use permission |
| Price, discount, trial, refund, guarantee | Current approved terms, market, currency, eligibility, duration, renewal, exclusions |
| Award or certification | Issuer, recipient, exact scope, date or validity, current source |
| Capability or compatibility | Current product source, plan, version, market, format, platform limits |
| Regulatory or sensitive statement | Current primary source, jurisdiction, product category, approved wording, appropriate review |

When support is unavailable, flag, remove, or narrow the copy to a separately supported fact. Do not invent a weaker percentage, unnamed customers, approximate prices, generic approval, or a substitute capability. A qualifier does not validate an unsupported claim.

## Conflicts

Keep conflicting sources visible. Prefer a current approved primary source that fits the exact scope over an older page, broader source, or stakeholder preference. Do not average values, choose the more persuasive value, or silently resolve a conflict.

For voice conflicts, prefer the current approved voice or legal source. For factual conflicts, revise only to the common supported truth or omit the disputed statement until resolved.

## Public And Private Sources

Start with the submitted copy and attributable public artifacts. Private analytics, CRM, interviews, research, account data, and internal documents are optional unless the requested claim specifically depends on them. Their absence is `unavailable`, not failure.

When attributable private evidence is supplied, use it only within its stated scope, date, permission, and confidentiality boundary. Do not expose private material beyond what the requested artifact needs.

## Risk Boundary

Never invent or approve health, financial, safety, legal, regulatory, employment, privacy, or other sensitive outcomes. If current applicability cannot be established, mark support unavailable, remove the statement from usable copy, and request the appropriate primary source or qualified review. A disclaimer does not make an unsupported claim acceptable.
