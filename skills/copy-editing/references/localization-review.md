# Localization Review

Localize the decision and meaning, not the source sentence structure.

## Separate Context Fields

Record market, language, locale, and channel independently. Do not infer one from another.

| Field | Review question |
| --- | --- |
| Market | Where is the offer made and the audience decision made? |
| Language | Which language must the asset use? |
| Locale | Which regional spelling, punctuation, formatting, terminology, and conventions apply? |
| Channel | Where will the audience encounter and act on the copy? |

Simplified Chinese does not imply China as the market. A China market does not imply Chinese-language copy. Use the declared channel; do not switch platforms because of language or market.

## Native Adaptation

1. Extract the source's audience situation, factual claims, proof scope, desired action, voice, qualifiers, and risk boundaries.
2. Identify idioms, metaphors, wordplay, source-language rhythm, abstract noun strings, and culturally dependent references.
3. Rebuild the message in the target language's natural information order for the declared audience and channel.
4. Recheck every claim against the same evidence record. Translation cannot strengthen, universalize, or localize proof that does not fit the market.
5. Put each material meaning change in the exact change ledger.

Do not preserve awkward syntax merely to look traceable. Traceability comes from the original, fact map, revision, and ledger.

## Simplified Chinese

Write native, concise Simplified Chinese with Chinese punctuation and a locally natural CTA. Prefer concrete reader situations, visible product facts, and clear actions. Keep approved product, entity, plan, price, date, unit, and legal names exact.

Remove literal translations of English hype, identity labels, aggressive metaphors, stacked adjectives, forced rhyme, and unsupported emotional intensity. Do not automatically introduce expressions such as `颠覆`, `秒杀`, `神器`, `王者`, `遥遥领先`, `轻松拿捏`, `秒变大神`, or `闭眼入`.

Do not replace audience research with stereotypes about Chinese or international audiences.

## China

Treat China as a declared market boundary. Keep the user-declared language, locale, and channel. Do not assume WeChat, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Kuaishou, Baidu, an app store, a payment method, or a distribution route.

Use current direct sources for time-sensitive platform requirements, sector restrictions, required disclosures, certifications, approvals, prices, terms, availability, data handling, or distribution claims. If the relevant current source is absent, mark the point `unavailable`; do not rely on memory.

Financial, medical, education, employment, safety, privacy, and other sensitive copy requires source scope appropriate to China and qualified review where applicable. Do not call the copy compliant or publish-ready merely because risky wording was removed.

## International Contexts

Do not treat `international` as one locale. Preserve the declared country, region, multi-market boundary, and exclusions. Keep currency, date, units, tax, availability, prices, guarantees, proof, and legal terms tied to market-specific sources.

For assets whose language differs from the market's dominant language, keep the requested language. Adapt terminology and conventions to the declared locale without translating into another language or changing the channel.

## Localization Completion Check

Confirm that the revision is native rather than literal; market, language, locale, and channel remain separate; supported facts retain their exact scope; no new local proof or platform assumption appeared; the CTA still names the same action; and every material meaning change is ledgered.
