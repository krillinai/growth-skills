## Version Conflicts And Canonical Records

Inventory every governing artifact before naming one canonical record.

| Artifact / owner / authority | Scope / definition | Version / effective period | Overlapping fields | Conflict / limitation | Acknowledgement / downstream consumers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Classify conflicts as `definition`, `scope`, `decision`, `authority`, `data`, `procedure`, `timing`, `market`, `permission`, `risk`, or `outcome`. Preserve each original. Create a contradiction ledger showing exact fields, affected populations and periods, accountable resolver, resolution evidence, canonical version, coexistence or exception, amendment, correction, supersession, expiry, archive, and unresolved effect.

Legal, product, finance, data, operations, support, and local-market artifacts may govern different decision classes. Do not let teams select the easiest artifact, infer priority from title, overwrite dissent, or manually propagate changes without version and acknowledgement. Track each downstream procedure, system, metric, communication, enablement asset, support article, report, and integration as `notified`, `acknowledged`, `accepted`, `rejected`, `blocked`, `updated`, `verified`, `diverged`, `expired`, or `not applicable` with evidence.

Bound coexistence of old and new states by eligible population, reason, owner, start, exit, exception, support, metric, risk, review, and expiry. An indefinite mixed state is an unresolved change, not completion.

Routing is not completion, approval, remediation, or authorization.

## Market Transfer

Treat each market as a distinct customer, product, commercial, operational, data, governance, and evidence boundary. Revalidate:

- customer problems, roles, eligibility, value, burden, trust, accessibility, adoption path, and support;
- legal entity, product version, offer, contract, price, tax and invoice workflow, payment, refund, and entitlement;
- channels, platforms, app distribution, identity, devices, connectivity, vendors, integrations, and availability;
- language, locale, content, communications, holidays, time zones, training, staffing, capacity, and incident coverage;
- data source, identity, consent, retention, transfer, metrics, baselines, maturity, privacy, security, and audit;
- local owner, reviewer, approver, authorizer, cutover, stop, rollback, correction, exit, and residual obligations.

For China, keep market, Simplified Chinese, currency, and legal or operating context separate. Use current direct sources and locally accountable expertise. Do not copy US owners, permissions, providers, messages, training, metrics, data practices, approvals, authorization, or performance assumptions. Do not make legal, tax, regulatory, accounting, provider-availability, approval, authorization, or performance conclusions from a generic framework.

## Privacy, Fairness, And Dissent

Use purpose-limited, aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary data. Record purpose, authority, consent or other accountable basis, source, population, access, use, retention, correction, deletion, audit, small-cell suppression, and owner.

Do not use or expose credentials, private messages, health data, compensation, protected traits, performance reviews, financial details, precise locations, raw identities, or full activity histories when bounded evidence suffices. Do not infer attitude, capability, intent, loyalty, or resistance from nonuse, questions, silence, errors, attendance, or activity traces.

Diagnose barriers separately: awareness, comprehension, authority, eligibility, access, permission, relevance, value, capability, capacity, workload, reliability, accessibility, trust, support, incentive conflict, and deliberate rejection. Preserve questions, dissent, anonymous or safe feedback where appropriate, nonretaliation, accessibility, correction, appeal, and independent review.

Do not score, rank, shame, discipline, price, deny service to, terminate, promote, hire, fire, or otherwise make consequential decisions about named employees or customers. Route such decisions to accountable HR, legal, risk, customer, privacy, and domain owners under separate authority and safeguards.

## External-Action Specifications

Default to local analysis and approval-ready requests. Separate these states:

```text
proposed -> reviewed -> decided -> approved -> authorized -> assigned
-> executed -> technically verified -> operationally verified -> adopted
-> outcome matured -> independently confirmed -> closed
```

An action request must specify:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Object | Exact system, record, message, cohort, permission, flag, data, workflow, or artifact and version |
| Scope | Entity, population, market, period, inclusions, exclusions, and residual obligations |
| Authority | Requester, owner, reviewer, approver, authorizer, executor, verifier, stop and rollback authority |
| Preconditions | Decision, readiness, capacity, access, privacy, security, legal, migration, support, and evidence gates |
| Controls | Dry run, least privilege, segregation of duties, staging, cohorting, dual control, monitoring, audit |
| Failure | Guardrails, hold, stop, rollback, fallback, recovery, correction, communication, and escalation |
| Completion | Technical, data, operational, customer, downstream, adoption, outcome, and independent proof as applicable |
| Lifecycle | Review, expiry, archive, retention, deletion, decommissioning, and remaining owner |

Do not log in, export, join, send, contact, assign, enroll, approve, authorize, configure, migrate, deploy, cut over, revoke, delete, decommission, or mutate merely because the user requested a plan or audit. Do not claim an action occurred without attributable execution and verification evidence under task-level authority.
