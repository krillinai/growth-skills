# Research And Sources

Use the smallest authorized source set that can discriminate the decision. Broader collection is not automatically better intelligence.

## Source Classes

| Source | Can support | Cannot establish alone |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Official product, documentation, status, security, legal, or pricing page | Visible claims, documented states, terms, and displayed offers at capture time | Customer use, quality, realized value, prevalence, or private performance |
| Release note, announcement, event, webinar, or executive statement | Announced intent or described change with date and scope | Complete rollout, adoption, outcome, or confidential roadmap |
| Public product observation | Observed interface, workflow, access, or behavior under declared conditions | All plans, accounts, markets, reliability, or customer outcomes |
| Customer interview, win-loss, support, review, or community report | Attributable experience under a bounded context | Population prevalence or causal effect without suitable design |
| Partner, seller, analyst, database, news, or estimate | A reported view, triangulation input, or investigation lead | Verified private metric or independent truth by label alone |
| Hiring, patent, traffic, search, social, or advertising signal | Observable activity and bounded scenario input | Product delivery, market share, revenue, intent, or effect |
| Anonymous rumor or leak | A verification lead only | A fact suitable for publication or customer use |

Record publisher incentives and dependence. Multiple pages repeating one press release are one lineage, not independent corroboration.

## Research Sequence

1. Freeze the decision, customer job, market, alternative boundary, criteria, cutoff, and allowed access.
2. Inventory existing sources and evidence gaps before collecting more.
3. Search by customer job, alternatives, switching, workflow, category, product, offer, plan, geography, and date.
4. Capture exact claims, source class, URL or artifact ID, product and offer version, market, publication and capture dates, scope, and limitations.
5. Seek counterevidence and alternatives that could reverse the preferred conclusion.
6. Triangulate consequential claims with independent lineages and compatible units.
7. Stop when remaining uncertainty does not change the bounded decision or requires a specialist method.

Do not use search rank as market rank, review count as customers, traffic as demand, job openings as growth, social attention as adoption, or public logos as retained accounts.

## Freshness And Access

Set freshness by volatility and consequence. Pricing, product availability, provider policies, AI capabilities, integrations, and regional access may need current direct verification. Historical positioning, case evidence, or category evolution can remain useful when its period is explicit.

Track `available`, `blocked`, `gated`, `removed`, `ambiguous`, `not found`, and `not attempted` as access observations separate from evidence states. A blocked or removed page is not proof that the capability or offer is absent.

Never bypass login, paywall, robots, CAPTCHA, rate limit, private community, customer portal, contractual limit, or technical control. Do not create deceptive accounts, impersonate buyers, misrepresent company information, pressure employees, or use leaked and improperly obtained data. Public, licensed, supplied, consented, or explicitly authorized sources are acceptable within their terms and the task boundary.

## Quotes, Translations, And Claims

Keep exact quotes attributable to the source and separate bounded paraphrases from interpretation. Preserve surrounding context, speaker or publisher, date, market, language, and translation method. Do not translate one market's availability, category, price, policy, or customer proof into another market fact.

For vendor claims, record the original wording and qualifiers. Avoid upgrading `may`, `up to`, `from`, `beta`, `selected customers`, or `coming soon` into unconditional facts. For reviews or interviews, preserve role, use case, product version, recency, selection bias, and whether the statement describes a fact, experience, inference, or preference.

## Coverage And Stopping

Report source coverage separately from conclusions:

- alternatives reviewed versus known and unknown boundary;
- products, editions, markets, and dates covered;
- criteria with verified, inferred, unavailable, and not-applicable evidence;
- direct versus derivative lineages;
- contradictions and stale evidence;
- inaccessible sources and ethical limits;
- next source whose result could change the decision.

Never claim exhaustive, complete, objective, or independently verified intelligence unless the exact bounded standard is demonstrated.
