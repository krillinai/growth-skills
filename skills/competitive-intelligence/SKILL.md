# Competitive Intelligence

Build a versioned, evidence-traced view of the alternatives a defined customer can choose in a defined situation. Keep public facts, vendor claims, customer reports, observed behavior, private outcomes, hypotheses, and strategic implications separate; competitive intelligence reduces decision uncertainty, not uncertainty itself.

Read [intelligence-contract.md](references/intelligence-contract.md) before accepting competitor, customer, capability, price, market, or performance evidence. Read [research-and-sources.md](references/research-and-sources.md) before collecting, classifying, quoting, or refreshing sources. Read [comparison-and-change-analysis.md](references/comparison-and-change-analysis.md) before building matrices, normalizing offers, scoring, or monitoring changes. Read [decisions-and-governance.md](references/decisions-and-governance.md) before recommending implications, transferring findings across markets, or handling private evidence. Read [output-contract.md](references/output-contract.md) before delivery. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `landscape` | Discover and bound alternatives for one customer job, buying situation, market, and decision |
| `compare` | Compare a defined set of alternatives against explicit decision criteria and evidence |
| `monitor` | Detect, verify, version, and route changes that may alter an existing decision |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, product or portfolio, customer and buying unit, job and trigger, market, language, locale, segment, alternative boundary, time horizon, evidence cutoff, review date, and external-action boundary. Public pages can verify visible wording, documented capabilities, displayed prices, release statements, and public proof at a dated capture. They cannot establish complete market coverage, private customers, realized value, win rate, retention, revenue, cost, market share, causality, confidential roadmaps, or future outcomes.

## Freeze The Intelligence Contract

Record product, edition, module, service, bundle, platform, version, status, owner, and claimed category; user, buyer, payer, administrator, partner, beneficiary, account, and buying-group roles; customer job, trigger, current workflow, decision criteria, urgency, switching conditions, and exclusion rules; direct product, adjacent product, general tool, internal build, manual process, spreadsheet, agency, service, partner, and inaction alternatives; capability, access, release, plan, entitlement, integration, quality, performance, reliability, trust, support, proof, price, package, discount, contract, tax, currency, channel, distribution, and availability states; source, publisher, source type, exact claim, URL or artifact ID, capture date, effective date, market, scope, freshness, contradiction, confidence, evidence state, privacy, and requested action.

Use exactly `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A vendor, customer, employee, analyst, or stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Never turn a missing source, inaccessible page, omitted feature, old review, or unanswered question into negative proof.

## Map Alternatives By Buying Situation

Start with what the customer would do if each product did not exist. A vendor list is not a competitive set. Include direct competitors, adjacent products, general-purpose tools, internal development, manual work, services, agencies, partner solutions, delay, and doing nothing where they can satisfy or avoid the same job.

Split the landscape when the customer, role, job, trigger, use case, product tier, geography, language, platform, route to market, company stage, regulation, price sensitivity, or switching state changes the choice. Preserve emerging, unknown, disputed, and excluded alternatives with reasons. Do not calculate market share or total addressable market from search results, vendor claims, review counts, traffic estimates, or an incomplete list.

## Protect Source Meaning

Separate first-party vendor claims, official documentation, release and status records, public product observations, pricing displays, customer and partner reports, reviews, win-loss evidence, sales reports, analyst or database estimates, news, social posts, rumors, and internal interpretations. Record provenance, capture date, supported scope, product and offer version, market, limitations, and counterevidence.

Do not fabricate citations, quotes, capabilities, customers, prices, scores, or missing cells. Do not bypass authentication, paywalls, robots, rate limits, CAPTCHAs, private communities, or access controls; impersonate prospects; create deceptive accounts; pressure employees; buy leaked data; or claim complete coverage. Use public, licensed, supplied, consented, or otherwise authorized evidence only.

## Compare Decision-Relevant States

Define comparison criteria before reading the preferred answer. Connect each criterion to a customer job, buying decision, operating constraint, or risk. For capabilities, distinguish announced, documented, observed, beta, limited, generally available, deprecated, removed, inaccessible, and unknown; then record plan, entitlement, geography, platform, language, version, integration, quality, reliability, support, and fit.

For offers, preserve list, negotiated, promotional, legacy, and observed realized price separately. Normalize only scenarios with compatible customer units, quantities, usage, commitment, currency and exchange-rate date, tax, minimums, overages, implementation, support, discounts, and eligibility. Cheapest list price is not lowest total cost or best value.

Feature counts and opaque scores do not establish superiority. If scoring is necessary for a declared decision, show criteria, weights, evidence, missingness, direction, sensitivity, and evidence coverage separately. Do not penalize unavailable evidence or let a total hide a verified blocker. Preserve tradeoffs, counterevidence, non-feature advantages, and conditions under which another choice wins.

## Monitor Changes Without Predicting Roadmaps

Version every observation. Separate source publication, observation, announced, effective, rollout, and verification dates. Keep historical and superseded states. Classify changes as `added`, `changed`, `removed`, `unchanged`, `reversed`, `rumored`, `disputed`, or `unknown`, with affected product, offer, market, customer, evidence, consequence hypothesis, owner, expiry, and next verification.

Hiring, patents, partnerships, job posts, executive comments, and anonymous reports may nominate scenarios; they do not reveal a confidential roadmap or exact launch, price, performance, adoption, or outcome. Require corroboration proportionate to consequence. Do not publish or operationalize rumors.

## Route Decisions, Do Not Absorb Them

This Skill owns the competitive set, evidence and freshness ledger, product and offer versions, capability and availability states, decision-specific comparison, pricing normalization, change log, uncertainty, and intelligence governance. Route customer research design and synthesis to `customer-research`; positioning and category decisions to `positioning`; segments to `icp-segmentation`; market sizing, entry, and local readiness to `market-entry-strategy`; pricing and packaging to `monetization`; launch decisions to `product-launch-strategy`; seller assets to `sales-enablement`; attribution to `attribution-analysis`; causal tests to `experiment-design`; channel allocation to `acquisition-strategy`; and primary growth constraints to `growth-diagnosis`.

Competitive timing can correlate with win rate, search, conversion, retention, or revenue without causing the change. Preserve alternative mechanisms and route counterfactual questions. Intelligence can trigger a decision review; it does not approve a roadmap, price, campaign, budget, claim, or customer action.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, product, customer, job, market, segment, alternative boundary, cutoff, review date, and action boundary;
2. source and evidence ledger with provenance, scope, freshness, contradictions, access limits, and unavailable inputs;
3. alternative landscape with inclusion, exclusion, emerging, disputed, and unknown states;
4. product, capability, access, offer, price, distribution, proof, trust, and switching comparison;
5. scoring or normalization logic, missingness, sensitivity, and evidence coverage when used;
6. versioned change log, rumor queue, consequence hypotheses, verification triggers, and expiry;
7. bounded implications, counterinterpretations, decision gates, owners, and specialist handoffs;
8. pinned Playbook sources, privacy controls, next evidence, review cadence, and approval-ready external actions.

For China work, keep market, language, locale, customer, buying situation, product and offer version, category, competitors, app and platform availability, price, currency, tax, payment, distribution, channel, proof, trust, identity, consent, data access, storage, transfer, review, and local owner separate. Do not transfer a US matrix through translation or currency conversion alone, and do not provide legal, tax, accounting, or provider conclusions.

## External-Action Boundary

This Skill may read supplied artifacts and public or otherwise authorized sources when the task and tools allow it, and may create local artifacts. Do not access internal analytics, CRM, sales, call, support, billing, finance, warehouse, app-store, ad, employee, customer, paid research, private community, or competitor accounts; bypass controls; export or reidentify people or accounts; contact customers, competitors, employees, partners, analysts, or vendors; create identities; change battlecards, claims, positioning, product, roadmap, prices, offers, campaigns, bids, budgets, alerts, or production data; publish; send; spend; deploy; or claim implementation or business improvement without separate task-level authorization and required controls.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that mode, decision, owner, product, customer, job, market, segment, alternative boundary, inclusion and exclusion rules, source classes, provenance, dates, versions, evidence states, contradictions, capability and access states, price basis, comparison criteria, missingness, uncertainty, changes, rumors, implications, causal boundary, privacy, market transfer, owners, review cadence, handoffs, pinned sources, and external actions are explicit; a vendor list is not the market; claims are not outcomes; omission is not absence; features are not value; list price is not total cost; correlation is not causality; signals are not roadmaps; unavailable is not failure; and no unauthorized action occurred.
