# Programmatic SEO System Contract

## Opportunity Contract

Capture:

- decision, owner, customer, job, query or intent class, market, language, locale, and device;
- product, page role, qualified next action, first value, retained value, and business outcome;
- demand source, query set, date, population, limitations, counterevidence, and seasonality;
- supply entity, stable ID, source, authority, license, coverage, change rate, and known candidate count;
- transformation that creates page-level utility beyond raw source reproduction;
- alternatives already satisfying intent and why this product can serve it credibly;
- expected maintenance, review, support, infrastructure, rendering, moderation, and removal cost;
- platform dependency, search risk, customer harm, brand, privacy, fairness, and applicable review;
- pilot size and sampling basis, decision window, maturity, success, contradiction, stop, and rollback rules.

Public evidence can support a hypothesis. It cannot verify private conversion, activation, retention, contribution, index coverage, or causal impact. Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable`; keep unsupported stakeholder statements as `reported signal` without an evidence state.

## Field Contract

For every field record canonical name, page family, definition, entity, type, enum or format, unit and currency, source, source field, evidence status, authority, license, retrieval and update time, freshness SLA, required or conditional status, null behavior, conflict precedence, validation, sensitivity, display rule, claim use, downstream consumer, owner, version, and retirement.

Never substitute a similar entity or infer a value from a name. Preserve unknown, unavailable, conflicting, expired, zero, not applicable, and intentionally withheld as different states.

## Page Family Contract

Define:

- family, intent, eligible entity and candidate rule;
- unique value and page-specific transformation;
- required and optional fields plus minimum useful state;
- common, conditional, evidence, comparison, CTA, and related-page modules;
- title, heading, metadata, visible breadcrumb, and accessible-name inputs;
- URL, slug, stable ID, parameter, pagination, locale, canonical, and duplicate rules;
- navigation, internal-link source and target eligibility, anchors, orphan prevention, and limits;
- response, robots, sitemap, indexability, and removal behavior per page state;
- product continuation, event lineage, owners, review, version, and rollback.

## Inventory Reconciliation

Track candidate, generated, validation pass, review pass, publishable, published, live, crawlable, canonical, sitemap, internally linked, indexed when verified, qualified-traffic, activated, retained, and retired sets separately. Every transition needs a reproducible rule and count. Never treat missing private index evidence as a failed page.
