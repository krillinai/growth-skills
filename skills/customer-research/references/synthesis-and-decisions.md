# Customer Research Synthesis And Decisions

## Prepare Evidence

Inventory every source and preserve raw artifacts unchanged. Separate facilitator notes, participant words, transcription uncertainty, translation, timestamps, and missing sections. Use one atomic evidence unit per bounded statement or observed action.

Do not clean up grammar inside quotation marks. Use ellipses or brackets only when meaning remains faithful and the transformation is visible. Label translations and paraphrases; retain the original text when permitted.

## Code And Form Themes

1. code concrete triggers, jobs, alternatives, actions, constraints, outcomes, risks, and decision criteria;
2. distinguish participant explanation from observed behavior and researcher inference;
3. cluster codes only where a shared mechanism or decision implication exists;
4. search for contradictions, negative cases, role differences, and missing sample cells;
5. compare findings with behavioral, retention, revenue, support, and experiment evidence when available;
6. version the theme and record what evidence would weaken or retire it.

For each finding report supporting evidence, counterevidence, source coverage, sample boundary, confidence language, and the decision it could change. A vivid quote does not outweigh contradictory behavior or an incompatible sample.

## Counts And Prevalence

Use counts only when coding is reproducible and the denominator is clear. Say "6 of 12 interviewed participants in this purposive sample" rather than "50% of customers." Do not combine overlapping codes as if they were mutually exclusive.

Qualitative frequency indicates recurrence within the collected evidence; it does not estimate market prevalence. Use a suitable survey or behavioral population when prevalence changes the decision.

## Segments, Personas, And Jobs

Keep segment, persona, cohort, lifecycle state, and propensity separate. Create a segment only when observable customer conditions change a product, positioning, channel, sales, service, or monetization decision. A role or persona does not establish account fit; a cohort pattern does not automatically define an operating segment.

Describe a job with trigger, desired progress, current alternative, constraints, outcome, and context. Do not turn every phrase into a universal Jobs-to-be-Done statement.

## Decision Record

Translate findings into:

- decision and owner;
- supported customer condition or mechanism;
- action, non-action, or bounded hypothesis;
- evidence and counterevidence;
- affected and excluded population;
- customer, trust, accessibility, economic, and operational guardrails;
- next validation through product evidence, survey, experiment, or follow-up research;
- review date and conditions that would revise the conclusion.

Recommendations must follow the evidence. Do not prescribe a feature, campaign, segment, price, or message merely because participants requested it; identify the underlying job, constraint, alternative, and decision first.
