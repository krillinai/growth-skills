# Elena Verna PLG Growth Lens

Apply Elena Verna's public methods to product-led and AI-native growth systems. This is a method lens, not an impersonation, endorsement, or substitute for first-party evidence.

## Establish the System

Collect product, segment, value event, retention, self-serve journey, free model, payer, pricing metric, expansion path, product signals, sales motion, channel mix, trust constraints, economics, stage, and decision. Mark unknowns; never invent metrics, company facts, or Verna's views.

## Select the Lens

Read [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md). Choose one primary and at most two supporting lenses.

| Symptom | Primary lens |
| --- | --- |
| Growth hiring is expected to repair weak value | PMF before growth organization |
| Teams run disconnected acquisition and retention tactics | Evolving growth model |
| Self-serve adoption stalls before repeated value | PLG company system |
| Sales contacts many free users with weak conversion | Product-Led Sales signals |
| Free access creates cost but little adoption | Freemium-versus-trial design |
| Revenue goals do not identify an operating lever | Monetization system |
| Paid acquisition hides weak advocacy or trust | Earned-first distribution |
| Experiment volume rises without strategic learning | Experimentation boundaries |
| AI product fit or feature advantage decays quickly | Moving PMF and defensibility |
| Launches produce announcements but little adoption | Concentrated launch distribution |
| Discovery depends on one search surface | Discovery portfolio |

## Diagnose

1. Define customer value, business outcome, and scope.
2. Map acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, expansion, and reinvestment.
3. Label evidence `verified`, `inferred`, or `missing`.
4. Select one system constraint and preserve alternatives.
5. Compare interventions by retained impact, reach, evidence, economics, trust, reversibility, and organizational control.
6. Recommend three bounded 30-day actions.
7. Define an experiment or evidence plan with population, intervention, primary metric, guardrails, maturity, and ship/iterate/stop rule.
8. State blind spots and an alternative lens.

Do not equate PLG with a free plan, PLS with contacting every active user, experimentation with strategy, revenue with an actionable KPI, a rebrand with demand creation, or shipping velocity with durable PMF.

## Deliver

Use: **scope and value**, **lens applicability**, **growth-system map**, **primary constraint and alternatives**, **evidence**, **three actions**, **experiment**, **metrics and guardrails**, **blind spots**, **sources**.

Read [sources.md](references/sources.md) before citing. Keep company examples time-bound and distinguish operating roles, advisory patterns, and public commentary.

## Required Boundary

> This analysis applies frameworks documented in Elena Verna's public work. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a simulation of Elena Verna.

Never write as Verna, imitate her voice, imply private knowledge, or claim she would make a decision.

## Common Mistakes

- Hiring growth before product value and retention are credible.
- Copying a PLG or freemium motion across incompatible products.
- Using product signals without testing incremental sales value.
- Optimizing local conversion while harming trust or retained value.
- Treating feature velocity as a moat in AI markets.
- Depending on paid media, SEO, or an answer engine as the only discovery system.
