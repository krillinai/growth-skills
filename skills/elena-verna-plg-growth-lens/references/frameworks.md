# Framework Reference

## PMF Before Growth Organization

Test product value, activation, mature retention, executive alignment, and data access before expecting a growth hire to scale the system. Match the first role to the actual constraint and give it the required authority and capabilities.

## Evolving Growth Model

```text
product value
-> acquisition and activation
-> retention and habit
-> monetization and expansion
-> reinvestment and distribution
```

For each transition record segment, rate, quality, cycle time, cost, saturation, and owner. Rebuild the model when audience, product, price, technology, or route to market changes.

## PLG Company System

Map discovery, first value, adoption, collaboration, repeated use, expansion, and support. Define what users can accomplish without human help and where marketing, sales, success, data, or security must intervene. PLG is cross-functional infrastructure, not a channel label.

## Product-Led Sales Signals

Define product-qualified users and accounts using activation, depth, breadth, team adoption, repetition, expansion behavior, and evidence of an organization-level problem. Compare assisted versus unassisted cohorts and measure incremental conversion, retained revenue, sales cost, and customer value.

## Freemium-Versus-Trial Design

Choose based on time to value, natural usage cadence, collaboration, bottom-up adoption, serving cost, buying process, and upgrade reason. Free access must deliver real value while preserving a credible paid boundary. A trial must last long enough to experience the natural value cycle.

## Monetization System

Decompose revenue into payer, paid value, pricing metric, package, trigger, conversion path, expansion, friction, cost, and retention. Revenue is an outcome; operate on these inputs. Keep product accountable when product behavior shapes conversion and expansion.

## Earned-First Distribution

Test advocacy, community, social proof, templates, ecosystems, partnerships, product outputs, and word of mouth before using paid spend to mask weak positioning or retention. Add paid scale when marginal economics and retained quality are understood.

## Experimentation Boundaries

Rank hypotheses by strategic impact, uncertainty, reach, reversibility, and learning value. Test local surfaces when they matter, but escalate to product, positioning, packaging, or model changes when the constraint is systemic. Evaluate whether friction creates useful qualification, setup, information, or trust.

## Moving PMF and Defensibility

In fast AI markets, repeatedly validate customer problem, activation, retention, willingness to pay, and competitive alternatives. Pair shipping speed with customer evidence. Invest beyond replicable features in data, networks, brand, trust, security, compliance, hardware, distribution, or ecosystem position.

## Concentrated Launch Distribution

Coordinate product substance, demonstrations, founders, employees, customer proof, community, and response around a focused launch window. Measure qualified adoption and retention, not announcement volume.

## Discovery Portfolio

Map SEO, AEO/GEO, brand, proprietary data, communities, integrations, partnerships, ecosystems, and product usage. Track contribution, retained quality, dependency, saturation, and defensibility for each surface.
