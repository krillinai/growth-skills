# Ad Creative Contract

## Intake

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, campaign or evergreen boundary, dates, budget assumptions, and allowed action;
- product, offer, price, incentive, availability, market, language, locale, and product surface;
- audience, customer unit, job, trigger, problem, lifecycle or buying state, exclusions, and current alternative;
- positioning, category, value pillars, promise, objections, proof, CTA, landing path, first value, and retained value;
- channel, platform, account, objective, placement, device, format, delivery, and sourced current constraints;
- brand, voice, visual system, creator identity, logo, product, image, footage, music, font, testimonial, and trademark rights;
- claim wording, source, scope, date, conditions, counterevidence, approval, prohibited wording, and expiration;
- concept and variant inventory, lineage, versions, performance evidence, fatigue signals, and active states;
- primary, downstream, economic, trust, safety, complaint, accessibility, and rights metrics;
- production owner, reviewer, approver, due date, capacity, cost, QA, stop, rollback, and archive.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder or platform statement without inspectable support may use `reported signal` with no evidence state.

## Source Boundaries

Public creative can verify visible content and public delivery context at a dated capture. It cannot establish audience targeting, ownership, permission, spend, attribution, customer quality, creator agreement, incrementality, or business outcome.

Do not infer endorsement from a logo, permission from a public post, authenticity from a creator label, customer prevalence from comments, or causality from a top-performing asset.

## Rights Record

Record asset, source, owner, license or agreement, media, platform, placement, market, term, paid and organic rights, edits, derivatives, whitelisting or identity use, exclusivity, music, voice, likeness, testimonial, attribution, expiration, evidence artifact, and reviewer. Missing rights are unavailable and block affected production or use; they are not assumed.

## Claim Record

Record exact proposed wording, claim type, audience, product and market scope, source, proof type, date, conditions, limitations, counterevidence, owner, approval, expiration, and prohibited stronger version. Never use fabricated testimonials, rankings, scarcity, guarantees, outcomes, comparisons, certifications, or sensitive claims.
