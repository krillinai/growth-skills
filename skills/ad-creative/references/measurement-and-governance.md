# Measurement And Governance

## Evidence Layers

Keep separate:

1. concept and production evidence;
2. platform delivery and cost;
3. attention and engagement under exact definitions;
4. platform-attributed response;
5. landing and product progression;
6. qualified customer, refund, contribution, and retained value;
7. incremental effect from a credible counterfactual;
8. trust, complaint, safety, accessibility, rights, and policy outcomes.

For every measure state entity, numerator, denominator, source, identity, audience, placement, format, market, window, attribution, maturity, currency, cost scope, and version. A zero denominator is unavailable.

## Portfolio Coverage

Summarize concept, audience, pillar, angle, objection, proof, offer, creator, format, placement, market, and journey-stage coverage. Report asset count separately. Concentration may represent a strong hypothesis, incomplete exploration, delivery preference, available rights, production constraint, or attribution bias. Do not call it a defect without decision impact.

## Fatigue Diagnosis

Require comparable time series and delivery context. Check audience size and saturation, reach and repeated delivery, spend and auction, placement, objective, bid or delivery changes, seasonality, offer, price, destination, product, inventory, policy, measurement, rights, concept mix, variant age, and downstream quality.

Use `fatigue signal` until evidence distinguishes creative decay. Never use a universal frequency or days-live threshold. A refresh can test a new execution; a new concept should test a new customer or value hypothesis.

## Creative Experiments

Define decision, concept or execution hypothesis, unit, audience eligibility, assignment and exposure, control, variants, unchanged elements, channel and placement, primary measure, landing and downstream guardrails, rights and policy checks, sample and power where applicable, window, maturity, decision, stop, and rollback. Delivery algorithms can create unequal exposure; preserve actual delivery and avoid naive asset comparisons.

## Localization

Validate local audience, problem, alternatives, terminology, value, proof, creator credibility, product and offer availability, channel, format, rights, music, subtitles, CTA, landing path, consent, review, and measurement. Translation alone does not validate a concept in another market.

## Learning Record

For each decision store concept and asset versions, evidence period, compatible scope, delivery, immediate results, downstream results, guardrails, limitations, alternative explanations, decision, owner, next brief, and review date. A winning execution is evidence about its tested context, not a timeless template.
