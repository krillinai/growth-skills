## Concept Versus Variant

Likely new concepts change customer unit, situation, problem, desired outcome, alternative, value pillar, central belief, objection, offer, or proof mechanism. Likely variants change visual treatment, hook, opening frame, edit, crop, duration, aspect ratio, caption, subtitle, sound, CTA phrasing, or creator expression without changing the hypothesis.

If a change affects both, record a child concept with explicit lineage rather than forcing it into one class.

## Creative Brief

Include concept and source IDs, single-sentence hypothesis, intended audience and state, customer tension, value pillar, angle, supportable promise, proof, offer, CTA, landing continuity, narrative beats, mandatory product behavior, visual and copy hierarchy, creator role, format and placement, duration or dimensions only from current supplied requirements, safe areas, captions, audio alternatives, contrast, text size, motion and flashing safety, localization, rights, prohibited elements, variants, deliverables, filename and version rules, review gates, QA, measurement, owner, and completion proof.

## Variant Matrix

Vary one purposeful execution dimension or a declared bundle. For each variant state parent concept, changed element, unchanged hypothesis, rationale, placement, audience, rights, expected observable mechanism, metric, guardrail, and test compatibility. Do not create a full combinatorial matrix merely because it is possible.

## Review Gates

Review in order: product truth and offer; claim and proof; rights and creator use; market and language; channel and placement requirements; brand; accessibility and safety; landing continuity; technical export; measurement and lineage; approval and archive. Record exceptions and approvers.

## QA

Check exact copy, prices and dates, CTA and destination, product UI, logos, creators, subtitles, captions, audio, crops, safe areas, readability, contrast, flashing and motion, file integrity, duration and dimensions, tracking labels, concept and variant IDs, locale, rights expiration, prohibited content, and final checksum or asset ID where the production system supports it.
