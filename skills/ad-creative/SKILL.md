# Ad Creative

Turn customer evidence and a bounded campaign decision into traceable creative hypotheses, production-ready briefs, governed variants, and a learning system. Optimize qualified customer progression and retained economics, not asset count, attention, clicks, platform conversions, or attributed ROAS alone.

Read [creative-contract.md](references/creative-contract.md) before proposing a concept, claim, creator, asset, or format. Read [concepts-and-production.md](references/concepts-and-production.md) before building concept maps, briefs, storyboards, variant matrices, adaptations, or review workflows. Read [measurement-and-governance.md](references/measurement-and-governance.md) before diagnosing winners, fatigue, tests, rights, or market adaptations. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `concept` | Develop evidence-bounded creative hypotheses and a prioritized concept portfolio |
| `brief` | Produce implementation-ready requirements for one concept and its purposeful variants |
| `audit` | Evaluate existing concepts and assets for coverage, claims, rights, continuity, performance evidence, fatigue, and learning |

Name one primary mode. Keep customer evidence, concept, execution, asset, delivery, attribution, business outcome, and causal conclusion separate.

## Freeze The Creative Contract

Record decision and owner; product, offer, price or incentive; audience, job, trigger, problem, lifecycle or buying state, and exclusions; market, language, locale, channel, placement, device, and format; positioning, value pillars, claims, proof, objections, CTA, landing path, and next value; brand, rights, accessibility, policy, safety, production, capacity, budget assumptions, dates, metrics, maturity, approvals, and external-action boundary.

Start from supplied and attributable public evidence. Private ad exports, creative inventories, research, analytics, CRM, product, sales, and economics are optional. Never invent audience size, customer motives, claims, proof, creator rights, channel access, benchmarks, performance, fatigue, conversion, CAC, revenue, retention, or uplift.

## Define The Concept Before The Asset

Every concept states:

```text
audience situation x problem or belief x value pillar x angle
-> supportable promise and proof mechanism
-> offer and next action
-> landing or product continuation
-> qualified downstream outcome and guardrails
```

A concept changes the audience, problem, belief, value hypothesis, objection, offer, or proof mechanism. An execution variant changes hook, opening, crop, layout, duration, caption, subtitle, sound, creator delivery, aspect ratio, or CTA wording while preserving the hypothesis. Label ambiguous changes and avoid claiming concept diversity from file count.

## Build Evidence-Led Concepts

Create a customer-evidence ledger with source, date, population, exact or bounded language, behavior, alternative, objection, desired outcome, counterevidence, and limitation. Translate evidence into a creative hypothesis, not an invented quote or universal persona.

For every claim record exact wording, source, scope, market, date, proof, conditions, counterevidence, approval, expiration, and prohibited stronger wording. A demo, testimonial, benchmark, public logo, review, certification, creator statement, and observed customer outcome require different substantiation and rights.

Build portfolio coverage across validated audience situations, value pillars, objections, proof mechanisms, offers, formats, creators, and journey stages. Coverage is not a quota and concentration is not automatically a defect; explain the decision risk and missing evidence.

## Create Production-Ready Briefs

Specify concept ID and lineage; hypothesis; audience; problem; pillar; angle; promise; proof; offer; CTA; landing continuity; required scenes or information beats; hook and narrative; visual, motion, sound, copy, product, creator, and accessibility requirements; channel and placement variants; safe areas and current sourced platform constraints; prohibited claims and elements; asset and music rights; market and locale; review gates; deliverables; versioning; due date; owner; QA; measurement; and completion proof.

The brief defines what must be produced and why. Route final copy to `copywriting`, images to the relevant image capability, video production or localization to the relevant video capability, and final edits to `copy-editing`. Do not pretend a brief is a finished asset.

## Learn From Delivery Without Overclaiming

Compare only compatible audience, placement, format, objective, offer, destination, market, dates, delivery, attribution, and maturity. Map asset results back to concept and variant lineage. Preserve spend and delivery concentration, attention, qualified response, product value, refunds, contribution, retention, trust, complaints, and rights incidents as separate evidence.

Use `fatigue signal` unless comparable time-series evidence distinguishes creative decay from audience saturation, auction, placement, offer, landing, product, seasonality, policy, or measurement changes. Do not use a universal frequency, duration, hook-rate, asset-count, refresh-cadence, or winner threshold.

Platform attribution helps operate delivery; business reconciliation defines qualified outcomes; incrementality requires a credible counterfactual. Do not copy a winning execution into every market or forecast its performance as constant.

## Deliver In Order

Return:

1. mode, decision, owner, scope, campaign boundary, and external-action boundary;
2. audience, customer, positioning, claim, proof, rights, and market evidence ledger;
3. concept map with concept-versus-variant lineage and portfolio coverage;
4. prioritized hypotheses and creative briefs;
5. asset, creator, format, placement, landing, accessibility, and review requirements;
6. variant and adaptation matrix with versioning and QA;
7. measurement, business reconciliation, experiment, fatigue, and learning plan;
8. production handoffs, owners, approvals, stop rules, and pinned Playbook sources.

Route campaign objectives, audiences, budgets, journeys, and launch operations to `campaign-planning`; platform account diagnosis to the relevant ads audit; positioning to `positioning`; landing pages to `landing-page-audit`; content portfolios to `content-strategy`; and causal tests to `experiment-design`.

For China work, keep market, language, locale, audience, channel, platform, account, placement, creator identity, content and music rights, product surface, app distribution, payment, consent, claims, content review, data access, and applicable rules separate. Do not infer Douyin, Xiaohongshu, Kuaishou, WeChat, Bilibili, Baidu, account access, format limits, creator rights, or performance from China or Simplified Chinese.

This Skill creates and reads local artifacts only. Do not access ad or social accounts, download restricted assets, contact creators or customers, acquire rights, generate final media unless separately routed and authorized, upload assets, create campaigns, change spend or delivery, publish, schedule, launch tests, or claim results.

## Completion Gate

Confirm that decision, audience, problem, pillar, angle, promise, proof, offer, CTA, concept, variants, format, placement, creator, rights, claims, brand, accessibility, landing continuity, market, owners, production, QA, lineage, delivery, attribution, business outcomes, incrementality, fatigue alternatives, tests, guardrails, approvals, and action boundaries are explicit; file count is not concept diversity; attention is not customer value; unsupported assets or claims are absent; market assumptions are not inferred from language; and no external action occurred.
