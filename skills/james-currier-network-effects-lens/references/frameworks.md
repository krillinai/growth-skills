# Framework Reference

## Network Effects Versus Virality

Virality describes how a product spreads. A network effect exists when relevant participation changes the value received by a participant. Test the causal chain:

```text
added relevant participation
-> changed interaction, selection, data, reputation, compatibility, or trust
-> improved participant outcome
-> stronger retention, usage, or willingness to depend on the product
```

Distribution without value reinforcement is not a network effect. Value reinforcement without viral acquisition can still be one.

## Network Unit and White-Hot Center

Define the smallest geographic, categorical, professional, social, organizational, workflow, or temporal boundary that can repeatedly deliver the core interaction. Start where need is urgent and density is achievable.

Measure availability, relevant density, interaction success, time to match or value, repeat interaction, and retention by network unit. Aggregate registrations can conceal fragmented networks.

## Hard Side and Core Interaction

Identify the participant, inventory, expertise, trust, or behavior that most constrains value. Test scarcity, quality, motivation, response, availability, retention, economics, and multi-homing.

Map:

```text
qualified participants
-> discovery or matching
-> successful interaction
-> satisfactory outcome
-> repeat participation
```

Supply count alone does not establish liquidity.

## Marketplace-Type Diagnosis

Classify structural dimensions before selecting tactics: local or global, horizontal or vertical, managed or unmanaged, B2B or consumer, high or low frequency, homogeneous or differentiated supply, and single-home or multi-home behavior.

Use the classification to reason about acquisition, operations, trust, matching, take rate, incentives, expansion, and defensibility. Do not treat the labels as proof of performance.

## Market Network

A Market Network combines:

- professional identity and reputation;
- communication and coordination;
- workflow software;
- a network of relevant participants;
- an exchange or transaction layer.

Test whether the software helps participants do work, the network helps them find or coordinate with one another, and the exchange improves participant value. A CRM with contacts or a SaaS integration directory is not automatically a Market Network.

## Disguised Marketplace

A SaaS, fintech, infrastructure, or workflow product may gradually aggregate supply, demand, services, or transactions. Test whether normal product use creates a credible exchange layer, whether participants consent to that role, and whether the exchange improves outcomes rather than merely adding fees or platform control.

## Multi-Homing and Defensibility

Evaluate unique supply, exclusive relationships, workflow depth, reputation portability, switching cost, interaction frequency, proprietary interaction data, trust, governance, and whether the product becomes a system of record.

Large scale is weak defense when participants can easily use competitors, move reputation, reproduce supply, or receive similar value elsewhere. Network effects can weaken when relevance, quality, economics, trust, or participant control deteriorates.

## Market Effect

Category leadership may attract customers, employees, partners, capital, press, and ecosystem attention, which can reinforce leadership. Treat this as a secondary reinforcement mechanism. Test whether resources convert into participant value and durable performance; attention can reverse.

## AI-Era Network Audit

AI agents can create supply, perform work, search, negotiate, or transact. Re-map:

- who or what is a participant;
- which participant or capability is scarce;
- whether synthetic supply improves quality and trust;
- which interactions create proprietary learning;
- who owns identity, reputation, workflow, and governance;
- whether agents increase multi-homing or commoditize the network.

AI activity volume is not evidence of network value or defense.
