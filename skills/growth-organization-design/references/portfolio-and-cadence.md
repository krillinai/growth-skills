# Growth Organization Portfolio And Cadence

## Portfolio Contract

For each initiative, record:

| Initiative | Outcome and constraint | Eligible reach | Contribution basis | Evidence and uncertainty | Full cost and capacity | Dependencies | Risk and guardrails | Horizon and option value | Owner | Decision and stop rule |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use compatible units only. Relative lift on a small surface may contribute less absolute value than a modest change with broad eligible reach. RICE, ICE, expected value, or similar scores are discussion aids; expose subjective inputs and never use a rank to hide missing evidence, dependencies, risk, maintenance, or opportunity cost.

Where useful, keep these capacity classes visible:

- `core`: protect and improve proven customer value;
- `adjacent`: extend the current model or market;
- `exploratory`: resolve uncertainty about a different mechanism;
- `system`: create reusable decision or execution capacity;
- `maintenance`: reliability, support, compliance, debt, and incident prevention;
- `retirement`: remove flags, tools, reports, campaigns, permissions, and processes.

Do not prescribe a universal percentage split. Reallocate when evidence changes the constraint, expected contribution, capacity, or risk.

## Operating Forum Contract

| Forum | Named decision | Owner | Required inputs | Required participants | Output and record | Cadence trigger | Cancel or merge rule | Escalation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use the fewest recurring forums needed to decide and preserve learning. Working reviews inspect evidence and blockers; readouts decide ship, stop, revise, extend, or investigate; portfolio reviews reallocate; metric reviews change definitions or actions; postmortems improve systems; strategy reviews can change the mandate. Status recitation, universal attendance, meeting hours, and review volume are not outcomes.

## Organization Health Ledger

| Layer | Measure | Definition and source | Baseline | Target basis | Owner | Review | Failure action |
| --- | --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Keep layers independent:

- `outcome`: customer and business movement within the owned boundary;
- `decision`: time, quality, reversal, escalation, and evidence adoption;
- `execution`: active time, dependency wait, diagnosis-to-exposure, rework, and blocked days;
- `learning`: conclusive work, changed decisions, reuse, and repeated mistakes;
- `platform`: adoption, reliability, self-service, queue, support, bypasses, and duplication;
- `portfolio`: capacity by outcome and horizon, stopped work, maintenance, and retirement;
- `sustainability`: incidents, workload, on-call, meetings, attrition, leave, and key-person risk.

Do not construct a composite organization score. Protect minimum cells and interpret people measures as signals requiring context, never individual performance proof.

## Performance And Maintenance

Evaluate controllable contribution through durable customer and business value, decision quality, causal humility, collaboration, learning, reusable system improvement, maintenance, quality, trust, and ethical operation. Do not pay for positive tests, launches, tickets, leads, local KPI movement, or hiding failures.

Every code path, flag, campaign, data flow, model, asset, vendor, permission, report, and operating commitment needs a long-term owner or removal date. Portfolio plans must include incident, support, compliance, migration, and cognitive load. Speed that externalizes maintenance is borrowed capacity.
