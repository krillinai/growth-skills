# Growth Organization Contract

## Decision Context

Capture independently:

- organization decision, sponsor, decision date, horizon, review cadence, and allowed action;
- product, market, language, locale, business model, stage, strategy, product topology, and regulatory context;
- customer state, business outcome, metric contract, guardrails, and current growth constraint with source;
- work required, available levers, capability needs, dependencies, shared surfaces, risk, and local context;
- current teams, mandates, reporting relationships, outcome owners, decision rights, interfaces, queues, workarounds, and conflicts;
- staffing, seniority, capacity, management bandwidth, budget, vendors, temporary support, and hiring constraints;
- initiative portfolio, horizon allocation, maintenance, incidents, technical and operational commitments, and retirement backlog;
- recurring forums, written artifacts, decision records, escalation, postmortems, and communication paths;
- outcome, decision, execution, learning, platform, portfolio, quality, risk, workload, attrition, and sustainability evidence;
- proposed model, alternatives, transition cost, affected people and work, unfinished ownership, review, adjustment, and rollback;
- source artifact, owner, date, version, evidence state, freshness, limitation, and requested external action.

## Evidence States

Use:

- `verified`: directly supported by an attributable inspectable artifact;
- `inferred`: reasoned from named evidence with the inference explicit;
- `unavailable`: required but not supplied or observable;
- `not applicable`: outside the scoped organization decision;
- `reported signal`: attributable stakeholder statement without inspectable support; leave evidence state blank.

Public evidence may verify dated public roles, visible teams, published operating claims, job descriptions, and company statements. It cannot verify private reporting lines, actual authority, workload, staffing adequacy, performance, portfolio quality, meeting usefulness, incidents, attrition causes, employee sentiment, or organization effectiveness.

## Minimum Evidence

Prefer attributable, aggregate, redacted, or pseudonymous artifacts:

- current mandates, organization and dependency maps, decision-rights records, and escalation summaries;
- customer and business outcome contracts, metric definitions, strategy and constraint decisions;
- aggregate capacity, workload, queue, cycle-time, dependency-wait, rework, and maintenance summaries;
- portfolio decisions, horizon allocation, stop records, initiative outcomes, and learning reuse;
- service contracts, adoption, reliability, support, incident, exception, and retirement summaries;
- forum charters, decisions, follow-through, attendance ranges, asynchronous paths, and cancellation records;
- aggregate role, hiring, vacancy, management-span, attrition, workload, and key-person-risk evidence under minimum cell rules;
- transition plans, affected systems and work, permission changes, communications, review, adjustment, and rollback records.

## Privacy Boundary

Exclude names, personal emails, phone numbers, compensation details, performance reviews, private messages, health information, protected attributes, immigration status, exact locations, raw access logs, customer identities, and complete calendars when aggregate evidence is sufficient. Document purpose, authority or consent, access, joins, retention, minimum cell size, output aggregation, appeal or correction, and deletion handling. Do not infer individual performance from team outcomes.
