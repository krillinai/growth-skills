# Google Ads Audit Methods

Analyze only compatible supplied evidence. Use the account's own history and declared economics; do not turn external practices into thresholds.

## Dependency Order

```text
decision and qualified outcome
-> conversion and input integrity
-> channel-specific structure and delivery
-> search, creative, commerce, and destination evidence
-> business reconciliation and customer quality
-> bounded findings and decision plan
```

When an upstream dependency is unavailable, continue with supported observations but name the decisions that remain blocked.

## Structure And Delivery

Use stable IDs and preserve channel type. Inventory campaigns, ad groups, asset groups, ads, keywords, products, statuses, bidding strategies, budgets, networks, destinations, dates, and conversion scopes. Describe internal distributions rather than prescribing a universal structure.

Compatible descriptive measures include:

- spend share = entity spend / compatible total spend;
- CPM = spend / impressions x 1,000;
- click-through rate = clicks / impressions;
- cost per click = spend / clicks;
- platform conversion rate = included conversions / declared eligible interaction;
- cost per platform conversion = spend / included conversions;
- platform value per cost = included conversion value / spend;
- impression-share or lost-share measures exactly as exported and defined.

Show denominator, currency, date, channel type, conversion scope, and attribution context. A zero denominator yields `unavailable`. Optimization score, recommendations, quality score, ad strength, asset labels, and impression share are platform signals, not standalone business decisions.

## Search Evidence

Connect reported query, keyword, match type, negative status, ad, audience state, destination, conversion action, and qualified outcome. Separate:

- query text from inferred intent;
- keyword targeting from the query actually reported;
- traffic exclusion from customer qualification;
- platform conversions from downstream value.

Review concentration, query-category movement, mismatch, conflicts, unavailable query coverage, and landing continuity. Never invent a negative keyword list from a few rows or treat a broad match as inherently good or bad. Search terms can contain sensitive information; minimize, redact, and never re-identify.

## Creative And Automated Inventory

Map persona, customer problem, promise, proof, offer, CTA, format, concept, variant, destination, language, locale, and rights. A new value hypothesis may be a new concept; a headline length, crop, aspect ratio, or edit is usually a variant.

For responsive ads and automated campaign types, distinguish asset availability, eligibility, platform performance labels, combination visibility, and customer-outcome evidence. Do not infer the exact serving logic, search-query coverage, audience composition, or causal value of an asset from automation or labels alone.

## Shopping And Performance Max

Separate campaign, asset group, listing group, product, category, feed label, market, inventory, and business economics where evidence permits. Check product eligibility, price and availability continuity, destination state, category or product concentration, conversion definitions, new-customer settings, returns, margin, and retained value.

Do not diagnose a feed issue from ads data alone, equate attributed revenue with contribution, or assume Performance Max replaces channel-level incrementality evidence. Merchant Center and feed changes require separate evidence and authorization.

## Destinations

Compare the dated ad or query state with landing audience, promise, product, price, promotion, proof, CTA, language, locale, device path, form or checkout, and next value. A mismatch is verified only when both sides are captured and compatible. Speed, form, checkout, consent, and tracking problems remain inferred until measured.

## Measurement And Reconciliation

Build a ledger for conversion action, category, primary status, counting, origin, value, attribution model, windows, timezone, currency, tag or import path, identity, deduplication, consent, new-customer logic, refunds, maturation, and business definition.

Keep four layers separate:

1. delivery: interactions and cost reported by Google;
2. attribution: conversions and value Google assigns under declared settings;
3. business reconciliation: qualified customers, revenue, refunds, contribution, and retained value recorded elsewhere;
4. incrementality: outcomes a credible counterfactual estimates would not otherwise have occurred.

Do not force the layers to match. Explain differences in windows, conversion scope, attribution, view-through or engaged-view credit, imports, consent, currency, timezone, duplicates, cancellations, refunds, late outcomes, cross-device modeling, and unavailable records.

Only a credible experiment or causal design supports an incremental claim. Platform attribution, model selection, or an observed before-after change does not.

## Customer Quality And Economics

When definitions align, calculate qualified customer acquisition cost, refund-adjusted revenue, contribution, contribution after media, payback, and matured retained value from declared inputs. Expose cost scope and unavailable components. Do not infer margin, new-customer status, capacity, retention, or payback from Google conversion value.

## Reported Change Diagnosis

Translate "Google Ads performance changed" into metric, definition, entity, channel type, market, conversion scope, window, magnitude, and source. Keep the original statement `reported signal` until verified. Check:

1. exports, conversion settings, imports, attribution, tags, consent, and definition changes;
2. maturity, dates, seasonality, policy, product, price, inventory, and market events;
3. spend, bidding, network, device, geography, and delivery mix;
4. queries, keywords, negatives, products, assets, and destinations;
5. qualified customer, refund, contribution, capacity, and retained-value evidence;
6. causal evidence.

Return competing explanations and the smallest discriminating evidence. Do not jump from correlation to a bid, budget, keyword, campaign, or feed action.

## Findings

For each finding include observation, decision impact, source and capture date, evidence state, bounded action, completion proof, owner, and relative effort. Sequence the 30-day plan around dependencies. Use declared economics or prospectively agreed experiment rules; never fabricate targets or operating thresholds.
