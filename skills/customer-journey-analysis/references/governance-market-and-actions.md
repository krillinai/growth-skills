## Version And Decision Governance

For every journey artifact record ID, owner, authority, source, scope, definition, version, effective period, evidence cutoff, current or target designation, status, review, expiry, correction, supersession, archive and downstream consumers.

Keep research artifacts, current-state maps, target proposals, decision records, implementation plans, operational procedures, measurement definitions and outcome reviews separate. Do not overwrite current evidence with a target path, silently edit prior maps after outcomes, or let several teams use conflicting state definitions without a reconciliation record.

Use a contradiction ledger:

| Field or state | Artifact / owner / version | Exact definition or claim | Conflict class / affected scope | Resolution / canonical / coexistence | Acknowledgement / expiry / unresolved effect |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Classify definition, identity, role, sequence, channel, ownership, policy, permission, metric, market, evidence, interpretation, cause, target and authority conflicts. Reconcile only through attributable evidence and decision rights; otherwise keep the conflict visible and the affected journey `review-required`.

For a target journey, record alternative, customer value, burden, operating effect, dependencies, assumptions, risks, affected roles, metric versions, evidence plan, decision owner, required approvers and authorizers, implementation owners, staged verification, guardrails, stop, rollback, correction, review and expiry. A recommendation is not a decision or authorization.

## Specialist Routing And Handoffs

| Need | Route | Return artifact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| New interviews, surveys, reviews, support or behavioral synthesis | `customer-research` | Attributable findings, counterevidence, roles, context and limitations |
| Customer and account segment rules | `icp-segmentation` | Observable segment, inclusion, exclusion and differentiated path |
| Quantitative state transitions and loss | `funnel-analysis` | Compatible state, numerator, denominator, window, cohort and loss evidence |
| Company strategy and product-market motion | `growth-strategy`, `go-to-market-strategy` | Bounded choices, GTM unit, motion, buying path and gates |
| First and repeated product value | `activation`, `retention` | Value-state and cohort mechanisms with guardrails |
| Price, purchase, revenue and expansion | `monetization`, `pricing-and-packaging-strategy`, `customer-expansion-strategy` | Commercial and expansion contracts with customer protections |
| Referral, lifecycle and content interactions | `referral-program-design`, `lifecycle-marketing`, `content-strategy`, `copywriting` | Permissioned mechanism, message or asset specification |
| Commercial states and handoffs | `revops-audit`, `sales-enablement` | Versioned lifecycle, ownership, routing, reconciliation and buyer support |
| Metrics, identity and events | `growth-metrics-design`, `tracking-plan`, `growth-data-quality-audit` | Metric contract, event schema, identity, lineage and quality evidence |
| Attribution and causal validation | `attribution-analysis`, `experiment-design` | Bounded source credit or causal design and mature result |
| Product release and cross-functional adoption | `product-launch-strategy`, `growth-change-management` | Release or change contract, readiness, rollout and adoption evidence |
| Infrastructure, organization and operating changes | `growth-infrastructure-assessment`, `growth-organization-design` | Service, dependency, ownership, capacity and governance decision |

For each handoff record the exact question, affected journey fields, supplied sources and versions, expected artifact, accountable owner, requested date, evidence and acceptance criteria, state, returned version, limitation, contradiction, acknowledgement, expiry and residual gap.

Keep `requested`, `routed`, `acknowledged`, `accepted`, `executed`, `verified`, `adopted`, `outcome matured` and `closed` separate. Routing is not research, design, approval, execution, verification or impact.

## Market Transfer

Treat every market as a new customer, product, channel, operating, data and evidence boundary. Revalidate:

- customer entities, roles, jobs, triggers, alternatives, trust, accessibility, value, burdens and exits;
- product and offer versions, access, identity, entitlement, price, contract, payment, invoice, refund and support;
- discovery, channels, platforms, app distribution, partners, devices, connectivity, language, locale and content;
- buying roles, procurement, security, implementation, service, incidents, assistance, cancellation and re-entry;
- systems, vendors, data sources, identity, permissions, consent, storage, transfer, retention, metrics and maturity;
- local owners, capacity, operating hours, holidays, review, approval, authorization, correction, stop, rollback and residual obligations.

For mainland China, keep the market separate from Simplified Chinese language and CNY currency. Do not copy US personas, channels, platform availability, app stores, identity, payment, invoice, data practices, support, metrics, benchmarks, owners, approvals or authorization. Use current direct sources and locally accountable expertise. Do not make legal, tax, regulatory, compliance, provider-availability, approval, authorization or performance conclusions.

## Privacy, Fairness, And Sensitive Inference

Use purpose-limited, aggregate, redacted, pseudonymous, minimum-necessary evidence. Record purpose, decision, authority, consent or accountable basis, source, fields, population, access, allowed use, retention, correction, deletion, audit, small-cell suppression and owner.

Do not access or combine credentials, private messages, call recordings, health data, financial data, protected traits, employee notes, precise location, browsing history, device graphs, full purchase histories, raw support records or other sensitive histories merely to make a journey map complete.

Do not infer a named person's emotions, personality, vulnerability, intent, loyalty, compliance, value, churn, willingness to pay or motive as fact. Separate observed behavior, reported experience, interpretation, inference, prediction, recommendation, decision, action and outcome. Preserve uncertainty, counterevidence and the right to correction or appeal.

Do not score, rank, target, pressure, price, deny service to, discipline, dismiss, hire, fire or otherwise make consequential decisions about named customers, employees or other people. Route such work to accountable privacy, security, legal, HR, customer, risk and domain owners under separate authority, fairness review and safeguards.

## External-Action Specifications

Default to local analysis and approval-ready requests. Every requested external action needs:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Object | Exact source, research activity, system, journey state, product, touchpoint, message, metric, routing rule or record and version |
| Scope | Customer entity, role, eligible population, market, period, inclusions and exclusions |
| Authority | Requester, owner, reviewer, approver, authorizer, executor, verifier, stop and rollback authority |
| Preconditions | Evidence, rights, access, privacy, security, legal, product, data, capacity, support and operating gates |
| Controls | Dry run, least privilege, segregation, staging, cohorts, monitoring, audit, accessibility and customer protection |
| Failure | Guardrails, hold, stop, rollback, fallback, recovery, correction, communication, appeal and escalation |
| Completion | Technical, data, operational, customer, adoption, outcome and independent proof as applicable |
| Lifecycle | Review, expiry, retention, deletion, archive, decommissioning and residual owner |

Do not scrape, access authenticated systems, export or join data, conduct or contact research participants, assign owners, send messages, modify tracking, routing, product, price, permissions, support or records, launch tests, publish, spend or deploy while mapping or auditing a journey. Do not claim an action, adoption or result without attributable execution and verification evidence under task-level authority.
