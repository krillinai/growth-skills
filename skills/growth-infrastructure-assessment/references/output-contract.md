# Growth Infrastructure Assessment Output Contract

## Required Order

Return:

1. primary mode, decision, owner, customer outcome, repeated workflow, consumers, horizon, and action boundary;
2. scoped maturity, next disposition, and limiting evidence;
3. current capability and dependency map;
4. evidence, reliability, adoption, decision, customer, risk, incident, and cost ledger;
5. centralization record and local-versus-platform boundary;
6. shared-service contract and controls;
7. sourcing, migration, consolidation, or retirement comparison where relevant;
8. dependency-ordered 30-, 60-, and 90-day roadmap;
9. health scorecard, operating cadence, specialist handoffs, and pinned Playbook sources.

## Capability Map

| Capability | Layer | Workflow and consumers | Inputs and outputs | Owner | Upstream dependency | Current system or workaround | Failure and impact | Evidence | Maturity | Disposition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use `unavailable` rather than inferred internal architecture. Keep each layer independently diagnosable and show the first broken dependency.

## Centralization Record

| Condition | Evidence | Supported? | Local context required | Coordination cost | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Do not centralize accountable product, market, customer, creative, or risk judgment merely because shared computation is useful. State configuration, exception, override, and review boundaries.

## Sourcing Record

| Option | Functional fit | Data and privacy | Reliability and latency | Extensibility | Ownership and staffing | Observability and controls | Horizon TCO | Lock-in, migration, and exit | Risk and uncertainty | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- |

List formulas and one-time versus recurring assumptions. Keep unknown cost, opportunity cost, volume sensitivity, foreign exchange, tax, and vendor changes visible.

## Roadmap

| Window | Capability task | Dependency | Decision or failure addressed | Owner | Service or quality gate | Decision rule | Stop rule | Completion proof | Route |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

The roadmap should usually repair foundations, contract one workflow, pilot end to end, and prove health before adding self-service, automation, or more consumers.

## Health Scorecard

| Health group | Metric | Contract and source | Baseline | Target basis | Owner | Review cadence | Failure action |
| --- | --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Report activity with decision, customer, risk, and cost outcomes. Do not use adoption alone as infrastructure success.

## Privacy And External Action

Use redacted, aggregate, or pseudonymous evidence under a declared purpose and access boundary. Exclude credentials, direct identifiers, raw private payloads, unnecessary sensitive attributes, and unrelated history.

Local assessment does not authorize system access, procurement, exports, schema or permission changes, vendor installation, model or automation deployment, migrations, traffic or budget changes, live stop actions, publication, or customer contact. Record the required architecture, security, privacy, finance, owner, approval, validation, migration, rollback, monitoring, and audit controls as handoffs.
