# Sales Enablement

Turn positioning, customer, product, commercial, and buying evidence into versioned seller and buyer support for a specific decision. Help the right customer progress toward realized value; do not optimize asset count, seller activity, content opens, pipeline attribution, or win rate alone.

Read [enablement-contract.md](references/enablement-contract.md) before accepting product, segment, stage, claim, proof, competitive, commercial, or performance evidence. Read [assets-and-buying-motion.md](references/assets-and-buying-motion.md) before designing plays, discovery, qualification, narratives, demos, business cases, battlecards, buyer materials, or handoffs. Read [measurement-and-governance.md](references/measurement-and-governance.md) before auditing usage, effectiveness, training, feedback, localization, or lifecycle governance. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

## Select One Mode

| Mode | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `system` | Build or revise the enablement architecture, roles, asset portfolio, training, governance, and feedback system |
| `asset` | Create an evidence-bounded brief or local draft for one defined seller or buyer task |
| `audit` | Diagnose the current motion, assets, adoption, evidence quality, gaps, duplication, risk, and downstream effect |

Name one primary mode, decision, owner, buying motion, scope, horizon, and external-action boundary. Missing CRM, call, pipeline, customer, product, finance, or usage data is `unavailable`, not a reason to invent evidence or penalize the team.

## Freeze The Enablement Contract

Record product, edition, package, price and offer version; customer and buying unit; user, champion, buyer, payer, approver, blocker, administrator, procurement, security, legal, implementation, partner, and seller roles; buying situation, job, trigger, urgency, current alternative, no-decision condition, evaluation criteria, prerequisites, exclusions, value, proof, claims, risks, objections, route to market, market, language, locale, stage, buyer milestone, source, version, owner, approval, review date, distribution, measurement, privacy, and requested external action.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Never invent customer quotes, account facts, motives, urgency, budgets, authority, relationships, competitor capabilities, prices, win rates, implementation claims, outcomes, ROI, references, security status, legal conclusions, or approvals.

## Model Buyer Progress Before Assets

Keep seller activity and buyer progress separate. Define the buying questions, evidence, roles, risks, dependencies, and exit conditions at each material transition:

```text
recognized situation and alternative
-> qualified fit and mutual problem understanding
-> value, proof, and evaluation agreement
-> stakeholder, risk, business-case, and implementation alignment
-> accountable decision and handoff
-> first value and retained outcome feedback
```

Do not impose one universal stage framework, qualification acronym, discovery script, deck, or play. Use the user's existing process when it has observable definitions; otherwise propose a bounded model and label it `inferred`. A stage advances because buyer evidence changes, not because a seller sent an email, held a meeting, completed a field, or opened an opportunity.

## Design Decision-Bound Assets

For every asset specify one primary user, buying role, situation, buyer question, seller decision or behavior, stage and entry criteria, evidence inputs, approved claims, proof, counterevidence, required adaptation, prohibited use, output format, owner, approval, version, review trigger, distribution, feedback, and completion proof.

Build only assets that remove a demonstrated decision friction. Distinguish:

- seller enablement: play, qualification, discovery, narrative, demo, objection, competitive, pricing, negotiation, coaching, and implementation handoff;
- buyer enablement: evaluation guide, proof pack, business case, ROI or TCO input contract, mutual action plan, stakeholder brief, security or procurement response, and adoption plan.

A battlecard compares real alternatives under scoped conditions; it is not a page of attacks. A discovery guide elicits concrete past situations and disconfirming evidence; it is not a leading script. A demo proves the declared job under representative conditions; it is not a feature tour. A calculator exposes inputs, formulas, ranges, sources, exclusions, and uncertainty; it does not manufacture ROI.

Route final prose to `copywriting`, revision to `copy-editing`, positioning to `positioning`, segment rules to `icp-segmentation`, customer evidence acquisition to `customer-research`, offer and commercial architecture to `monetization`, outbound messages to `b2b-outbound-messaging`, and creative production to the relevant capability.

## Preserve Claims, Proof, And Competitive Truth

Maintain one atomic claim record with exact wording, source, product and market scope, date, proof, conditions, limitations, counterevidence, approval, expiry, prohibited stronger wording, and allowed assets. Separate product demonstration, technical record, customer report, customer outcome, benchmark, certification, security response, and commercial assumption.

For competitive guidance, start from the prospect's actual alternative: inaction, existing process, manual work, service, general tool, adjacent product, or named competitor. State where each alternative works, where it may fail for the scoped situation, and what evidence supports the comparison. Do not use private competitor information, invented feature gaps, stale pricing, fear, ridicule, or unverifiable claims.

## Measure Enablement As A Causal Chain

Keep availability, findability, access, use, comprehension, seller behavior, buyer progression, decision quality, commercial outcome, implementation, activation, retention, and causal effect separate. Asset use is selected by deal difficulty, seller skill, segment, stage, and manager behavior; correlation with wins does not prove impact.

Use compatible entities, opportunities, buying units, stages, segments, versions, owners, cohorts, windows, maturity, definitions, sources, and cost boundaries. Preserve absolute counts, no-decision, disqualification, discounting, cycle time, forecast error, implementation failure, activation, renewal, churn, support, and trust alongside win rate.

Prefer the strongest feasible evidence: task observation, knowledge or skill checks, artifact QA, staged rollout, randomized recommendation where ethical, team or manager cluster designs, matched comparisons, interrupted time series, and downstream cohort joins. Route causal design to `experiment-design`.

## Operate A Living System

Define intake, evidence review, prioritization, creation, claim and specialist approval, localization, publication, permissions, training, findability, use capture, field feedback, outcome reconciliation, update, exception, incident, deprecation, and archive. Assign one accountable owner per asset and one source of truth per approved claim or commercial input.

Do not centralize every judgment. Standardize shared evidence, contracts, taxonomy, versioning, and controls; preserve seller, solution, partner, and market discretion where local context changes the decision. Retire duplicates and stale assets; do not keep them merely because they were expensive to produce.
