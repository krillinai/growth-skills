# Assets And Buying Motion

## Buyer Progress Model

Adapt these states to the actual motion rather than imposing the labels:

| Buyer state | Decision evidence | Common seller support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Situation recognized | Trigger, consequence, current alternative, urgency | Insight, qualification, problem framing |
| Fit explored | Job, prerequisites, stakeholders, exclusions, value conditions | Discovery guide, fit rubric, play |
| Evaluation defined | Criteria, alternatives, proof needed, decision process | Narrative, demo plan, evaluation guide, battlecard |
| Value and risk tested | Proof, technical fit, business case, implementation, trust | Proof pack, pilot plan, ROI or TCO contract, security pack |
| Alignment built | Roles, authority, timeline, dependencies, unresolved risks | Mutual action plan, stakeholder brief, procurement handoff |
| Decision and transition | Accountable choice, commercial terms, implementation owner | Proposal requirements, implementation and success handoff |
| Value realized | First value, repeated value, outcome, support, renewal evidence | Adoption plan, success evidence, feedback to qualification |

A seller action can support a state but cannot prove it. Record observable buyer evidence and unresolved conditions.

## Asset Contract

Every asset requires:

- asset ID, type, title, status, owner, reviewers, version, effective and review dates;
- primary seller or buyer user, role, segment, situation, motion, stage, and entry criteria;
- one decision, question, friction, behavior, or deliverable it supports;
- source evidence, claims, proof, counterevidence, alternatives, risks, and limitations;
- required inputs and explicit unavailable fields;
- output structure, format, channel, accessibility, localization, and adaptation rules;
- prohibited claims, audiences, situations, edits, sharing, and use;
- approval, distribution, permissions, source of truth, findability, and access;
- usage event, feedback, downstream evidence, cost, owner, and retirement trigger;
- completion proof and dependent handoffs.

Do not make one asset serve every role, stage, segment, market, and product when their decisions or evidence differ.

## Seller Enablement Patterns

### Sales Play

Define situation, fit and exclusions, buying roles, alternatives, value and proof, stage evidence, discovery, objections, next decisions, specialist handoffs, risks, and measures. A play guides judgment; it is not a script to force every deal through.

### Qualification And Discovery

Ask about concrete past or current situations, actions, alternatives, consequences, constraints, stakeholders, decision process, implementation conditions, and failure evidence. Record disconfirming answers and disqualify poor fit. Do not embed the product benefit or assumed pain in every question.

### Narrative And Demo

Connect market or workflow context, actual alternatives, desired state, evaluation criteria, differentiated value, capabilities, proof, tradeoffs, and next decision. Design demos around the declared job and representative conditions. Record data, environment, permissions, reliability, accessibility, and fallback; do not use a curated happy path as universal proof.

### Competitive Guidance

Compare scoped alternatives on buyer criteria, strengths, limits, switching cost, migration, risk, and proof. Cite public or supplied sources with dates. Include when the alternative is the better fit. Never invent private roadmap, pricing, security, customer, performance, or legal claims.

### Objection And Negotiation Guidance

Separate question, misunderstanding, real risk, missing proof, product gap, poor fit, price or package issue, authority issue, and no-decision. Provide evidence and next diagnostic action, not manipulative rebuttals. Route commercial architecture to `monetization`.

## Buyer Enablement Patterns

### Evaluation Guide

Help the buying group define the job, criteria, alternatives, proof, implementation conditions, risks, and decision process. Avoid criteria designed solely to make the seller win.

### Business Case And ROI Or TCO Contract

Expose baseline, entity, population, formula, source, horizon, currency, cost boundary, adoption, implementation, uncertainty, scenario, owner, and validation plan. Keep customer inputs, benchmarks, predictions, and observed outcomes separate. Never fabricate a baseline or guarantee a return.

### Mutual Action Plan

Record mutual outcome, buyer and seller owners, dependencies, decisions, evidence, dates supplied by owners, risks, exit criteria, and implementation transition. It is not a seller-controlled close-date checklist.

### Risk, Security, And Procurement Pack

Use current approved specialist sources. Record scope, product and deployment version, owner, freshness, confidentiality, allowed recipient, unanswered item, and escalation. Do not answer legal, security, privacy, hosting, accessibility, procurement, or compliance questions from general marketing copy.

### Implementation And Success Handoff

Preserve promised use case, scope, success condition, prerequisites, stakeholder commitments, risks, configuration, proof, unresolved gaps, commercial assumptions, first-value event, owner, and review date. Do not hide exceptions or overpromises to protect a forecast.
