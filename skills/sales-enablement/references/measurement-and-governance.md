# Sales Enablement Measurement And Governance

## Measurement Layers

Keep these layers separate:

| Layer | Question | Example evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Coverage | Is support available for the priority decision? | Decision-to-asset map, known gaps |
| Findability and access | Can the intended user find and use the approved version? | Search task, permissions, retrieval time |
| Use | Was the asset opened, shared, completed, or referenced? | Versioned usage event with scope |
| Comprehension and skill | Can the user apply it correctly? | Scenario assessment, artifact review, observed task |
| Behavior | Did qualification, discovery, proof, or handoff change? | Call or workflow coding with rubric |
| Buyer progression | Did buyer evidence and decision quality improve? | Stage evidence, no-decision, risk resolution |
| Commercial outcome | Did compatible mature opportunities change? | Wins, losses, discount, cycle, forecast, contribution |
| Realized value | Did promises become implementation, activation, retention, and renewal? | Use-case cohorts and handoff quality |
| Causal effect | What changed because of enablement? | Credible assignment or bounded comparison |

Do not call logins, opens, downloads, training completion, seller satisfaction, pipeline influenced, or content-associated wins impact by themselves.

## Compatibility And Bias

Before comparison, require compatible product, segment, buying unit, motion, market, seller role and tenure, manager, stage, asset version, opportunity source, deal complexity, price, cohort, window, maturity, and outcome definition.

Preserve selection: stronger sellers may use more assets; difficult deals may trigger more content; managers may assign training to weak teams; successful opportunities may create better documentation. Do not infer causality from associated win rate, deal size, cycle time, or pipeline.

## Evidence Designs

Choose according to risk and feasibility:

- task observation and artifact QA for findability, comprehension, and correct use;
- predeclared scenario or knowledge assessments for training;
- staggered release or phased market rollout;
- randomized recommendation or access where ethical and operationally valid;
- team, manager, or market cluster assignment when spillovers dominate;
- matched comparison or interrupted time series with assumptions explicit;
- downstream implementation, activation, retention, and renewal joins.

Preserve assignment, exposure, actual use, version, contamination, manager effects, and maturity. Route statistical design and interpretation to `experiment-design`.

## Portfolio Audit

Inventory each asset and assign one disposition: `keep`, `update`, `consolidate`, `split`, `localize`, `retire`, or `needs evidence`. Evaluate decision relevance, source quality, claim freshness, role and stage fit, actual alternative, duplication, access, usage, correct application, feedback, downstream outcome, risk, ownership, and maintenance cost.

Asset volume, production cost, executive sponsorship, or historical popularity is not a reason to keep an item. Preserve redirects, superseded versions, dependencies, citations, and field communication when retiring.

## Operating Flow

```text
field or buyer evidence -> triage and priority
-> source and claim review -> asset contract and creation
-> specialist approval -> localization and publication
-> access and training -> use and feedback
-> buyer, commercial, and realized-value evidence
-> update, split, consolidate, or retire
```

For each transition specify owner, input, output, service expectation, approval, exception, audit artifact, and completion proof. Reserve capacity for updates, evidence maintenance, localization, experiments, and retirement, not only new assets.

## Governance

Maintain:

- one registry for assets, owners, versions, status, scope, sources, approvals, access, review, and disposition;
- one atomic claim and proof registry linked to every reusable asset;
- controlled commercial, security, legal, privacy, and product inputs owned by specialists;
- a field feedback queue with evidence, urgency, affected scope, decision, and closure;
- a win, loss, no-decision, implementation, activation, churn, and exception feedback loop;
- permissions, confidentiality, localization, accessibility, rights, and retention controls;
- incident, correction, withdrawal, supersession, archive, and audit procedures.

Do not let decks, chat threads, personal drives, CRM notes, or local translations silently become the source of truth.
