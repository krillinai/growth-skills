# Network Effects Strategy

Determine whether additional relevant participation changes product value for other participants inside a declared network unit. Optimize durable participant value, quality, trust, and contribution, not registrations, seats, invitations, transactions, content, data, integrations, or switching friction alone.

Read [network-contract.md](references/network-contract.md) before accepting participant, graph, interaction, value, unit, retention, or defense claims. Read [mechanisms-and-evidence.md](references/mechanisms-and-evidence.md) before classifying or measuring a network effect, marketplace liquidity, data or AI reinforcement, or defensibility. Read [cold-start-and-expansion.md](references/cold-start-and-expansion.md) before seeding, solving a hard side, or replicating a network. Read [governance-and-output.md](references/governance-and-output.md) before proposing experiments, rankings, reputation, moderation, incentives, data use, or a roadmap. Use [playbook-sources.md](references/playbook-sources.md) to cite the pinned Growth Playbook basis.

| Mode | Use |

## Freeze The Network Contract

Record participant and nonparticipant roles; customer, user, account, workspace, supply, demand, content, data, agent, and payer entities; network unit and boundary; core interaction and success state; eligibility, availability, relevance, identity, hierarchy, deduplication, entry, exit, re-entry, migration, and unknown rules; participant value before and after the interaction; added-participation mechanism; density, match, completion, quality, time, repeat value, retention, contribution, cost, incentives, concentration, multi-homing, portability, switching, disintermediation, congestion, trust, safety, privacy, permissions, versions, sources, evidence states, counterevidence, owners, and requested actions.

Use `verified`, `inferred`, `unavailable`, or `not applicable` for evidence-bearing rows. A stakeholder statement without inspectable support may be a `reported signal` with no evidence state. Do not penalize inaccessible private evidence or repeatedly request raw graphs and identities.

## Distinguish The Mechanisms

```text
virality or referral: participation spreads to additional participants
growth loop: one value cycle produces a qualified input to a later cycle
network effect: added relevant participation changes value for other participants
scale economy: unit cost changes with operating scale
switching cost: leaving becomes costly
defensibility: competitors have difficulty reproducing the valuable system
```

These can reinforce one another but are not interchangeable. Preserve a reported K-factor, transaction count, user total, seat count, data volume, or retention measure without treating it as network-effect proof. Route detailed propagation and closure work to `growth-loop-design`.

## Assign One Bounded Verdict

| Verdict | Gate |
| --- | --- |
| `verified positive` | Compatible evidence shows added relevant participation improves declared participant value within the scoped unit and period, with material quality and risk visible |
| `conditional or mixed` | Value improves for some sides, states, or density bands but weakens or remains unresolved elsewhere |
| `negative or congested` | Compatible evidence shows added participation materially reduces relevance, quality, safety, economics, trust, or participant value |
| `hypothesis` | A plausible marginal-value mechanism exists but operating or causal evidence is incomplete |
| `not a network effect` | The supported mechanism is propagation, scale, switching, brand, workflow, data collection, or another mechanism without participant-driven value change |
| `not assessable` | The decision, unit, entities, interaction, value definition, or evidence boundary is too incomplete or incompatible |

Do not score by default. If explicitly requested, score only declared verified checks, publish weights and deductions, and show evidence coverage separately. A mechanism verdict does not certify incrementality, future persistence, or defensibility.

## Audit In Dependency Order

1. `value and unit`: participant value, nonparticipant effects, smallest functional network, and core interaction;
2. `sides and hard side`: eligibility, availability, motivation, scarcity, quality, trust, and role-specific economics;
3. `interaction`: discovery, matching, response, completion, reputation, repeat use, and cross-side retention;
4. `marginal mechanism`: how one added relevant participant or contribution changes another participant's outcome;
5. `quality and economics`: contribution, subsidy dependence, support, moderation, fraud, congestion, and externalities;
6. `durability`: multi-homing, unique supply or relationships, portability, switching, disintermediation, concentration, and competitor reproducibility;
7. `causality`: counterfactual, interference, equilibrium, carryover, exposure, and maturity;
8. `governance`: permissions, privacy, safety, fairness, transparency, appeals, incidents, stop rules, and retirement.

Keep every atomic market or functional unit separate until its definitions and evidence are compatible. Aggregate users, inventory, seats, content, prompts, or transactions can hide fragmented or failing networks.

## Cold-Start And Expansion Rules

For cold start, choose a narrow white-hot center where need is urgent and a complete interaction can become dense. Treat the hard side as a local, stage-specific hypothesis. Seed useful availability or coordination only when it improves the core interaction; profile, listing, content, or subsidy volume is not success.

For expansion, treat a working unit as a prior, not proof. Preserve local density, regulation, identity, payments, behavior, supply shape, competition, quality, economics, and operating capacity. Require each new unit to graduate independently before bridging or further replication. Never copy a ratio, threshold, ranking, incentive, or forecast as universal.

## Diagnose Defense Without Captivity

Separate current participant value from competitive defense. Audit unique supply, relationships, reputation, workflow position, interoperability, portability, multi-homing, switching behavior, frequency, trust, data rights, learning quality, cost, and competitor reproducibility. Large scale can coexist with weak defense.

Do not recommend blocking export, concealing commercial intent, degrading nonparticipants, coercing invitations, trapping reputation, or creating artificial switching costs. Captivity, dark patterns, and ungoverned graph control are not healthy network effects.

For data or AI claims, trace permissioned interaction data through coverage, labels, learning, product change, exposure, accepted customer outcome, repeat value, and later data. Data or prompt volume is not improvement; improvement is not automatically a growth loop; and either can be reproducible by competitors.

## Measure And Test Carefully

Use local density, available supply, qualified demand, match and completion, time to value, quality, repeat behavior, side-specific retention, contribution, concentration, multi-homing, disintermediation, trust, safety, congestion, and marginal participant value. Compare compatible units, sides, cohorts, windows, maturity, product versions, incentives, and market conditions.

Observational slopes and before-after growth can nominate mechanisms but do not prove causality. Networks create interference, equilibrium, saturation, congestion, and carryover. Define the estimand and exposure unit, then route cluster, geographic, switchback, saturation, phased, or other causal design details to `experiment-design`.
