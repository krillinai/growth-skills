# Network Contract

## Decision And Scope

Capture independently:

- decision, owner, approver, product, market, horizon, cadence, and allowed action;
- participant, nonparticipant, customer, user, account, workspace, supply, demand, creator, consumer, content, data, agent, administrator, buyer, and payer roles;
- candidate network unit and boundary: geographic, categorical, professional, social, organizational, workflow, product, platform, or time-based;
- core interaction, participant job, value before interaction, success state, recurring value, and failure state;
- eligibility, availability, relevance, identity, hierarchy, deduplication, entry, exit, re-entry, migration, deletion, unknown, and cross-unit rules;
- added-participation hypothesis, affected participant, expected value change, delay, density band, and countervailing mechanism;
- interaction, quality, retention, economics, incentive, trust, safety, privacy, concentration, congestion, and defense measures;
- source artifact, owner, collection period, extraction date, version, evidence state, counterevidence, limitation, and privacy boundary.

Split the assessment when participant roles, units, interactions, value definitions, product versions, incentives, markets, maturity, or operating conditions are incompatible.

## Core Terms

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Network unit | Smallest functional boundary in which participants can repeatedly affect one another's value |
| Core interaction | Observable exchange or coordinated job that creates participant value |
| Relevant participation | Eligible, available, trustworthy participation capable of improving the interaction |
| Hard side | Locally constrained participant, contribution, trust, or capability whose absence limits the interaction |
| Liquidity | Probability and speed of completing a qualified interaction at acceptable quality inside a declared unit |
| Density | Relevant available participation relative to the interaction opportunity, not registrations alone |
| Network effect | Change in participant value caused or credibly associated with changing relevant participation |
| Multi-homing | Participant use of multiple competing networks or tools |
| Disintermediation | Participants moving a relationship or transaction outside the network |
| Congestion | Added participation degrades discovery, quality, speed, economics, safety, or trust |
| Defensibility | Difficulty competitors face reproducing the valuable participant system, not captivity alone |

## Evidence States

Use:

- `verified`: directly supported by an attributable inspectable artifact;
- `inferred`: reasoned from named evidence, with assumptions and alternatives visible;
- `unavailable`: decision-relevant but absent, inaccessible, declined, stale, or incompatible;
- `not applicable`: outside the declared scope, with reason;
- `reported signal`: attributable statement without inspectable support; leave evidence state blank.

Public pages, reviews, terms, help docs, integrations, listings, visible profiles, policies, and dated claims can verify visible mechanics. They cannot verify private identities, graph edges, availability, matching, quality, retention, economics, incentives, multi-homing, causal value change, or defense.

## Verdict Contract

Assign one verdict to one unit, side set, product version, density band, and period. Record the observed or proposed value change, evidence, counterevidence, excluded units, limiting dependency, and causal status.

`verified positive` requires compatible evidence of improved participant value as relevant participation increases. `conditional or mixed` preserves side, unit, state, or density differences. `negative or congested` requires evidence of worsening value. `hypothesis` names a plausible mechanism without sufficient operating proof. `not a network effect` preserves another supported mechanism. `not assessable` preserves an incomplete contract.

## Privacy Minimum

Prefer schemas, redacted interaction examples, local aggregate cohorts, suppressed graph statistics, mature quality and retention summaries, and experiment readouts. Do not request raw names, contacts, messages, graph edges, exact locations, payment details, credentials, sensitive attributes, or small cells when an aggregate changes the decision.

Document purpose, permissible use, consent, minimization, access, joins, retention, deletion, portability, transfer, small-cell suppression, review, and incident handling. This Skill does not determine legal compliance.
