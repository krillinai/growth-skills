# Governance And Output

## Negative Effects And Externalities

Audit benefits and costs by participant side, nonparticipant, unit, density band, and time. Common failure mechanisms include congestion, search overload, spam, harassment, fraud, low-quality supply, adverse selection, creator dilution, crowding, price pressure, participant earnings decline, moderation load, reputation error, discrimination, exclusion, privacy loss, disintermediation, concentration, and ecosystem dependency.

For each finding record observed condition, source, affected group and unit, mechanism candidates, counterevidence, confidence, severity or `needs evidence`, owner, immediate containment, guardrail, stop rule, and completion proof. Do not call every complaint a network effect or infer one root cause from a correlated trend.

## Causal Measurement

Define the estimand before the design: value change for which participant, caused by what change in relevant participation, within which unit and density band, over which horizon. Separate assignment from actual exposure and preserve mature quality, retention, contribution, trust, and safety outcomes.

Networks violate independent-user assumptions through shared supply, inventory, ranking, prices, reputation, graph neighbors, equilibrium, congestion, learning, and carryover. Consider cluster, geographic, switchback, saturation, phased, encouragement, or other designs only when their assumptions fit. Route power, randomization, sample-ratio mismatch, statistical analysis, and readout to `experiment-design`.

Observational designs may use discontinuities, natural changes, matched units, fixed effects, or dose-response patterns when justified, but must retain assumptions, pre-trends, spillovers, time effects, and alternative explanations. Do not convert association into causality.

## Decision Matrix

For each action include:

```text
decision and affected network unit
mechanism and limiting dependency
owner and approver
required evidence and current state
participant and nonparticipant value
expected leading and matured signals
quality, trust, safety, privacy, fairness, and economic guardrails
interference and counterfactual plan
capacity, cost, dependency, and reversibility
advance, hold, pause, stop, rollback, and retirement rules
completion proof and review date
```

Prioritize foundational unit, identity, interaction, value, and evidence repairs before ranking, reputation, incentives, automation, AI, or expansion.

## Output Contract

Deliver:

1. decision contract and evidence inventory;
2. bounded verdict and evidence coverage;
3. participant-side, nonparticipant, network-unit, and core-interaction map;
4. positive, null, negative, and competing marginal-value mechanisms;
5. hard-side, local liquidity, quality, retention, economics, and congestion analysis;
6. multi-homing, portability, switching, disintermediation, concentration, and competitor-reproducibility analysis;
7. cold-start, replication, bridge, or containment plan as applicable;
8. causal measurement and governance plan;
9. dependency-ordered roadmap with owners, gates, stop rules, completion proof, and unresolved evidence.

Use tables where repeated fields matter. Do not hide unavailable evidence in prose or combine incompatible units. Show formulas, substituted values, units, windows, and residuals for every calculation.

## Change And Automation Gate

Before changing ranking, reputation, matching, incentives, permissions, portability, graph use, data collection, moderation, or expansion, require a versioned contract, affected units and participants, owner, approvals, privacy and safety review, test boundary, monitoring, incident path, fallback, rollback, audit log, and retirement rule.

Automate only stable, observable decisions with trustworthy inputs, bounded exceptions, human override, and tested failure handling. Do not automate a disputed identity, unsafe match, uncalibrated reputation score, opaque enforcement action, or unowned network policy.

## Completion Proof

Accept attributable interaction cohorts, quality and retention reports, reconciled local-unit metrics, versioned configuration, experiment readout, participant research, incident record, moderation audit, access review, portability test, subsidy taper result, or other artifact appropriate to the decision.

Do not accept a strategy slide, network diagram, user total, press claim, tool list, dashboard tile without definitions, or task status as proof of a network effect or completed improvement.
