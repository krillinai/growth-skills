# Cold Start And Expansion

## White-Hot Center

Choose the narrowest participant, job, unit, and time boundary where:

- the problem is urgent and recurring enough to observe;
- one complete interaction can deliver material value;
- the constrained side can be reached and retained responsibly;
- relevance, trust, quality, service, and support can be maintained;
- density can be achieved without misleading inventory or coercion;
- economics and operating capacity can be measured;
- failure can be contained and the unit can be stopped.

Broad total addressable market, registrations, waitlists, profiles, listings, integrations, or subsidized volume do not define a white-hot center.

## Hard-Side Diagnosis

The hard side may be supply, demand, expertise, trust, permission, reputation, content quality, integration, workflow authority, payment, or another scarce input. It can change by unit, time, category, stage, and density band.

For each candidate record scarcity, availability, motivation, opportunity cost, onboarding, time to first interaction, quality, retention, participant economics, trust, and alternatives. Distinguish lack of supply from poor discovery, low demand, weak conversion, bad matching, unreliable completion, and measurement error.

## Seeding Rules

Seed the interaction, not a vanity inventory. Possible bounded approaches include manually curated supply, single-player utility, owned inventory, narrow partnerships, scheduled aggregation, workflow tools, trusted communities, or temporary value-aligned incentives. Treat each as a hypothesis with owner, cost, quality, expiry, taper, stop rule, and transition to an endogenous network.

Do not create fake participants, deceptive availability, coerced invitations, unsafe matches, unlicensed content, unauthorized contact uploads, or permanent subsidies presented as organic network strength.

## Graduation Gates

Use stages such as:

| Stage | Graduation evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Problem and unit | Attributable urgent job, bounded unit, participant roles, and success state |
| Interaction viability | Repeated qualified interactions with acceptable quality and trust |
| Local liquidity | Stable availability, match, completion, time to value, and repeat behavior in the unit |
| Durability | Mature side-specific retention, contribution, lower subsidy dependence, and controlled harm |
| Repeatability | A second comparable unit reaches its own gates under an explicit adaptation record |
| Bridge or scale | Cross-unit interaction adds value without draining local liquidity or creating unmanaged risk |

Define numeric thresholds only from supplied baselines, capacity, economics, risk tolerance, and decision needs. Do not invent universal targets.

## Replication Contract

For every candidate unit record local problem, participants, hard side, density, regulation, platform, identity, payments, language, behavior, competition, supply shape, operating capacity, service level, costs, quality, safety, and evidence. Classify each element as invariant, adapted, unknown, or incompatible relative to the source unit.

Treat source-unit evidence as a prior. Pilot in dependency order, preserve local cohorts, and require independent value, quality, retention, and economics before calling the unit repeatable. Do not pool units merely to make totals look healthy.

## Bridging Networks

Connect units only when the bridge creates participant value. Audit cross-unit relevance, identity, permissions, discovery, quality, governance, load, fraud, concentration, and whether bridging drains or congests local interactions. A global directory or shared login does not prove that local networks form one functional network.

## Pause And Stop Rules

Pause or stop when verified evidence shows worsening safety, trust, quality, participant economics, fraud, regulatory or policy risk, moderation overload, subsidy dependence, disintermediation, local liquidity, retention, or contribution beyond the declared tolerance. Also stop expansion when evidence coverage cannot support the next irreversible decision.

Record containment, owner, affected unit, participant communication owner, rollback, evidence preservation, re-entry criteria, and review date. This Skill specifies the plan but does not execute it.
